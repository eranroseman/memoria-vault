# Claim

Much of the apparent cardiovascular benefit of the Mediterranean diet in cohort studies could be confounded: people who eat this way also tend to exercise more, smoke less, and be wealthier, so the diet may be a marker of a healthy life rather than its cause.

# Evidence

- A systematic review grading diet–heart evidence flagged that observational associations, including for dietary patterns, are vulnerable to lifestyle confounding and cannot establish causation on their own [mente2009].
