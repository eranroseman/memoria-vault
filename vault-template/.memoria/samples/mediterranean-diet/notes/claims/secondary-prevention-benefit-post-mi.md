# Claim

In patients who have already had a heart attack, a Mediterranean-style diet reduces recurrent cardiac events — this is secondary prevention.

# Evidence

- The Lyon Diet Heart Study randomized 605 post-myocardial-infarction patients and found a large reduction in cardiac death and non-fatal recurrent infarction on a Mediterranean-style diet [delorgeril1999].

# Connections

This is the secondary-prevention case for the headline claim: it `supports` [[meddiet-reduces-major-cv-events|A Mediterranean diet reduces major cardiovascular events in high-risk adults]] in an already-sick population. It is narrower than primary prevention — the benefit here is measured in people who were already patients.
