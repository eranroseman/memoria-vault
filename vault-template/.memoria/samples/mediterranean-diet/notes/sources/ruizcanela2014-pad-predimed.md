# In my words

This PREDIMED analysis asks whether the Mediterranean-diet intervention was associated with fewer peripheral artery disease events, not just the headline composite of myocardial infarction, stroke, and cardiovascular death. It extends the same high-risk Spanish trial family into another vascular outcome. That makes the cardiovascular signal broader, but not more transportable: the population and intervention context are still PREDIMED.

# Worth distilling

- PREDIMED reported fewer peripheral artery disease events in Mediterranean-diet intervention arms than in the control group.
- The result broadens the vascular-outcome picture while staying inside the same high-risk Spanish trial context.

# Tensions

This supports the broader vascular reading of [[meddiet-reduces-major-cv-events|A Mediterranean diet reduces major cardiovascular events in high-risk adults]], but it does not answer the sample hub's open gap about low-risk primary prevention or non-Mediterranean populations.
