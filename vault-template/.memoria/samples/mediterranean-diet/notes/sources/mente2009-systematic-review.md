# In my words

This systematic review graded the strength of evidence linking dietary factors to coronary heart disease across cohort studies and trials. It found that a Mediterranean dietary pattern was among the few with strong supporting evidence — but it was careful to separate the observational signal from causal proof, noting that people who eat this way also tend to be healthier in other ways (more exercise, less smoking, higher income), which can confound the association.

# Worth distilling

- A Mediterranean dietary *pattern* had some of the strongest evidence among diet–heart-disease links reviewed.
- Observational diet–heart associations are vulnerable to confounding by overall healthy lifestyle, so cohort evidence alone cannot establish causation.

# Tensions

This is the deliberate counterweight in the corpus: it cautions against reading the cohort evidence (trichopoulou2003, sofi2010) as causal. It does *not* overturn the trial evidence (estruch2018, delorgeril1999) — which is exactly why the trials matter, and why the randomization seam in PREDIMED is worth tracking.
