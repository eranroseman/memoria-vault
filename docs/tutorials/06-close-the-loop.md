# Tutorial 06: Close the loop

*Session 3 of 3 · Close the loop and graduate.*

**You will end with:** the gap your draft exposed turned into a discovery task that re-triggers reading — one full turn of the loop, the move that makes the vault compound.

**Time:** 15–20 minutes.

**You will use:** the Inbox and the command palette.

**Prerequisite:** [Tutorial 05: Verify it holds](05-verify-it-holds.md) complete — a defended draft under `projects/<slug>/`, and the gap card from the map still in your Inbox.

You oriented, captured, distilled, drafted, and verified — one full pass. But Memoria is a loop, not a line: the gap you produced your way into is the reason to read again. Closing that loop is this short tutorial; making the vault your own is the next.

---

## Step 1 — Find the gap card again

Open the **Inbox** space → **Needs me** and find the **`gap` card** the map surfaced in Tutorial 01 and verification confirmed in Tutorial 05:

> "Corpus has no evidence on **primary prevention in low-risk people**, and nothing on whether the benefit **transports beyond Mediterranean populations and diets**." — `certainty: likely`, with the full honesty body

A gap card is a *proposal*, not a verdict — "your corpus is missing X" — carrying the same honesty body as a candidate card. You drafted *around* this gap (your section is honest about who its evidence covers), and that honesty is exactly what tells you where to read next. The gap is not a flaw in the draft; it is the draft pointing at the next source.

---

## Step 2 — Hand the gap back to discovery

For a gap you agree with, hand it straight back as a discovery task. Run `Cmd/Ctrl-P` → **Memoria: delegate task** → `catalog`, or ask the Co-PI to route it. Phrase it as a research question, not keywords:

> "My corpus on Mediterranean diet and heart health only covers high-risk adults in Mediterranean populations. Find sources on **primary prevention in low-risk people**, and on whether the benefit **transports to non-Mediterranean populations and diets** — what am I missing?"

The Librarian searches, compares hits against your Catalog so nothing you already hold is resurfaced, and lands **`candidate` cards** in the Inbox — judged keep-or-skip exactly as you did when you brought in your own source. The full mechanics are in [Find new sources](../how-to-guides/library/find-new-sources.md).

**Watch what just happened.** A *Produce* artifact (the draft) raised a gap; the gap became an *Accumulate* task (discovery); kept candidates become sources, sources become claims, and the next map or verify pass finds the next gap. That is one full turn of the loop — and it compounds, because each turn leaves the corpus denser than the last ([The knowledge cycle](../explanation/knowledge/knowledge-cycle.md)).

You re-enter this loop from wherever your work is — a paper in hand, a deadline, a draft, a thin spot. There is no "first" step, because it is a loop, not a line. The next tutorial steps you off the scaffold to run it on your own material.

---

## What you have

- The gap turned back into a discovery task — your first full, self-sustaining turn of the loop
- The shape of the cycle in view: producing exposes a gap, the gap pulls new reading, new reading feeds the next produce — and the corpus compounds
