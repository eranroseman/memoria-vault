> **Vocabulary.** This doc uses **OKF** nouns for portable bundle roots (§6.1). A **Bundle** is the unit of distribution; a **Memoria workspace** contains three OKF-compatible bundle roots — `catalog/`, `knowledge/`, and `capabilities/` (§6.2). The Obsidian vault is `knowledge/`. A **Concept** is **one markdown doc + frontmatter** (a source, note, hub, person, operation…), distinguished by its `type`; a Concept may carry **referenced attachments** (a source's `content.md`/`raw/`) — the Concept is the *doc*, not the folder. A **Link** is a Memoria typed cross-reference between Concepts; a **Citation** is a Link to a backing source. Legacy terms are retired **only as Memoria-domain synonyms for Concept/Link/Bundle** — `record` (the durable substrate), `entity` (the catalog person/org/venue kind + PROV vocabulary), and `vault` (Obsidian's name for `knowledge/`) stay valid **in their own scopes**; `relation` stays retired in favour of Link. These nouns and the markdown+frontmatter+git substrate are **Memoria's own** (a 4th independent re-derivation — see Provenance). M0 validated a local OKF-compatible contract and round-trip; external OKF tooling remains a boundary check when available.

## 0. What Memoria is

**Memoria is an opinionated, personal thinking-and-writing tool.** The identity is the *activity*, not the artifact: a tool for the two human activities — **thinking** and **writing** — and a paper is simply what those produce. It is not a knowledge *base* you consume, and not a paper *factory*; the Bundle is the **workshop**, produced scholarship is the **product**.

Each word is load-bearing:
- **opinionated** — the structure, Concept types, and loop are the design, not configurable surfaces.
- **personal** — one researcher, private, unfiltered, owns judgment; thinking is separate from communication. Not a team tool.
- **thinking** — read → annotate → connect → synthesize → find the gap. The ZK/Razlogova cognitive work.
- **writing** — draft from the Concepts; the output.
- **tool** — the human wields it. The LLM drives the labour; the human supplies the **direction**.

### Genesis
Memoria began as an attempt to combine **Razlogova's *Doing History with Zotero and Obsidian*** (a manual knowledge-*production* methodology: sources → atomic notes → synthesis → writing, driven by the scholar's judgment) with **Karpathy's LLM wiki** (an LLM-driven, compounding markdown store). The divergence from Karpathy is exact: **his LLM wiki is an LLM-driven knowledge *base*; Memoria is an LLM-driven knowledge *production methodology* — Razlogova's.** One maintains a store you query; the other drives the methodology by which a scholar produces new knowledge.

### Direction, not authorship
Knowledge creation is **directed by the human — which does not mean the human writes a single sentence of content.** This is the augmentation boundary:
- **Human: direction + judgment.** What to investigate, what is relevant, what is true, what the argument is, what to keep/cut/pursue. The human *owns* the knowledge because they **direct** it — not because they typed it.
- **LLM: production, including writing.** It can write every Concept. "LLM-driven Razlogova" means the LLM does the writing she did by hand; her "in your own words" was *her manual practice*, not our boundary.

The human's authored artifact is the **direction** (the project/thesis, the steering, the methodology) — Karpathy's `autoresearch` makes this literal: the human edits `program.md` and never touches the code. The test for every feature: **does it help the human *direct*, or take the direction away from them?** (Not "does it write for them" — it should be able to.)

### One paragraph
Memoria captures any digital source into an objective **catalog**, then — through a **conversation in which the agent interviews the researcher** — compiles a compounding **machine synthesis** over it (Karpathy: one source touches 5-15 nodes — per-source digests + topic hubs), and the *same* conversation continues into the researcher's own atomic **notes** (a Zettelkasten), directed toward a project's thesis. **One knowledge graph** — the machine's synthesis and the human's notes woven together, told apart by provenance not by store (§5) — and agents do the labour (capture, interview, compile, cross-reference, suggest, verify, draft) under the human's direction. Because the human directs rather than authors, integrity cannot rest on approving each write; it rests on a **tamper-evident derivation graph + continuous structural checks + cascade rollback**, with a human gate only where an action is irreversible. The record is **plain files** the researcher owns and syncs — a **Memoria workspace with OKF-compatible bundle roots** (§6.2; local contract validated at M0). Obsidian is the daily editor; a Memoria plugin carries the rest.

## 1. Requirements

### Functional
Open a project (question/thesis) → seed it → **gap analysis** surfaces what's missing → source to fill gaps → for each source the **ingest spine**: capture (immutable raw + content + metadata) → **conversation** (the agent interviews the PI) → **digest + hub compounding** (the 5-15 nodes it touches) → **notes** (the PI's directed ZK) → re-run gap analysis. **New/expanded draft / verify / paper export work is post-alpha.11 writing-module scope** that consumes this graph later; existing draft/export affordances are preserved, not redesigned. Also support **emergent** daily reading (the same spine, no project). Deterministic and LLM-assisted; background and foreground work.

### Non-functional (these outrank any tool choice)
- **Personal & opinionated.** One human directs and owns judgment. The structure is the design. Not a team tool.
- **Knowledge production, not storage.** The deliverable is produced scholarship; the Bundle is the means. Value compounds with connection density, not source count.
- **Direction without required authorship.** The system must work with the human directing and the LLM writing everything — so the safety net is traceability, not authorship (§2).
- **Single-user, multi-device.** Several of one person's machines, converging by file sync. **Local-first is an implementation constraint, not a goal.** No multi-tenancy, no always-on control plane.
- **Memoria-owned canonical state, as files.** Ownership and export are intrinsic — the record *is* portable human-readable files.
- **External text is untrusted data.** Every PDF, web page, and bibliographic record is a prompt-injection surface (§12).
- **Scarce attention.** The researcher's time is the bottleneck the whole design optimizes around.

## 2. The two theses

**A research base produced mostly by machine, under human direction, has one hard problem: how does it stay trustworthy when the human directs but does not author or approve each change?** Two linked moves answer it.

**Thesis 1 — direction, not authorship.** The human directs; the LLM produces, including writing. Not Karpathy's "the LLM maintains a base for you" — the human steers a production methodology toward an output, exercising judgment at every turn, just not transcribing. Authorship is optional; direction is mandatory.

**Thesis 2 — traceability, not approval.** Because the human doesn't author each sentence, they can't catch errors by writing them; and approving every machine write rubber-stamps under load and checks the wrong object (integrity is **structural** — it lives in the Links, warrants-as-evidence-sets, and the supersession trace, not in any atomic Concept — FEVER; LongMemEval/Omni-SimpleMem). So integrity comes from **attributability + reversibility + structural detection**, gated by a human only where an action cannot be undone. Per-atom approval is also empirically dubious: human+machine is not reliably > machine, and a confident wrong output drags the reviewer *below* baseline (Jacobs 2021).

The two are one system: **traceability is what makes "human directs, LLM writes" safe.** Without authorship as the safety net, the trace *is* the safety net — so the integrity spine (§4) is load-bearing, not optional. It also makes the gate **falsifiable** (§14): prove the trace+check+rollback loop holds the error rate down, or it is theatre.

## 3. The use-case loop (what the researcher does)

The product is defined by the use case. The loop has **two modes** and **no single first step**.

**The ingest spine — how a source becomes knowledge: capture → conversation → wiki → notes.** You add a source; the agent **catalogs** it (objective), then **interviews you** about it — what's interesting, what matters for the project (Karpathy's "discuss takeaways"); from that conversation it **compiles the wiki** — a per-source digest plus the 5-15 related pages it touches (the **compounding effect** that makes the agent a Co-PI, not a chatbot); and the *same* conversation continues into **your own notes** (the Zettelkasten). The conversation is the spine; the wiki is the machine's compounding synthesis; the notes are your directed knowledge.

**Directed mode — producing a paper (top-down).**
1. **Open a project** — a bounded question, optionally a provisional **thesis** (one project type; the thesis is an optional field that switches on the argument-health gap lens, §9).
2. **Seed bibliography.**
3. **Gap analysis** — over **both** the catalog and the notes graph and the *mismatch* between them (§9): what's covered, where the argument is thin/unsupported/uncountered/fragile.
4. **Source to fill gaps** — directed discovery.
5. **Ingest** each source through the spine above (capture → conversation → wiki → notes).
6. **Re-run gap analysis** → iterate until the argument **saturates**.
7. **Alpha.11 stops at argument saturation**: the hub and notes are ready for drafting, but **new draft / verify / paper export design is deferred**; existing affordances are preserved.

**Emergent mode — daily reading (bottom-up).** Same ingest spine, no project: capture → conversation → wiki → notes → structure emerges → hubs (Razlogova's daily practice).

The directed loop is the **writing/production mode**; emergent accumulation is the **daily default**. The project is the *writing container*, not a gate — re-enter from wherever you are: a paper in hand → capture; a deadline → draft; a thin spot → discover.

**Gap analysis is first-class** — the thread that makes Memoria *directed*: the map surfaces the gap, the draft names it, verification confirms it, discovery fills it (Karpathy's lint "data gaps"; taxonomy: additive / conflict / fragility / structural / unstated-warrant / refutation; convergence = saturation).

**The researcher meets all of this through the guided conversation (the Co-PI), not a task menu.** The agent — **grounded in the wiki + the project** — interviews, suggests anchored notes and other notes, and answers queries; the human directs (steers, accepts, edits, rejects, links, or authors directly via the §4.1 PI path). The one distinct read surface is **Ask**: a retrieval-backed, **cited** query over wiki + notes (with temporal/supersession filters, §9). (This supersedes the earlier Explore/Ask/Transform triad — "Explore" *is* the grounded conversation; "Transform" *is* the ingest spine.)

## 4. The integrity spine

Three guarantees, a machine read-barrier, and one human carve-out. The write/integrity model is stated **here once**; §6 and §8 reference it rather than restating it.

**4.1 Attributable — a trusted writer + a tamper-evident derivation graph.**
Agents **emit write-requests** (trusted-writer *input*, not PI-approval work); **trusted code validates structure and atomically commits the Concept file and its derivation record together** — one agent run = one git commit. Each derivation records `output ← derived-from {inputs} by {operation, run} at {time}, {model/prompt version}`, content-hashed (`sha256`), appended as **`derived` events in the journal** (§6) — **one event per output**, so a run is a manifest of per-output `derived` events sharing a `run_id`/`commit` (this is what makes rollback per-output, §4.2).

There are **two write paths**:
- **Agent path** — write-request → validate → atomic `{file + derivation}` commit (one run = one commit; `actor: operation`).
- **PI path** — the PI is trusted and edits the working tree directly in Obsidian; the worker **observes the edit** (file-watch), **back-fills the derivation** (`actor: pi`; `inputs` = **the prior upstream inputs ∪ the prior Concept hash from HEAD** — editing machine-derived content *keeps* the upstream so cascade rollback still reaches it; PI-authored-from-scratch has only the prior HEAD; output = the new hash), and commits `{file + derivation}`. Atomicity ("one run = one commit") is the agent path; a PI edit is observe-then-commit.

**Both paths produce the same durable payload — `{file + derivation}` — and a Concept is not consumable until the worker marks it so.** The authoritative field is frontmatter **`check_status`**. Allowed values: **`unchecked`** (default for new/changed Concepts), **`checked`** (all required on-write checks passed), and **`quarantined`** (foreign/untraced or failed boundary validation). **Only the trusted worker/check runner transitions this field**; agents, plugin commands, and model outputs never set it directly. Consumers — agents, retrieval/qmd projections, gap analysis, Ask, and later draft/export — must filter to `check_status: checked`; missing or unknown values fail closed as not consumable. The file and derivation event are committed together; check results and status transitions are later journal events.

Local git cannot *prevent* another committer (Obsidian Git, the user, a script), so the worker is the **supported** write path, **not an enforceable sole committer**. The enforced boundary is **trace integrity on scan** (§10): a commit lacking a coupled `derived` event is **quarantined and routed through a backfill/import command**. Multi-device: per-machine append-only journals; **partition, don't chain.** **Alpha is git-only, single-writer-at-a-time** (converge by pull/merge); real-time multi-writer is deferred.

**4.2 Reversible — cascade rollback.**
Invalidate the offending **output Concept** — not the whole run's commit (a run may emit many good Concepts) — and everything **transitively derived from it**, via the per-output derivation DAG; apply it as a **new inverse commit** touching only those files, which itself **appends journal events** (a `resolved` event, plus a `derived` event for any re-run output) so the rollback is *traced*, not flagged as foreign by §10. Machine-derived downstream auto-reverts; **human-directed downstream halts auto-revert but still propagates a flag to it and everything transitively below it** — the full blast radius is enumerated even where it is not auto-reverted — never silently rewritten. Rollback can re-run with the bad input excluded. **The commit is the atomic *write* unit; the *rollback* unit is the output Concept.**

**4.3 Detected — continuous structural checks.**
Rollback only fires once an error is spotted; if nothing spots it, the base silently rots. Detection is first-class (§10): structural checks over the Links run on write and on schedule, plus the PI noticing, plus external signals (retractions). A fired check raises a flag carrying the Concept *and its derivation*. Detection is imperfect (NLI miscalibrated; contradiction detection is the least-reliable capability) → **detection recall is measured (§14), never assumed.**

**4.4 The read barrier — consume only `checked` (a machine gate, not a human one).**
Detection is useless if poisoned content is *re-used before the scan runs*, so **consumption is gated, not just writing**: a Concept is `unchecked` until its on-write checks pass, and **agents, retrieval, gap-analysis, Ask, and later draft/export consume only `checked` content** — an `unchecked`, `quarantined`, or **foreign/untraced** Concept is invisible to consumers until it passes (the on-write window is brief; `checked` is **affirmative and worker-assigned**, so anything lacking it — including a foreign commit awaiting the scan — is **not consumable by default**). **Enforced by a universal staging path, not just a flag:** machine write-requests land in **`.memoria/staging/{catalog,knowledge,capabilities}/`** (gitignored, outside the Obsidian-opened `knowledge/` tree) with `check_status: unchecked`; the worker runs on-write checks and the trusted writer **promotes** a file into the target bundle (`catalog/`, `knowledge/`, or `capabilities/`) only with `check_status: checked`. Imported `capabilities/` also require a trust/quarantine check before promotion (§12/§13). Foreign/untraced commits already in a bundle are moved to **`.memoria/quarantine/`** by the trace-integrity scan until backfilled. PI edits land live in Obsidian, but the worker still owns **machine consumer** eligibility; native Obsidian visibility is not the enforcement boundary. This is a *machine* barrier (compatible with gateless), and it closes the persistence window for **everything the checks can catch**. The **uncheckable slice** (crafted injection, semantic-grounding) still passes checks and becomes consumable — that remains the §12/§14 residual the read barrier cannot close.

**4.5 The carve-out — a human gate only at irreversible boundaries.**
Rollback can't un-send an exported draft or un-poison the PI's own absorbed belief (Greshake persistence). So the one human approval gate sits where the action **leaves the system / can't be undone**: export, external send. Directing the knowledge graph is *not* a gate — it's the PI's judgment as a director. *(Open lever — §18: the default keeps this thin gate.)*

**Net:** the PI is a **director + spot-corrector**, not an approval queue. Trust shifts from "a human approved this" to "the system guarantees this trace" — and the spine is the substitute for the scalar metric autonomous loops rely on (§11): a paper has no `val_bpb`, so the spine keeps machine-written knowledge honest.

## 5. The knowledge graph — one graph, machine + human by role

There is **one knowledge graph**; the Zettelkasten is its *structure*, and the LLM-wiki is *machine synthesis woven into that structure*. They are two **authoring roles over one graph** — machine (comprehensive, compounding, *suggested*) and human (selective, atomic, *directed*) — told apart by **provenance, not by store**, and they **converge at the hub**. (Genesis made literal: catalog = Razlogova's Zotero; atomic notes = Razlogova's ZK; the compounding synthesis = Karpathy — the net-new role neither had.)

**The catalog stays separate — objective, deterministic.** Source Concepts (incl. datasets and repositories — *captured*, so they're sources) + bibliographic/citation Links + the immutable `raw/` content + objective entity identity (person/org/venue). Identifier resolution + parse, **no LLM generation**; ambiguous identity → a **forced flag, never a silent merge**. Luhmann's bibliographic slip-box (Razlogova's Zotero), in-house. (No "Work" Concept — version grouping is a `version_of` Link between sources.)

**The knowledge graph — three node-kinds by granularity, each with a defined authoring role:**
- **`digest`** (per-source, **machine**) — "what this source says," in corpus context, compiled from the capture conversation; the reading unit, and what hubs draw from. 1:1 with a catalog source; compounded as context grows; born `unchecked` + read-barrier'd; the human may *adopt* (rewrite) it.
- **`note`** (atomic, **human-directed**) — the ZK permanent note: one idea, source-independent, densely Linked, *distilled* from digests + sources. May carry a **`quote` + provenance** (an *anchored* note — "an annotation"; `annotation_ref` = a W3C Web-Annotation selector subset, §7) and/or **assert** (a *claim*, warranted by its supporting Links; the fuller Toulmin shape is an *optional, project-scoped* overlay). Non-claim notes (questions, connections, **dead-ends/negative results**) are first-class. A reminder is a **`#todo` tag**; **no `maturity`** (trust = the checks).
- **`hub`** (per-topic, **machine-synthesized + human-curated**) — the merged Karpathy concept page × ZK MOC: the machine keeps the topic synthesis current, the human owns the structure + argument; a project's hub *is* its draft outline and the **entry point** (no separate ZK *index* type).

**The wiki is a role, not a store.** "The wiki" = the machine-authored aspect across `digest` nodes + the synthesis in `hub`s; "the ZK" = the human-directed aspect (atomic `note`s + hub curation). Compounding (one source touches 5-15 nodes — cross-referencing, contradiction-flagging, keeping-current) is **the Co-PI differentiator**, the bookkeeping humans abandon. It is *not* a separate bundle.

**Two roles, no collision — ownership + suggestion, never co-editing:** `digest` is machine-owned (human may adopt → `actor: pi`); `note` is human-owned (the agent *proposes* candidates; the human directs the *whether*); `hub` is **human-owned once curated, kept fresh by machine synthesis *suggestions*** (the read-barrier'd candidate stream), never rewriting the curation. An **un-curated hub = a machine-only concept page** not yet adopted. So the two promotions are **adoption** (machine concept page → curated hub) and **distillation** (digest → atomic notes).

**Coverage + selectivity coexist:** comprehensiveness (Karpathy) lives in a `digest` per source + a `hub` per topic *including un-curated machine-only hubs* (the full machine view); selectivity (ZK) lives in the human's atomic notes + the *subset of hubs they curate* into their argument.

**Links, projects, ZK fidelity.** **Typed Links** (`supports / challenges / extends / supersedes / in_project` + associative; **Citations** to a source) are the structure; automatic = candidates, high-risk directional Links (contradiction, supersession) are the human's. **Notes cite sources** — `note → anchored-note(span) → catalog source`, a plugin-resolved deep link — **not** machine-synthesis nodes, so the machine can recompound freely without breaking citations. **Project↔source/note membership = a typed `in_project` Link, never a folder**; a **project is not a bundle** — a Link-defined subgraph + a small project-local workspace (thesis/framing/draft/exports), exported as an OKF directory/git/tar package; RO-Crate is later archival wrapping (§13). Concepts titled by *idea* (`title:` frontmatter); **path = a stable slug ID** (immutable, §6.1). ZK is relaxed deliberately: "in your own words" → *direction* (graduated by role, §11); plus **verbatim provenance** for §10 checks. ZK mapping: catalog source ≈ *literature note*; `note` = *permanent note*; `hub` = *structure note*; the wiki-role is **not ZK** (it's Karpathy). *(Adopt vs build the machine-synthesis engine: §18 — M0 verified that the installed Hermes wiki pattern can produce the markdown compounding shape. Alpha.11 wraps/adapts that pattern first; MkDocs/static-site rendering is not an alpha.11 dependency.)*

## 6. Storage — a small record, everything else a projection

The first-principles spine: a small **record**; everything else regenerates.
1. **Record (authored, not regenerable):** the **catalog sources** + the **knowledge graph** (`digest` machine-authored records, `note` human records, `hub` machine-synthesized/human-curated) + **`steering.md`** (direction — the `program.md` of §11, incl. the wiki `SCHEMA.md`) + the **journal** (append-only, content-hashed, per-machine; **W3C PROV-DM** — output=*entity*, operation/run=*activity*, actor=*agent*, `derived`=`wasDerivedFrom`; events `derived`/`run`/`model_call`/`check-fired`/`resolved`, shapes in §7). Machine-authored nodes (`digest`, hub-synthesis) are **records too** — *checked + read-barrier'd because they're machine-made over untrusted sources, not because they're disposable*.
2. **Durable non-text:** the `raw/` source blobs (immutable, separately-syncable) + persisted **Canvas** positions (layout, not data — Card & Moran).
3. **Disposable projections** (gitignored, rebuilt): the **query index** — an *interface* (BM25 + vector + hybrid over the knowledge graph); **`qmd` is the backend** (Karpathy names it; disposable-bundle BM25 index/search verified pre-alpha.11), SQLite FTS5+sqlite-vec the zero-dependency baseline/fallback (LanceDB/Tantivy if vector-/lexical-heavy; DuckDB beside it for eval; Turso a post-alpha spike) — plus the embeddings cache (content-addressed by `text_sha256`), the **derivation DAG**, the **open-flags list**, the worker queue.

**Gitignore, three categories:** committed text (record + tracked projections) · the `raw/` blob store (gitignorable for copyright, synced out-of-band) · never-committed disposable projections. **Sync = git**, merge at the text layer; **partition, don't chain** (per-machine journal). Tracked projections that regenerate: `index.md` / `ai-catalog.json` / `references.bib`. `log.md` is generated only on export from the journal, not tracked in alpha. The **journal, worker, and disposable index are workspace-level** (they span bundles).

### 6.1 Native format: the Memoria profile over OKF
A Memoria install is a **workspace** (local contract + round-trip validated at M0; no external validator was found locally) containing three OKF-compatible bundle roots: `catalog/` (objective), `knowledge/` (the knowledge graph — digests + notes + hubs; the Obsidian vault), and `capabilities/`. The parent workspace is **not** an OKF bundle; it holds cross-bundle state (`steering.md`, journal, worker/index state) so those local files do not have to pretend to be Concepts. Memoria is a **strict producer, permissive consumer**; the §4 integrity model lives in the space OKF leaves to the producer.
- **Concept** = one markdown doc; **Concept ID = its path** — a **stable slug** (derived from the title at creation, **immutable thereafter**), so links survive retitling. The human **title lives in `title:` frontmatter** (retitle freely; the path doesn't move); an actual file **rename is a first-class journal event** the worker repairs (link fix-up). `type` frontmatter distinguishes kinds. The Concept is the *doc*; some carry referenced **attachments** — a source = `<source_id>/source.md` (the Concept) + `content.md` + `raw/` (attachments, *not* Concepts). Portable preview fields: `description` is required on `source`, `digest`, `hub`, and `operation`, optional on atomic `note`; `resource` records a canonical external URI when one exists (DOI URL, web URL, repo URL, dataset landing page).
- **OKF core:** each Concept = one OKF Concept; required `type`; CommonMark body; YAML frontmatter; standard Markdown links; generated `index.md` at bundle roots; optional OKF-friendly `title`, `description`, `resource`, and `tags` where useful. OKF consumers may ignore every Memoria extension and still read the files.
- **Memoria profile:** Memoria owns the `type` vocabulary, typed Links, Citations, `check_status`, `evidence_set`, hashes, journal, attachments, and generated projections. These are profile extensions over OKF, not claims that generic OKF tools understand Memoria semantics. `title` is required for every Memoria Concept; `description` is the generic preview text, not a second summary model.
- **Tags** are controlled-vocabulary **facets** in `tags[]` (taxonomy in the wiki `SCHEMA.md`; add-before-use) for filtering/faceting — **orthogonal** to `type` (the kind) and Links (the graph); `#todo` is the one operational tag. Membership stays a Link (`in_project`), not a tag.
- **Source identity:** the catalog path is an **immutable `source_id`**, not the citekey — citekeys are metadata-derived and get corrected, so they are a **generated display/export alias**, never the path (same stable-handle rule as Concept IDs; avoids broken cross-bundle links on a metadata fix; fixture-verified across a citekey correction).
- **Projects** follow the same rule: `projects/<id>/` is a **stable slug ID** (immutable; the project title/thesis lives in its frontmatter) so links to project artifacts survive a retitle — "slug" here *is* the stable ID.
- **Links/Citations:** within a bundle, standard Markdown links are OKF-readable; typed Links/Citations live in Memoria frontmatter. **Cross-bundle references** use a **bundle-qualified Concept ID** (`catalog/sources/<source_id>`) as Memoria metadata; export/projection renders relative Markdown links when the target bundles are packaged together. Dense ZK linking stays inside `knowledge/`; only the annotation→source provenance crosses — an anchored note stores a bare `source_id`, which the plugin resolves against the fixed `catalog/sources/<source_id>/source.md` scheme (the ID names the source folder; it resolves to its `source.md` Concept). Claim-bearing `note`s and `digest`s render a portable **`# Citations`** body section on export/projection from the typed Citations; the typed data remains the checked source of truth.
- **Validation:** `okf-core` is permissive and checks only OKF shape (frontmatter parses, required `type`, CommonMark body, standard links, bundle-root `index.md`); imported content can pass OKF core and still be untrusted. `memoria-profile` is strict before promotion: it checks Concept types, folder homes, required `title`/`description`, typed Links, warrants, supersession, `check_status`, generated projections, and §10 graph semantics. JSON Schema validates shape, not the graph.
- **`index.md` / `ai-catalog.json` / `references.bib` are *generated* tracked projections** — conformance is a regeneration target, not hand-maintained. **Source of truth:** Concept files + frontmatter + the journal (`references.bib` from catalog sources; `ai-catalog.json` from capability Concepts). **Generator/check expectation:** alpha.11 must add one worker-owned projection generator and a drift check that regenerates these files into a temp tree and diffs them against the tracked copies; a hand-edited projection does not satisfy acceptance. Export adds `okf_version` only at exported OKF bundle roots; the workspace marker is Memoria-owned, not OKF.
- **Provisional:** the local bundle contract is no longer provisional for alpha.11, because M0 validated shape and round-trip. **External OKF conformance/interop** remains a boundary claim until compatible tooling is available. If an external validator later rejects the profile, keep the substrate + vocabulary and narrow the interop claim; export remains Memoria-owned text, and external OKF import stays permissive (§13).

### 6.2 On-disk layout
The **workspace** owns what spans bundles — the journal, worker + queue, disposable index, and `steering.md` (the program). **Obsidian opens `knowledge/`** (the one graph the human reads + works in); `catalog/` and `capabilities/` are siblings the plugin reads.

```text

│

│   ├── index.md  SCHEMA.md                     # SCHEMA = synthesis/wiki conventions (⊂ steering)
│   ├── digests/<source_id>.md  # type: digest — per-source MACHINE synthesis (1:1 with a catalog source)
```

The **worker (workspace-level) is the supported write path** for all bundles (§4.1). The plugin (a process, not Obsidian's indexer) reads `catalog/` + `capabilities/` to render catalog views and resolve `note → source` deep links (§8.1). Within `knowledge/`, the wiki/ZK split is **provenance, not folders** — `digests/` are machine, `notes/` human, `hubs/` both.

## 7. Domain model (sketch, not final DDL)

Everything in `catalog/`, `knowledge/`, and `capabilities/` is a **Concept** (markdown + frontmatter; `type` distinguishes the kind) — **three Concept families**: the catalog's deterministic; the knowledge graph's by authoring role (`digest` = machine, `note` = human, `hub` = both); the capabilities' are policy/executable Concepts (§11). Only the **journal events** are non-Concepts — machine records (our extension over OKF).

### 7.1 Target alpha.11 schema/folder model

When implementation starts, the canonical owners become `vault-template/.memoria/schemas/types/*.yaml` and `vault-template/.memoria/schemas/folders.yaml`; this design is the target contract until those YAMLs land.

| Family | Target Concept types and homes | Migration mapping from the current tree |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Catalog | `source` at `catalog/sources/<source_id>/source.md`; attachments at `content.md` + `raw/`; `person` / `organization` / `venue` at `catalog/entities/<id>.md`. | Current `paper`, `dataset`, and `repository` records become `source` with `item_type`; `citekey` is preserved as an alias, not the path. Current `source` notes split: objective bibliographic fields move to `source`; human appraisal/summary prose becomes a linked `note`. Current person/org/venue records map by external ID first, then canonical name. |
| Knowledge: machine synthesis | `digest` at `knowledge/digests/<source_id>.md`. | No direct migration. Digests are re-derived from catalog sources through the alpha.11 ingest/conversation/wiki pipeline. |
| Knowledge: human-directed notes | `note` at `knowledge/notes/<id>.md`. | Current `claim` becomes `note` with an optional claim block and embedded `evidence_set`; current annotations become anchored `note`s; kept `fleeting` items become ordinary notes or are reported as dropped; `maturity` is dropped; `#todo` remains a tag. |
| Knowledge: structure | `hub` at `knowledge/hubs/<id>.md`. | Current human-curated hubs map to `hub`; machine-only synthesis inside them is re-derived and checked. |
| Projects | `project` metadata at `knowledge/projects/<id>/project.md`; drafts/exports are later workspace folders under the project. | Current `project` and `thesis` records map to project metadata/framing; `code-note` is outside alpha.11's knowledge model and must either become a linked project note or be archived with an explicit migration-report entry. |
| Capabilities | `operation`, `skill`, `mcp`, `workflow` at `capabilities/{operations,skills,mcp,workflows}/<id>.md`. | Seed from existing operations, skills, MCP/profile config, and workflow docs only where they are actually used by alpha.11; otherwise leave absent. |
| Operational/transient | Queue, flags, gaps, work cards, dashboards, and indexes are projections or worker state, not durable Concepts. | Current `candidate`, `gap`, `flag`, `alert`, `work-prompt`, `queue`, `space`, `worker-card`, `worklist-item`, `maintenance`, and `pattern` records are regenerated, archived, or dropped with a migration-report entry; they are not silently carried as Concepts. |

**Catalog Concepts.**
- **`source`** `source_id, citekey(alias), item_type{article|book|webpage|dataset|repository|software|audio|video|note|…}, title, description, resource?, date, identifiers{doi,arxiv_id,pmcid,isbn,url}, csl_json, raw_copy_path, content_path, raw_text_sha256, normalized_text_sha256, metadata_status{verified|partial|unverified|not-indexed}, lifecycle`; Links: `authored_by, published_in, references, cited_by, version_of`. `resource` is the canonical external URI when one exists; `description` is a short portable preview (abstract/summary), not the generated digest. (Creators/venue are **not** standalone fields — they live in `csl_json` + the `authored_by`/`published_in` Links. Per-`item_type` metadata profile, §13: CSL-JSON core; DataCite for `dataset`; CFF/CodeMeta for `software`/`repository`.)
- **`person` / `organization` / `venue`** `canonical_name, external_ids{orcid,ror,issn}, metadata`.

**Knowledge-graph Concepts.**
- **`digest`** (machine) `source_id, title, description, body, links[], confidence{high|medium|low}, contested?, contradictions[], massw{context,key_idea,method,outcome,projected_impact}` — the per-source synthesis (1:1 with a catalog source); born `unchecked` + read-barrier'd; `description` is the one-line OKF preview, while the body carries the synthesis; `massw` fields feed gap analysis; the human may adopt (rewrite) it. Export/projection renders `# Citations` from typed source Citations.
- **`note`** (human-directed) `title, description?, body, links[]` — the atomic unit. `description` is optional because most atomic notes are already small; add it only when it improves generic OKF previews. *Optional* **anchor** (→ "annotation"): `quote, text_sha256, source_id, annotation_ref` — `annotation_ref` = a **W3C Web Annotation selector subset** (`TextQuoteSelector{quote,prefix,suffix}` + `TextPositionSelector{start,end}` + optional `page`/`bbox`); the parser must preserve `page`/`span`/`bbox` so it resolves regardless of parser (§9). *Optional* **claim block** (→ "claim"): `claim_text, qualifier, tense, temporal_scope_*, status{candidate|accepted|rejected|superseded|needs_review}, evidence_set, extraction_confidence`. Claim-bearing notes render `# Citations` on export/projection from typed Citations. A reminder is a `#todo` tag; **no `maturity`**. `extraction_confidence` is internal telemetry — **never surfaced**.
- **`evidence_set`** (a claim's warrant) — an **embedded frontmatter structure**, not a Concept: `warrant_type{single_span|multi_sentence|multi_document|implicit|computed}, completeness` + the claim's **supporting Links** to anchored notes (Citations). Always a set; `single_span` = one-member. Toulmin (qualifier/rebuttal/backing) is an *optional, project-scoped* overlay.
- **`hub`** (machine-synthesized + human-curated) `title, description, body, members[], project?, confidence?, contested?` — the merged concept-page × MOC: the machine keeps the synthesis current (suggestions), the human curates structure/outline; `description` is the topic preview used by `index.md`; an un-curated hub is machine-only until adopted.

**Typed Links** (knowledge graph) `predicate{supports|challenges|extends|supersedes|in_project}, subject_id, object_id, evidence_set?, status, created_by_run_id`. `in_project` realizes the §5 project↔source/claim membership-by-Link; **`version_of` is a *catalog* source↔source Link, not a ZK predicate** (it lives only on source Concepts). Automatic = candidates; high-risk directional Links are the human's.

**Capability Concepts** (`capabilities/`, the third family) — `operation` / `skill` / `mcp` / `workflow`, each with `title, description, resource?`, and policy in frontmatter (allowed tools, model/provider, prompt version, I/O schema, risk class; §11). `resource` points to an external package/repository/spec endpoint when there is one.

**Toulmin = fields + Links on a `note`** (optional, project-scoped, §5): claim → `claim_text`; grounds → the `evidence_set` members (Citations to anchored notes); warrant → `evidence_set.warrant_type` + reasoning; qualifier → `qualifier`; rebuttal → a `challenges` Link; backing → an optional backing Link. No new node-kinds.

**Journal events + projections (not Concepts).** The **journal** (§6) is the durable, append-only, content-hashed record; its event types:
- **`derived`** `{output_id, output_sha256, inputs[{id,sha256}], operation, run_id, actor{pi|operation}, model, prompt_version, commit}` — for an in-place/PI edit, `inputs` includes the **prior Concept hash from HEAD** (the baseline for rollback/trace-integrity).
- **`run`** `{run_id, workflow, status, started, ended}` · **`model_call`** `{runner{direct_api|hermes}, provider, model, route, purpose, prompt_version, seed, toolset, fallback_used, compression_used, input_hash, output_hash}` (hashes only, never secrets; raw request/response may be retained only under the run's privacy policy) · **`check-fired`** + **`resolved`** `{check, severity, target_id, resolution{rolled_back|accepted|edited|false_positive}}`.

**Projections** rebuilt from the journal (never separately authored): the **derivation DAG** (per-output → per-output rollback, §4.2), the **open-flags list**, and non-blocking **Gap**/**Nudge** signals.

## 8. The stack — five planes

| Plane | Responsibility | Choice |
|---|---|---|
| **Surface** | Converse / read / direct / correct | **Obsidian** (daily editor) + a **Memoria plugin**: the **Co-PI conversation** (grounded in the graph + project — the ingest interview + note suggestions), **knowledge-graph browsing** (Bases / Canvas / graph), the project/gap + integrity/flags dashboards, and trace→rollback — **part of 0.1.0-alpha.11** (the cycle isn't usable without them, §15). New export-gate work belongs to the deferred writing module. A **CLI** backs development / headless runs. |
| **Knowledge** | Store the data + the trace | **catalog** (objective) + the **one knowledge graph** (machine `digest`s + topic `hub`s + human `note`s; the "wiki" is a *role*, not a bundle — adopt vs build the synthesis engine, §18) + **capabilities**; the append-only content-hashed journal; a disposable index (qmd). |
| **Orchestration** | Schedule + run + check | cron + a per-machine worker; disposable queue; the **trusted writer**; **gap analysis + structural checks (§10)** on write and on schedule. |
| **Agent runtime** | Execute LLM tasks, sandboxed | Per-operation tool allowlists, no-exfiltration, channels separate, data last. Agents **emit write-requests**; only the worker commits. Agent code execution is **read-only/mechanical only** — canonical writes still go through the trusted writer. LLM execution is a runner choice: direct OpenAI-compatible API by default, Hermes library adapter only where a specific Hermes feature pays for itself. |
| **Integrations** | External services | identifier-lookup APIs (Crossref/arXiv/PMC); retraction feeds; **LLM runner** (live default = direct OpenAI-compatible API such as Kilo Gateway or direct vendor; local `qwen2.5:7b` = test fixture only; Hermes = specialized adapter); local embeddings; **Zotero/JabRef as importers, not authority** (§13); optional NLI behind acceptance tests. |

**Deliberately absent at alpha:** per-write approval, message broker, workflow engine, graph database, multi-user auth, hosted sync primary, profiles/fleet, an authoritative control-plane DB — and the heavy agent/infra frameworks: **LangChain / LlamaIndex / Haystack, Celery / Redis, Neo4j, Elasticsearch/OpenSearch, any custom workflow engine, and UI frameworks as state owners**. The backend is a per-machine worker + files + a disposable index + cron + git. **Boring-library defaults** (implementation, not architecture): Pydantic (schemas) · watchfiles (file-watch) · NetworkX (the DAG projection) · PDF parser selected by a span-preservation spike (PyMuPDF4LLM / PDF++ candidates; no local extractor was installed in the start-blocker pass) · Pandoc + CSL (export) · pyzotero / habanero (import) · RapidFuzz (dedup candidates) · Inspect / promptfoo (seeded-error evals).

### 8.1 Surface — the Obsidian plugin (thin over native)
**Thin in code, but load-bearing in alpha.11.** **Obsidian opens `knowledge/`** — the one graph the human reads + works in (§6.2); native Obsidian carries most of the surface, and the plugin is only what native can't do — *but* it is **required** for the Co-PI conversation, cross-bundle `note→source` deep links, and catalog views, so it is part of alpha.11 (not optional). A CLI backs development.
- **Native Obsidian renders almost everything:** markdown = notes; wikilinks = Links; **Bases** (native) + generated `views/` projections = the project/gap dashboard, the integrity/flags dashboard, and knowledge views; **JSON Canvas** = the claim-debate map (persisted layout, §6); the graph view = browse. (**Dataview** is a *community plugin*, not native — used only if declared a required third-party dependency.)
- **Canvas is a visual projection only** — *spatial arrangement; Concepts/Links/journal own truth.* Alpha paths: `projects/<slug>/argument.canvas` (claim/evidence map), `projects/<slug>/corpus.canvas` (only once the corpus floor is met — else a gap card, not a partial map), `views/trace/<run_id>.canvas` (generated from the DAG for rollback inspection). Positions are persisted (§6); the rest is generated.
- **The plugin carries:** the **Co-PI conversation** (the ingest interview + note suggestions, grounded in wiki + project — the primary surface, built on the Agent Client pane / Local REST API); worker-enqueuing **actions** (capture, gap-analyze, trace→rollback — never doing the work itself); and **two panels** — the **trace→rollback panel** (cascade blast-radius before the PI confirms) and the **flags/derivation (integrity) panel** (projected into `views/` as Bases-readable files). Surfaces show **raw evidence/derivation first, any suggestion second** — never an "agent verdict" (Jacobs). New export enqueueing is deferred with the writing module.
- **Plugin mutation boundary:** the plugin is a control panel, not a writer. It may read generated status, audit, queue, flags, trace, and projections; it may request `enqueue_operation`, `acknowledge_attention`, `resolve_attention`, and `open_trace` through the worker-owned API. It never writes canonical Concepts, the journal, generated projections, or `check_status` directly, and every requested mutation is recorded by the worker as a journal/audit event.
- **Plugin boundary options (pre-approval decision).** The pre-approval spike proved the control-panel API shape but not a hard platform sandbox ([pre-approval-test-results.md](pre-approval-test-results.md)); choose the boundary explicitly before implementation:
  - **Option A — Obsidian control-panel plugin with static/provenance guardrails.** The plugin stays inside Obsidian, reads projections, and calls only the narrow worker API; CI/static checks deny direct vault adapter writes, raw JS/HTML agent UI, journal writes, projection writes, and `check_status` writes; bundled plugin provenance remains locked. **Pros:** smallest alpha.11 surface; best Obsidian-native workflow; uses the already-validated Local REST/plugin shape; keeps Co-PI, flags, and rollback in the daily editor. **Cons:** ordinary Obsidian plugin JavaScript is trusted code, not an OS sandbox; safety depends on provenance lock + static checks + review discipline; a malicious or compromised plugin is outside the worker's policy boundary. **Recommendation:** alpha.11 default if the approval decision accepts "trusted bundled plugin + static/provenance guardrails" as sufficient for this local single-user tool.
  - **Option B — read-only Obsidian plugin plus separate worker-owned action UI.** Obsidian shows status, projections, deep links, and maybe opens a localhost page; enqueue/resolve/rollback actions happen in a worker-served UI or CLI outside the Obsidian plugin. **Pros:** much smaller trusted Obsidian code; clearer proof that Obsidian JS cannot mutate canonical state; still preserves Obsidian as the reading/editing surface. **Cons:** extra local UI/auth surface; more context switching; Co-PI conversation may feel split from the note; more packaging and support than Option A. **Recommendation:** choose if Option A's trusted-plugin assumption is not acceptable, but keeping Obsidian as the main workspace still matters.
  - **Option C — sandboxed companion app as the primary surface.** A browser/Tauri/Electron companion owns Co-PI, flags, and rollback; Obsidian becomes the markdown editor and deep-link target, with no mutating plugin. **Pros:** strongest boundary: the app can be denied direct filesystem writes and forced through the worker API; easier to reason about UI privileges; less reliance on Obsidian plugin trust. **Cons:** largest product surface; weaker native Obsidian feel; duplicate navigation/state problems; more install, update, and localhost-auth work. **Recommendation:** choose only if approval requires a hard sandbox before alpha.11 starts.
  - **Option D — generated Obsidian views + CLI/QuickAdd only.** No custom plugin beyond existing native/community primitives; dashboards are generated markdown/Bases/Canvas and mutations are CLI or QuickAdd commands. **Pros:** least custom UI code; smallest plugin attack surface; fast first slice for worker/schema work. **Cons:** does not satisfy the alpha.11 requirement for an integrated Co-PI conversation and trace→rollback panel; awkward PI attention workflow; too easy for the plugin requirement to be deferred away. **Recommendation:** acceptable as an early implementation scaffold or fallback, not as the final alpha.11 surface.
  - **Rejected option — direct-writing rich plugin.** A plugin that edits Concepts, journals, projections, or `check_status` itself gives the best apparent UX but violates the design's worker-owned write boundary. **Pros:** fewer round trips and simpler demos. **Cons:** collapses generator/checker separation, bypasses staging/promotion, makes rollback/audit incomplete, and turns UI code into the state owner. **Recommendation:** reject for anything except disposable throwaway spikes.
- **Cross-bundle reach:** the plugin — a *process*, not Obsidian's indexer — **reads `catalog/` + `capabilities/`** to render catalog views and resolve **`note → source` deep links**. The honest cost of the layered split.
- **The worker↔vault bridge** is the **Local REST API** community plugin (HTTPS + MCP + bearer) for automation/verification, not bespoke IPC.
- **M0/start-blocker smoke result:** a disposable plugin was installed/enabled in `Memoria-test`, its manifest was readable through Local REST, and a mock command/view activation exercised the Co-PI, Flags, and Rollback sections. That verifies static registration + bridge reachability, not visual panel activation inside the Obsidian window; panel behavior remains alpha.11 implementation work.
- **The plugin never writes canonical Concepts** — it enqueues; the worker (workspace-level) commits.
- **Avoid as architecture:** **Templater** (executes arbitrary JS/commands — security; use QuickAdd + Modal Form) and **Obsidian Git auto-commit** (a foreign committer that fights the supported write path — the worker commits).
- **Risk:** if the trace→rollback or flags panels outgrow Obsidian's (sandboxed) plugin API, fall back to a custom Electron app. Keeping the plugin thin keeps that ceiling far off.

### 8.2 Orchestration — the worker (one worker, one queue)
- **What "workflow state" is (alpha.11):** queued/running/done/blocked + retry/idempotency + what-needs-PI-action + what-was-written. The **live** part is `.memoria/queue/` jobs (disposable); the **durable** part is `journal/` events; **visibility** is generated projections. **Rejected as canonical workflow state:** Hermes Kanban, Obsidian notes/Tasks, a canonical SQLite/DB, LangGraph, any UI-framework session state — they may *execute* or *render*, never *own* it.
- **Attention is generated, not authored:** flags, proposals, gaps, blocked jobs, and check failures become attention rows because a worker-owned view says they need PI attention. Acknowledge / snooze / resolve / defer / dismiss are small journal events; markdown, Bases, Canvas, or plugin "cards" are projections and never canonical Concepts.
- **A single per-machine worker** — at the **parent-bundle level**, committing across all sub-bundles — claims jobs from **one local queue** (`.memoria/`). No broker, no workflow engine.
- **Three trigger sources, all enqueue jobs:** **cron** (scheduled sweeps — full structural checks, retraction, gap re-analysis, wiki lint), **plugin/CLI** (foreground — the ingest spine: capture → conversation → wiki compile (5-15 pages) → note suggestions; gap-analyze), **file-watch** (on-write — cheap checks, trace-integrity, + the PI-edit derivation back-fill, §4.1). New draft triggers arrive with the deferred writing module.
- **The worker is the *supported* write path** (the trusted writer is its commit path for agent results), per §4.1: agent write-requests land in **`.memoria/staging/{catalog,knowledge,capabilities}/`**, are checked, and **promoted into the target bundle only on pass** (the read-barrier mechanism, §4.4); imported capabilities also pass the trust/quarantine check before promotion, and foreign commits are caught by the trace-integrity scan and moved to **`.memoria/quarantine/`** / backfilled (§10).
- **Checks run twice:** cheap deterministic ones **on write** (with the commit); the expensive sweep (retraction, corpus-wide dedup, NLI) **on schedule**.
- **Idempotency keys** on every job (`ingest:<source_id>`, `check:<concept>:<hash>`) so retries and duplicate triggers are safe; bounded retries → a flag; bounded loops.
- **What bounds it:** in a gateless model the bottleneck is **the PI's attention to flags**, not an approval queue — so WIP back-pressure means *don't flood the flags dashboard* (the `act/ask/drop` router, §10, does most of this) and *cap background loops*.
- **LLM execution split:** the default runner is a direct OpenAI-compatible API call owned by the worker (OpenAI SDK shape; Kilo Gateway is the current gateway candidate). Hermes is a **library adapter**, not the framework: use it only for operations that benefit from its agent loop or `llm-wiki` skill. In either runner, the worker owns the queue, trace, workflow state, schema validation, checks, and promotion — a model result becomes Memoria state only via the trusted writer + a Memoria event.

## 9. Mechanisms (the information flow that serves the loop)

The §3 ingest spine — **capture → conversation → wiki → notes** — *is* the mechanism; the researcher meets it through the guided conversation (the Co-PI), grounded in the wiki + project (§3).

**Capture (catalog the source).** Record the capture event first → (1) immutable raw copy (`raw/`); (2) extract content to markdown behind a coherence gate; (3) extract metadata (CSL-JSON; ACM minimum), best-source-wins with per-field provenance, **ambiguous identity → forced flag**; (4) add the source + entity Concepts to the **catalog** (objective). Capture adapters are a small fixed set (identifier lookup / Zotero / BibTeX / web-snapshot), **not a translator engine** — lean on the Zotero connector + **Zotero Local API** (§13). Start-blocker verification reached the live Zotero Local API and item/annotation list shapes, but found no annotation sample; the first capture fixture still needs a real annotated PDF. Every output committed by the trusted writer with a derivation.

**Conversation (the interview — the hinge).** The agent reads the source and **interviews the PI** — what's interesting, what matters for the project (Karpathy's "discuss takeaways") — grounded in the existing wiki + the project. This is what turns capture into knowledge; it is *not* an optional side chat.

**Synthesis compile (the compounding step).** From that conversation the LLM writes the source **`digest`** and **updates the 5-15 related `digest`/`hub` nodes it touches** — cross-referencing, flagging contradictions, keeping summaries current (the **compounding effect**; Karpathy/Hermes wiki schema). Updates the generated knowledge `index.md`; born `unchecked`, then checked + read-barrier'd. This is the connective tissue and the primary retrieval target (qmd). Hub updates land as *suggestions* — never overwriting the human's curation (§5).

**Notes (the conversation continues).** The same conversation continues into the PI's **notes**: the agent **suggests** anchored notes and claims grounded in the wiki + sources; the PI directs (accepts/edits/rejects/links) or authors directly (§4.1 PI path). Candidates commit as machine-derived and are checked immediately. The *whether*-to-note is the PI's; the writing is delegable (§11).

**Gap analysis (over *both* graphs and their correspondence).** Compute density on the **catalog** (sources) and the **knowledge** graph (notes/claims), then read the **mismatch** — the primary signal:
- **thin / thin** → a *new topic*; expected, **not a gap** (flag "early", propose a seed).
- **dense / dense** → the desired mature state.
- **sources dense / notes thin** → *undigested corpus* → read/annotate these.
- **notes dense / sources thin** → *under-warranted* → find sources for these claims (the fragility / unstated-warrant gap).

A **thesis**, when present, adds the **argument-health** lens (supported / countered / fragile); without one it is coverage-only. **Cold start (no seed) is legal:** the whole thesis is the gap, so the first output is a *proposed seed* (discovery); a coverage map renders only once a corpus floor is met (below it, an "add sources" card, never a partial map shown as complete). Iterates to saturation; the hub is the convergence/outline; the wiki digest's MASSW fields are a per-source input.

**Error recovery.** A check fires (or the PI spots it) → the flag shows the Concept + its derivation → **trace → cascade rollback** (machine downstream reverts; human-directed downstream flagged) → optionally re-run.

**Query / Ask (evidence first; answer from compiled synthesis).** Answer from the **compiled wiki + notes/hubs** first (the synthesis already reflects the reading — Karpathy), with span retrieval as grounding/fallback; apply temporal + supersession filters; never present a superseded claim as current. **Retrieval indexes on the atom but returns the value:** claims/atoms are the index *keys*; what's returned is the **anchored-note chunk + claim** (the canonical *value*) — fact-only retrieval loses synthesis context (LongMemEval: K=value beats K=fact). **Retrieval quality is the corpus's #1 lever** (it beats model size and agentic search), so it gets the budget when discovery lands: deterministic **hybrid union** (dense + BM25, appending BM25-only hits, not score-fusion), **indexed over the wiki + notes, not PDFs**; **reasoning-trace query expansion** + **offline index enrichment** (hypothetical-questions-this-claim-answers) to recover the reasoning-based relevance cosine misses; a small-k LLM-judge rerank; cap aggregated evidence at ~3 docs for a small model. Cosine alone never arbitrates relevance or equivalence. **Per-source context level** (full / summary / excluded) is an operation/retrieval input — a privacy+cost control the PI sets; a *summary* is a **derived Concept with a `derived` event** (traced), never an untracked shortcut. **The Ask/Query answer contract is sources-plus-unknowns:** every answer carries its citations *and* an explicit statement of its edges — unknowns, staleness, contradictions, missing evidence (which double as gap signals, §3); a confident answer with no stated edges is a defect.

**Text processing follows a baseline ladder** (the §14 discipline applied to text): stdlib/regex normalization + identifier extraction → source APIs → qmd/embedding retrieval or optional topic modelling → LLM only for the residual. **Raw extracted text is immutable** (`raw_text_sha256`); any normalized text used downstream records its own `normalized_text_sha256`.

**Draft & paper export (preserved, not expanded).** Alpha.11 does not redesign, extend, or depend on draft/export. Existing affordances may remain as compatibility surfaces, while the new writing module is deferred: later work drafts from the hub's notes/claims, and paper export remains the gated, irreversible boundary (Pandoc + CSL; agents propose, the PI confirms the send). Alpha.11 only needs the graph, trace, and checks that later writing consumes.

## 10. The detection layer — structural checks (= Karpathy's "lint")

The integrity mechanism the human gate used to be. Zero-LLM where possible; flag, never silent-mutate; on write and on schedule.

- **Knowledge-integrity checks:** **Citation/identifier resolves** (status per source `verified | partial | unverified | not-indexed`, with **field-level diffs** against Crossref/OpenAlex/PubMed/etc. — per-field status, never a single "integrity score") · **Span entailment** (model-free anchor check first; NLI only behind a HANS-style acceptance test, high-precision flag; verdict tri-class **SUPPORTED/REFUTED/NOTENOUGHINFO** carrying its evidence sentence(s), not just a label — FEVER) · **Counterfactual faithfulness** (inject a claim's span into a decoy source and re-extract; if the engine re-cites the decoy, flag low-confidence — grounds ≠ co-mentions, Wallat) · **Contradiction** (candidates only; human sets the Link) · **Supersession consistency** (no superseded-as-current; partial/scoped supersession) · **Orphaned warrant** (every claim has a resolvable evidence_set) · **Near-duplicate** (dedup; false-merge is the costly error → **merge candidates carry field-level provenance and are reviewed; auto-merge only on an exact identifier/hash match**) · **Retraction** (against feeds) · **Trace integrity** (every Concept has a derivation; content hashes match the files; an **untraced or foreign commit** — Obsidian Git or manual git, which git cannot prevent — is **quarantined and routed through backfill/import**).
- **Bibliographic-hygiene check (`check_source_metadata`):** a separate zero/low-LLM check on catalog Concepts (missing DOI/PMID/arXiv, bad date, missing creators, duplicate identifiers, unresolved attachment, snapshot-hash mismatch) — **bibliographic hygiene is split from knowledge integrity**.
- **Data gaps** (coverage/argument-completeness) are the gap-analysis half of lint (§9), distinct from the correctness checks above.

**Flags route act / ask / drop (not approval).** A fired check isn't a queue item by default — it's routed: **`act`** = a deterministic, reversible fix the worker applies and records (re-check, retrace, auto-rollback a clearly-bad machine write); **`ask`** = high-risk, or a **provenance/risk-class trigger** for the blind classes no check fires on (crafted-injection / semantic-grounding, §12) — the router surfaces a Concept whose derivation includes **freshly-ingested, uncorroborated untrusted source** (the "injection-prone" trigger of §14), *not* a check result → to the PI; **`drop`** = a low-value candidate, logged not shown. This is the gate-router applied to the *flag stream*, not to writes — it bounds the PI's attention (§8.2) without restoring per-write approval; thresholds follow the calibration below.

Thresholds follow a **shadow-first, error-budgeted calibration discipline**: no score affects routing until grounded within an error budget; ungrounded ⇒ report-only; drift ⇒ revert to shadow. Prompts are **validated per model** (small models are order/wording-sensitive); that validation is a real, recurring labelling cost — budgeted, not assumed.

## 11. Agent & model policy, and the autonomy ceiling

Policy binds to **operations**, not profiles/fleet identity. An operation policy is the runtime boundary: `operation_id`, allowed input types, allowed tools, allowed read paths, allowed write paths, model/provider (+ routing), prompt version, I/O schema, risk class, network allowance, required checks before promotion, required audit events, and whether human approval is required at an irreversible boundary. Initial alpha.11: `resolve_source, extract_annotations, index_claims, propose_links, gap_analyze, check_*, summarize`; post-alpha.11 writing adds redesigned `draft` + verify/export operations without removing existing affordances. **Separation of duties:** the operation that *generates* is never the one that *checks* — e.g. `extract_annotations` → span-entailment; `index_claims` → warrant + counterfactual-faithfulness; `propose_links` → link/contradiction validation (high-risk directional → human sets it); later `draft` → an independent verify pass. The loop is **not** generate→approve→repeat: an output is **born `unchecked`**, an independent checker (a different op/model) flips it `checked` *or* raises a flag routed `act`/`ask`/`drop` (§10); bounded auto-retry only on `act`; a human enters only via an `ask`-flag or, later, the irreversible export gate; everything is journaled. **No per-write approval loop.** **Trusted code owns the write** (§4.1).

**Provider routing is per-operation, with a hard pin on checks.** Each operation's policy sets cost/quality/provider/data-retention rules, runner (`direct_api` or `hermes`), model, seed when supported, toolset, fallback allowance, compression allowance, and retention/privacy for raw call logs. **Direct API is the baseline** because it leaves Memoria in charge of request shape, schema validation, replay evidence, and tool-call logging. **Fallback providers are allowed for drafting/discovery but the model used for checks and the seeded-error evals is pinned to a certified one** — an unvalidated fallback or server-side auto-router must never run a calibrated check. **Context compression is off for evidence-critical operations** (a lossy summary must not silently replace evidence); fine for chat/admin.

**Runner-specific rules.** Direct API operations may use an OpenAI-compatible gateway such as Kilo for provider access, usage accounting, bring-your-own-key routing, tool calling, structured outputs, and normal SDK ergonomics, but Memoria still logs the raw model route and rejects opaque/auto-changing routes for checks. Hermes operations instantiate a fresh `AIAgent` with `quiet_mode`, `skip_memory`, `skip_context_files`, explicit `enabled_toolsets`, bounded iterations, and no canonical write tools; they may use Hermes trajectories/transcripts as evidence, but Hermes session DB, memory, context files, compression, fallback, profiles, Kanban, cron, goals, and API server are never Memoria's source of truth.

**Direction is authored as the program, not the content.** The human's authored surface is `steering.md` + the project framing + operation policy + **the wiki `SCHEMA.md`** (conventions the human co-evolves — Karpathy: what makes the agent "a disciplined maintainer rather than a generic chatbot") — the `program.md`-equivalent (Karpathy `autoresearch`): the human programs *how* the research is done; agents produce *what* it yields. LLM output is candidate data, future draft prose, a triage/gap suggestion, or a check flag — **never authority**.

**The direction schema (what's PI vs agent).** Three buckets: **(a) agents act autonomously** within the current program — act-routed catalog ingest, candidate extraction (anchored notes/claims/links as candidates), gap/link suggestions, and checks; later writing adds drafting from existing claims; **(b) PI direction is required** — open/scope a project + its thesis, the steering/screening intent, responding to gaps (what to pursue next), the judgment calls (accept/reject/edit/roll back), and, later, the irreversible export gate; **(c) a *program change*** — editing `steering.md`, a project's thesis/scope, or operation policy — re-directs the agents and is always the PI's. The test for any feature: does it stay in (a), or does it silently do (b)/(c)? If the latter, it's over the line.

**Delegation is graduated by role, not uniform.** A **`digest`** is **fully delegated** — machine-owned, compounding; the PI may adopt (rewrite) it. A **`hub`** is **human-curated**; the machine keeps its synthesis fresh by *suggesting* updates, never overwriting the curation. A **`note`** is *two* tasks — *whether* to make it (PI judgment, needs context) and *writing* it (delegable): the agent **suggests** candidates through the grounded conversation (over the digests + sources + project), but the *whether* stays PI direction. The **catalog** is deterministic. (Digest compile + hub-synthesis suggestions = bucket (a); the note-*whether* + hub curation = bucket (b).)

**Capabilities are Concepts; discovery is ai-catalog (deferred).** Operations, skills, MCP configs, and workflows live in the `capabilities/` bundle as **OKF Concepts** (one per capability, policy in frontmatter). A **generated tracked `ai-catalog.json` projection** indexes them (one entry per Concept) — the right standard (ai-catalog catalogs MCP servers / agent cards / plugins). **Source of truth:** the capability Concepts, not the JSON. **Generator/check expectation:** the alpha.11 projection generator writes `capabilities/ai-catalog.json`, and the projection drift check fails if regenerating it from capability Concepts changes the tracked file. The file exists at alpha; its **trust manifest is exercised only on third-party import**, and **exposing Memoria's operations as MCP tools externally is deferred** (post-alpha.11, read-only first, §13). The **full ARD federated discovery service is rejected** — it solves a cross-org, web-scale problem the single-user local design doesn't have. (Two registries: **OKF** catalogs *knowledge*; **ai-catalog** catalogs *capabilities*. Direction — `steering.md`, the `program.md` — sits at the parent root.)

**The autonomy ceiling.** An autonomous keep/discard loop is admissible **only** where three preconditions hold (Karpathy `autoresearch`): a **scalar, monotonic metric that exists before the loop**, **reversible** changes, **independent, comparable** experiments. They hold for **code/engineering** — so a code lane *may* run an autonomous loop (deferred). They **fail for synthesis** (quality non-scalar, non-monotonic, interdependent) — so the knowledge lane is **human-directed**, and the **integrity spine is the substitute for the missing metric**. Hermes may *execute* an operation; its state never becomes Memoria state without a Memoria event.

## 12. Security model

**Threats:** poisoned source text manipulates extraction; injection persists through Concepts and re-poisons later sessions (Greshake); agent APIs become exfiltration paths; confident wrong outputs create overreliance; human/agent/system error corrupts state.

**Controls:** data/instruction channel separation; untrusted data delimited and ordered last (with output caps + stop-sequences); raw untrusted text never in system prompts; no outbound network in an operation that just consumed untrusted data unless required; per-operation tool allowlists; context reset between tasks; **agents never commit canonical files or the journal — only the worker commits**, and agent code execution is read-only/mechanical (§8); agent-produced content flagged in its derivation until checked; the **read barrier (§4.4)** keeps unchecked/quarantined/foreign content out of every consumer; structural + content-hash checks (§10); **trace+rollback bounds blast radius to a cascade** (complementing, not replacing, the sandbox). Perplexity-based cleaning of ingested text is **ingest hygiene / quality preprocessing, not a security control** — the security boundary is channel separation + tool allowlists + no-exfil.

**Capability import is supply chain, not just data.** Importing a `capabilities/` bundle brings in untrusted **code/instructions the agents will execute** (prompts, tool configs, MCP endpoints) — a higher-stakes surface than untrusted *content*. Vet it before the worker runs it via **ai-catalog's trust manifest** (attestations, provenance links, signatures); an unsigned/unattested imported capability is **quarantined, not run**. Alpha vetting = content hashes + source repo + quarantine + **SPDX license IDs** (required on imported executable capabilities, with an **SBOM**); **in-toto / SLSA** digest-bound attestations are the **deferred upgrade** once third-party import is real. This is the one place the design needs more than the untrusted-data controls above.

**Agent-rendered UI is declarative and catalog-bound.** If the plugin ever renders agent-produced UI, it must come from a **fixed component catalog** (`TaskStatus`, `GapCard`, `TraceGraph`, `RollbackPreview`, `SourceCandidate`, `ClaimEvidence`) — **never raw HTML/JS from an agent**, which is a code-execution / injection vector. The catalog is deferred with the plugin (§8.1); the *rule* holds now. AG-UI / A2UI / MCP-Apps are deferred external-surface references, not alpha dependencies.

**Residual risk (the load-bearing one).** Optimistic write — the cost of "direction not authorship" — **widens the persistence window** (Greshake): poisoned or semantically-wrong content can land in the canonical graph (flagged, unchecked) and be re-read before detection or the human catches it. The structural checks (§10) catch *structural* errors but **not** a crafted injection or a well-formed-but-unwarranted claim — "a crafted injection won't trip a structural check," and span-existence ≠ grounding. So the gateless model's safety rests on **detection recall**, which the refutation pass doubts for exactly this slice. Mitigations — channel separation, provenance flags, on-write checks, cascade rollback, and the read barrier (§4.4) — **close the window for the *checkable* classes**; this residual is the *uncheckable* slice. The design therefore **does not assume detection works**: it makes recall on this slice the falsifiable **seeded-error bar (§14)** and **re-introduces gating where recall is low** (the export gate, plus a **provenance-triggered human checkpoint on injection-prone content** — derived from a freshly-ingested, uncorroborated external source — before it becomes re-readable; the trigger is provenance/risk-class, not blind-class detection, §10/§14). Machine egress on every LLM API call / identifier lookup is a separate, always-on surface governed by tool allowlists + no-exfil + channel separation, *not* the human gate. This is the single risk most likely to force a gate back in.

**Non-claims:** the checks prove correctness; NLI proves contradiction; rollback un-rings every bell. **Claim only:** every change is attributable and reversible; violations leave evidence; integrity is checked structurally; blast radius is bounded; detection recall is measurable — and gating returns where it is not high enough.

## 13. Integrations — ownership splits

- **Memoria owns the catalog graph** — source identity (`source_id`/identifiers), an immutable snapshot, metadata, and the citation/authorship Links. It **does not become a reference manager** (no library management, no in-editor CSL/Word citation rendering — those stay with Zotero/JabRef). Alpha capture = identity + snapshot + metadata + graph, nothing more. **`references.bib` / CSL-JSON are generated tracked projections** (§6) for export interchange — Memoria does not hand-maintain a bibliography. Per-`item_type` metadata profile: **CSL-JSON** core, **DataCite** for datasets, **CFF/CodeMeta** for software/repositories. **SPDX license IDs** everywhere.
- **Zotero / JabRef = importers + design references, not authority.** Pull via the **Zotero Local API** (`localhost:23119`, read-only, offline — no SQLite scraping); import Zotero PDF annotations as **candidate anchored `note` Concepts** (Memoria stores the canonical quote/span/hash itself; Zotero annotations are intake, not the provenance store). JabRef's *.bib-is-the-database* validates §6; never mutate `raw/` (XMP-embedded metadata, if ever wanted, is a *derived* export, not an edit to the raw copy).
- **OKF-profile bundle roots = a native Memoria import/export path** (export is identity for the local profile), **per bundle root**: import external OKF bundles permissively after boundary validation (someone's `capabilities/` bundle, a shared `catalog/`); export any bundle root alone (your `knowledge/` without the copyrighted `catalog/`). Consume imported content as **untrusted data** (§12), produce the local profile strictly. Alpha export is an OKF directory/git/tar package; **RO-Crate / BagIt are deferred archival wrappers**, not alpha.11 work.
- **ai-catalog = the capability registry** (OKF is the knowledge registry): `capabilities/` emits a **generated `ai-catalog.json` projection** cataloguing operations/skills/MCPs/workflows, with a **trust manifest** to vet imported capabilities as supply chain (§11/§12). ARD's federated discovery *service* is out of scope. Exposing Memoria's operations over **MCP** (Resources/Tools) is **post-alpha.11, read-only first** — not a alpha dependency.
- **Obsidian owns** the editing environment + human prose. **Memoria owns** file structure, plugin views, the trace, recovery.
- **LLM execution belongs to Memoria's runner abstraction.** The default `direct_api` runner calls an OpenAI-compatible gateway/vendor endpoint directly (Kilo Gateway is the current gateway candidate); the optional `hermes` runner embeds Hermes as a Python library for selected agentic operations such as Co-PI/wiki synthesis. Hermes may own only the internal execution of that one operation plus preference/session memory when explicitly enabled — never Concepts, claims, sources, queue, journal, checks, or workflow state. Operation policy chooses model + routing; prompts are versioned; outputs are schema-validated; calls, costs, fallback, compression, and runner are logged.

## 14. Evaluation and calibration

The load-bearing question: **does the trace + check + rollback loop hold integrity without per-write approval?** — measurable. Evals run through a **batch runner** (the seeded-error gold set replayed repeatedly), not by hand.

**Integrity-loop verdict (seeded-error gold set).** Inject known errors into a frozen gold Bundle — including the cases structural checks are *blind* to: a **crafted injection** that reads as legitimate content, and a **well-formed-but-unwarranted claim** (the cited span exists but doesn't ground it) — alongside the structural ones (poisoned spans, wrong extractions, false Links, a planted contradiction, a stale-as-current claim). Measure, **per error class**: **detection recall** (make-or-break), time-to-detection + false-positive rate, **rollback completeness**, and **residual error vs a raw no-checks baseline**. These define the **seeded-error bar**, which explicitly covers the **security/persistence slice**, not just quality. Recall too low on any class ⇒ **re-introduce gating for that class** (e.g. a human checkpoint before injection-prone content becomes re-readable), by evidence. **Calibrate the human checkpoint itself, too:** measure whether PI-checkpointed items (esp. crafted-injection / unwarranted-claim) are more correct than the same items un-checkpointed — applying to "the PI noticing" (§4.3) and the gate (§4.5) the same falsifiability as to the structural checks; a checkpoint that doesn't beat that baseline is theatre (thin it, §18).

**Capability-regression eval (ongoing).** Recall@k / support-rate / citation-correctness on known sources — diagnostic, never a gate. **Certify stochastic engines before they run unattended:** state-based regression (judge the resulting Bundle state, not the trajectory), **pass^k consistency** (all k reruns succeed, not best-of-k), trivial-control baselines (so triage gains aren't distributional shortcuts), and any LLM-judge **validated against the PI's own accept/reject decisions** before it is trusted. **Operational metrics:** flags/period, time-to-resolution, rollback frequency, accept-vs-edit-vs-false-positive distribution (rubber-stamp/anomaly detection), cost, and **lapse/tending signals** (is the Zettelkasten being tended — anchored notes, other notes, and Links being added — or stalling? abandonment is the dominant PKM failure). **Production readiness:** does the corpus reach writable density (linked-claim density), and does gap analysis converge to saturation?

**Temporal & supersession eval.** Test supersession as *behaviour*, not principle: **`Temporal Coverage@k` / `Temporal Precision@k`** (does top-k span both the baseline *and* the current time window?) and step-wise **baseline-vs-current** retrieval on a supersession query — temporally-wrong evidence is worse than none (TEMPO). The seeded-error set's stale-as-current case exercises it.

**Baseline discipline (a standing rule).** *Every capability declares a cheap baseline and must beat it before it counts as a design claim* — BM25 (retrieval), no-checks (integrity), unranked batch (triage), long-context whole-read (synthesis), no-suggestion UI (the gate). The corpus's strongest empirical warning is that apparent gains are often distributional shortcuts (a one-line BM25 query beats agentic search); this rule keeps every claim honest.

## 15. Milestones

**Principle:** every milestone delivers a *usable increment*. A milestone whose only outcome is "proceed to the next" is meaningless — the current design hit exactly that wall. So the shape is **validate-before-building → ship the whole basic cycle + plugin → test it → extend only if needed.** Optimistic writes stay on a **disposable test Bundle** until the §14 seeded-error bar is met; that gate guards *real-Bundle* use — it is not a separate crawl-milestone.

- **M0 — Validate everything checkable without app code.** Completed locally for implementation start: OKF-compatible local contract + round-trip, schema reset fixture, git-backed trusted-writer smoke, deterministic wiki fixture compile, qmd disposable-bundle search, source_id/citekey stability, disposable plugin static + Local REST + mock activation smoke, and seeded-error harness shape. Partial/not completed by M0, and not blockers to start alpha.11: live Zotero annotation sample, PDF span-preservation toolchain, external OKF validator interop, model-quality wiki evaluation, visual Obsidian panel activation, and final seeded-error verdict. Evidence: [m0-spike-results.md](m0-spike-results.md), [git-writer-smoke-results.md](git-writer-smoke-results.md), [partial-pass-completion-results.md](partial-pass-completion-results.md), and [start-blocker-verification-results.md](start-blocker-verification-results.md).
- **0.1.0-alpha.11 — the basic knowledge cycle, end to end, *with the plugin*.** The whole loop usable in Obsidian on a test Bundle — **open a project → seed → gap analysis → source → capture → conversation → digest + hub compounding → notes → re-run gap analysis** — on the **integrity spine** (trusted writer, journal, structural checks, read barrier, trace→cascade-rollback) with the **plugin** (the Co-PI conversation, knowledge-graph browsing, project/gap + flags panels, trace→rollback of §8.1). *Exit:* a real source goes round the cycle in Obsidian — interviewed, compiled into digests + hubs, distilled into notes; every write traced and checked; a seeded error caught and rolled back.
- **Test the implementation.** Run the §14 seeded-error verdict (batch runner) against alpha.11; set the bar. *Exit:* bar met → cleared for **real-Bundle** use; missed → targeted gating for the weak class, then re-test.
- **Later, only if needed:** redesigned/expanded drafting/verification + export, directed-discovery at scale, project argument-graph maturity, the code-lane autonomy exception (§11), advanced graph exploration.

Anything that doesn't reach the usable cycle + plugin isn't a milestone — it's a task inside alpha.11.

## 16. The one bet, and the first slice that settles it

There are **two sequenced bets**, settled at different points so a failure localizes cleanly:
- **Bet A — storage/trace works** (the storage/capture spine of alpha.11). Can a real source be captured into files and the **trace + index rebuilt from scratch**, with every write carrying a coupled derivation? Failure here is *plumbing*.
- **Bet B — integrity recall holds** (§14; **alpha.11 builds the mechanism, the post-build verdict returns the recall**). Can trace + check + rollback keep the base trustworthy **without approving every write** — is "human directs, LLM writes" safe? The build carries ordinary risk; the *thesis* is settled only by the **seeded-error measurement** that gates real-Bundle use.

**First slice = 0.1.0-alpha.11 itself** (on a test Bundle, §15): the full **capture → conversation → wiki → notes** spine + gap analysis + the integrity loop. **Bet A** is its storage/capture half; **Bet B** is its conversation/synthesis/notes/check/rollback half, with the **seeded-error measurement** the gate to any real Bundle. Keeping them separable means a failure points at storage *or* the bet, not both. **Excluded from alpha.11:** draft/export redesign or expansion, automatic Link/contradiction *promotion*, graph visualization beyond the Canvas, nightly discovery, multi-user, a sync-capable/authoritative DB, in-editor CSL rendering.

## 17. What the reset must not lose (carried forward as first-class)

Carry forward the **requirements**, not the old implementation machinery:
- **Runtime safety:** the clean-slate boundary is the **operation contract**: PI intent → operation request → worker execution under the contract → staging output → independent checks → promotion or quarantine → audit/journal event. A role/profile name is never the enforcement boundary; enforcement comes from the operation's tool, path, network, check, and audit rules.
- **Integrity spine:** tamper-evident provenance (§4.1), structural checks (§10), gold-set eval (§14), generator≠checker separation, retraction monitoring, dedup, and shadow-first calibration (§10).
- **Enforcement boundaries:** capability-vs-path separation, direct-tool denial, audited mutation, and tests that prove boundaries fire. The old five-profile/fleet shape is not carried forward.
- **Co-PI safety split:** the conversational front reads directly, delegates mutations, and never commits canonical files; memory is preferences/session context only, never Concepts or claims.
- **Project readiness:** gap analysis / project argument graph (§3/§9), project framing, question history, thesis/survey mode, saturation/refutation readiness, and gap state stay first-class.
- **Attention model:** attention is a generated view over journal events, Concept state, check results, and queue state. Proposals keep an honesty body (action / for / against / what tipped it / certainty); flags lead with evidence and derivation; loudness/block semantics survive. Old Inbox card types do not remain durable Concepts.
- **Plugin boundary:** the plugin is read-only for inspection and enqueue-only for mutations. It may request operation enqueueing and attention acknowledgements/resolutions through the worker API, but it never writes Concepts, projections, the journal, or status fields directly.
- **Schema drift controls:** one canonical schema/folder source, schema-valid templates, view/property drift checks, generated-form checks, and installer skeleton checks.
- **Retrieval currency:** retrieval hides superseded/non-current content by default; historical lookup must be explicit.
- **Operational observability:** read-only telemetry over audit, lint, worker, and lane health remains; plugin provenance locks remain. This preserves the inspector safety property, not a read-only-only plugin boundary.
- **Future writing/engineering handoff:** alpha.11 preserves the graph, evidence links, refutation state, provenance, and project argument structure that later `writing.*` and `engineering.*` operation families need. It does not carry the old writer/engineer lanes into alpha.11 as live capabilities.

Deliberately shed or defer (machinery, not the useful property): per-write approval, durable Inbox cards as the canonical workflow model, profiles/fleet as the primary runtime model, the Hermes control-plane DB, the seven-layer ceremony, alpha.11 draft/export redesign or expansion, and the code-lane autonomy exception.

## 19. Decisions to record as ADRs
Grouped for a small, reviewable ADR set (one cluster ≈ one ADR or a tight family). **The reset is a mass-retirement event:** one *reset* ADR supersedes the obsolete set en masse; these clusters are written **current-state, self-contained, no history/lineage prose**, pruned to the minimum, so each can later be retired or replaced cleanly. History lives in superseded ADRs + releasing notes, not in the live set.

**Identity** — opinionated personal thinking-and-writing tool (Razlogova × Karpathy; **production methodology, not knowledge base**) · **direction, not authorship** (human directs the program; LLM produces, incl. writing) · the **direction schema** (agents-autonomous / PI-direction-required / program-change, §11).

**Storage & format** — **record = catalog sources + notes + `steering.md`** (incl. the wiki SCHEMA) **+ the append-only content-hashed journal** (**PROV-DM**); machine-authored nodes (`digest`/hub-synthesis) are **records too** (checked + read-barrier'd, not disposable); **durable non-text = Canvas positions + `raw/` blobs**; everything else a **projection** (tracked: index.md/ai-catalog.json/references.bib; export-only: log.md; disposable: index/embeddings/DAG/open-flags/queue; **index = interface** (BM25+vector+hybrid); **qmd backend** verified for disposable BM25 index/search, SQLite FTS5+sqlite-vec the baseline/fallback — LanceDB/Tantivy if vector/lexical-heavy; Turso a post-alpha spike); three-way gitignore (committed text / out-of-band `raw/` / disposable); **frontmatter = JSON Schema 2020-12, bodies = CommonMark**; raw + normalized text hashes · **Memoria workspace with three OKF-compatible bundle roots** (`catalog/` objective · `knowledge/` the one graph [`digest`/`note`/`hub`] · `capabilities/`; Obsidian opens `knowledge/`; trace/worker/index at the workspace) · **OKF core + Memoria profile** (OKF = Markdown/frontmatter/required `type`/standard links/generated `index.md`, plus OKF-friendly `title`/`description`/`resource`/`tags`; external interop boundary-tested when tooling exists) · **Concept ID = stable slug** (immutable; title in frontmatter; rename = journal event); **source path = immutable `source_id`, citekey a generated alias, `resource` the canonical external URI when present** (fixture-verified across citekey correction) · **cross-bundle refs = bundle-qualified Concept ID** (Memoria metadata; raw copies isolated in `catalog/` for shareability · **Concept = one doc + referenced attachments** (a source = `source.md` + `content`/`raw`); **scoped retirement** (record/entity/vault valid in their own scopes; **Writer, trace & integrity** — **supported write path** (worker commits {Concept + derivation event}) + **trace-integrity scan** quarantines/backfills foreign/untraced commits (**not an enforceable sole committer**); **two write paths**, **both committing `{Concept + derivation}` and born `unchecked`** (on-write checks flip `unchecked → checked`; the commit never bundles check results); PI-edit back-fills `inputs = prior upstream ∪ prior-HEAD hash` (keeps cascade reachable; `actor: operation` vs `pi`) · **traceability + structural detection + cascade rollback** as the integrity model; **rollback unit = output Concept** (the inverse commit appends `resolved`/`derived` events; flags propagate transitively past human-directed nodes) · **read barrier** (consume only `checked`; **enforced by `.memoria/staging/{catalog,knowledge,capabilities}/` → promote-to-target-bundle-on-pass + `.memoria/quarantine/`, not just a flag**) · structural-check suite (= lint) + separate `check_source_metadata` (bibliographic hygiene); near-duplicate is review-only (auto-merge only on exact id/hash); shadow-first calibration; check-fired + resolution = journal events; **flags route act/ask/drop** (not approval).

**Security & gating** — gate only at irreversible boundaries; **gateless is conditional** (gating returns per error class where the seeded-error detection recall is insufficient, esp. crafted-injection / semantic-grounding) · sandbox necessary-not-sufficient + channel separation; agent code-exec read-only (canonical writes via trusted writer); context compression off for evidence-critical ops · **capability import = supply chain**, vetted by ai-catalog trust manifest (unsigned ⇒ quarantined) · **agent-rendered UI declarative + catalog-bound** (no raw HTML/JS).

**Surface & orchestration** — **Obsidian + a Memoria plugin** (thin in code, **part of alpha.11**); CLI backs development; QuickAdd/Modal-Form/Buttons to prototype enqueue; **Local REST API** as the bridge; Bases/Canvas native, Dataview only if declared; **avoid Templater + Obsidian-Git auto-commit** · **plugin = control panel** (read generated status/projections; enqueue operations and attention resolutions only through the worker; no canonical writes) · **single per-machine worker + one local queue** (cron / plugin / file-watch triggers); direct API default LLM runner + Hermes specialized adapter · **workflow state = queue (live) + journal (durable) + projections (visibility); Hermes/Obsidian/SQLite/LangGraph/UI-frameworks never own it** · **attention = generated views over journal/check/queue/Concept state, not durable card Concepts** · **Co-PI conversation** (grounded in wiki+project) + **Ask** cited query (the Explore/Ask/Transform triad superseded) · heavy frameworks (LangChain/LlamaIndex/Celery/Neo4j/ES) rejected.

**Knowledge model** — **one knowledge graph, machine + human by role**: catalog (objective) + `digest` (machine, per-source) / `note` (human, atomic) / `hub` (machine-synthesized + human-curated); **the wiki is a role, not a store** · the **ingest spine = capture → conversation → digest+hub compounding → distill notes → curate hubs** (the conversation is the hinge; compounding 5-15 nodes/source is **core — the Co-PI differentiator, not deferrable**; two promotions: **adoption** concept-page→hub, **distillation** digest→notes) · **one note type** (`note`: optional anchor = "annotation", optional assertion = "claim"; `evidence_set` = supporting Links, embedded; **`annotation`/`claim`/`fleeting`/`source-digest`/ZK-`index` are not separate types**; `#todo` tag not `fleeting`; **no `maturity`**) + `hub` · notes cite **sources, not machine-synthesis nodes**; **`version_of` catalog-only**; **`annotation_ref` = W3C Web-Annotation selector subset** (parser preserves page/span/bbox); dead-end/negative notes queryable; **Ask answer contract = sources + unknowns/staleness/contradictions/missing-evidence** · **one project type** (thesis optional → gap lens); **project = Link-defined subgraph, not a bundle** (`in_project` membership) · **gap analysis first-class, over both graphs + their mismatch** · **graduated delegation by role** (digest full; hub machine-suggests/human-curates; note-*whether* = PI); **Toulmin optional/project-scoped**; **no Work Concept** · operation-level policy with generator≠checker + per-operation provider routing (checks pinned) · **autonomy ceiling** (autonomous loops only where scalar+reversible+independent hold — code lane, never synthesis).

## 20. Migration & cutover (clean rebuild, not in-place)
The reset is **re-derived, not refactored** (§Method), so cutover is a **one-way rebuild onto the new bundle tree**, never an in-place mutation of the current vault.
- **Inventory** the current implementation's data: sources, annotations, claims, links, source-notes, projects, provenance.
- **Map using the §7.1 target model — preserve the *record*, regenerate the *machine layer*:** existing source records → `catalog/sources/<source_id>/source.md` (+ `content.md`/`raw/`); current source-note prose, annotations, claims, and kept fleeting notes → `knowledge/notes/<id>.md` (anchored/asserting as needed); existing links → typed Links; current projects/theses → `knowledge/projects/<id>/project.md` + `in_project` Links. The **wiki layer (`digest` + machine-only hub synthesis) is re-derived by re-ingesting** through the new pipeline (machine-derivable — don't migrate it). Current operational/transient records become regenerated projections/queue state or explicit migration-report drops.
- **One-way migrator**, **dry-run on a test bundle (memoria-test) first**; the **real vault migrates only after the seeded-error bar clears** (§14). `git` is the rollback; the source `raw/` is never mutated.
- **Data-preservation checklist (must round-trip):** every source's identity + raw + provenance; every human note + its Citations; every accepted claim's warrant; project membership. A migration report names what mapped, what was re-derived, and what was dropped — **nothing silently**.
- **No compatibility mode:** old and new trees don't run live together; cutover is per-bundle, verified against the checklist before the old tree is archived.

## Provenance
- **Genesis:** Razlogova, *Doing History with Zotero and Obsidian* (the manual knowledge-production methodology) × Karpathy's **LLM wiki** (the LLM-driven markdown store). Memoria = the former, made LLM-driven — Karpathy's wiki engine pointed at *producing* knowledge, not *storing* it, via the **capture → conversation → digest+hub → notes** ingest spine (the conversation produces the machine synthesis; the human distills notes; compounding makes the agent a Co-PI).
- **Forward:** first-principles derivation from §1 requirements and the §2 theses.
- **Backward:** `_papers/REVIEW-REFUTATIONS.md` (surviving skeleton: durable state, verbatim warrants, sparse review, deterministic grounding, supersession-as-precondition), `_papers/REVIEW-SUMMARY.md` (the 401-paper adopt pass), and `_notes/ai-research-systems-survey.md` (borrow/adapt/ignore). The design adopts the corpus's strongest validated moves and **refuses every "ignore" pattern** (full autonomy, tree-search-over-synthesis, autonomous keep/revert, co-trained reviewer, preference-into-weights, confidence-routed gate bypass); it leaves the corpus consensus on exactly **one** point — replacing the human approval gate with traceability+detection+rollback — following the later refutation evidence and making that bet falsifiable by the seeded-error verdict.
- **Convergent:** OKF (`GoogleCloudPlatform/knowledge-catalog`) independently specifies the §6 substrate and supplies the vocabulary — a fourth re-derivation of the storage layer.
- **Reference scans (borrow the boring parts):** Kilo Gateway / direct OpenAI-compatible API (boring default runner: SDK-shaped calls, model listing, bring-your-own-key routing, usage accounting, tool calling, structured outputs, no workflow state); Hermes (specialized Python-library adapter + the **`wiki` skill** — a ready Karpathy-pattern markdown workflow; M0 compiled a five-page linked fixture); the **`llmwiki` compiler** (future benchmark for review/eval/qmd patterns, not an alpha.11 dependency); the **Karpathy LLM-wiki gist** (the compounding pattern + qmd); Open Notebook (per-source context levels); Zotero/JabRef (importers + `source_id`/citekey split + membership-not-location + bibliographic-hygiene lint); the generative-UI survey (declarative catalog-bound UI as a security rule, not a framework). MkDocs/static-site output from these tools is ignored for alpha.11; the dependency is the markdown compounding pattern.
- **The sharpenings the evidence forced:** integrity is structural, not per-atom; the human **directs, does not author**; the gate is sparse and falsifiable, not axiomatic (Jacobs 2021); the dominant threat is poisoned data, met by trace + rollback + channel separation (Greshake); the canonical unit is the verbatim span/anchored note, the claim an index over it (LongMemEval/Omni-SimpleMem); autonomy is admissible only where a scalar metric + reversibility + independence hold (Karpathy `autoresearch`).
