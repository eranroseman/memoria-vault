# Beta.1 Paper Adversarial Review

Scope: beta.1 charter + system design, pressured with the paper-centered
findings folded into [0.1.0-beta.1-research-evidence.md](0.1.0-beta.1-research-evidence.md). Findings are numbered
by severity. Conflicts between corpus findings are adjudicated inline.

---

## Findings

### 1. [BLOCKER] The Checker's uncalibrated .8 NLI thresholds sit on the trust chain's only promotion path

**Pressure:** Utama et al. 2021 (HANS lexical-overlap heuristic: median non-entailment accuracy collapses below 10% while entailment hits ~100%); REVIEW-REFUTATIONS.md (Sainz 2021: zero-shot NLI tops 57.8 F1 and the default threshold is badly miscalibrated — 30.1 vs 45.7 dev-tuned; ExpertQA TRUE-NLI precision .88 but recall .38); Ji et al. 2022 (off-the-shelf NLI "transfers poorly" to faithfulness; "Putin is U.S. president" can score entailed from "Putin is president"; sentence-level entailment cannot localize what is wrong).

**Failure scenario:** A digest engine writes "X causes Y," reusing the anchor's vocabulary where the anchor says "X correlated with Y in one 1997 sample." Lexical overlap drives entail ≥ .8; the Checker promotes m→mc. In the same run, a paper that genuinely contradicts the claim — phrased with shared vocabulary, as scholarly contradictions are — never reaches contradict ≥ .8 and is filed as "mentions." Both errors are silent, mechanical, and repeat at machine-write scale, and the review queue risk-ranks them low precisely because they passed the Checker. This is the highest-probability route to a trust incident against a pre-registered budget of zero.

**Adjudication:** The corpus does not support replacing the local NLI. CiteGuard (Choi et al. 2026) shows LLM-judge citation attribution with recall as low as 16–17%; Perez et al. 2022 shows LLM stance judgment inherits scale-worsening sycophancy toward the asker's framing; Xu et al. 2022 shows conventionally fine-tuned classifiers resist adversarial triggers that break prompt-based ones. The instrument class survives; the untested operating point does not.

**Recommendation:** Before the 10-week clock starts: (a) build a small claim–anchor calibration set from corpus-like scholarly text and dev-tune both thresholds (Sainz shows a ~15-point swing from tuning alone); (b) add HANS/PAWS-style high-overlap negation and direction-reversal probes to the Checker's acceptance test; (c) treat "mentions" as *unknown stance*, never as no-contradiction; (d) add Checker false-promotion rate to the weekly citation audit, weighted toward unsupported-added-detail cases, which NLI scores neutral (Ji et al.).

### 2. [CRITICAL] "Corpus has nothing" is an ontological claim the retriever cannot support

**Pressure:** Su et al. 2025 (BRIGHT: on reasoning-intensive queries BM25 averages 14.5 nDCG@10, BGE 13.7; best of 13 models 24.3); Yang et al. 2018 (HotpotQA: bridge-entity questions collapse from EM 43.41 to 19.76 when retrieval is required; gold-paragraph Hits@10 only 55.88); Abdallah et al. 2026 (TEMPO: BM25 worst-in-class at 2.8–13.4 nDCG@10; cross-period comparison consistently hardest).

**Failure scenario:** The researcher asks "what else in my corpus uses this mechanism" (analogical), "what connects A to B" (bridge), or "how has X changed since 2015" (cross-period). The $0 stack misses reasoning-linked, second-hop, or off-period evidence at measured high rates; Ask prints "corpus has nothing" over a confident document-count denominator. The researcher trusts the absence, writes the gap into a draft, and later finds the evidence by hand — a trust incident charged to the design's most honest-looking feature. The denominator compounds it: it counts documents, not periods or hops searched, so it certifies coverage the search never performed.

**Adjudication:** Hu et al. 2026 (BM25 41.5 vs every graph/agentic memory system ≤ 27), Neekhra et al. 2026, Tam et al. 2022, Buchmann & Gurevych 2026, and Omni-SimpleMem's autonomous convergence on hybrid dense+sparse all validate the stack as the right default architecture. The conflict resolves cleanly: keep the retriever, shrink the claim. The attack is on the epistemics of the surface, not the stack.

**Recommendation:** (a) Reword the empty answer to "nothing matched lexical/semantic search over N documents" — a searched-set claim, never corpus ontology; (b) count truncated/abstract_only works as *suspect* in the denominator, not covered (Zou et al. 2024: silent truncation drives the worst document-QA failures); (c) add a cheap second-hop query on entities extracted from first-round hits (HotpotQA's named mechanism); (d) print publication-year spread of retrieved anchors as a temporal flag (TEMPO); (e) scope the ≤3x-ChatGPT parity claim away from cross-period and multi-hop question classes in the pre-registration.

### 3. [CRITICAL] "Contrasts" is doubly blind: contradicting evidence is under-retrieved, then under-labeled

**Pressure:** Zhang et al. 2026 (MRMR: contradiction retrieval below 25% Hit@1 on Negation — chance level; the best model drops from 67.4 to 36.6 nDCG@10 moving from Knowledge to Contradiction); Utama et al. 2021 (shared-vocabulary contradictions rarely reach contradict ≥ .8).

**Failure scenario:** Two independent failures compound. Similarity-based retrieval systematically fails to surface the contradicting paper; when it does surface, the NLI fails to label it. The researcher reads an empty contrasts list as "my corpus does not disagree," and composes accordingly. This is the precise failure the design's own Govers et al. 2026 rationale (conformity amplification, d = 0.81) exists to prevent — beta.1 correctly excluded agreement meters, but an empty contrasts list *is* an implicit agreement meter.

**Recommendation:** Render contrasts as "contradictions found *within the retrieved set*"; never render an empty list as absence of contradiction; add contradiction-recall cases to the stance calibration set from finding 1.

### 4. [HIGH] mc reads as "grounded," but the Checker can only certify citation-correctness

**Pressure:** Wallat et al. 2024 (up to 57% of RAG citations are post-rationalized: a statement planted in a relevant-but-unused document gets cited, passing correctness/NLI checks that do not reflect how the answer was produced); Si et al. 2023 (~15% parametric-override residue even with contradicting in-context evidence).

**Failure scenario:** Paid synthesis answers from parametric memory, attaches an anchor that happens to entail the sentence, and the Checker promotes to mc. The researcher reads mc as "derived from my corpus." No mechanism in beta.1 can distinguish derivation from post-rationalization — NLI entailment is structurally the wrong instrument for faithfulness.

**Recommendation:** Relabel mc as "citation-correct (machine-checked)" in UI and docs; document the faithfulness gap explicitly. The never-green taint already handles rendering correctly (Sundar 2020's machine heuristic independently supports it); the fix is semantic honesty, not new machinery. Byte-substring quotes remain safe — the text is real even when the derivation is not.

### 5. [HIGH] No supersession concept: a retracted or corrected work stays clean-provenance forever

**Pressure:** Uddin et al. 2026 (Memora: models and memory agents "frequently reuse obsolete information" under mutation; standard accuracy rewards mere inclusion; FAMA penalizes reliance on invalidated memory).

**Failure scenario:** A captured paper is later retracted or corrected. Taint is monotone and permanent, but there is no *invalidation* dimension; Ask resurfaces the claim with fully-green provenance, export passes, and the deliverable cites a retracted result — a trust incident the entire provenance apparatus is blind to.

**Adjudication:** LOCOMO (Maharana et al. 2024) and ClawArena (Ji et al. 2026) show beta.1's stateless recompute-per-Ask correctly sidesteps machine belief-maintenance failure — but the vault itself is the mutable memory, and nothing down-ranks stale entries. Brandt et al. 2010 (Blueprint) supplies the borrowable mechanism: a lightweight source-changed-since-capture check, distinct from the taint/manifest path.

**Recommendation:** Add a researcher-settable (and capture-refresh-detectable) superseded/invalidated flag that down-ranks and visibly labels affected anchors in Ask; add a FAMA-style probe to the weekly citation audit — does Ask resurface flagged items?

### 6. [HIGH] The payoff claim lives exactly where top-k fragment retrieval is measurably weakest

**Pressure:** Hu et al. 2026 (every RAG agent degrades long-range understanding vs long context: BM25 LRU 35.6 vs 46.2; smaller chunks make it worse); Lála et al. 2023 ablations (agentic re-query and LLM relevance re-scoring account for ~30% relative accuracy over single-pass RAG).

**Failure scenario:** The thesis-deciding corpus-grounded compose moment is integrative by definition. Fed top-k paragraph fragments, the priced synthesis produces a disjoint answer; the same-day ChatGPT counterfactual with whole papers pasted into long context wins on coherence. The payoff thesis then fails for evidence *granularity*, not for grounding — and the three-verdict exit misreads it as flow failure.

**Recommendation:** For synthesis and compose evidence assembly, expand retrieved anchors to coherent document slices (whole sections/notes), keeping anchor citability; make the priced button include iterative retrieval and LLM relevance re-scoring (Lála's +30%; BRIGHT's +12.2 query-expansion gain — cost-honest on a paid path); instrument synthesis-shaped Asks separately so the exit verdict can attribute failure correctly.

### 7. [MEDIUM] Ambient provenance cannot carry the anti-overreliance load in the tolerance claim

**Pressure:** Jacobs et al. 2021 (wrong ML recommendations dragged clinicians below their own unaided baseline, 0.299 vs 0.357, p = 0.0018; adding interpretable explanations made it *worse* — "explanations are insufficient for addressing overreliance").

**Failure scenario:** The zero-trust-incident argument implicitly assumes the researcher notices markers on a wrong machine write. The evidence says visible explanation does not prevent overreliance, so a post-incident analysis crediting "the researcher would have seen it" is unsound.

**Adjudication:** The same literature (Jacobs' qualitative studies, AutoResearchClaw's HITL ablation) confirms per-item approval is worse, not better. The correction is attribution, not gating.

**Recommendation:** In the pre-registration, attribute the trust-incident defense only to vigilance-independent mechanisms (never-green taint, sha256 manifest gate, byte-identical revert, export gate); if an incident occurs, measure whether reviewed items were actually corrected (gate calibration) rather than reverting to approvals.

### 8. [MEDIUM] The evaluation risks the reverse-funnel: an interpretive question in quantitative costume

**Pressure:** Boehner et al. 2007 (HCI converting interpretive instruments into formalized metrics manufactures false certainty — "reversing the funnel"); Zheng et al. 2026 (in an incentivized 8-week pilot, engagement was the single missed criterion — 39.5% vs 70% target — despite payment and 91.5% retention; the protocol response was revision, not abandonment).

**Failure scenario:** A borderline reuse-rate or plateau number in an n=1 study mechanically triggers a kill/redesign verdict that qualitative reality contradicts — or false confidence in a "pass." Unpaid n=1 engagement will look worse than the incentivized trial's, so a missed engagement number alone is expected, not diagnostic.

**Adjudication:** Mueller et al. 2019 (work-system measurement), Epstein et al. 2020 (resumption-gated abandonment), and Zheng validate the instruments themselves; Boehner's correction is precedence, not deletion.

**Recommendation:** State in the pre-registration that the devil's-advocate memo and researcher judgment are the primary verdict basis, with event-log numbers as evidence into that judgment; map a missed engagement criterion alone to targeted redesign, never kill; add Luger & Sellen 2016's relegation indicator (cluster of failed/empty Asks followed by narrowing to capture-only) as a leading signal ahead of the 14-zero-event-day trigger.

## Survivors

Decisions the corpus attacked from multiple angles and failed to refute:

- **Containment-only security; injection classifiers excluded.** Zhan et al. 2024 (InjecAgent), Greshake et al. 2023, Kang et al. 2023 (100% filter bypass), Branch et al. 2022 (every detection metric fails), Debenedetti et al. 2024 (AgentDojo), Andriushchenko et al. 2025 (AgentHarm), Perez & Ribeiro 2022 (injection success scales *with* capability), Shaikh et al. 2023. One residual audit from AgentDojo: verify no single engine both reads untrusted text and holds a capability pairing sufficient to exfiltrate. Xu et al. 2022 adds the supply-chain corollary: pin and hash the deberta-mnli and bge weights.
- **Single-writer VaultWriter, human-span sha256 pre==post gate, block-level read-only.** Bernstein et al. 2010 (Soylent: ~30% of external edits to human prose were bad; verify caught only 50%), Yao et al. 2024 (τ-bench: prompt-level policy fails), Ruan et al. 2024 (ToolEmu: 23.9% risky-action floor), Blumenthal & Clark 2001 (endpoint check principle).
- **No per-item approval; capped expiring zero-nag queue.** Liu et al. 2026 (AutoResearchClaw: targeted checkpoints beat step-by-step approval 7.27 vs 5.19), Waldman 2020 (consent theater), Jacobs et al. 2021, Chordia et al. 2023, Erickson et al. 2002, Feng & Liu 2026.
- **Export citation-resolve gate + weekly random audit.** Meng et al. 2026 (ScientistOne: retrieval-bound citation = architecturally zero fabrication), Li et al. 2026 (CiteTracer; 647 ICLR desk-rejects), Press et al. 2024 (CiteME: frontier LMs 4.2–18.5%), Lála et al. 2023 (0% vs 29–43% hallucinated citations), Rao & Callison-Burch 2026, Malaviya et al. 2024. AutoResearchClaw's registry ablation: pre-commit to reading export refusals as the gate working, and log refusal counts.
- **Byte-substring quote assertion, kept hard.** Wu et al. 2026 (Intern-atlas: same gate at 9.4M-edge scale), Zhong et al. 2021 (only mechanical grounding separates recall from prior), Liu et al. 2026 (ARA: ~21-point gain from claim–evidence bindings), Ouyang et al. 2022 (~21% fabrication even in aligned closed-domain output — size the audit sample accordingly).
- **Hybrid BM25+embedding RRF, small-k MMR, anchored paragraphs, no graph/agentic memory.** Hu et al. 2026, Neekhra et al. 2026, Buchmann & Gurevych 2026 (both legs load-bearing), Tam et al. 2022, Omni-SimpleMem, Maharana et al. 2024 (assertion-granularity retrieval; SNR beats recall), Wu et al. 2025 (LongMemEval: verbatim beats summaries), Kang & Xiong 2024 (agents lose to keyword retrieval).
- **Abstention and every printed number computed in code, never by the model.** Maharana et al. 2024 (abstention collapses 13.1→2.1 as context grows), Zou et al. 2024 (37.1% vs 70.2% — pipeline property), Parrish et al. 2022, Somasekharan et al. 2026, Zhang et al. 2026 (KnowledgeBerg), Gao et al. 2023 (PAL), Karpas et al. 2022, Merrill et al. 2026.
- **Local deterministic NLI/embeddings over LLM judgment in the trust chain.** Perez et al. 2022 (sycophancy), Zhao et al. 2021, Lu et al. 2022, Liu et al. 2023, Workman et al. 2024 (13.8-point swing from rewording), Webson & Pavlick 2022, Schick & Schütze 2021, Choi et al. 2026 — subject to finding 1's calibration gate.
- **The exclusions.** Discovery: Xiong et al. 2026 (AutoResearchBench <10%), PaperScope, Chamoun et al. 2026, Gridach et al. 2025, Majumder et al. 2024, Chen et al. 2025, Huang et al. 2025 (architecture-protecting, not scope-trimming). Aggregate meters: Govers et al. 2026 (d = 0.81), Hearst 2009, KnowledgeBerg. Machine critique: Zhao et al. 2026 (PaperMind). Agentic tool loops: Rabinovich & Anaby-Tavor 2025, τ-bench.
- **In-file automatic provenance + ambient rendering; runs and one-click revert.** Kandel et al. 2012, Brandt et al. 2010, Crothers et al. 2023 (post-hoc detection is dead), Zhicheng Liu et al. 2008, Amershi et al. 2019, Weisz et al. 2024, Sundar 2020, Shneiderman & Maes 1997, Beth Kery & Myers 2017, Yang et al. 2020, Alkhatib & Bernstein 2019 (also: weight audits toward marginal/unusual items).
- **Capture and thesis mechanics.** Zotero CSL-JSON over LLM metadata (Rao & Callison-Burch 2026); flag-not-repair extraction (Li et al. 2025 SciLitLLM); durable anchored fulltext (Wang & Luan 2026); post-hoc citation paradigm (Saxena et al. 2025); grounding-beats-scale for the payoff claim (Izacard et al. 2022, Wei et al. 2024, Si et al. 2023, Schwappach 2025 — the named competitor fabrication); human compose (Funkquist et al. 2023, Yuan et al. 2022, Zhang et al. 2026, Weng et al. 2025, Bisht et al. 2026); cost arithmetic realistic (Lála et al. 2023 $0.18/question, Schmidgall et al. 2025 $2.33/run); onboarding as retention mechanism (Luger & Sellen 2016, Epstein et al. 2020, Brown et al. 2007); nonce delimiters dual-purpose (Zheng & Huang 2022), with Zhao et al. 2021's recency-bias caveat that data-last ordering is not a defense — containment carries it.

## Recommended minimum edits

1. **Checker calibration gate (finding 1):** dev-tuned thresholds + HANS-style negation/direction probes as a launch-blocking acceptance test; "mentions" = unknown stance; false-promotion rate added to the weekly audit.
2. **Retrieval epistemics (findings 2, 3):** "nothing matched lexical/semantic search over N documents"; denominator counts truncated works as suspect; second-hop query; year-spread flag; contrasts labeled within-retrieved-set and empty ≠ no contradiction; parity claim scoped away from cross-period/multi-hop classes.
3. **Relabel mc** as "citation-correct (machine-checked)" and document the faithfulness gap (finding 4).
4. **Superseded/invalidated flag** with Ask down-ranking + FAMA-style audit probe (finding 5).
5. **Coherent-slice evidence for synthesis/compose**, iterative retrieval on the priced button, and separate instrumentation of synthesis-shaped Asks (finding 6).
6. **Pre-registration edits (findings 7, 8):** trust-incident defense attributed only to vigilance-independent mechanisms; qualitative judgment declared primary with metrics as evidence; missed engagement alone → targeted redesign; relegation-cluster leading indicator; export-refusal counts logged as gate-working events.
