# alpha.15 → beta.1 gaps — design-history analysis

For every gap in [`0.1.0-beta.1-alpha15-gap-analysis.md`](0.1.0-beta.1-alpha15-gap-analysis.md),
this document situates the gap in Memoria's evolution: where the alpha.15 side of the
gap came from, how it evolved step-by-step across the alpha line (reaching back to the
pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton" / Karpathy-Wiki-LLM × Razlogova origins), any earlier forms
the element changed from, and how beta.1's direction maps onto that history (reverts to
a named earlier era, continues an existing trajectory, or has no precedent in the alpha
line).

**Descriptive only** — no pros/cons, no "regression/improvement" verdicts, no
recommendations. Grounded in the verified design-history reconstruction
([`../../design-history/memoria-design-history-alpha.1-to-alpha.15.md`](../../design-history/memoria-design-history-alpha.1-to-alpha.15.md)),
its supporting sources, and `main`'s git history; ADRs used as reference only.

Sections mirror the 16 subsystems of the gap analysis, followed by the cross-cutting
historical arcs the individual gaps cluster into.

---
## 1. Host / runtime / distribution

*Framing note: every gap in this subsystem is one facet of a single historical movement — the progressive removal of the three original tool bets (Obsidian, Hermes, Zotero) and their resident/hosted machinery, culminating in the alpha.15 standalone CLI+engine, and beta.1's re-attachment of an Obsidian plugin + local daemon on top of that engine. The per-gap analyses below trace each facet; they share one spine, which the design history states as the through-line of the whole alpha series: "a steady transfer of authority away from autonomous agents and configurable runtime toward a deterministic, gated, single-writer engine" (Part IV).*

### Gap: beta.1 re-introduces Obsidian as the *required* researcher UI; alpha.15 had removed Obsidian as a required surface

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** Obsidian is one of the three founding tool bets, present from before alpha.1. The pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton" (snapshot dated 2026-05-29) names it in the three-layer model: "the vault (Obsidian folders) = durable store," and lists it as chosen "for its popularity and its markdown-plus-frontmatter substrate." The researcher's own origin notes flag it as an unexamined default from the start — "Obsidian… does not appear to be the best option for a structured knowledge graph" — and Part I calls confronting this the story "this entire history is, in effect." In the seven-layer elaboration Obsidian is L2, "the Obsidian workspace." The removal of Obsidian-as-required was therefore a *hard-won, late-arriving* property built over the strip-down arc, not an early design choice:

- **alpha.1–3:** Obsidian is the human surface on the Hermes stack; alpha.3 is "the first dedicated Obsidian UI research/build pass."
- **alpha.7:** a full clean-slate rebuild of the Obsidian UI — "the persistent-shell four-space model (Inbox/Library/Knowledge/Project nav row) + Bases view layer + capture forms" (ADR-81, superseding the ADR-68 workspace-swap). Obsidian was still the primary surface, only redesigned.
- **alpha.11:** Obsidian survived but was constrained to a control-panel — ADR-121 "Obsidian control panel mutates only by enqueueing worker jobs… It never writes Concepts, journal files, generated projections, or `check_status` directly," superseding the read-only Inspector (ADR-84).
- **alpha.12:** "CLI-first, Obsidian demoted to optional adapter" (Part IV timeline); design §2 "don't make Obsidian, Zotero, Hermes, or any adapter the architecture."
- **alpha.14:** Obsidian was *dropped as a required surface, not deferred*. PR #1124 (`5a93b142`) "Retired the shipped Obsidian workspace payload from `vault-template`: the entire `.obsidian/**` tree, QuickAdd command scripts/buttons, and the plugin provenance lock." Every palette action was re-homed to a CLI command, under the rule that the product "must remain fully operable if the editor is only `vim`, `nano`, or a file browser" (§1). An `rg`-based negative gate was added so the dropped surface "cannot silently return."
- **alpha.15:** finalized. The scope boundary states alpha.15 "cannot close with Obsidian plugin implementation counted as release scope," and it is "an engine and integrity checkpoint, not an Obsidian plugin release… No alpha.15 slice adds… an Obsidian plugin, plugin manifest, command-palette command, Inspector panel." Shipped surfaces are "CLI, scoped MCP, and loopback HTTP" (ADR-130).

**Prior states of the same element.** Obsidian-as-UI existed continuously and changed form repeatedly: Old Skeleton durable store → alpha.7 clean-slate persistent-shell UI → alpha.11 enqueue-only control panel (ADR-121) → alpha.12 optional adapter → alpha.14 removed from required baseline → alpha.15 excluded from scope.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 makes "the Obsidian plugin… the only researcher UI." This *returns the design to the pre-alpha.12 era* in which Obsidian was the researcher's primary/required surface (the posture held continuously from the Old Skeleton through alpha.11), reversing the alpha.12→alpha.14 demotion-and-removal. The reversal is partial in one recorded respect: beta.1's plugin keeps the alpha.11 write-boundary discipline — the gap analysis (§5/§10) and the beta.1 host section confirm the plugin is enqueue-only, which is ADR-121 carried forward. The design history is explicit about why the surface could be dropped at all and, by implication, what beta.1 is re-attaching: "because the UI never owned truth, dropping it cost the engine nothing" (arc (i)).

**Recorded context.** The history records both the adoption (Old Skeleton, an admitted unexamined default) and the abandonment: alpha.14 design §0/§13 dropped Obsidian "not deferred"; alpha.15's scope boundary fenced it out. Arc (i) also records the standing requirement that "any future editor adapter now 'require[s] their own ADR instead of inheriting an alpha.15 commitment.'"

### Gap: beta.1 adds a resident daemon (`mem serve`); alpha.15 explicitly had none

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** "No internal daemon" is a property of the standalone engine that arrived in the strip-down arc and hardened at alpha.15 — but a resident process is *not* unprecedented in Memoria's history; it is what alpha.1 was built on and then removed. From alpha.1 the resident runtime was the external **Hermes** runtime — "the dispatcher, the memory tiers, the MCP host, and the API server (port 8642)" (ADR-22). Cross-machine design carried the load-bearing invariant "exactly one Hermes dispatcher per vault" (ADR-63). Notably, even in the Hermes era a *write-gate* daemon was refused: at alpha.2, "a file-watcher write-gate daemon was deliberately not built — 'the commit gate + sweep bound the risk acceptably' under the solo premise" (ADR-49). The resident-runtime role then fell:

- **alpha.11:** Hermes "demoted to a specialized library adapter"; the default runner became "a direct OpenAI-compatible API call owned by the worker" (§8.2/§13). "Hermes as control-plane… demoted."
- **alpha.12:** engine standalone, CLI-first; the shipped subset added trigger scaffolding (`scan`, `run-scheduled`) funnelling through one request envelope.
- **alpha.14:** stated flatly — "There is no internal daemon. A scheduler invokes normal CLI commands with `--schedule-id`" (design §5/§2); jobs "may be cron, systemd timers, launchd, Task Scheduler, or a human-run script"; "dead Hermes cron installer code removed."
- **alpha.15:** distribution is "no always-on process"; scheduled work is "plain CLI invocations under cron/systemd/Task Scheduler." The one watcher that exists is opt-in and non-resident: `memoria serve --watch` (stdlib polling over the existing `workspace scan`/`observe-pi-edits` path, `--once` for deterministic gating), and "watcher-as-required-daemon rejected — the accepted form is 'real-time when the on-demand process runs, scan + consumption guard otherwise, guard never retired' (ADR-127), preserving the no-daemon architecture."

**Prior states of the same element.** The resident-process role existed as the Hermes runtime/dispatcher/API server (alpha.1–alpha.10, peak alpha.9/10), was demoted to a library adapter (alpha.11), and removed (alpha.12–14). An opt-in, non-resident watcher (`serve --watch`) was introduced at alpha.15.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 adds "a resident two-process daemon (`mem serve` = supervisor + per-run worker) supervised by the plugin with LSP lifecycle, resident while Obsidian is open." Two mappings are factual: (1) the *`serve`/watch token continues* the alpha.15 `memoria serve --watch` surface — beta.1 builds on the same scan/observe path — but *promotes* it from an opt-in, non-resident, deterministically-gateable process to a resident, supervised daemon, which reverses ADR-127's recorded rejection of "watcher-as-required-daemon." (2) The *resident supervised process* role has no precedent in the standalone alpha line (alpha.12–15 are all no-daemon by construction) but rhymes in shape with the pre-alpha.11 Hermes resident control-plane (an always-available dispatcher/API server), which the strip-down arc had removed. beta.1's "write-event push, not watcher faith" continues from the alpha.15 watcher path but changes its lifecycle to daemon-resident.

**Recorded context.** The history records the decision at multiple points: alpha.2's refusal to build a write-gate daemon (ADR-49); alpha.14 design §5 "There is no internal daemon"; alpha.15 ADR-127's rejection of a required watcher-daemon; and arc (f), which names "no always-on process" as "the design goal all along" once the live engine became an API and the Hermes control-plane became "weight the single-user local tool doesn't need."

### Gap: beta.1 demotes the CLI from product to survival floor and drops `ask`; alpha.15 shipped a verb-first CLI including `ask`

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** A CLI existed as a *human channel* from alpha.1, but it was one of several — "the human channels (Obsidian, CLI, Telegram)" (ADR-22 / `why-hermes.md`), not the product. CLI-*as-product* is a strip-down-era arrival:

- **alpha.12:** the CLI-first reframe. Design §2: "The engine is the product… don't make Obsidian, Zotero, Hermes, or any adapter the architecture"; the shipped subset added CLI subcommands `scan`/`run-scheduled`/`recover`.
- **alpha.14:** the CLI became "the product surface for alpha.14, not operator-only" (§5). The entire ~2274-line grouped `memoria <resource> <action>` grammar was new, including `ask` in the roster (`init`, `status`, `doctor`, `ask`, `work …`, `note …`, `project …`, …). A **parity floor** was enforced: "Alpha.14 is non-regressing only when every current Obsidian command-palette action and every current Inspector worker action has a CLI/engine equivalent."
- **alpha.15:** the surface cut over to verb-first (`new note`, `work add/enrich/interview/digest`, `new hub <tag>`, `link`, `check`, `show`, `list`, **`ask`**, `export`, plus `project`/`workspace`/`vocab`/`attention`/`request`/`journal` groups) (ADR-130, PR #1165). `ask` is in the CLI verb set. This surface carries "PI direct access… every routine action reachable by the researcher directly without conversing with an agent" (ADR-72 carried into ADR-130). Ask itself has been "the one distinct read surface" since alpha.11 (§3), realized then as the `answer-query`/`summarize-for-recall` operations.

**Prior states of the same element.** The CLI was a secondary human channel pre-alpha.12; it became the product surface at alpha.12 and remained so through alpha.15. `ask`/`answer-query` has been the single distinct read surface since alpha.11 and was CLI-reachable at alpha.14 and alpha.15.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 makes the CLI (`mem`) a "survival + ops floor" (`serve/capture/import/export/rebuild/backup/restore/migrate/revert-run/cost/hat`) with **no `mem ask`**, Ask being plugin-only. Two mappings: (1) demoting the CLI from product to floor *reverses the alpha.12→alpha.15 elevation* and returns the CLI to a secondary role reminiscent of the pre-alpha.12 "CLI is one human channel among several" era — while, per the first gap, the researcher's primary UI moves back to Obsidian. (2) Dropping `ask` from the CLI has **no precedent in the alpha line**: Ask has been CLI-reachable since it became a first-class command (alpha.14), and alpha.15 explicitly held the ADR-72 invariant that every routine action is "reachable by the researcher directly without conversing with an agent," with `ask` in the verb set. Making Ask a plugin-only rich pane also stands against the alpha.14 parity floor ("every… command-palette action has a CLI/engine equivalent").

**Recorded context.** alpha.12 design §2 ("the engine is the product"); alpha.14 §5 (CLI as product surface) and the §5 parity floor; ADR-72/ADR-130 direct-access "never agent-only." The history does not record any alpha-era decision to remove `ask` from the CLI or to make any read surface plugin-only.

### Gap: beta.1 adds a plugin-distribution + binary-acquisition story that alpha.15's installer had removed

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The alpha.15 installer posture — "installs no Obsidian plugin, no Hermes profiles, requires no Zotero" — is itself a late arrival that replaced an elaborate Hermes+Obsidian+profiles+cron install unit:

- **alpha.1:** "The repo is the install unit" (ADR-26); the install flow "scaffold → populate → stage golden copy" (ADR-55) then "wires the pre-commit gate, installs Hermes + the profiles (pruning stale `memoria-*` profiles)… installs Obsidian if absent, and wires the crons." Windows was WSL2-only because "Hermes runs only on Linux/WSL2" (ADR-64).
- **alpha.4–5:** the packaging debate — wheel (ADR-76) recast to "a versioned vault release; deploy via a source-agnostic reconciling installer," with editable `pip install -e .` as "the principled default… no wheel is shipped."
- **alpha.7–alpha.11:** the installer *did* carry a plugin-distribution story: vendored community plugins governed by a `plugin-provenance-lock.json` and a **plugin provenance doctor** wired into required lint (alpha.9, ADR-74); the read-only `memoria-inspector` plugin (alpha.9, ADR-84); QuickAdd scripts; the homepage plugin (retired alpha.10/11, "one fewer vendored dependency and one fewer provenance lock").
- **alpha.14:** the plugin-distribution story was retired — PR #1124 removed `.obsidian/**`, QuickAdd, and the plugin provenance lock; profile/Hermes installer modes were removed; install target became "`pip install -e .` + `memoria init`/`doctor`", "standalone by default," and §11 mandated: "Install no Obsidian plugin. Install no Hermes profiles. Require no Zotero setup."
- **alpha.15:** distribution is "`uv tool install memoria`, one command, no external runtime, no always-on process."

**Prior states of the same element.** A plugin-distribution mechanism existed in the alpha line through alpha.13: the installer vendored community plugins directly (cloned from the repo-as-unit), tracked them with a checksum provenance lock (ADR-74), and pruned stale ones. It was removed wholesale at alpha.14.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 "ships an Obsidian plugin (catalog-clean, desktop-only) with a consented/package-manager binary-acquisition flow, code-signed Windows builds." Mapping: (1) *shipping an Obsidian plugin at all* returns to the alpha.7–alpha.13 posture where Memoria distributed Obsidian plugins, reversing the alpha.14 retirement. (2) The *acquisition mechanism* has no precedent in the alpha line: alpha's installer vendored plugins through the repo-as-unit with a provenance lock; beta.1 instead goes through the Obsidian community **catalog** ("ships only `main.js`/`manifest.json`/`styles.css`") plus a *consented binary-download* flow (package manager `uv tool install`/winget/brew, or "the rust-analyzer model — explicit user consent, checksummed, version-matched download"). (3) **Code-signed Windows builds** are new to the whole history: the alpha Windows story was WSL2-only through the workspace releases, and native Windows (ADR-64) was left `deferred`; there is no prior code-signing story to return to.

**Recorded context.** alpha.14 §11 (installer installs no plugin/profiles/Zotero); alpha.15 `uv tool install`. beta.1 itself records the discontinuity: its host section (design §, line ~1152) states the plugin+daemon host decision "amends ADR-125 (standalone CLI + engine architecture) and ADR-130 (surfaces) and requires that ADR before implementation begins," and notes that `docs/reference/obsidian-plugins.md` currently reads — as the correct current-state form — "no plugin required; a Memoria plugin is a future adapter and needs its own ADR."

### Gap: beta.1 §11 itself records the host/runtime pivot as new alpha.16 scope on top of alpha.15

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** This gap is beta.1's own classification: item (10), "the host decision itself — beta.1 is an Obsidian plugin + local daemon, which the alpha.15 plugin design anticipates but does not yet center… Items (1)–(3) and (10) are new alpha.16 scope." The design history corroborates that the host pivot is entirely post-alpha.15. alpha.15's scope boundary explicitly fenced out precisely this work: "No alpha.15 slice adds… an Obsidian plugin, plugin manifest, command-palette command, Inspector panel," and alpha.15 "cannot close with Obsidian plugin implementation counted as release scope." The shipped alpha.15 host was CLI/scoped-MCP/loopback-HTTP surfaces over one `engine/api` module, plus the opt-in non-resident `serve --watch`.

**Prior states of the same element.** There is no alpha.15 daemon-plus-plugin host to build on. On the daemon side, alpha.15 (ADR-127) *rejected* a required watcher-daemon; on the plugin side, alpha.14 removed the plugin payload and alpha.15 fenced plugin work out of scope. The nearest prior state of a "plugin + resident process" host shape is the pre-alpha.12 configuration — Obsidian surface + resident Hermes runtime — which the strip-down arc dismantled.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 correctly situates the host/runtime pivot as an *addition on top of* alpha.15, not a continuation of an alpha.15 trajectory. The word "anticipates" in beta.1's self-record maps, in the design history, to a *forward reference to a future adapter ADR* rather than to any partial alpha.15 build: arc (i) records that any future editor adapter must "require their own ADR instead of inheriting an alpha.15 commitment," and `obsidian-plugins.md` frames a Memoria plugin as "a future adapter [that] needs its own ADR." In shape, the alpha.16-scoped host (plugin surface + resident local daemon) returns toward the pre-alpha.12 Obsidian+resident-runtime era, but re-derived on the standalone engine rather than on Hermes.

**Recorded context.** beta.1 §11 lists the host decision among items that "are new alpha.16 scope"; beta.1's host section names the amendment to ADR-125/ADR-130 and the requirement for a new ADR "before implementation begins." The alpha.15 design index (Scope Boundary) records the deliberate negative fence that kept the plugin/daemon host out of the alpha.15 cut.

## 2. Vault structure / bundle layout

Four gaps sit in this subsystem. All four are, at bottom, moves in one long argument the design history calls out explicitly: the origin flaw that "Layer 7, the vault, evolved into a mix of the original notes and additional data repositories, leading to a lack of clarity" (Part I, quoting the origin notes). The alpha line spends fifteen versions splitting that fused vault into distinct stores; beta.1's bundle layout re-fuses several of them. Each gap below is one axis of that re-fusion.

### Gap 2.1 — beta.1 nests `Memoria/` as a subfolder inside an unrelated vault; alpha.15's workspace *was* the bundle

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** That "the workspace is the install" is a property held continuously across the entire arc, never a late arrival. In the pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton (snapshot dated 2026-05-29) the vault *was* the Obsidian workspace — one of the seven layers ("L2 the Obsidian workspace … L7 the vault"), a dedicated Memoria store, not a folder living among foreign notes. At the alpha.1 baseline (release commit `0e9ed9dd`, 2026-06-14) the design is explicit that the **vault root is the workspace root**: "The vault root is organized by category," with `.memoria/` (runtime) and `.obsidian/` (app config) as **hidden peers of the vault**, the installer scaffolding "the skeleton and populat[ing] it from `src/`." There is no notion of a Memoria region nested inside a larger, unrelated vault.

**Prior states of the same element.** The relationship was sharpened, not weakened, at the alpha.11 clean-slate reset (2026-06-27): "A Memoria install is a **workspace** … containing three OKF-compatible bundle roots," and the design deliberately makes "the parent workspace … **not** an OKF bundle so cross-bundle state (`steering.md`, journal, worker/index) do not have to pretend to be Concepts" (§6.1/§6.2). alpha.11 also physically separated the two `src/` meanings — "moved the installable package to `src/memoria_vault`, … the shipped vault source to `vault-template`" (commit `a966cb54`) — precisely so the workspace skeleton could be validated as a self-contained unit. By alpha.15 (2026-07-01/02) `folders.yaml` collapses to `categories = [knowledge]`, so the workspace narrows to essentially a single bundle plus hidden `.memoria/`; the install still owns the whole root.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** beta.1's `Memoria/` subfolder beside unrelated existing notes — two sibling roots inside one user-owned vault — has **no precedent anywhere in the alpha line or the Old Skeleton**. In every prior era the Memoria install owned the entire workspace root. The nesting is a direct consequence of the beta.1 host pivot (§1: an Obsidian plugin over the user's *existing* vault), and the gap analysis notes beta.1 §11 itself files "the host decision (Obsidian plugin + local daemon)" as **new alpha.16 scope** that "alpha.15 … does not yet center." So this is not a return to an earlier era's layout; it is a new element that follows from a host decision the alpha line never took.

**Recorded context.** The design history records *why* the workspace-owns-the-root form was held: the alpha.11 design keeps the parent workspace non-bundle so cross-bundle state "do[es] not have to pretend to be Concepts," and the `src/` → `vault-template` split existed because "Clean separation was a precondition for the clean-slate skeleton to be validated independently."

### Gap 2.2 — beta.1 re-materializes the catalog record as `record.md` and co-locates record + fulltext + digest + pdf per work; alpha.15 kept the catalog in SQLite with no `record.md`

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** SQLite-as-catalog-authority is the *end* of a multi-version migration, not an original property — and the arc actually starts on the opposite side. The alpha.1/alpha.2 catalog was **markdown**: alpha.2 (ADR-49, dated 2026-06-10/12) split the `paper-note`/`item-note` hybrid into "structured **catalog entities** (paper, person, organization, venue, dataset, repository — flat Bases-queryable frontmatter under `catalog/`)," and at that point a SQLite store was **explicitly rejected**: "SQLite/JSON/graph-DB store — 'opaque, not git-diffable, outside the plain-text discipline.'" The turn to SQLite came only at the **alpha.12 keep-test** (2026-06-28, ADR-122): "The thing you keep if Memoria dies must be markdown files in OKF … Everything else is free to be SQLite," making SQLite "SSOT for the catalog." alpha.13 built enrichment on that SQLite catalog; alpha.14 removed markdown Works entirely ("**no markdown Work/Author/Source/Institution Concepts** at all"); and alpha.15's ADR-124/125 hardened it into the split-authority rule — "files own meaning, the DB owns records/verdicts," references "generated from SQLite," `knowledge/works/<id>/` holding "only the subjective `work` Concept."

**Prior states of the same element — the per-work folder specifically.** beta.1's co-location shape (`works/<citekey>/{record, fulltext, digest, pdf}`) has a direct structural ancestor at **alpha.11**: the catalog was "`catalog/sources/<source_id>/{source.md, content.md, raw/}`" — i.e. a per-source folder holding the Concept (`source.md`), the extracted text (`content.md`), and the blob (`raw/`), described as "a source = `source.md` (the Concept) + `content.md` + `raw/` (attachments, *not* Concepts)." That is beta.1's `record.md` + `fulltext.md` + `source.pdf` triad. The one piece that lived *elsewhere* in alpha.11 was the digest — `knowledge/digests/<source_id>.md`, a separate machine-authored file 1:1 with the source (the "net-new Karpathy 'wiki' role").

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** beta.1 moves the catalog record's *authority* back to markdown (`record.md`, full CSL-JSON, "the fidelity/re-import authority"), which reverses the alpha.12→alpha.15 trajectory (ADR-122/124/125) and returns to the pre-alpha.12 markdown-catalog era of alpha.1..alpha.11. Simultaneously the per-work folder that co-locates record + fulltext + blob **returns to the alpha.11 `catalog/sources/<source_id>/` co-location shape**, and by pulling `digest.md` back into the same folder it also re-merges the one element alpha.11 kept separate. beta.1 also makes the **citekey the path key** ("citekey pinned at import"); alpha.11 had deliberately demoted the citekey — "the catalog path is an **immutable `source_id`**, not the citekey — citekeys … get corrected, so they are a **generated display/export alias**, never the path (… fixture-verified across a citekey correction)," carried to alpha.15 as `work_id` primary with citekeys as aliases. So beta.1 reverses that specific alpha.11 decision as well.

**Recorded context.** The history pins both the *why* of the SQLite move and the *why* of the identity model. The keep-test replaced "the fuzzy 'ownership feels important'" with "does the user need this to keep thinking and writing outside Memoria" (alpha.12 §1), reserving markdown for the keep-set only. The catalog-in-SQLite decision "explicitly overturn[s] ADR-49's rejected 'SQLite: never the source of truth'" (ADR-125). And the citekey-is-not-the-path rule is recorded as fixture-verified against a citekey correction (alpha.11 §6.1).

### Gap 2.3 — beta.1 has no `hubs/`, splits project artifacts into separate files, and restores a per-work `digest.md`; alpha.15 had `knowledge/{works,notes,hubs,projects}` with `digests` folded into `work`

This gap has three distinct sub-lineages.

**Hubs.** The hub is one of the most continuously-held elements in the entire history — present in some form from the pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton onward, never absent across any alpha. The Old Skeleton had "wikilinks + MOCs"; alpha.1 shipped a first-class `hub` type in a gated zone (`notes/hubs/` 🔒, in `folders.yaml`'s `gated_prefixes`); alpha.7 added a dedicated `hubs.base`; alpha.11 reshaped it into "the merged Karpathy concept-page × ZK MOC: the machine keeps the topic synthesis current, the human owns the structure + argument"; and alpha.15 (ADR-126) tightened it to "**own[ing] exactly one ID-backed controlled-vocabulary tag** via a required `tag:` field," so "membership = everywhere that checked tag appears." Evolution arc (c) roots it in Luhmann: "hub = structure note/Map-of-Content." beta.1's removal of `hubs/` therefore drops an element that had an unbroken presence across the whole arc; there is **no earlier alpha or Old-Skeleton era with no hub** for it to return to.

**Digest / per-work `digest.md`.** The digest-as-separate-file is a *narrower* lineage: it was **new at alpha.11** — "New **`digest`** type (`knowledge/digests/<source_id>.md`) — machine-authored per-source synthesis, 1:1 with a catalog source," the "net-new Karpathy 'wiki' role." It existed as a separate file/type through alpha.11..alpha.14. **alpha.15 removed the separate `digests/` folder and folded digest materialization onto `work`** (`folders.yaml` diff: "`knowledge/works` added, `knowledge/digests` removed"; ADR-126: "`digest` materialization moved to `work`"), because "The old `digest` type conflated the human-authored subjective take with the machine-owned bibliographic record" (ADR-124/125/126). beta.1's per-work `digest.md` therefore **reverses the alpha.15 fold**, restoring the separate machine-digest file — landing it in the alpha.11-era "separate digest" model, though co-located inside `works/<citekey>/` rather than in a standalone `digests/` folder.

**Project split into files.** The `project` element also has a documented shape-history. Pre-alpha.11 there were "separate `project`/`thesis`/`code-note` types under `projects/`"; alpha.11 collapsed these to "a single **`project`** type at `knowledge/projects/<id>/project.md` with an *optional* `thesis` field"; alpha.15 kept it a single Concept that "absorbs ADR-77 (project gate), ADR-79 (argument graph/warrant/saturation floor …), and ADR-78 (thesis is now a *role* link, not a type)," adding `outcome_frame`/`paper_plan` and defining deliverable targets as "artifacts, not Concepts." beta.1 splits the project into a *folder of files* (`project.md/evidence.md/gaps.md/draft.md/export`). The pre-alpha.11 decomposition was into separate **types**, not files-in-a-folder, so beta.1's mechanism (sibling artifact files) has no direct type-level precedent; the closest anchor is alpha.15's own "deliverable targets (`outline`/`section`/`figure`/`code` — **artifacts, not Concepts**)," which beta.1's `draft`/`export` files continue. beta.1's `evidence.md` and `gaps.md`, by contrast, materialize as workspace files what alpha.15 kept as engine/DB outputs — alpha.15 gap analysis is "`--json` findings with `why`/`next_actions`" reading "checked inputs only," not a durable `gaps.md` file (§7).

**Recorded context.** ADR-126 records why the four-type homes were fixed and why the digest was folded ("multiplicity itself caused drift and confusion"; the digest type "conflated" subjective take and bibliographic record). Arc (c) records the hub's genealogy (Luhmann MOC → Karpathy concept-page × ZK MOC → tag-owning hub).

### Gap 2.4 — beta.1 re-introduces `inbox/`, adds `reports/`, and surfaces a visible `system/` tree; alpha.15 had none of these as workspace folders

**`inbox/` lineage.** This is the clearest "return to an earlier era" of the four. `inbox` traces back to the Old Skeleton's numbered lifecycle folders — "`10-inbox/02-answers/`" — and survived the alpha.2 reorganization as a **type-first category folder**: ADR-47's "catalog · notes · projects · inbox · system," holding "agent→human message cards (candidate · gap · flag · alert · work-prompt)." It was still present, reshaped, at alpha.10 (ADR-115: "Inbox is the queue not a space; retire homepage front door"). Then **alpha.11 retired it outright**: "Retired the `inbox/` category and all inbox-resident durable Concepts; replaced with **generated attention projections** backed by `.memoria/queue/{pending,running,done,failed}`," on the thesis "**Attention is generated, not authored** … 'cards' are projections and never canonical Concepts" (§8.2), with §17 stating "Old Inbox card types do not remain durable Concepts." alpha.15 kept attention in the database ("every `attention resolve` journals the routing class"); the word "inbox" survives in alpha.15 only as *attention-prompt* terminology (e.g. edge-candidate/work prompts), **not as a workspace folder of durable Concepts**. beta.1's `inbox/` (staging) therefore re-introduces a workspace-level inbox folder that was **retired at alpha.11** — returning, in placement, to the alpha.1..alpha.10 inbox-as-folder model (beta.1's "staging" function maps onto what alpha.11 moved into the hidden `.memoria/staging/`, now surfaced as a visible folder).

**`system/` lineage.** `system/` is nearly as old: the Old Skeleton had a "SYSTEM: visible infrastructure" region, and alpha.1/alpha.2 shipped a `system/` category — "SYSTEM: visible infrastructure — logs · templates · patterns · dashboards · board · eval · metrics" (ADR-47). It persisted through alpha.11 as `system/scripts/`, `system/eval/`, and `system/dashboards/`. It was dismantled across the standalone cut: alpha.14 retired the shipped `system/scripts/*.js` (Obsidian dropped as required surface, #1124) and the `fleet-health.md`/`skill-state.md` dashboards (#1122/#1119); alpha.15's `folders.yaml` narrowing to `categories = [knowledge]` plus the move of operations/capabilities into packaged product code (ADR-125: "Operations are product code") left the surviving infrastructure under the **hidden `.memoria/`** (`journal-head`, `overrides.jsonl`, `index/`) rather than a visible `system/` tree. beta.1's visible `system/` (manifest.jsonl, incidents/, metrics/) therefore **re-surfaces a visible infrastructure tree** that the alpha line had progressively hidden — returning, in visibility, to the Old-Skeleton-through-alpha.11 model after alpha.14/alpha.15 pushed it under `.memoria/`.

**`reports/` lineage.** Unlike `inbox/` and `system/`, `reports/` has **no directly-named folder precedent** in the alpha line. Its nearest analogues are always *generated projections/views*, never a durable folder of "machine report notes": alpha.1/alpha.2 `system/dashboards/`; alpha.11 `spaces/` projections + `knowledge/views/`; alpha.14/alpha.15 `knowledge/_views/index.md` and the "OKF-style" `index.md` bundle projections. Across the arc these were consistently framed as *disposable/regenerable* ("a small **record**; everything else regenerates," alpha.11 §6), and alpha.11 mandated a drift check because "a hand-edited projection does not satisfy acceptance." beta.1's `reports/` as durable machine report *notes* in the workspace is thus a new element rather than a revival of a specific alpha-line folder.

**Recorded context.** The history pins the retirement rationale precisely. The inbox retirement is recorded at alpha.11 §8.2/§17 ("workflow state belongs in 'queue (live) + journal (durable) + projections (visibility),' never in card Concepts"). The state-not-folder principle behind moving `system`/lifecycle out of visible folders is arc (b): "a Concept's position in the system is its type, never its topic, and read state is record state, not a folder," because "a state change does not move a file, so it cannot break wikilinks." The dashboards/`system/scripts` teardown is recorded in the alpha.14 retirement table ("Obsidian dropped as required surface; product moves to package"), and the capabilities-to-product-code move at alpha.15 in ADR-125.

## 3. Concept / type model

The §3 gaps all sit on one lineage: the per-type schema roster that Memoria carried, in some form, from before its first tagged release. What follows traces each gap through that arc. The single most useful anchor is ADR-126's own account of the roster's growth, quoted in the design history: *"The type roster grew by accretion (47, 50, 78, 117, 119): claim, question, excerpt, synthesis, thesis, steering, vocabulary, asset, digest, fleeting … each with folder homes, lifecycle fields, and per-type rules … 12+ types across three bundles"* — and the corrective test alpha.15 applied: the discriminator became *"a fundamentally different artifact"*, so *"a flippable check regime is a mode, not a type, and a layer-difference is not a knowledge type."*

### Gap: B1 replaces the four typed-concept schemas with a flat file-class enum; per-type frontmatter+body schemas are absent

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The per-type schema is one of Memoria's oldest and most continuously-held structures. The pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton" (2026-05-29 snapshot) already shipped *"~15 typed notes with lifecycles, a controlled-vocabulary frontmatter schema, wikilinks + MOCs"* (Part I). alpha.1 (`0e9ed9dd`, 2026-06-14) formalized this into the **18-type roster** in four categories (6 catalog / 5 notes / 5 inbox / 2 system), with the machine contract living in `src/.memoria/schemas/types/<type>.yaml` — declared *"THE single schema source"* — and the reference page explicitly subordinate: *"the schemas win on any disagreement."* The roster then accreted: alpha.5 added the `thesis` note type (ADR-78); alpha.6 added `code-note` as the "22nd type." The schema *machinery* was then hardened, not shrunk, at **alpha.10 by ADR-119**, which *"promoted the type schema from a fields+enums fragment to (the design for) the complete declarative contract covering all eight concerns — identity/placement, fields-as-specs, controlled vocab, lifecycle state machine, typed edges, invariants, generation metadata, evolution,"* with the Linter becoming *"a generic engine that executes declared rules and forms/templates/`folders.yaml`/reference docs generated from the schema."* So by alpha.10 the per-type YAML schema was not incidental — it was the single source from which validation, creation forms, and docs were generated.

The **collapse to four** was two-staged. alpha.11 (2026-06-27) cut *"from 25 types to 12"* into three OKF families (catalog / knowledge-graph / capabilities), the knowledge graph being a three-node model (`digest`/`note`/`hub`). alpha.15 (2026-07-01/02) then cut *"from 12+ types across three bundles to four knowledge Concept types — `note`, `work`, `hub`, `project`,"* deleting nine schemas outright (`source`, `person`, `organization`, `venue`, `digest`, `adapter`, `operation`, `skill`, `workflow`) — the catalog entities became SQLite records, operations became product code, and `digest` folded into `work`. Crucially, the four *retained their per-type schemas* (`note.yaml`/`work.yaml`/`hub.yaml`/`project.yaml`), and alpha.15 *tightened* validation from permissive to closed: `validate_frontmatter` began *"reject[ing] undeclared fields ('put custom data under x')"* with an `x: map` escape hatch on every type (PR-AC #1197).

**Prior states of the same element.** The per-type schema existed continuously and only changed *shape*: ~15 controlled-vocab schemas (Old Skeleton) → 18 (alpha.1) → 22 (alpha.6) → the ADR-119 "complete declarative contract" (alpha.10) → 12 (alpha.11) → 4 with closed validation (alpha.15). At no point in the alpha line was the per-type schema removed; it was the constant through every reset.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 introduces something with **no precedent in the alpha line on two axes**. First, it drops per-type schema files entirely ("per-type frontmatter+body schemas are absent") — a construct present from before alpha.1 through alpha.15. Second, its `type` value is a **flat file-class enum** (`work|fulltext|digest|note|evidence|gaps|project|draft|ruled-out|export|report|incident|conflict`) rather than a roster of durable-Concept types. Several of those classes (`gaps`, `evidence`, `draft`, `export`, `report`, `incident`, `conflict`, `fulltext`, `digest`) name artifacts the alpha line had deliberately moved *out* of the Concept-type space: alpha.11 held that *"Queue, flags, gaps, work cards, dashboards, and indexes are projections or worker state, not durable Concepts"* and *"Attention is generated, not authored,"* and alpha.15 made operations/verdicts *"product code"* and DB records, not typed files. So beta.1's flat class enum does not return to any earlier alpha *roster* (its members are a different kind of thing than the 18/12/4 Concept types); it re-materializes machine artifacts and derived outputs as first-class file classes — a direction the alpha line trended steadily away from after alpha.11.

**Recorded context.** The history pins *why* the alpha.15 side took its form: ADR-126 records that *"the multiplicity itself caused drift and confusion"* and that the four types *"earn their place by creating distinct obligations the gap engine feeds on."* The schema machinery's high-water mark is dated to alpha.10/ADR-119 (*"Memoria's own single-source-of-truth philosophy applied to creation, not just validation"*).

### Gap: the hub type is dropped

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** `hub` is, with the note lineage, the longest-lived object in the system. It descends from the Zettelkasten Map-of-Content (MOC) present in the Old Skeleton (*"wikilinks + MOCs"*, Part I). alpha.1 renamed MOC→`hub` under **ADR-50**, which *"keeps the threshold-alert mechanism (ADR-19) under a plainer name"* while retiring the separate `reference` type; alpha.1 shipped `hub` as a review-gated (🔒) note type in `notes/hubs/`, required `title`/`topic`, keyed on a single `topic:` string. alpha.11 *"reshaped [`hub`] from a review-gated topic node keyed on a single `topic:` string to a `check_status`-governed machine-synthesized + human-curated node with `members[]`, optional `project`, `contested`, `confidence`"* — described as *"the merged Karpathy concept-page × ZK MOC: the machine keeps the topic synthesis current, the human owns the structure."* alpha.15 reshaped it again so that *"`hub` now owns exactly one ID-backed controlled-vocabulary tag via a required `tag:` field; it dropped `members: list`, `confidence`, `contested`, `project`,"* with membership redefined as *"everywhere that checked tag appears."* The history records the reasoning: hand-curated membership *"rots (every relevant new note requires a hub edit),"* and the tag mechanism *"splits membership (mechanical, inclusive) from salience (human, curated in prose), directly answering ADR-19's recorded hazard 'a generated list that looks curated but isn't.'"*

**Prior states of the same element.** MOC (pre-alpha.1) → `hub` gated topic node keyed on `topic:` (alpha.1, ADR-50) → `check_status`-governed machine+human node with `members[]` (alpha.11) → tag-owning node, membership = checked-tag occurrence (alpha.15). The type was continuously present and only changed its membership mechanism.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 drops the hub type entirely ("no `hub` type; no hub concept anywhere"). This has **no precedent anywhere in the recorded arc**: a hub, or its direct MOC ancestor, existed continuously from the 2026-05-29 Old Skeleton through alpha.15 — it is one of the very few structures never absent across any of the three clean-slate resets (alpha.7 UI, alpha.11 architecture, alpha.14/15 standalone-engine). Part IV names it as a genealogical fixture: *"hub = structure note/Map-of-Content"* in the Luhmann mapping. beta.1 removing it does not return to any earlier era; even the earliest recorded design had the MOC.

**Recorded context.** The design history records that the hub's *purpose* (Karpathy concept-page × ZK MOC, structural navigation) was reaffirmed at each reset rather than questioned; the only recorded churn is the *membership mechanism* (ADR-50 threshold-alert → alpha.11 `members[]` → alpha.15 owned-tag), driven by the ADR-19 "list that looks curated but isn't" hazard.

### Gap: B1 drops the note claim/question mode and claim-context fields

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The `note` lineage traces to Luhmann's *permanent note* (*"claim (= permanent note, 'what the PI thinks')"*, alpha.1). The Old Skeleton had a `claim-note`; alpha.1 shipped `claim` as a gated (🔒) type in `notes/claims/` requiring `title`/`maturity`/`sources`, enforcing atomicity (*"one claim per note"*), carrying `maturity{seedling|budding|evergreen}`. alpha.5 added the `thesis` type and the argument-graph/warrant apparatus (ADR-77/78/79). alpha.11 performed the first collapse: *"`claim` + `fleeting` + note-`source` merged into one `note` type … an atomic Concept with an optional anchor … and an optional claim block (`claim_text`, `qualifier`, `tense`, `temporal_scope_*`, `status{candidate|accepted|rejected|superseded|needs_review}`, `evidence_set`, `extraction_confidence`)"* — dropping `maturity` (*"trust = the checks"*) and the `fleeting` type (*"A reminder is a `#todo` tag"*).

The specific alpha.15 features named in this gap — the **claim/question mode** — were a **late arrival at alpha.15**. alpha.15's `note` *"gained `mode: claim | question` (declared intent), `question_status: open | resolved`, and claim-context fields `temporal_scope`/`tense`/`qualifier` + `anchors`."* The recorded rationale: a mode is *"declared intent creating obligations the gap engine feeds on — a claim without evidence is an `under-warranted` gap; an open question is a compose-flow lead,"* and *"field-derived typing could not tell a plain thought from a claim missing its evidence"* (ADR-126). ADR-126 explicitly rejected making these separate types: *"flippable states and roles, not artifacts; separate types make the question→claim transition destructive."* So `mode` was alpha.15's device for absorbing the accreted `question`/`claim`/`thesis` types back into one `note` while preserving their distinct gap-engine obligations. The claim-context fields (`temporal_scope`/`tense`/`qualifier`) are older — they were part of alpha.11's optional claim block.

**Prior states of the same element.** permanent note / `claim-note` (Old Skeleton) → gated `claim` type with `maturity`+`sources` (alpha.1) → collapsed into `note` with an optional claim block (alpha.11) → `note` with `mode: claim|question` + claim-context + anchors (alpha.15).

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1's `note` = *"atomic + fleeting notes (human); no `mode`, no claim/question distinction, no question_status."* This **continues the alpha.11 collapse trajectory but reverses the alpha.15 addition**. The "atomic + fleeting (human)" framing echoes alpha.11's merge of `claim`+`fleeting`+note-`source` into one `note` — i.e., beta.1 lands roughly at the alpha.11-era single note-type conception. But it goes one step further than alpha.11 (which retained an optional claim block) by dropping the claim-context fields, and it specifically undoes the alpha.15 `mode`/`question_status` machinery — the newest addition to the note type. In lineage terms: beta.1 unwinds alpha.15's mode innovation and lands near alpha.11's plainer note, minus the optional claim/anchor blocks.

**Recorded context.** The design history dates the mode to alpha.15 and records its purpose as feeding the gap engine (*"a `claim` mode without evidence is an `under-warranted` gap; an open `question` is a compose-flow lead"*, Part IV arc (c)), and records that separate claim/question/thesis *types* were deliberately rejected in favor of the mode because *"separate types make the question→claim transition destructive"* (ADR-126).

### Gap: B1 has no argument-graph project concept

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** `projects/` existed as a category folder from alpha.1. The argument-graph apparatus arrived at **alpha.5**, whose "single biggest design change" (Part IV timeline) was *"the fourth navigation gate plus the thesis note, argument graph + warrant (ADR-77/78/79)."* alpha.11 collapsed the multiple project-side types: *"the old model had separate `project`/`thesis`/`code-note` types under `projects/`; now a single `project` type … with an optional `thesis` field and a `scope` map,"* defined as *"project = Link-defined subgraph, not a bundle"* — explicitly rejecting the folder/bundle model. alpha.15 enriched the single `project` type: it *"gained `outcome_frame: map` and `paper_plan: map`, kept `thesis: str`, and dropped `scope: map`,"* absorbing *"ADR-77 (project gate), ADR-79 (argument graph/warrant/saturation floor → the gap engine's saturation block), and ADR-78 (thesis is now a role, not a type)."* So by alpha.15 the project *is* the argument-graph concept — a typed Concept carrying the thesis role, outcome frame, and paper plan, with saturation computed by the gap engine.

**Prior states of the same element.** `projects/` folder (alpha.1) → separate `project`/`thesis`/`code-note` types + argument graph + warrant (alpha.5 → alpha.10) → single `project` type, thesis as optional field, "Link-defined subgraph, not a bundle" (alpha.11) → single `project` type with `outcome_frame`/`paper_plan`/`thesis`, absorbing the argument graph and saturation (alpha.15).

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 is *"a `project/` folder of files (project.md/evidence.md/gaps.md/draft.md); `project` is one class value; no argument-graph project concept."* This maps onto the history as a **reversal of the alpha.11 decision** that *"project = Link-defined subgraph, not a bundle."* beta.1 re-expresses the project as a bundle/folder of files, which is closer in *form* to the pre-alpha.11 world where `projects/` was a category folder holding separate typed files — but the specific decomposition (`evidence.md`/`gaps.md`/`draft.md`) has **no direct precedent**: in the alpha line, `gap` was an inbox-card/attention type (alpha.1's 5 inbox cards; retired to a "generated projection" at alpha.11), evidence lived in the note `evidence_set`/warrant, and drafts were compose-flow outputs — none were project-folder files. So beta.1 continues no single alpha trajectory here: it dismantles the alpha.11–15 typed argument-graph `project` Concept and re-materializes its parts (evidence, gaps, draft) as flat files in a folder, a shape the alpha line had specifically moved away from.

**Recorded context.** The history records the argument graph's introduction at alpha.5 (ADR-77/78/79) and its consolidation into the single `project` Concept, with ADR-126 recording that alpha.15's project *"absorbs ADR-77 … ADR-79 (argument graph/warrant/saturation floor) … and ADR-78 (thesis is now a role, not a type)."*

### Gap: ULID identity is dropped; identity is path/citekey-derived again

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** ULID identity is the **single newest decision in this subsystem** — it arrived only at alpha.15. The history: *"ULID identity introduced as a schema kind (`id: ulid`); `schema.py` added `ulid` and `links` field kinds and `is_ulid` validation,"* with the rationale *"'identity ≠ filename ≠ title' (ADR-126 meaning-only section) — decoupling identity from the path so renames/moves don't break references."* Before alpha.15, identity was path/key-derived. alpha.11 had already taken a partial step for catalog: *"the catalog path is an immutable `source_id`, not the citekey — citekeys are metadata-derived and get corrected, so they are a generated display/export alias, never the path (… fixture-verified across a citekey correction),"* demoting `citekey` to an optional alias. But alpha.1's `paper` type had `citekey` as a required identity field, and pre-alpha.11 identity was broadly filename/citekey-based.

**Prior states of the same element.** citekey/filename identity (Old Skeleton, alpha.1 `paper.citekey` required) → immutable path `source_id` for catalog, citekey demoted to alias (alpha.11) → ULID `id` schema kind, "identity ≠ filename ≠ title" (alpha.15).

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1's *"identity is `citekey` + file path (works keyed by citekey; no ULID)"* **reverses the alpha.15 innovation** and returns identity to a pre-alpha.15 scheme. Keying works by `citekey` specifically echoes alpha.1's `paper` type, where `citekey` was the required identity — the very metadata-derived-and-correctable key that alpha.11 had deliberately demoted (*"citekeys … get corrected, so they are a generated display/export alias, never the path"*) and alpha.15 had superseded with a stable ULID. So beta.1 undoes both the alpha.11 immutable-path-not-citekey move and the alpha.15 ULID move on the identity axis, landing back near the alpha.1 citekey-and-path identity.

**Recorded context.** The history dates ULID to alpha.15 and records its purpose (decouple identity from path so *"renames/moves don't break references"*), and records the alpha.11 predecessor reasoning that citekeys are unstable because they *"get corrected"* — the exact fragility beta.1's citekey-keyed identity reintroduces.

### Gap: typed authored relation links are dropped from frontmatter

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** Authored links trace to the Old Skeleton's *"wikilinks + MOCs"* and a controlled-vocabulary schema. alpha.1 notes *"carry authored `links:`."* The *typing* of those relations was formalized in **ADR-08 ("typed-relations-frontmatter")**. alpha.11 standardized the predicate set: *"Typed Links standardized to the predicate set `{supports|challenges|extends|supersedes|in_project}` + Citations; `version_of` restricted to catalog source↔source only,"* and *"old ADR-08 'typed-relations-frontmatter' retired"* (folded into the reset, not abandoned as a concept). alpha.15 made `links:` *"a nested, relation-typed map validated against a fixed relation set `{supports, contradicts, extends}`, rejecting scalar values, absolute/URL/mail targets, and traversal-like (`..`) targets"* (PR-AE #1199), carrying ADR-52's distinction *"verbatim — authored `links:` are the human/argument layer; given `relationships` are engine-built DB records."* So by alpha.15 there were two distinct link tiers: authored, relation-typed `links:` in frontmatter (the argument layer) and engine-built `relationships` in the DB (given facts).

**Prior states of the same element.** untyped wikilinks + MOCs (Old Skeleton) → authored `links:` (alpha.1) → typed relations in frontmatter (ADR-08) → predicate-set typed Links (alpha.11) → validated relation-typed `links:` map, closed against `{supports, contradicts, extends}` (alpha.15).

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1's *"links are Obsidian wikilinks in body + a DB `links` backlink table + `%%prov%%` block markers; no relation-typed frontmatter links map."* On the **authored-relation-typing axis this returns to the Old Skeleton / pre-ADR-08 world of untyped wikilinks** — the relation typing (supports/contradicts/extends), a property held continuously from ADR-08 through alpha.11's predicate set through alpha.15's validated map, is removed from frontmatter. beta.1's *DB `links` backlink table* is lineage-consistent with the engine-built `relationships`/backlink store that alpha.11–15 kept DB-side, so the *derived* half of the two-tier model survives in spirit; what is dropped is the *authored, relation-typed* half — the human/argument layer that ADR-52 (carried verbatim into alpha.15) drew a hard line around. So beta.1 keeps a derived backlink store (continuous with the alpha DB relationships) while reverting authored links to untyped body wikilinks (a return to the earliest recorded state on that axis).

**Recorded context.** The history records the two-tier split as an explicit, verbatim-carried decision: ADR-52's distinction that *"authored `links:` are the human/argument layer; given `relationships` are engine-built DB records"* (alpha.15), and alpha.11's rationale for keeping high-risk directional relations human-set (*"High-risk directional Links (contradiction, supersession) stay human-set ('automatic = candidates')"*). The typed-relations concept was *retired-into-the-reset* at alpha.11 (ADR-08 folded into the predicate set), never abandoned as a goal until beta.1 drops it from frontmatter.

## 4. Frontmatter discipline

The three gaps in this subsystem all turn on the same underlying question — *what carries structured metadata, how tightly is that metadata policed, and where does verdict/provenance state live* — so their lineages interlock. What the history shows most clearly is that the two alpha.15 properties the gap analysis flags as dropped (universal enforcement, closed reject-undeclared validation) are both **very late arrivals** — alpha.14 and alpha.15 respectively, the final two releases — while the property alpha.15 flags as *removed* (in-file verdict state) was **present continuously from the pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton through alpha.14** and deleted only in the last cut. beta.1 moves on all three against that grain.

### Gap: B1 drops universal-frontmatter-on-every-concept; human/freehand files carry none

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** Universal frontmatter — the requirement that *every* Concept carry the same baseline fields — is a late arrival, not a hard-won property built across the arc. The pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton" (2026-05-29 snapshot) shipped "**~15 typed notes** with lifecycles, a **controlled-vocabulary frontmatter schema**, wikilinks + MOCs" (Part I). That schema was *per-type*, not universal. alpha.1 (`0e9ed9dd`, 2026-06-14) formalized it as per-type contracts at `src/.memoria/schemas/types/<type>.yaml` with `required:`/`optional:` maps, and — crucially — the design explicitly exempted whole zones: "`system/` infrastructure (except `system/patterns/`) and the three vault-root nav pages (`home.md`, `research-focus.md`, `troubleshooting.md`) are **untyped and exempt**" (Part II §3). So through alpha.1 frontmatter was type-scoped, and untyped human-facing files already existed by design.

Universality as a *hard enforced rule* appears only at **alpha.14**: PR #1157 (`88b7f0f5`, 2026-06-30) — "Universal Concept frontmatter is now *enforced* for every file under `knowledge/` and `capabilities/`: each Concept must carry `id` … `standing` … and a map-shaped `links: {}`" (Part III alpha.14). The gap analysis's phrase "alpha.14 enforce universal concept frontmatter" pins exactly this PR. **alpha.15** then kept the universality but changed its *content* to meaning-only: "the universal contract is ULID `id`, `type`, `title`, `tags`, typed `links`, `aliases`, optional `archived`, plus per-type fields" (PR #1164 + #1179, Part III alpha.15). So the alpha.15 form is one release old on its universality and two PRs old on its exact field list.

**Prior states of the same element.** The element existed earlier in materially different forms: per-type-and-permissive with exempt untyped zones (Old Skeleton → alpha.14 pre-#1157), then universal-with-`standing`/`check_status` (alpha.14 #1157), then universal-meaning-only (alpha.15). Arc (d) in Part IV summarizes the whole trajectory: "Early frontmatter was per-type and optional … **Alpha.14 forced universal frontmatter** … **Alpha.15 then inverted it**" (to meaning-only).

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 — "frontmatter appears **only on Memoria-created files**; user notes need none; 'absence of all `mem-*` markers = human'" — does **not** return to any specific earlier alpha era. Even the Old Skeleton and alpha.1 gave human prose notes (`fleeting`/`source`/`claims`) typed frontmatter; the only files that ever legitimately carried *no* frontmatter were `system/` infra and nav pages (alpha.1 §3), which are not human knowledge notes. So beta.1's "human knowledge files carry no frontmatter" has **no precedent in the alpha line** as applied to knowledge content. What beta.1 *does* continue is the deeper machine/human trust asymmetry introduced at the alpha.11 reset: "the human is trusted and reads live content directly; the barrier gates machines, not people" (alpha.12 read-barrier, Part III; "PI live edit can exist in `knowledge` while machine consumers filter it out"). beta.1 extends that asymmetry down to frontmatter *presence itself* — the marker's absence is the human signal — which is a new mechanism on an existing trajectory, not a restoration.

### Gap: B1 drops the closed/reject-undeclared frontmatter validator and the `x.*` extension namespace

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The reject-undeclared validator is the single latest-arriving property in this subsystem — it lands in the final release. For essentially the entire alpha line the design was deliberately **permissive**. alpha.1 states it outright: "**Unknown extra fields are allowed** — *'the schema constrains, it does not enumerate.'*" (Part II §3). Custom/reference data was parked in underscore-prefixed namespaces rather than rejected — reference taxonomies "living in each note's `_enrichment` namespace 'for browsing, not querying'" (Part II §6 vocabulary), and the Linter section names "the agent-written `_proposed_classification`/`_enrichment` namespaces" (Part II gate). "Strictness" entered only on *values*, not *fields*: ADR-71 (alpha.5-era) declared Memoria "**strict, not liberal** — an unknown *value* for a fixed-set field is rejected, not coerced" (Part III alpha.5, #765) — controlled-vocabulary enforcement, still atop a permissive field set. Field *closure* was designed at ADR-119 (alpha.7, `76b2a148`) as part of "the complete declarative contract," but that was design-only and ADR-119 was later narrowed at alpha.12 ("Concept schemas own field, home, read-state, and form contracts").

Closure as *enforced product code* appears only at **alpha.15, PR-AC #1197**: "Frontmatter validation flipped from **permissive to closed**: `validate_frontmatter` now rejects undeclared fields ('undeclared field: … (put custom data under `x`)'); every type schema gained an `x: map` extension escape hatch." The history is explicit that this "**closes the field-closure part of ADR-119** … now enforced in product code rather than as a gated content edit" (Part III alpha.15). The `x:` namespace itself is brand-new at alpha.15 — it is the closed validator's necessary companion, replacing the older permissive `_`-prefixed convention with a single declared escape map.

**Prior states of the same element.** The validator's posture changed once, decisively: permissive-with-underscore-namespaces (Old Skeleton → alpha.14) → closed-with-`x:`-escape-hatch (alpha.15, #1197). Value-level strictness (ADR-71) is a separate, earlier tightening that did not touch field closure.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 — "no reject-undeclared validator; a freehand file without frontmatter surfaces a one-tap proposal, 'flags never block'" — **returns to the permissive posture that held from alpha.1 through alpha.14**, i.e. it reverts a property that existed for exactly one release. In lineage terms the closed validator is a single-release-old feature (#1197, alpha.15) and beta.1 restores the long-standing default. The "flags never block" half also has deep roots that predate alpha.15: the low-confidence→`flag` escape valve is ADR-56 (alpha.1/alpha.2, "mechanical facts flow ungated … with the low-confidence→`flag` escape valve"), and the non-blocking, detect-after-the-fact stance is the alpha.11 thesis "**traceability, not approval**" plus the Linter's "**detective backstop**" role ("the finding means the trail no longer pins that file's state," Part II gate). So beta.1's non-blocking flags continue an existing trajectory, while the removal of the closed validator specifically reverts the alpha.15 arrival.

### Gap: B1 re-introduces in-file provenance/verdict markers; A15 kept verdicts DB-only with no in-file marker

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** Here the alpha.15 property is the *removal* — verdicts DB-only, no in-file marker — and that removal is what is one release old. In-file verdict/provenance state was, by contrast, one of the most durable properties in the whole history, present from the very beginning:

- **Old Skeleton:** the structurally blocking gate *was* an in-file field — "a card cannot promote until the human sets `review_status: approved`" (Part I).
- **alpha.1:** in-file verdicts pervasive — `agent_recommendation` (`inconclusive → issues-found → clean`) is "a **soft agent verdict**" carried in frontmatter (Part II §3; flag cards "keep a verdict (`agent_recommendation`)," §5), and the board `review_status` state machine is "the **authoritative state of human approval**" (ADR-03, Part III alpha.1 gate); `lifecycle` likewise lived in the file.
- **alpha.11 reset:** `lifecycle` "was removed from knowledge Concepts and replaced by `check_status`" as the read-barrier verdict field — still in-file: "The **authoritative field is frontmatter `check_status`**" (Part III alpha.11).
- **alpha.14:** universal `standing` + `check_status` enforced in frontmatter across 74 files (#1157).

The in-file verdict marker was deleted only at **alpha.15**, via ADR-126/128 (PR #1164/#1179 meaning-only; PR-B #1163 moving verdicts to SQLite): "`check_status`, `standing`, `note_status`, and every derived/verdict field were removed from *all* type schemas and the templates." The recorded reason: "`check_status` lived in frontmatter, so checking a note **rewrote it**, hand-edits **forged status**, and every consumer had to distrust the file it was reading" (ADR-126 Context). The history flags that this even "*retires* the enforcement PR #1157 landed the same day." The reset-summary table pins the same event: "`check_status`/`standing`/`note_status` frontmatter fields … → Meaning-only frontmatter; verdicts are DB-only; frontmatter checking caused hash churn + forged-status foot-gun (ADR-126/128)." Fail-closed followed: "DB loss fails closed to `unchecked` … rebuild ignores any forged frontmatter status" (Part III alpha.15, ADR-127).

**Prior states of the same element.** The carrier of verdict/provenance changed repeatedly but was *always in-file* until alpha.15: `review_status` (Old Skeleton) → `agent_recommendation` + `review_status` + `lifecycle` (alpha.1) → `check_status` (alpha.11) → `standing` + `check_status` (alpha.14) → **removed to DB** (alpha.15). Arc (d) frames the alpha.15 removal as "the same provenance-integrity principle … applied to state instead of location."

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 re-introduces in-file provenance/verdict markers — `%%prov:h|m|mc%%` block markers plus `mem-prov`/`mem-taint`/`mem-flags` frontmatter keys — which **returns the design to the pre-alpha.15 state** where verdict/provenance lived in the file (a property continuous from the Old Skeleton through alpha.14). But two lineage-specific qualifications hold. First, the *authority* relationship inverts relative to the alpha line: through alpha.14 the in-file field *was* the authority ("the authoritative field is frontmatter `check_status`"), whereas beta.1 keeps alpha.15's DB-authoritative verdict and makes the marker subordinate — the gap analysis §5 records "in-file `%%prov:mc%%` marker is **display shorthand**; green requires marker + current block hash + DB `checks` row to agree." Second, the specific *mechanism* — Obsidian `%%…%%` comment block markers as a provenance carrier — has no alpha precedent; verdicts were always frontmatter *fields*, never body comment markers. Notably, beta.1's non-authoritative-in-file-marker shape is close to the exact alternative ADR-126 **rejected** at alpha.15 — "keeping `check_status` as a generated-but-ignored projection — 'hash churn plus a forged-status foot-gun; meaning-only is stronger'" — with beta.1 answering the forgery hazard not by removing the marker (alpha.15's route) but by requiring marker + block hash + DB row to agree.

## 5. Integrity / review / gate model

The three gaps in this subsystem all sit on the single longest-running arc in Memoria's history: the transfer of the trust guarantee away from *a human approving each write* toward *the system structurally guaranteeing a trace* — the through-line the design history names as "an inherited, tool-shaped skeleton … progressively examined … and re-derived from first principles — culminating in the **alpha.11 clean-slate reset** ('direction not authorship / traceability not approval') and the **alpha.15 standalone-engine + four-type + quarantine-and-verify cut**" (Part I). Each gap is a place where beta.1 reaches back into some earlier station of that arc. The analysis below traces each one.

### B1 re-introduces a review/disposition surface that A15 had removed

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The alpha.15 state — the *removal* of the review-queue approval surface — is the terminal point of a property that was present from before day one. The pre-alpha.1 **Old Skeleton** (2026-05-29 snapshot) already carried "**A structurally blocking human review gate:** a card cannot promote until the human sets `review_status: approved` — positioned deliberately against the surveyed autonomous-research systems, whose LLM reviewers only advise" (Part I). This was not incidental; it was the skeleton's defining epistemic stance, framed as "*vibe researching made durable (the vault) and gated (blocking review)*."

At the **alpha.1** baseline the gate became first-class state: `review_status` as a state machine `unreviewed → requested → approved / rejected`, "the authoritative state of human approval — not comments, tags, or conversations" (ADR-03, `why-human-gate.md`), with `disposition.jsonl` recording `accept | edit | reject` marked *un-backfillable*, a WIP cap creating back-pressure, and the agent verdict explicitly separated from the review ("informs; it never replaces"). The *surface* for this was the alpha.1 **Inbox and honesty card** (ADR-51): `inbox/` as "the agent→human message category; the kanban board and queue dashboards are *views* of it (one Base grouped by type)," where proposals carry "an **honest argument, no verdict**: `action` · `argument_for` · `argument_against` … `what_tipped_it` · `certainty`" and verification cards "**lead with the finding**." Two beta.1 mechanics have exact alpha.1 precedents here: **batch worklists** trace to ADR-54's rule that high-cardinality screening becomes "*one aggregate work-prompt*" over a Bases worklist, "never N cards, which *would flood a queue meant to converge to zero and invite select-all-accept*"; and the **ribbon pending count** traces to the alpha.1 homepage Status glance "*N review(s) pending · N blocked · N HIGH/CRITICAL*."

**Prior states of the same element.** The disposition surface was rebuilt, not merely removed, at each reset. At **alpha.7** the Inbox became one of the four persistent-shell spaces (Inbox/Library/Knowledge/Project nav row). At the **alpha.11** clean-slate reset the durable form was retired: "Retired the `inbox/` category and all inbox-resident durable Concepts; replaced with **generated attention projections**" — "**Attention is generated, not authored** … markdown, Bases, Canvas, or plugin 'cards' are projections and never canonical Concepts" (§8.2). alpha.11 kept the honesty-card obligations and added ADR-121's "**enqueue-only**" control-panel rule (a UI "may mutate only by enqueueing worker jobs … never writes Concepts, journal files, generated projections, or `check_status` directly"). So through alpha.11–14 a review/disposition *worklist* persisted in generated form; what was progressively dismantled was the *authored, durable card* and the *approval gate*, not the worklist itself.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** The removal that alpha.15 actually performed was narrow and specific: "The review queue as an **approval surface** was removed; replaced by a consequence-routed **attention worklist** keeping the honesty-card obligations (arguments not verdicts; batch worklists; graded loudness quiet/notice/alert/block)" (ADR-128), and "ADR-51's honesty card and ADR-121's enqueue-only rule carried verbatim; the inbox-as-Obsidian-category mechanism is dead." alpha.15 also carried, via ADR-130, "**Honesty-card + code-review ergonomics** … high-cardinality decisions become one batch worklist; loudness graded … `--apply` one click." Read against this, beta.1's Review surface maps onto the history in two distinct ways:

- Its *content model* — batch worklists (apply/reject/defer), graded triage, honesty "processing card" triage, one-click revert — **continues the trajectory that alpha.15 retained** (the consequence-routed attention worklist with honesty-card obligations, graded loudness, and `--apply`). apply/reject/defer is the alpha.1 `accept | edit | reject` disposition vocabulary; one-click revert maps onto cascade-rollback (alpha.11 §4.2 / ADR-127); batch worklists map onto ADR-54/ADR-130. None of these reintroduce the *approval-confers-correctness* gate that ADR-128 removed.
- Its *surface form* — a **dedicated main-pane Obsidian Review view with a ribbon pending count** — reaches back to an earlier era: the alpha.1 Inbox-board-as-action-queue and homepage Status glance, and the alpha.7 persistent-shell Inbox space. That first-class Obsidian disposition *pane* is precisely what alpha.14/15 removed when Obsidian was dropped from the product ("the shipped surfaces are 'CLI, scoped MCP, and loopback HTTP'"; ADR-130 makes "The engine's **JSON read-API … the sole read path** for every Memoria-built surface"). So the gap is most precisely a return of the *hosted review surface*, not a return of the removed *approval gate* — the underlying disposition machinery is the one alpha.15 kept.

**Recorded context.** The design history pins exactly when and why the approval surface was abandoned: ADR-128's Context "records the epistemic reversal from the `_papers/` adversarial pass (Jacobs 2021; AutoResearchClaw; Feng & Liu verification-asymmetry)" (alpha.15 theme), and the removal is logged as "Structural write-gate reversed to quarantine-and-verify; approval no longer confers correctness (ADR-128)." The alpha.15 date is 2026-07-01/02.

### B1 adds the pre==post manifest gate + block-level byte-immutability + auto-rollback

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The alpha.15 side — "no-write-time-oracle (ADR-128)" plus propagation-only demotion — is the current head of a hash-based integrity spine built up incrementally over the whole arc, and a posture that was *detective/consumption-time*, never *write-time byte-enforcing*.

- **alpha.1** established hashing as a *detective* mechanism, explicitly not preventive. The policy gate computed per-write `before_hash` / `after_hash` pairing ("The design chose **per-write hash pairing, not a cross-entry hash chain**"; ADR-25), and the Linter's `vault-hash-drift` (CRITICAL) "compares each recorded `after_hash` to the current on-disk SHA-256. A mismatch means 'the file was edited outside the audited write path… A legitimate human edit in Obsidian surfaces here too, by design.'" The alpha.1 gate was candid that it "cannot be truly fail-closed at the Hermes layer" — "the pairing catches tampering **after the fact** rather than preventing it" (ADR-25). alpha.1 also shipped the **golden copy**: "a SHA-256-manifested canonical copy of every system file at `.memoria/golden/`, with `restore` propose-only by default (`--apply` writes bytes back)" — a manifest-checked byte-integrity-and-restore mechanism, though scoped to *system* files. The no-write-time-oracle stance is prefigured here too: the four-decision engine "only *decides*"; a `dry_run` "preserves the agent's work product **without letting it reach canonical**" — it blocks, it does not edit.
- **alpha.11** introduced **cascade rollback** as a first-class operation over a content-hashed PROV-DM journal ("the **atomic *write* unit; the *rollback* unit is the output Concept**"; §4.2), the **trace-integrity scan** (untraced/foreign commits quarantined), and content-hashed journal events. This is the origin of quarantine-and-rollback.
- **alpha.14** made foreign-write classification concrete: "a direct edit to a checked keep-set Concept is marked `unchecked`/non-consumable and re-promoted only through the `mark-checked` capability … A change to a generated projection, worker-owned path, `references.bib`, or capability manifest is *quarantined*, never re-consumed" — "the structural read-barrier substitute for per-write approval," preserving "prior checked hash for cascade rollback."
- **alpha.15/ADR-127** set the current form: the **consumption guard** — "at machine consumption the on-disk hash must equal the recorded `checked` hash; mismatch **refuses to serve and enqueues scan** — no write on the read path" (synchronous demote-on-read was *rejected* because "a write on the read path races the workspace lock; refusal alone is already fail-closed") — plus **two-tier demotion propagation** ("depth-1 machine consumers flip to `unchecked`; PI-authored descendants raise an ask-routed flag and stay live … depth-≥2 machine descendants get a DB `stale` flag"). This two-tier propagation is exactly "A15's propagation-only demotion" the gap analysis references.

So across the whole arc the hash unit was the **file/path** (per-write `before/after_hash`; per-Concept checked hash), the check fired at **consumption or on scan** (never as a write-time byte-equality gate), human content was **freely edited by the trusted PI** (the PI "edits Obsidian directly" and `observe-pi-edits` backfills; alpha.11 §4.1), and recovery was **cascade rollback of the output Concept**, not byte-restoration.

**Prior states of the same element.** The manifest-with-byte-restore existed once and was removed inside the same subsystem: alpha.1's golden-copy SHA-256 manifest with `restore --apply`, which **alpha.15 deleted**: "`golden_restore.py` **removed** (155 lines deleted; … 'Remove golden restore product path') … package reinstall is the restore path for product files (supersedes ADR-26/55/74 repo-as-install-unit + golden-copy)." A faint sub-file-hash precedent also existed and was moved: alpha.11 note anchors carried `text_sha256` on a quote/anchor (a block/span hash), which alpha.15 relocated off the type into a W3C `TextQuoteSelector` `anchor` — but that was for anchoring quotes into sources, never a write-gate over human content.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 keeps the no-write-time-oracle invariant verbatim (its export gate "blocks, does not edit," which is the alpha.1 `dry_run` / alpha.15 ADR-128 posture), but adds three things that shift the model:

- The **pre==post byte gate with auto-rollback** relocates hash-checking from alpha.15's *consumption-time refusal + propagation* back toward *write/commit-time byte-equality enforcement*. beta.1 §11(4) itself states this is "**stronger than A15's propagation-only demotion**." The alpha line never enforced byte-equality at write time — alpha.1 was explicitly detective and fail-open at the Hermes layer, and alpha.15 deliberately refused a write on the read path. So a byte-equality *gate that auto-rollbacks* has **no precedent in the alpha line as write-time enforcement**; it is a new locus for a mechanism (SHA hashing) whose lineage is deep but whose prior role was always detective/consumption-time.
- The **block-level human-content read-only rule** introduces a **sub-file granularity** that the alpha line never used for gating: the alpha hash unit was the file (`before/after_hash` per path; checked hash per Concept). The only sub-file hash in the arc was the alpha.11 anchor `text_sha256`, which was not an enforcement boundary and was relocated off the type at alpha.15. Block-level *immutability of human content* is therefore a new axis with no alpha precedent.
- The **human-span hash manifest with pre==post byte gate** re-introduces a *manifest-checked byte-integrity* mechanism after alpha.15 had removed the last one (the golden manifest / `golden_restore.py`). beta.1 points it at human content rather than system files, but the shape — a SHA manifest that gates byte-equality and can roll back — is genealogically the alpha.1 golden copy re-aimed, arriving after alpha.15 retired that machinery.

**Recorded context.** The alpha.15 posture is recorded as a deliberate design choice with stated rejections: ADR-127 rejected synchronous demote-on-read ("refusal alone is already fail-closed"), rejected hard exclusion of stale content ("hard exclusion breaks cross-period queries"), and framed the whole thing as making "errors … detectable, attributable, and reversible **instead of gated at creation**" (ADR-127 Context) — i.e. the alpha end-state deliberately declined a write-time byte gate in favor of quarantine-and-verify. The golden-manifest removal is recorded as "Package reinstall is the restore path (ADR-125; PR #1206)."

### B1 adds the in-file `%%prov:mc%%` marker layer that A15 did not have

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** "verdicts DB-only, no in-file marker" is a **late arrival, reached only at alpha.15**, and it is the endpoint of evolution arc (d): "Frontmatter: optional → universal enforcement (alpha.14) → meaning-only (alpha.15)." The design history is explicit that a verdict/status *lived in the file* for essentially the entire history before alpha.15:

- **Old Skeleton / alpha.1:** `review_status: approved` was an in-file frontmatter field on the board card — the human-approval verdict lived in the file. Verification cards "keep a verdict" (`agent_recommendation`), also in-file; Diátaxis mode was declared per-file in frontmatter.
- **alpha.11:** `check_status` lived in frontmatter (`initial_check_status: unchecked`), though only "the trusted worker/check runner transitions this field." `extraction_confidence` was carried but deliberately "**internal telemetry — never surfaced**" — "a direct response to Jacobs 2021 (don't show an agent verdict)."
- **alpha.14:** the peak of in-file verdict state — PR #1157 forced "`id`/`standing`/`check_status` frontmatter enforcement across templates and operation manifests" (74 files).
- **alpha.15:** the inversion. "**Meaning-only frontmatter** enforced … `check_status`, `standing`, `note_status`, and every derived/verdict field were removed from *all* type schemas and the templates," with verdicts moved to SQLite (`concept_verdicts` / `concept_flags`). arc (d): "`check_status` (and all verdict/derived state) is *not written to frontmatter at all*, and a gate asserts frontmatter carries no verdict fields." The split-authority rule of ADR-125 ("files own meaning, the DB owns verdicts and records") is the governing principle, and `checked_terminology_gate.py` "even 'blocks checked = approved/verified/trusted drift.'"

There is also a genuine **in-file machine-marker precedent** earlier in the arc, distinct from frontmatter: at **alpha.5**, `structural_impact.py` wrote a generated `project-gate-index.md` "embedding a machine-readable JSON block **between HTML comment markers**" with `computed_at`/`stale` and per-note derived values — and the design deliberately chose "a **single index-note** rather than per-note stamps," to "honor the 'write notes once' discipline / avoid the ADR-25 append-only audit write-amplification a per-component re-stamp would cause." That is the closest alpha-line ancestor of an in-body, non-rendering marker carrying machine verdict data (and it self-excluded from the scan).

**Prior states of the same element.** The element "a status/verdict marker inside the concept" therefore existed *continuously from the Old Skeleton (`review_status: approved`) through alpha.14 (`check_status`)*, always as in-file frontmatter, and was **removed at alpha.15** as a named hazard, not by drift.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1's `%%prov:mc%%` in-file marker (Obsidian `%%…%%` comment syntax, non-rendering) re-introduces an in-file status/verdict token — a return, in surface form, to the pre-alpha.15 world where the concept file carried a status, and specifically resembling the alpha.5 HTML-comment machine block embedded in a note. But it maps onto the arc as a **hybrid** rather than a straight reversion:

- Its *surface form* (a marker in the file) reaches back past alpha.15 to the entire alpha.1–14 in-file-status era, and to alpha.5's in-body comment-marker block.
- Its *authority model* **continues the alpha.15 posture verbatim**: the marker is "display shorthand," "kept as a claim, not authority"; "green requires marker + current block hash + DB `checks` row to agree." The DB remains the authority (alpha.15 split-authority rule), and the block-hash triangulation is the mechanism that lets an in-file marker coexist with meaning-only frontmatter without reinstating the forged-status foot-gun. Framing the token as a machine-check *claim* rather than a correctness verdict is consistent with alpha.15's "**checked ≠ approved**" and with the Jacobs "never show an agent verdict" line carried since alpha.11.

So beta.1 does not return the file to being the authority (the specific thing alpha.15 forbade); it returns the *marker* while keeping the *authority* in the DB — a combination with no exact precedent in the alpha line (the alpha line either put the verdict in the file *as* authority, pre-alpha.15, or had no in-file marker at all, at alpha.15).

**Recorded context.** The design history records precisely why the in-file verdict was removed at alpha.15 and, tellingly, records the exact rejected alternative that beta.1's marker most resembles: "`check_status` lived in frontmatter, so checking a note rewrote it, hand-edits forged status, and every consumer had to distrust the file it was reading" (ADR-126 Context), and the *rejected alternative* was "keeping `check_status` as a **generated-but-ignored projection** — 'hash churn plus a forged-status foot-gun; meaning-only is stronger'" (ADR-126). beta.1's `%%prov:mc%%` is exactly a generated-display projection of verdict state into the file, and it answers that recorded rejection by making the marker non-authoritative and requiring block-hash + DB-row agreement (a forged marker fails the triangulation) — mapping onto the history as a construction that revisits, under a triangulation constraint, the very projection alpha.15 had rejected as a foot-gun.

## 6. Machine judgment / LLM allocation

Across all four gaps the subsystem sits on one long spine the design history names repeatedly: the pre-alpha.1 thesis that "**Maintaining a knowledge base is a bookkeeping problem, not an intelligence problem**" (Old Skeleton `vision.md`, quoted in Part I), hardened over the arc into ADR-57's "**engines write, agents judge**" (alpha.2) and finally into alpha.15's "**proposers, never authorities**" rule for every machine judge (ADR-129). The four beta.1 gaps are four different points where that spine was instantiated in alpha.15 and re-shaped in beta.1.

### Gap 6.1 — No Tier-2 LLM tension/contradiction judge; contradiction is NLI-only within the retrieved set

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The alpha.15 form is a *two-tier* machine: a Tier-1 pinned-local NLI proposer plus a Tier-2 LLM tension judge, both bound by "proposer, never authority." Neither tier is a from-scratch alpha.15 idea, but they arrive at very different points in the arc.

- **Contradiction-as-NLI is one of the oldest signals in the system.** It shipped in the alpha.1 baseline as part of ADR-30's optional Tier-2: alongside the `[!brief]` narrative and arXiv→code cross-linking, alpha.1 carried "an **advisory NLI contradiction signal**" (Part II, §Pipeline). The typed-layer split (ADR-52) was explicitly justified in part because it "enabl[es] the contradictions dashboard (ADR-09) and claim supersession (ADR-10)" (Part II). So a contradiction facility is present from the first tagged release, but as an *advisory* Tier-2 extra, not a gate.
- **The heavy precision engine was designed and then deliberately deferred at alpha.9.** The design history records alpha.9 as "The truth-alignment checkpoint," and it is there that contradiction detection was "**re-tiered down to a cheap soft-flag** … the NLI + decomposed-variable-match gate + MaxSAT apparatus **deferred**" on an explicit N=1 error-cost model: "a missed/late contradiction is *self-correcting and low-cost* for a single PI, so a precision engine is over-built." The same entry pins the empirical finding that shaped every later NLI decision: "**NLI *confidently mints false contradictions* (0.94-1.00) on same-topic/different-variable pairs, unrecoverable by a confidence threshold**; the decomposed gate helped (recall 4/6, FP 6/7→1/7) but still leaks negation/argument-swap" (alpha.9). alpha.9's "Deferred/killed" list is explicit: "**the high-precision contradiction engine** (NLI + decomposed gate + MaxSAT), **blanket self-consistency** … and **auto-applied contradiction/supersession links**."
- **The operation form appeared at alpha.11.** The clean-slate reset materialized `surface-tensions` and `compare-and-contrast` as operation Concepts and `integrity-contradiction-check` as an integrity-* checker, under the new separation-of-duties rule that "the operation that *generates* is never the one that *checks*" (alpha.11, §Profiles/runtime). High-risk directional links stayed human-set — "contradiction, supersession … automatic = candidates" (alpha.11, Typed Links).
- **The two-tier NLI+judge machine is an alpha.15 late arrival.** Tier-1 (NLI proposer) landed as PR-I #1176: "a pinned local NLI comparator, tri-class SUPPORTED/REFUTED/NOTENOUGHINFO with **first-class abstain** and an FP budget … it may gate candidates **only after passing a HANS-style overlap-but-opposite acceptance set**; failing HANS is a **loud degraded mode** … no automatic `contradicts` writes," on the recorded reasoning that "**NLI shares cosine's lexical-overlap blind spot**" and "NLI silence is never presented as 'no contradiction.'" The **Tier-2 tension judge** is even later — PR-AF #1200 (`0a1c1b09`): "an LLM judge admissible **only** on Tier-1 abstain/HANS-degraded hard cases, retrieval-grounded, abstain-first, temperature 0, requires quote grounding from both consumable checked files … never writes checked `contradicts` links — 'a proposer, never an authority.'" Its recorded rationale is a rejection: "LLM-judge-as-authority rejected (GBrain's failure; '~28% wrong belief-flips in ConCoRD-class systems')" (ADR-129 Alternatives).

**Prior states of the same element.** The contradiction element changed form three times before alpha.15: advisory Tier-2 NLI signal (alpha.1, ADR-30) → soft-flag with the precision engine deferred (alpha.9) → `surface-tensions`/`integrity-contradiction-check` operations (alpha.11) → Tier-1 NLI proposer + Tier-2 LLM judge (alpha.15). The LLM *judge* specifically had no prior existence in the alpha line before PR-AF #1200.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 keeps NLI within the retrieved set as its contradiction primitive and drops the Tier-2 LLM tension judge. Factually this splits into two different movements against the arc:
- The Tier-2 LLM judge is an element that *only ever existed in alpha.15*. Removing it does not return to any earlier era — it removes the single newest layer, leaving the NLI-only posture that ran continuously from alpha.9 through alpha.14 (NLI as the contradiction primitive, no LLM judge on top). In that sense beta.1's "NLI within the retrieved set" **continues the pre-alpha.15 trajectory** and undoes only the alpha.15 addition.
- The alpha.9 measurement that "NLI confidently mints false contradictions on same-topic/different-variable pairs" and the alpha.15 finding that "NLI shares cosine's lexical-overlap blind spot" were precisely why alpha.15 wrapped NLI in a HANS gate plus an LLM fallback; beta.1's NLI-only-within-retrieved-set has no recorded precedent for running NLI as the sole surfacing mechanism *without* that abstain-and-escalate second tier.

**Recorded context.** The design history pins both the deferral reason (alpha.9's N=1 error-cost model: missed contradiction is "self-correcting and low-cost") and the alpha.15 admission reasons (HANS gate against NLI's lexical-overlap blind spot; LLM-judge-as-authority rejected on the ~28%-wrong-belief-flip evidence).

### Gap 6.2 — No per-field authority merge, no `coverage=degraded`, no `field_provenance`/`enrichment_runs` tables; enrichment is empty-field-fill only

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** This is the most hard-won property in the subsystem — the field-authority merge is present from the very first tagged release and is grounded in empirical evidence, not late polish.

- **alpha.1 established it, spike-grounded.** ADR-30's Tier-1 defined "Resolve/merge is **S2 + OpenAlex co-primary, Crossref for non-arXiv DOIs**, merged **per-field best-source-wins with provenance** (authors/ORCID/affiliations ← OpenAlex; intents/tldr/embedding ← S2; references = **union deduped by DOI**)" (Part II, §Pipeline). The design history is explicit that the *shape* was forced by evidence, "arrived at through **two adversarial red-team rounds and an empirical spike on the user's 867-entry library** … it found the three metadata sources **disagree and are each incomplete** (reference counts of 151/129/146 for the same paper across S2/OpenAlex/Crossref; ORCID present in OpenAlex but ~0 in S2), which is *why* the merge is per-field-best-source rather than single-source-precedence. The single-source (S2-only) alternative was **rejected** precisely because 'the spike showed each source is incomplete.'" alpha.1 also already carried the low-confidence escape valve — a "`calibration.yaml` **entity-resolution confidence floor of 0.85** that routes low-confidence merges to an Inbox `flag` card per ADR-56."
- **alpha.13 rebuilt it on the SQLite catalog.** The DOI-enrichment release shipped "**Deterministic field-authority merge + per-field provenance** (`enrichment._merge_doi_source`) — Crossref is authoritative for title/authors/issued/DOI/container/type; Unpaywall for `oa_location`/license; OpenAlex supplies the OpenAlex ID and external IDs. Each canonical field records a `_provenance(field_path, value, winning_provider)` row," with the design's §8 rule "**Field-authority merge with per-field provenance; high-authority conflicts → flag + `metadata_status`, never silent**" and the exec-plan's top risk "Provider disagreement — never silently pick a winner on a critical field" (alpha.13).
- **alpha.14** carried the merge forward while massively expanding the graph import (references/topics/authorships/relations into `work_graph_edges`), with discovered candidate Works "marked unchecked attention/worklist rows, never checked."
- **alpha.15** hardened it into the form the gap analysis cites: "**Enrichment merge by static field authority**: per-field best-source-wins with provenance; references are the DOI-deduped **union**; author sub-fields never paired by index across sources; `provider_coverage = full|partial|degraded`; high-authority conflict marks `degraded` and raises attention … new `field_provenance`/`enrichment_runs`/`provider_payloads` tables" (ADR-129; PR-F #1173). The recorded rationale ties directly back to alpha.1: it "**supersedes ADR-30's tiered-ingest merge**; 'no single provider is complete'; 'provider output is claims, not ground truth' … ADR-30's spike lessons (capture-commits-first, scriptable-before-LLM) are binding."

**Prior states of the same element.** Per-field authority merge existed in three concrete forms: alpha.1 Python pipeline (`pipeline.py` four-stage, 0.85 entity-resolution floor) → alpha.13 `_merge_doi_source` on SQLite with `metadata_status` → alpha.15 static field-authority table with `provider_coverage=full|partial|degraded` and the three new tables. The `provider_coverage=degraded` state and the `field_provenance`/`enrichment_runs`/`provider_payloads` tables are alpha.15-specific hardening; per-field-best-source-with-provenance itself is unbroken from alpha.1.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1's "OpenAlex singleton fills **empty fields only** (JabRef rule); conflicts render as per-field diffs through Review, never auto-applied; raw responses in a `payloads` store." This does not return to any earlier *shipped* alpha era. The alpha line has carried a multi-source per-field merge continuously since alpha.1; a single-provider empty-field-fill is the shape alpha.1 **considered and explicitly rejected** — "the single-source (S2-only) alternative was rejected precisely because the spike showed each source is incomplete." So beta.1's direction maps onto the pre-decision alternative at the origin, i.e. it reverses the alpha.1 867-paper-spike verdict rather than continuing any point on the arc. Two beta.1 elements do echo the alpha lineage in altered form: the `payloads` raw-response store parallels alpha.15's `provider_payloads` table, and routing conflicts as per-field diffs "through Review, never auto-applied" carries the alpha.13/alpha.15 discipline ("never silently pick a winner," "high-authority conflict … raises attention, never silent overwrite") — but beta.1 realizes it via a human diff at Review rather than the `provider_coverage=degraded` state and `update-work`-cannot-mark-degraded-checked machinery of alpha.15.

**Recorded context.** The 867-paper spike is the load-bearing recorded reason the merge is per-field rather than single-source (alpha.1), and ADR-129 records the alpha.15 restatement — "no single provider is complete," "provider output is claims, not ground truth."

### Gap 6.3 — No generalized shadow-first-until-grounded discipline; beta.1 uses per-engine frozen calibration sets and no `calibration.yaml` floor framework

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** Shadow-first is a property built up incrementally over the whole arc, not a late arrival, and the `calibration.yaml` container predates the discipline that eventually governed it.

- **The container is alpha.1.** `.memoria/schemas/` held "`calibration.yaml` (drift-bound thresholds)" from the alpha.1 baseline, and the alpha.1-era `ingest.md` already carried "a `calibration.yaml` **entity-resolution confidence floor of 0.85** that routes low-confidence merges to an Inbox `flag` card per ADR-56."
- **The shadow-vs-enforce split matured through the ADR-38 ratchet.** ADR-38 (pre-file similarity) was deferred at alpha.1; at **alpha.8** it shipped as "**Pre-file similarity shadow telemetry** … report-only … **never blocks, auto-merges, or applies a calibrated threshold**," with the ADR-38 prose updated to say "shadow primitive, not enforcement." The same alpha.8 entry added the "**`clustering.quality_thresholds` calibration contract** … `production_enabled: false`, null silhouette/coherence floors," with the file's comment "**Null thresholds mean shadow/report-only** — the same shadow-first discipline as the pre-file gate."
- **alpha.11 named it a discipline.** The reset shipped "`calibration.yaml` with `production_enabled: false` and `null` thresholds across `clustering`, `hybrid_scores.candidate_rank`, and pinned model-version slots," under §10's named rule: "**shadow-first, error-budgeted calibration discipline: no score affects routing until grounded within an error budget; ungrounded ⇒ report-only; drift ⇒ revert to shadow**" (anchored to ADR-66).
- **alpha.15 generalized it to every score.** "**Shadow-first calibration generalized** (ADR-66 → the rule for every score): a new score reports/journals but changes no routing/promotion/visibility 'until its thresholds are grounded on recorded PI dispositions with a named fixture and pinned model'; … the file itself and its `entity_resolution`/`classify`/`hybrid_scores` floors **survive**." The recorded reason is that nothing was ever validated: "**None of calibration.yaml's constants were ever validated, so all scoring stays shadow-first; the first grounded thresholds arrive with beta-era disposition data**."

**Prior states of the same element.** The discipline evolved: alpha.1 `calibration.yaml` + 0.85 floor (a live threshold routing to flags) → alpha.8 `production_enabled:false`/null clustering thresholds + shadow telemetry → alpha.11 named "shadow-first, error-budgeted" discipline (ADR-66) → alpha.15 generalized to every score.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 "**tunes NLI thresholds on a small pre-registered calibration set then freezes** them, gated by a HANS/PAWS launch-blocking acceptance test," per-engine, with no `calibration.yaml` floor framework. Mapped onto the arc, this introduces a mechanism with **no precedent in the alpha line**: the alpha line from alpha.8 through alpha.15 deliberately *avoided* committing to a validated/frozen threshold, holding scores at `production_enabled:false`/null precisely because "no constant was ever validated." beta.1's freeze-after-pre-registered-calibration is the opposite move — it sets and fixes thresholds rather than keeping them shadow-until-grounded-on-PI-dispositions. One element does carry forward in altered form: beta.1's **HANS/PAWS launch-blocking acceptance test** echoes alpha.15's HANS-style acceptance set that gated whether the NLI Tier-1 "may gate candidates" (PR-I #1176) — but in alpha.15 HANS was an admissibility gate feeding a loud degraded mode, not a launch-blocker that licenses freezing a threshold. The design history also records that the alpha.15 end-state *anticipated* the beta era as the moment thresholds would finally be grounded — "the first grounded thresholds arrive with **beta-era disposition data**" — so beta.1 arrives at the point the arc pointed to, but grounds thresholds on a pre-registered set rather than the recorded-PI-disposition mechanism the arc specified.

**Recorded context.** The shadow-first discipline's "why" is recorded at each step: alpha.8 ("null thresholds mean shadow/report-only"), alpha.11/ADR-66 ("no score affects routing until grounded within an error budget"), and alpha.15 ("none of calibration.yaml's constants were ever validated … first grounded thresholds arrive with beta-era disposition data").

### Gap 6.4 — No per-operation `method_class`/capability-manifest registry; beta.1 uses a prose "operation test"

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The deterministic/hybrid/generative taxonomy has the deepest pre-alpha.1 root of any element in this subsystem, but its *machine-readable per-operation declaration* is a late formalization.

- **Pre-alpha.1 root (the "computational toolbox").** The Old Skeleton Drive snapshot carried the design docs `why-computational-methods.md` and `capability-stack.md` (Old Skeleton inventory), plus `why-no-autonomous-synthesis.md` — the pre-alpha.1 thinking that classified computational methods and drew the line at where generative/autonomous methods are admissible. The Origins prologue records the survey's decision boundary in the same terms: the field survey made Memoria *decline* "**scalar-metric keep/revert**" and "**tree-search over synthesis**" because "none of those numbers is plausible for 'is this synthesis a faithful, well-cited, non-redundant addition,'" and the Old Skeleton was "Deliberately NOT L4/L5 — synthesis correctness is not scalar."
- **Formalized as a governing principle at alpha.2.** ADR-57 recorded "**engines write, agents judge** — any write whose correct content is derivable by rule is performed by an engine (deterministic code), never by an LLM agent," the deterministic/generative split as a system-wide rule (though not yet a declared per-operation field).
- **Operations-as-policy-Concepts at alpha.11.** The reset made operations Concepts carrying policy in frontmatter — "`allowed_tools`, `allowed_paths`, `allowed_network`, `runner`, `model`, `prompt_version`, `io_schema`, `risk_class`, `required_checks`" — with the `runner: direct_api | local | hermes` field already separating deterministic-local writers/checkers from generative direct-API generators, and separation of duties encoded per operation.
- **`method_class` itself arrived at alpha.14.** The alpha.14 design §4 introduced the exact three-value field: "**Every operation declares `method_class: deterministic | hybrid | generative`. Hybrid/generative operations must name the deterministic baseline they beat**, required checks, allowed paths, allowed network, runner/model policy, risk, and output intents. **LLM use without that manifest evidence fails closed**" (alpha.14 design.md §4). The release also made the loader enforce manifests: "Directory-only capability manifests are rejected by the loader … `operation list` validates manifests … **Every operation declares `method_class` and runner policy or fails loading**."
- **alpha.15 made the manifest product code.** ADR-125 relocated "operation/capability policy … into packaged product data … Operations are product code," so the manifest is a product-code capability manifest; ADR-129's runner policy pins per-operation `runner.test`/`runner.live`, and the baseline-beating rule is explicit — "each tier must beat a declared cheap baseline or it is disabled," with non-sandbox use "licensed only by the **live** model's seeded-error verdict per `(operation, model, mode)`."

**Prior states of the same element.** The decision-criterion existed continuously but changed representation: pre-alpha.1 prose (`why-computational-methods.md`/`capability-stack.md`) → alpha.2 governing principle (ADR-57 engines-write/agents-judge) → alpha.11 operation-policy Concept with a `runner` field → alpha.14 declared `method_class` field + loader-enforced manifest registry → alpha.15 product-code capability manifest with `runner.test`/`runner.live` and the "beat a declared baseline or be disabled" gate.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 "replaces the per-operation `method_class`/manifest declaration with a prose 'operation test' (pinned prompt+model, deterministically checkable, arithmetic price, per-item corpus-scale)" and has "no capability-manifest registry." Mapped onto the arc, this is a two-part movement:
- The *decision criterion* — deterministic-first, LLM admitted only when it beats a cheap baseline, priced explicitly — **continues the arc's substance**, directly descending from ADR-57 (alpha.2) and the alpha.14/alpha.15 "name the deterministic baseline you beat / beat a declared baseline or be disabled" rule.
- The *representation* — a prose test rather than a declared, loader-enforced `method_class` field and manifest registry — **returns to the pre-alpha.14 state**. The machine-readable `method_class` field and the loader that "fails loading" without it were the specific artifacts introduced at alpha.14 and hardened at alpha.15; before alpha.14 the deterministic/generative split lived as a governing principle (ADR-57) and in the *shape* of operations, not as a declared manifest field. beta.1's "operation test" is genealogically closer to that earlier prose/principle form of the taxonomy — the pre-alpha.1 `why-computational-methods` framing and ADR-57 — than to alpha.14/15's enforced capability-manifest registry.

**Recorded context.** The design history pins the enforcement intent at both formalizing versions: alpha.14 "**LLM use without that manifest evidence fails closed**" and "**Every operation declares `method_class` and runner policy or fails loading**"; alpha.15 "the manifest is the hard ceiling and non-sandbox use is licensed only by the **live** model's seeded-error verdict," grounded in the honest premise that "**no threshold in the prior corpus was ever validated**."

## 7. Catalog / bibliographic authority

### Gap 7a — Where the bibliographic record lives, and what keys it: SQLite catalog authority + `work_id` primary (A15) vs. `record.md` full CSL-JSON authority + citekey-primary (B1)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The A15 arrangement — the SQLite catalog + source-content blobs as *the* citation authority, `work_id`/provider IDs as the primary runtime keys, citekeys demoted to aliases, and `references.bib` generated *from* SQLite — is one of the most incrementally hard-won properties in the whole arc. It was assembled across five releases, and each of its three components (record location, primary key, and bibliography-as-projection) has a distinct entry point.

- *Pre-alpha.1 Origins / Old Skeleton.* The catalog began as **Zotero (with Better BibTeX) "as the bibliographic backbone"** — one of the three tool bets the researcher's notes admit were "defaults adopted without evaluating fit." In this era the *bibliography lived with Zotero/BBT* and the **citekey was the natural key**, because the citekey is a Better BibTeX artifact. This is codified in the early Zotero-authority ADRs (ADR-05/06), which Part IV arc (h) records ADR-124 later "absorbed."
- *alpha.1 baseline.* The catalog was a markdown category folder: `catalog/papers · people · organizations · venues · datasets · repositories`, rendered through Obsidian Bases. The `paper` type lived at `catalog/papers/` and was **keyed on `citekey`** (the baseline table lists its key fields as "`citekey`, `title`"). Identity at capture time was resolved from a local `.memoria/memoria.bib`. So at alpha.1 the record was markdown and citekey-keyed — the same *shape* B1 returns to.
- *alpha.11 — the citekey demotion.* This is the pivotal step. `paper` + `source` merged into one `source` type at `catalog/sources/<source_id>/{source.md, content.md, raw/}`, and the design made **`source_id` (an immutable path) the key, explicitly demoting the citekey to a generated alias**: "the catalog path is an **immutable `source_id`**, not the citekey — citekeys are metadata-derived and get corrected, so they are a **generated display/export alias**, never the path (… fixture-verified across a citekey correction)." At the same time alpha.11 established `references.bib` / CSL-JSON as **generated tracked projections**: "**`references.bib` / CSL-JSON are generated tracked projections** … Memoria does not hand-maintain a bibliography," produced by `regenerate-references-bib`. So the "bibliography is a projection, not the record" invariant is an alpha.11 property.
- *alpha.12 — the record moves out of markdown into SQLite.* The catalog "is now SQLite-authoritative" via `catalog_sources(source_id PK, doi UNIQUE, …, citekey, csl_json, …)`. The keep-test judged "the *relational catalog graph* as working state that fails the survival test," so it moved to SQLite "while the source *concept file* survives as the projection." `references.bib` generation "flips to SQLite-first with a file fallback." From alpha.12 onward the markdown `source.md` is a *projection of the DB row*, not the authority.
- *alpha.13 — enrichment owns the record.* `enrich-source` becomes the promotion gate; a deterministic per-field authority merge writes canonical CSL into SQLite; `references.bib` "renders from checked SQLite catalog rows."
- *alpha.14 — the authority is formalized and `work_id` becomes primary.* The design removed "**no markdown Work/Author/Source/Institution Concepts at all**"; "Checked SQLite catalog rows plus blob content are now the sole source authority (ADR-124)," and the gate required "Lookup, digest, references, attention, rollback, and recovery work by **`work_id`** without a legacy markdown Work path." Capture went SQLite-only and stopped writing any Work markdown; `references.bib` "render[s] from checked SQLite rows only."
- *alpha.15 — the split-authority rule crystallizes.* The four-type cut made `work` "a source's subjective digest + tags, 1:1 with the catalog work (`work_id`); **the objective record stays in SQLite**," under "the split-authority rule — files own meaning, the DB owns records/verdicts (ADR-124/125)." `folders.yaml` "cut `catalog`/`capabilities` folder-homes"; "catalog sources/entities are now SQLite records (not markdown Concepts)." There is no `record.md` anywhere in the A15 end-state.

So the A15 side is *not* a late arrival: citekey-as-alias dates to alpha.11, catalog-in-SQLite to alpha.12, `work_id`-primary and "SQLite is the sole authority" to alpha.14 (ADR-124), and the files-own-meaning/DB-owns-records split to alpha.15.

**Prior states of the same element.** The bibliographic record has held three prior forms: (1) Zotero/BBT + local `.bib`, citekey-keyed (Origins → alpha.1); (2) a markdown `paper`/`source` **Concept**, keyed on `source_id`-path with citekey demoted to alias (alpha.11); (3) a **SQLite `catalog_sources` row** keyed on `source_id`/`work_id`, citekey a mere column (alpha.12 → alpha.15). A markdown file that *is* the catalog authority last existed at alpha.11 (`source.md`) before being demoted to a projection at alpha.12 and deleted outright at alpha.14/15.

**How B1's direction maps onto the history.** B1 splits into two distinct reversals plus one continuity:
- *`record.md` full CSL-JSON as the fidelity/re-import authority* returns catalog authority **to markdown** — undoing the alpha.12 move into SQLite and reoccupying the alpha.11 arrangement where the markdown source Concept carried `csl_json` and `references.bib` was its projection. This is precisely the "catalog markdown as SSOT" shape that ADR-122 (alpha.13) explicitly rejected and that ADR-49's "catalog-frontmatter-as-SSOT" was superseded to escape.
- *citekey-primary, "pinned at import (inherited from Better BibTeX)"* reaches back **past alpha.11** to the alpha.1 `paper` type keyed on `citekey`, and its BBT provenance reaches all the way to the pre-alpha.1 Zotero+BBT backbone. It directly reverses the alpha.11 decision that "citekeys … get corrected, so they are a **generated display/export alias, never the path**" and the alpha.14 `work_id`-primary rule (ADR-124).
- *`bibliography.bib` generated from stored CSL-JSON* is the one **continuity**: bibliography-as-generated-projection has been the invariant since alpha.11 ("Memoria does not hand-maintain a bibliography"). B1 keeps that trajectory; only the *source* the projection reads from changes (markdown CSL-JSON in B1 vs. SQLite rows from alpha.12 on).

**Recorded context.** The history pins *why* each A15 property was adopted and each earlier one abandoned. alpha.11 §6.1 records the citekey demotion rationale ("citekeys are metadata-derived and get corrected … a **generated display/export alias**, never the path"). The alpha.12 keep-test records why the record left markdown: the relational catalog graph "fails the survival test," so it "is free to be SQLite" while the concept file "survives as the projection." ADR-122 (alpha.13) records the abandonment of markdown-as-catalog-SSOT — "catalog markdown as SSOT (couples worker state to Obsidian-shaped files)" was a rejected alternative, and ADR-49's frontmatter-SSOT was declared "now too strong." ADR-124 (alpha.14) is the decision that made "the standalone catalog its own citation authority" with `work_id` and no legacy markdown path.

### Gap 7b — Zotero's role: optional importer (A15) vs. day-1 import + Zotero-first reading with annotation sync-back + "capture in Zotero" acquisition (B1)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** "Memoria does not require a live Zotero library" is, like Gap 7a, an end-state reached by *progressive demotion* rather than a single decision.

- *Pre-alpha.1 Origins.* Zotero was one of the three founding tool bets — "**Zotero (with Better BibTeX)** as the bibliographic backbone" — and the researcher's notes explicitly flag it as "adopted without evaluating whether it was the best fit." At this stage Zotero was structurally central.
- *alpha.1.* Zotero remained embedded but already read-only and peripheral to reading: the Librarian profile got a **`pyzotero` (read-only Zotero)** MCP server; the full-text acquisition chain listed "**local Zotero PDF**" as one late fallback ("`S2ORC → CORE → PMC → arXiv → Unpaywall → local Zotero PDF → pymupdf4llm → OCR`"); and the deployment story ran "Zotero on localhost, $0 infra," with the `obsidian-sync` topology carrying "`.bib`-only Zotero." Reading itself happened in Obsidian/Memoria, not in Zotero.
- *alpha.11 — demotion to one capture adapter, and the annotation-ownership decision.* Zotero import became one member of "a **small fixed set**" of capture adapters (`capture-pdf/url/bibtex/zotero-source`), "**not a translator engine** … lean on the Zotero connector + **Zotero Local API** only for item/source import." Crucially, alpha.11 made an explicit, load-bearing decision that Memoria owns its own reading/annotation layer: "**Zotero annotations are not an alpha.11 import path; Memoria-owned PDF capture stores canonical quote/span/hash selectors itself.**"
- *alpha.13.* Provider enrichment (Crossref/OpenAlex/Unpaywall) displaced reliance on Zotero-supplied metadata; the docs were updated so enrichment "calls Crossref, OpenAlex, and Unpaywall."
- *alpha.14 — the dedicated Zotero surface is removed.* The Zotero-specific import path — "CLI `--format zotero-export`, worker operation, runtime helper, capability file `capture-zotero-source.md`" — "was removed (#1123)," though a Zotero export file "still stages as an unchecked Work via **generic** [BibTeX/CSL] import." ADR-124 states plainly: "Zotero and Better BibTeX are **optional user tools** … **Memoria does not require a live Zotero library**," and the gate made "`work import --format zotero-export` fail as an unsupported format." OA full-text acquisition became a baseline provider pipeline (Unpaywall → OpenAlex → Crossref OA locators), not a Zotero handoff.
- *alpha.15.* Zotero "survives only 'as importers, not authority'" (Part IV arc (h)); the standalone cut dropped Obsidian/Hermes/MCP/Zotero as architectural dependencies, the product required to "remain fully operable if the editor is only `vim`, `nano`, or a file browser."

So the A15 "optional" status was built up over alpha.11 → alpha.14: backbone (Origins) → one read-only capture adapter with Memoria-owned annotations (alpha.11) → dedicated import surface deleted, ADR-124 declares it non-required (alpha.14).

**Prior states of the same element.** Zotero has occupied: (1) *bibliographic backbone* (Origins); (2) *read-only metadata/PDF fallback* — `pyzotero`, last-resort local PDF in the full-text chain (alpha.1); (3) *one of a fixed set of capture adapters via the Zotero Local API, with annotations explicitly excluded* (alpha.11); (4) *a generic BibTeX/CSL file source only, dedicated surface removed* (alpha.14/15). Reading-*in-Zotero* and annotation-sync-back have **no adopted prior state** — the closest is Zotero PDFs as a fallback download source in the alpha.1 acquisition chain, and alpha.11 affirmatively rejected importing Zotero annotations.

**How B1's direction maps onto the history.** B1 re-centers Zotero along three axes, which land differently against the arc:
- *Zotero import as day-1 / Zotero-first import.* This reverses the alpha.14 removal of the dedicated Zotero import surface and re-elevates Zotero from "optional generic-bib source" back toward its alpha.11 "Zotero Local API capture adapter" role and, further, its pre-alpha.1 backbone role. It reoccupies an earlier era rather than continuing the alpha trajectory (which was monotonically *away* from Zotero centrality).
- *Zotero-first reading, "annotations sync back."* This has **no precedent in the alpha line as an adopted design**, and it specifically reverses the alpha.11 decision that "Zotero annotations are **not** an alpha.11 import path; **Memoria-owned PDF capture stores canonical quote/span/hash selectors itself**." Reading/annotation is itself flagged new-in-B1 (gap-analysis §16); making the *Zotero PDF reader the primary read* is a beta.1 introduction with no alpha antecedent beyond Zotero PDFs as a mere fallback download.
- *PDF acquisition via a "capture in Zotero" handoff.* This reverses the alpha.13/14 baseline in which full-text acquisition ran through Memoria's own ordered OA-locator pipeline (Unpaywall → OpenAlex → Crossref), handing that responsibility back to Zotero.

**Recorded context.** The history records both the founding centrality and the reasons for the demotion. Origins: Zotero was "adopted without evaluating whether it was the best fit." alpha.11 §9/§13 record the adapter-only framing and the annotation-ownership decision quoted above. ADR-124 (alpha.14) records the optional status: "Zotero and Better BibTeX are optional user tools … Memoria does not require a live Zotero library." Part IV arc (h) records *why* it was demoted: "**Zotero-as-authority conflicted with the standalone direction** — ADR-124 made the catalog its own citation authority **so the product no longer *requires* an external reference manager** (Zotero survives only 'as importers, not authority')."

## 8. Discovery / gap analysis

The two beta.1 gaps in this subsystem sit on opposite sides of the same alpha-era read model. One (gap analysis) removes the **argument-graph half** of a two-part gap engine while keeping the coverage half; the other (discovery) removes the **topic/concept-graph half** of the discovery read model while keeping the citation-graph half. Traced through the history, both are severings of trajectories that were built up incrementally from alpha.5 and alpha.13–14 and were still intact at alpha.15.

### Gap: A15 gap analysis is argument/knowledge-graph based (note-mode obligations + project saturation floor + OpenAlex Topics in the read model); B1 gap analysis is retrieval-coverage-based only

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The alpha.15 gap engine is not a single-version feature; it is the accreted end-state of two lineages that were joined late.

The *argument-graph* lineage begins pre-alpha.1. The `relations:`/typed-links model (ADR-08) was split by **ADR-52** into "*two typed layers — a citation/identity graph over entities and an argument graph over notes,*" which is the first appearance of an argument graph as a distinct object (it also enabled the contradictions dashboard ADR-09 and claim supersession ADR-10). This layer became a *gate* at **alpha.5** — "The Project gate (the fourth navigation gate becomes real)" (dated 2026-06-15). alpha.5 added the `project` and `thesis` types and shipped `operations/processing/project/structural_impact.py` (~800 lines, "the whole gate spine"), which reads "the thesis-rooted `supports`/`contradicts` subgraph" and computes impact/maturity/**saturation**. This is the origin of the **saturation floor** the beta.1 gap analysis lacks: saturation gated in code on `maturity == mature`, `high_open_gaps == 0`, and a structural refutation floor, where maturity itself required "`>= 5` addressed relations including `>= 1 supports` AND `>= 1 contradicts`" — the Popperian `>=1 contradicts` requirement ("a thesis corroborated only by accumulated `supports` 'looks complete but is intellectually untested'"). alpha.5 also introduced the `gap` type's `gap_type` enum `[additive, conflict, fragility, structural, unstated-warrant, refutation]`, ruled by **ADR-79** (argument graph and warrant). ADR-79 is the recorded home of the saturation floor; alpha.15's gap section states plainly that its saturation block "**absorbs ADR-79's saturation floor**."

At **alpha.11** (clean-slate reset, 2026-06-27) gap analysis was re-derived and promoted: "**Gap analysis is first-class — the thread that makes Memoria *directed*.**" It shipped as `analyze-gaps` plus `analyze-project-argument`, `surface-tensions`, `render-project-argument-canvas`, and `compare-and-contrast`, reading "over ***both* graphs and their correspondence** … the **mismatch** — the primary signal" (thin/thin, dense/dense, sources-dense/notes-thin, notes-dense/sources-thin). Crucially `analyze-gaps` "reads **checked current Concepts** only," installing the read-barrier on the gap signal that alpha.15 carries forward ("gap analysis reads checked inputs only").

At **alpha.14** (2026-06-30) the two halves were explicitly joined: `memoria project gaps <project_path>` became project-scoped and "feeds checked project argument/Toulmin health into the shared `analyze-gaps` runtime path." The design records the argument-health dimension in so many words: "*With a thesis, gap analysis also reads argument health from the project's Toulmin graph: a claim that is unsupported, uncountered, or fragile … is a gap even where work/note coverage looks dense*" — "the directed/writing half of the loop, not just coverage." alpha.14 simultaneously wired the *coverage/topic* half: it "counts checked SQLite catalog source terms — from `csl_json.memoria.tags`, `topics`, `research_area`," "counts checked topic/keyword graph-edge terms as source evidence," and flags missing-full-text. (The OpenAlex-Topics-in-the-read-model element is older still: `research_area` was "*seeded mechanically from OpenAlex topics* by `classify.py`" as far back as the alpha.1 vocabulary design.)

At **alpha.15** (2026-07-01/02) the engine reached its shipped form: "**Native gap analysis**: checked-only inputs, canonical gap kinds (`full-text-missing`, `citation-neighborhood`, mapped `argument-*`), fixed `{0,1,2}` ordinal severity … project `saturation` block … Advisory, never gating" (ADR-129, PR-G #1174). Saturation was reduced to "a claim with ≥1 addressed support AND ≥1 counterpoint" — the same `supports`+`contradicts` shape ADR-79/alpha.5 set. The *note-mode obligation* mechanism the beta.1 analysis lacks is the alpha.15 arrival: **ADR-126**'s four-type collapse made `note.mode: claim|question` "declared intent creating obligations the gap engine feeds on — a claim without evidence is an `under-warranted` gap; an open question is a compose-flow lead," because "field-derived typing could not tell a plain thought from a claim missing its evidence." So the alpha.15 side of this gap is a **hard-won, multi-version property**: ADR-52 (pre-alpha.1) → ADR-77/78/79 saturation + argument graph (alpha.5) → gap-analysis-first-class over both graphs (alpha.11) → project-scoped Toulmin argument-health merged into `analyze-gaps` + topic/facet coverage (alpha.14) → native engine with saturation block + note-mode obligations + `argument-*` kinds (alpha.15).

**Prior states of the same element.** The "gap" object itself changed form across the arc. In the Old Skeleton and alpha.1 it was a *durable Inbox `gap` card* (ADR-51), and alpha.5 enriched it with the `gap_type` taxonomy on `gap.yaml` plus derived cache fields (`impact`, `on_path`, `saturation_state`, `graph_maturity`). At **alpha.11** the `gap` type was **retired** — it is named among the 19 removed types ("Queue, flags, **gaps**, work cards, dashboards, and indexes are **projections or worker state, not durable Concepts**") — so a gap became analysis output rather than a stored Concept. Saturation likewise changed form: full three-condition structural computation with a PI `refutation_sufficiency` stamp (alpha.5) → `analyze-project-argument` (alpha.11) → a two-condition `saturation` block (alpha.15). The claim/question distinction also has prior states: `claim` was a first-class type from the Old Skeleton (~15 types) through alpha.10, merged into `note` as an optional claim block at alpha.11, and became `note.mode: claim|question` at alpha.15.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1's gap analysis is "**retrieval-coverage** gaps itemized by cause (unsearchable/partial/superseded) + content gaps (question facets with corpus df 0), with a printed denominator." Factually, this **continues the coverage lineage and severs the argument-graph lineage**. The coverage half has clear alpha precedent — alpha.11's dense/thin correspondence mismatch and alpha.14's "counts checked topic/keyword … terms" / missing-full-text flags are the direct ancestors of beta.1's unsearchable/partial/superseded + df-0-facet accounting. But the argument-graph/saturation/thesis-health half — built at alpha.5 (ADR-77/79), preserved through alpha.11 (`analyze-project-argument`), merged into `analyze-gaps` at alpha.14, and shipped as the `saturation` block + `argument-*` kinds at alpha.15 — has **no counterpart in beta.1**. The mechanism that fed it is also gone at the source: beta.1 drops `note.mode: claim|question` (§3), so the ADR-126 obligation feed ("a claim without evidence is an `under-warranted` gap; an open question is a compose-flow lead") has no input, and beta.1's `project` is "a `project/` folder of files … no argument-graph project concept" (§3), so ADR-79's saturation floor has nothing to compute over. So beta.1 does not return to a specific earlier *era* wholesale (no alpha state was coverage-only — coverage and argument health arrived together in alpha.14); rather it keeps the newer coverage machinery and removes the older argument-graph machinery that had been continuous since alpha.5. The **printed denominator** is the one element with no explicit alpha precedent in this lineage (alpha.15 had ordinal `{0,1,2}` severity and `score 0..8` bands, but not a stated coverage denominator).

**Recorded context.** The design history records *why* the saturation floor was adopted: alpha.5 exists to "know when to stop (saturation), **inverting the treadmill where a dashboard's implicit purpose is to keep generating work**," and ADR-77 fixes the maturity threshold as "the gate's trust switch … **the parameter the gate's safety rests on, not a knob to tune after launch**" ("Lowering this threshold is a safety change, not a preference"). The note-mode obligation was adopted at alpha.15 on ADR-126's reasoning that "field-derived typing could not tell a plain thought from a claim missing its evidence." The read-barrier-on-gaps rule ("gap analysis reads checked inputs only") was adopted at alpha.11 and reaffirmed at alpha.15 "so unverified content can't drive the compose-flow phase signal."

### Gap: A15 discovery is informed by the imported topic/concept/keyword/citation graph; B1 discovery is bounded citation-graph-only (snowball ≤2 + scheduled monitor)

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** Discovery is one of the oldest *reserved* capabilities and one of the latest *shipped* ones. Pre-alpha.1, **ADR-61** defined a "nightly proactive discovery loop" (Hermes cron reads `research-focus.md`, picks top-N priorities, runs `find` per priority), with **ADR-37** reserving a separate Retriever/Scout profile and **ADR-66** reserving batch triage / consensus pre-filter / tournament ranking — all `deferred`, "nothing runs in alpha.1." The only live analogue was structural: the knowledge cycle's map/verify tasks "raise Inbox `gap` cards that re-trigger *catalog* — '*the output end of the cycle feeds the intake end*.'" Discovery stayed deferred through alpha.2 ("The **nightly discovery loop** (ADR-61) is **deferred**") and was re-filed but not shipped at **alpha.8**, which "unbundled" the omnibus into individual ADRs including "ADR-92 discovery relevance scoring … ADR-95 nightly proactive discovery loop," explicitly noting "**No discovery *behavior* ships this checkpoint**."

The *topic/concept/keyword-graph* read model that alpha.15 discovery draws on is an **alpha.13/14 arrival**. alpha.13 built the multi-source enrichment subsystem (OpenAlex/S2/PubMed/Unpaywall/Crossref/arXiv, ADR-123/124); **alpha.14 "imported the citation graph itself": "OpenAlex authorship/primary-topic/keyword edges, Crossref relations and full-text links … and OpenAlex concepts/MeSH/SDG as graph topics"** into `work_graph_edges`. On top of that read model, alpha.14 made discovery *behavior* real for the first time: "SQLite-detected source/topic gaps carry checked source IDs into **discovery candidate generation**, so candidate Works are proposed from first-order reference/related graph edges without qmd rediscovery," and "**Crossref `related`/OpenAlex graph edges feed discovery**." alpha.14 thus drew discovery candidates from *both* citation/reference edges *and* OpenAlex topic/related edges. **alpha.15** added "**Discovery relevance scoring** (ADR-92 accepted): `analyze-gaps` annotates provider-discovered candidates with deterministic `steering.md` relevance, sorts ranked ahead of a separate exploration channel," with "model embedding similarity remains out until candidate abstracts/full text are reliably present," and kept "discovery candidates as unchecked attention rows (never promoted Works)." So alpha.15's discovery read model = the full alpha.13/14 imported topic/concept/keyword/mesh/SDG/authorship + citation/reference graph, ranked by deterministic steering relevance (ADR-92).

**Prior states of the same element.** Discovery-as-shipped-behavior did not exist before alpha.14 — it was a continuously-deferred design reservation (ADR-61 → ADR-92/95) from the Old Skeleton through alpha.13, with only the operator-triggered `find` and the gap-card re-trigger as live analogues. The *scheduling* substrate also changed under it: ADR-61's discovery loop was a **Hermes nightly cron**; by alpha.14/15 "**There is no internal daemon. A scheduler invokes normal CLI commands with `--schedule-id`**" — so any scheduled discovery runs as an ordinary CLI invocation, and the "dead Hermes cron installer code [was] removed."

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 discovery is "**bounded citation-graph only** (snowball depth ≤2 + scheduled citation monitor), identifiers-only egress; output never enters gap/coverage denominators." This **continues the citation-graph trajectory and severs the topic/concept-graph trajectory**. The citation-graph half is the direct descendant of alpha.14's "candidate Works … proposed from first-order reference/related graph edges" and alpha.15's `citation-neighborhood` gap kind + relevance scoring; beta.1's snowball depth ≤2 is a specific parameterization of that same first-order-neighborhood idea (extended one hop), and the "scheduled citation monitor" is the surviving form of the ADR-61/ADR-95 nightly-loop reservation, now run under beta.1's scheduler-invokes-the-app model rather than a Hermes cron. The topic/concept/keyword-graph-driven half is gone at its source: per §9, beta.1 "imports a much narrower graph (refs + capped cited-by only) — **no OpenAlex topic/concept/keyword/mesh/SDG/authorship edges, no Crossref relation edges**," which is precisely the alpha.13/14 read model that alpha.14/15 discovery fed on. So beta.1's citation-graph-only discovery is the paired consequence of the §9 ingest-graph narrowing. Two disciplines are **continued**, not introduced: "output never enters gap/coverage denominators" continues alpha.14's "**never silently imports a discovered Work as checked knowledge**" and alpha.15's "unchecked attention rows (never promoted Works)"; and "identifiers-only egress" continues alpha.14's provider-allowlist rule. beta.1's direction here therefore returns nothing to an earlier era — it prunes the alpha.13/14 topic-graph addition while retaining the citation-graph discovery and the never-auto-promote guarantee that alpha.14/15 had established.

**Recorded context.** The bounded-but-non-promoting posture is recorded at alpha.14: "*Automated discovery is baseline but bounded … Discovery writes unchecked attention/worklist rows. It never silently imports a discovered Work as checked knowledge and never bypasses the provider allowlist*" (`design.md` §10). The deferral of ML-based (embedding-similarity) discovery ranking in favor of the deterministic path is recorded at alpha.15 under ADR-92: "the cheap deterministic path clears the bar; ML deferred until data exists" ("model embedding similarity remains out until candidate abstracts/full text are reliably present"). The routing of graph edges straight to candidates (rather than re-searching) was adopted at alpha.14 to "avoid redundant qmd search for edges the graph already knows."

## 9. Ingest / enrichment graph

### Gap: B1 imports a much narrower enrichment graph (outgoing refs + capped cited-by only) vs A15's first-order citation/reference/topic graph

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The A15 graph is a late-arrival, two-step build layered onto much older foundations — it was not present in the alpha line until alpha.14, and what it replaced reaches back to the pre-alpha bibliographic backbone.

- *Pre-alpha / Old Skeleton origins.* Early catalog metadata "rode **Zotero + Better BibTeX** as the bibliographic backbone (ADR-05/06)" (design history arc (h), line 2314). At that stage there was no Memoria-owned graph at all; relationships were whatever a reference manager exported.

- *alpha.1 baseline (Part II).* The alpha.1 cut already carried the two edge kinds beta.1 keeps. Catalog entities carried "**given `relationships`** (`cited_by`, `authored_by`, `published_in`)" (line 195), and the Tier-1 resolve/merge produced "references = **union deduped by DOI**" (line 518). So *reference edges* and a *cited_by relationship* are hard-won alpha.1-era properties, built from the multi-source merge grounded by the 867-paper spike ("reference counts of 151/129/146 for the same paper across S2/OpenAlex/Crossref … *why* the merge is per-field-best-source," line 521). OpenAlex topics also fed the catalog this early — `research_area` was "*seeded mechanically from OpenAlex topics* by `classify.py`" (line 218) — but as a *frontmatter facet*, not as graph edges, and MeSH/ACM CCS were deliberately "kept out of the queryable facet, living in each note's `_enrichment` namespace" (line 218).

- *alpha.13 — the enrichment subsystem lands.* alpha.13 ("DOI catalog enrichment: capture stages, enrichment promotes," 2026-06-30, line 1838) built the "dedicated multi-source enrichment subsystem" on the alpha.12 SQLite catalog: a capture→enrich split fetching Crossref/OpenAlex/Unpaywall, "merges canonical CSL with per-field provenance," gated by a retraction check (lines 1840, 1856, 1858). Its graph output was still narrow: identity via `external_ids` with "`authored_by`/entity edges carry DOI/OpenAlex/ORCID/ROR/ISSN namespaces" (line 1872). alpha.13 explicitly *deferred* the wider graph — `docs/reference/ingest.md`'s deferral list still named "Semantic Scholar, PubMed, arXiv, **graph enrichment**, embeddings" as not-yet-built (line 1864).

- *alpha.14 — the graph itself is imported.* This is where the A15 side of the gap was actually created. The design history flags Pipeline/Ingest as "the change-heaviest area" (line 1970) and records that "First-order citation/reference/topic graph import was expanded **far beyond alpha.13**. During DOI enrichment Memoria now imports: reference edges from portable BibTeX/CSL metadata into `work_graph_edges` (#1139); OpenAlex `topics` + `primary_topic` (#1142), `concepts` as `topic` edges (#1149), `keywords` as `keyword` edges (#1143), `mesh` and `sustainable_development_goals` as topic edges (#1155), and `authorships` + nested institutions + location Source metadata as graph edges (#1154); plus Crossref `relation` rows as `related` edges (#1150)" (line 1978). Depth was bounded by design — "'Exhaustive or provider-specific graph expansion' is a non-goal (§13)" — but first-order breadth across all those providers was the whole point. Arc (h) summarizes: "**Alpha.14 imported the citation graph itself**: OpenAlex authorship/primary-topic/keyword edges, Crossref relations and full-text links, OpenAlex OA full-text URLs, and OpenAlex concepts/MeSH/SDG as graph topics" (line 2314).

- *alpha.15.* alpha.15 did not widen the graph further; it "hardened the merge (PR-F, #1173: 'static field-authority table … deterministic blob replay … `provider_coverage = full|partial|degraded`') and added record-linkage attention on exact/blocked duplicate IDs (PR-V…PR-Z, ADR-94) plus MASSW-aligned work aspects (PR-AB, ADR-99)" (line 2314). So the A15 breadth is precisely the alpha.14 import, carried forward and hardened.

**Prior states of the same element.** The two edge kinds beta.1 keeps are the *oldest* form of the element: `cited_by` was a "given relationship" and references a "union deduped by DOI" at the alpha.1 baseline (lines 195, 518). The topic/concept/keyword/mesh/SDG/authorship/relation edges never existed as edges before alpha.14 — OpenAlex topics existed only as a mechanically-seeded frontmatter facet (`research_area`, line 218) and MeSH lived in a non-queryable `_enrichment` namespace (line 218). The `work_graph_edges` table itself was formalized in the alpha.15 loaded DDL (`runtime/schema_v1.sql`, line 2098); beta.1's "`edges` table" is a renamed/reshaped successor.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 reverses the alpha.14 expansion and returns the graph to roughly its **alpha.1-baseline shape**: reference edges plus a cited-by relationship, which are exactly the "references = union deduped by DOI" and "`cited_by`" that shipped at alpha.1 (lines 518, 195). Concretely:
- Dropping OpenAlex topic/concept/keyword/mesh/SDG edges and Crossref `relation` edges undoes the entire alpha.14 import block (#1142/#1149/#1143/#1155/#1150, line 1978) with no successor — a return to the *pre-alpha.14* state.
- Dropping authorship edges makes beta.1 *narrower than alpha.13*, which already carried `authored_by`/entity edges via `external_ids` (line 1872).
- The **capped cited-by** edge is the one element with no alpha precedent: the alpha line carried `cited_by` uncapped as a relationship (alpha.1) and imported first-order edges without a count cap (alpha.14 bounded *depth* to first-order but not *fan-out*); beta.1's cap on cited-by fan-out is a new bound introduced in beta.1.
- On the merge policy: alpha.1 merged "per-field best-source-wins with provenance" (line 518) and alpha.13 formalized a "**field-authority merge**" (Crossref authoritative for title/authors/issued, Unpaywall for OA, OpenAlex for IDs, line 1856), hardened in alpha.15 to a "static field-authority table" (line 2314). beta.1's "OpenAlex fills empty metadata fields" is a **fill-gaps** rule — narrower than, and distinct from, every alpha merge policy, which always chose a *winner* per field rather than only filling blanks. beta.1's "Crossref used for retraction check + title fallback" *does* map cleanly onto alpha.13's retraction gate (`enrichment._is_retracted`, "retracted/contested Crossref record → `blocked_status`," line 1858) and its Crossref/OpenAlex `_title_conflict` detection (line 1858) — those two Crossref roles are continuations of the alpha.13 design.

**Recorded context.** The design history records *why* the alpha.14 breadth was built: enrichment "'Imports the first-order citation/reference/topic graph available from required providers …'" because "the graph feeds retrieval query expansion and gap analysis (§10 'First-order citation/reference graph, OpenAlex Topics, controlled facets … part of the read model')" (line 1978). The alpha.14 arc explicitly wired those edges into downstream consumers — gap analysis "counts checked topic/keyword graph-edge terms as source evidence (#1138, #1143, #1149, #1155)" (line 2004) and discovery routes "first-order reference/related graph edges" and "Crossref `related`/OpenAlex graph edges" straight into candidate generation (line 2006). Historically, then, the topic/concept/authorship edges beta.1 drops were adopted in alpha.14 specifically as read-model and discovery inputs; the record shows when/why they were added, not any recorded decision to remove them.

### Gap: B1 extraction is pdfium-only with no OCR, vs A15's ported ingest extractor chain (pymupdf4llm + OCR)

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The A15 extractor is the terminal state of a chain present since the alpha.1 baseline, with OCR at its tail throughout.

- *Pre-alpha / Old Skeleton origins.* The pre-existing skeleton relied on an "`obsidian-paper-note` skill … 'fully LLM-orchestrated — costly, non-reproducible, and fragile'" whose PDF dependency `ocr-and-documents` "literally 'currently fails to install'" (ADR-30 Context, line 521). This is the recorded reason the deterministic ingest spine (ADR-30) was created — the failing OCR dependency was a named fragility (Risk 3 in the readiness review, line 521).

- *alpha.1 baseline.* The Tier-1 pipeline shipped a full-text chain "`S2ORC → CORE → PMC → arXiv → Unpaywall → local Zotero PDF → pymupdf4llm → OCR`, pre-extracted preferred" (line 518). Two properties beta.1 touches are alpha.1-era: **pre-extracted/OA text is preferred over local parsing**, and **OCR is the last-resort tail** after `pymupdf4llm`.

- *alpha.5.* The chain was extended: "New OA extraction sources in `operations/processing/ingest/extract.py` — Unpaywall (DOI → best OA PDF → `pymupdf4llm`) … PDF parsing runs in a resource-limited (`RLIMIT_CPU`/`RLIMIT_AS`) subprocess" enforcing "ADR-30's deterministic-ingest safety (a malformed/hostile PDF cannot exhaust the host)" (line 1009). So `pymupdf4llm` as the local parser and the rlimit subprocess sandbox are alpha.5-era hardening.

- *alpha.8.* "**ADR-108 LiteParse** proposed (deferred): evaluates replacing the `pymupdf4llm` + OCR local-parse fallback in `extract.py` with LiteParse (LlamaIndex, Rust, Apache-2.0, local-only, no LLM, **built-in Tesseract OCR**, PDF+DOCX/XLSX/PPTX); keeps the subprocess `rlimit` sandbox and **coherence checks**" (line 1292). This pins several facts: the local parser through alpha.8 was `pymupdf4llm`; OCR was part of that fallback; **coherence checks already existed** by alpha.8; and the recorded motivation to change was that "PyMuPDF is AGPL-3.0 (the strictest license obligation in the dependency set) and a C parse surface with a CVE history (the reason ADR-30's sandbox exists)." LiteParse was "**Deferred, not adopted**" because "the local-parse tier is a last-resort fallback (most full text arrives pre-extracted), so the upside is bounded and 'does not justify betting the deterministic spine on a library with no track record'" (line 1292).

- *alpha.14.* Full text became "**required before a checked digest**": "metadata-only/abstract-only digest attempts fail in the shared runtime path, create a stable full-text attention projection" (line 1974), and OA acquisition became "an ordered candidate retry loop" across Unpaywall/OpenAlex/Crossref locations (line 1976). This is the lineage of beta.1's `abstract_only` distrust flag and its `extract_failed:needs_ocr` attention path — the "no checked digest from abstract-only or metadata-only input" gate (§14) is alpha.14's.

**Prior states of the same element.** The local extractor was `pymupdf4llm` continuously from alpha.1 (line 518) through alpha.8 (line 1292); OCR sat at the tail of the chain across the same span. The only recorded *proposed* change was ADR-108's swap to a Rust/Tesseract engine (alpha.8), which was never adopted in the alpha line. The **coherence check** is a pre-alpha.8 property (ADR-108 lists it among things LiteParse "keeps," line 1292), and the rlimit subprocess sandbox dates to alpha.5 (line 1009).

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.**
- **pdfium as the extractor has no precedent anywhere in the alpha line.** The alpha implementation was `pymupdf4llm` throughout (alpha.1–alpha.8+), and the one considered alternative was *LiteParse*, not pdfium (line 1292). pdfium is a beta.1 arrival with no alpha ancestor.
- **However, beta.1's pdfium choice continues the *motivation* recorded in alpha.8's ADR-108.** ADR-108's driver was escaping the AGPL-3.0 PyMuPDF obligation and its C-parse CVE surface (line 1292); moving off `pymupdf4llm` is exactly the trajectory ADR-108 opened. beta.1 realizes that trajectory with a different library (pdfium) than the one alpha.8 evaluated (LiteParse).
- **Dropping OCR reverses a property present since the alpha.1 baseline.** OCR was the terminal fallback in the alpha.1 chain (line 518) and remained in the `pymupdf4llm + OCR` fallback through alpha.8 (line 1292). Notably, the deferred LiteParse proposal would have *retained* OCR (via built-in Tesseract, line 1292). beta.1's explicit "no OCR" (`extract_failed:needs_ocr`) therefore removes a capability that had been continuously present in the alpha line and that even the alpha.8 replacement candidate preserved — a return, on this one axis, toward the pre-alpha state where the OCR dependency did not work (`ocr-and-documents` "currently fails to install," line 521), though beta.1 reaches it deliberately (fail-closed with an attention flag) rather than by broken dependency.
- **beta.1's text-coherence gate continues an existing alpha property**, not a new one: coherence checks were already in `extract.py` by alpha.8 (ADR-108 "keeps the … coherence checks," line 1292), themselves an expression of ADR-30's deterministic-ingest safety posture.
- **The distrust flags (`truncated`/`abstract_only`) map onto alpha.14.** The `abstract_only` state is the alpha.14 "no checked digest from abstract-only or metadata-only input" gate (line 1974); beta.1 continues that policy as a deterministic flag.

**Recorded context.** The history records *why* the local-parse tier was always treated as low-stakes: it "is a last-resort fallback (most full text arrives pre-extracted), so the upside is bounded" (ADR-108, line 1292) — pre-extracted/OA text was "preferred" from alpha.1 onward (line 518) and made a hard precondition in alpha.14 (lines 1974–1976). The record also states why the extractor runs sandboxed at all — the AGPL/C-CVE parse surface and ADR-30's rule that "a malformed/hostile PDF cannot exhaust the host" (lines 1009, 1292). What the design history does **not** record is any alpha-line decision to select pdfium or to remove OCR; both are introduced at beta.1 with no alpha precedent for the library choice and against a continuous alpha-line presence of OCR.

## 10. Surfaces / navigation / control

### Gap: B1 builds an Obsidian pane/surface UI + a generative-UI view-spec renderer; A15 was CLI-first with `status --json`

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The alpha.15 "CLI-first, no panes" position is the *terminal* state of the longest single arc in the design history, and it arrived late — only two versions before beta.1. Part IV names it directly: "By **alpha.14/15 the entire Obsidian UI was removed from the required product**" (arc (i)). To see how hard-won that removal was, the arc has to be read against everything it undid:

- **Pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton":** surfaces were baked into the founding three-layer model — "the **vault (Obsidian folders) = durable store**" — and elaborated into the seven-layer architecture where "**L2** [is] the Obsidian workspace" and "**L3** the co-PI (the permanent ACP-pane agent)." The Old Skeleton already shipped a "**10-dashboard suite** whose entry point / cockpit was `00-meta/01-dashboards/daily-health.md`" (§Part II.10). So *surface = Obsidian workspace + panes + dashboards* was an inherited premise, never a first-principles choice.
- **alpha.1 baseline:** the ACP pane (`agent-client` plugin) hosting "**exactly one agent — the co-PI**," "pinned in the right sidebar," plus `home.md` as a "front door (`ADR-13`)" carrying a Status glance, Act button row, and grouped dashboards; **11 dashboards** and 6 Bases views (ADR-49) (§Part II.7, II.8).
- **alpha.2 (2026, the "consolidation checkpoint"):** the two unshared layouts became "**three under one shared contract: Desk / Library / Studio**" with `home.md` "rewritten as a **four-block control panel** (ADR-68)"; command Buttons added; dashboards grew "**eleven → twelve**."
- **alpha.3:** navigation was *modeled* as "**intent-named gates**" (ADR-70) — three gates, Project deferred; system health made "**ambient, not a gate**"; **Commander** adopted so "every Memoria action is reachable directly from the Obsidian UI without the Co-PI" (ADR-72), with the "Co-PI remains additive."
- **alpha.4–alpha.6:** the gate dashboards (`desk.md`/`library.md`/`studio.md`) shipped; **Modal Forms** (ADR-71) added structured capture; the Project gate surface was fitted "**inside the Studio workspace** — *not* a fourth saved workspace"; an `assist.js` verb surface (Find/Search/Ask/Draft/Explore/Patterns) landed.

This is where the surface stack reached its richest, most Obsidian-native form.
- **alpha.8–alpha.11 — steady refinement, not retreat:** gates→spaces rename (ADR-101); the `memoria-inspector` **read-only plugin** (ADR-84); a **left-pane navigation rail** `_nav.md` with a live "Now" count (ADR-114); Inbox reclassified "**from a space to the queue**" and the homepage front-door retired for native session-restore (ADR-115); the **View / Collection / Rail** vocabulary (ADR-116); dashboards consolidated 13→5 (ADR-118); and the pivotal **enqueue-only** rule — "**Obsidian control panel mutates only by enqueueing worker jobs**" (ADR-121, superseding the read-only-inspector rule). Through all of this the surface was still an Obsidian pane UI.

**The alpha.14 cut is where the alpha.15 side actually originates.** alpha.14 is "**The standalone CLI + engine cut**": "`status --json` is the replacement for dashboard glance surfaces … Old board export and metrics aggregation become `status --json`, journal summaries, and generated `knowledge/_views/` reports; **no board daemon survives**" (`design.md` §5). It retired `fleet-health.md` and `skill-state.md`, mass-rewrote docs "to the standalone CLI baseline," and its gate "fail[s]… if current runtime or installer paths still require Hermes profiles, Obsidian, Zotero, MCP." **alpha.15** then formalized the read side: "The engine's **JSON read-API is the sole read path** for every Memoria-built surface and agent," with CLI/MCP/HTTP as "**pure marshalling skins**" over one `engine/api` module (ADR-130), and an explicit Scope Boundary — "**No alpha.15 slice adds … an Obsidian plugin, plugin manifest, command-palette command, Inspector panel**." So the alpha.15 side of this gap is ~2 versions old as a *product posture*, resting on a `status --json` mechanism introduced at alpha.14.

**Prior states of the same elements.** Every element beta.1 reintroduces existed before, under different names:
- beta.1's **5 Obsidian surfaces (Capture / Reading / Ask / Compose / Review)** are the fourth distinct naming of the navigation set: Desk/Library/Studio (ADR-68, alpha.2) → Inbox/Library/Knowledge/Project *gates* (ADR-81, alpha.7) → the same four *spaces* (ADR-101, alpha.8) → beta.1's phase-named five. The beta.1 names are activity/workflow phases rather than the alpha "job" spaces, so they do not map one-to-one onto any prior set.
- beta.1's **read-only inspector pane** is the direct descendant of the alpha.8 `memoria-inspector` "read-only Obsidian Inspector" (ADR-84), which alpha.10 turned into "**the system index**" (ADR-118 §5), and which alpha.11's ADR-121 folded into the enqueue-only control-panel rule.
- beta.1's **2 rich panes (Ask, Review)** continue the ACP/Co-PI pane that ran unbroken from the Old Skeleton L3 through alpha.1's pinned ACP pane, and the alpha.15 review ergonomics ("honesty-card + code-review ergonomics… presentation span-first," "`--apply` one click," ADR-130).
- beta.1's **Bases for meaning-only views** continues the Bases-as-view-layer bet from ADR-49 (alpha.1) through the alpha.7 "Bases is the universal tabular view layer" decision.
- beta.1's **`index.md` + `log.md` projections** trace to ADR-107 (proposed alpha.8), which borrowed OKF's "standardized `index.md` progressive-disclosure manifest" and reserved `index.md`/`log.md` convention.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** It is a **hybrid**, and the two halves anchor to different eras:
1. *The write/read contract is a straight continuation of alpha.15.* beta.1's **`ViewSpecRenderer` over `view-spec.v1`** directly continues alpha.15's "**Declarative verdict-carrying view-specs from a fixed component vocabulary (table/card); clients render known kinds only**" (ADR-130, PR-U #1189). The enqueue-only write rule ("a UI mutates only by enqueueing") is "carried verbatim from ADR-121." Agent-as-client under the read-API is ADR-130's model. None of this is new.
2. *The Obsidian pane/surface housing is a return to the pre-alpha.14 world.* An Obsidian UI with named surfaces and rich panes is exactly what alpha.14/15 removed from the required product. Because Part IV records that the removal was *cheap precisely because the UI never owned truth* — "dropping it cost the engine nothing, and the read/view/action contracts could be re-expressed over CLI/HTTP/MCP" — reintroducing an Obsidian surface layer in beta.1 is the inverse move: it re-adds a surface housing on top of contracts that were deliberately made surface-agnostic. Part IV also anticipated exactly this: any future editor adapter "**require[s] their own ADR instead of inheriting an alpha.15 commitment**" (exec-plan PR-AG).

So: beta.1's *renderer and write path* have a two-version-old alpha.15 precedent (continuation); its *pane/surface UI* returns to the alpha.7–alpha.11 Obsidian-surface era that alpha.14 retired (a reversal of the alpha.14 cut), but re-derived rather than restored — the surfaces carry new phase-names and sit on the alpha.15 view-spec contract, not on the retired Bases-embed-in-dashboard-note mechanism.

**Recorded context.** The history pins why the alpha.15 side was adopted: alpha.14's `design.md` §5 states `status --json` "**is the replacement for dashboard glance surfaces**," and alpha.15's Scope Boundary explicitly forbids any Obsidian plugin/panel. It also pins the founding reason the surface could be dropped at all — the alpha.11 discipline that "the plugin **is a control panel, not a writer**: it never writes canonical Concepts … it enqueues; the worker commits" (`0.1.0-alpha.11-design.md` §8.1), which Part IV calls "**exactly what let the whole Obsidian surface be removed by alpha.14 without touching the integrity core.**"

### Gap: neither ships hub navigation; B1 additionally lacks the hub concept that A15 retained

**Two distinct things named "hub."** The history separates (a) a **hub navigation surface** — a dedicated top-level space for navigating hubs — from (b) the **hub Concept type**. The gap turns on that split.

**(a) The hub navigation surface never existed in the alpha line.** The four navigation spaces settled at alpha.7 were **Inbox / Library / Knowledge / Project** (ADR-81), preserved through the alpha.8 rename to "spaces" (ADR-101) and the alpha.11 space projections. There was never a top-level "Hub" space. Hubs surfaced *inside* Knowledge as a Bases view — alpha.7 added "New `hubs.base` (`src/notes/hubs/hubs.base`, one **'Hubs index' view**)," and the design deliberately chose "hubs fold into a single view rather than a heavyweight dedicated base ('avoid a dedicated base for a handful of files')." So "neither A15 nor B1 ships hub navigation" is, historically, a property that held across the *entire* arc: no version ever promoted hubs to a navigation surface.

**(b) Origin & lineage of the hub Concept (the part A15 retained, B1 drops).** The hub *type* is one of the oldest and most continuous elements in the design:
- **Old Skeleton / alpha.1:** the type roster carried `moc` (Map of Content) alongside wikilinks — "**wikilinks + MOCs**" (§Part I). alpha.1's five note types included **`hub` 🔒** in a gated zone (`notes/hubs/`).
- **alpha.2:** "`hub` is the **renamed MOC**" (ADR-50): "MOC→hub keeps the threshold-alert mechanism (`ADR-19`) under a plainer name." So the concept predates the name.
- **alpha.5:** ADR-19 tier-2 shipped — retitled "Agent-proposed MOCs" → "**Agent-proposed hubs**," adding the `hub_handoff.py` Mapper handoff, while keeping "`notes/hubs/` explicitly forbidden" to agents because "the annotation *is* the curation that defines the type, and that is the human's."
- **alpha.11:** the "**three-node knowledge graph — digest (machine) / note (human) / hub (both)**" (`0.1.0-alpha.11-design.md` §5) — hub survives the first great type-collapse as a first-class node.
- **alpha.15:** the four-type model (ADR-126) keeps hub as one of exactly four Concept types — "**hub** (owns exactly one controlled-vocabulary tag; membership is mechanical, salience is curated)" — genealogically "Luhmann's… hub = structure note/Map-of-Content" (arc (c), `hubs-and-navigation.md`). The alpha.15 verb-first CLI exposes it directly: `new hub <tag>` and a `hub` verb group (ADR-130, PR-B3). So at alpha.15 hub is a hard-won, continuously-carried concept — MOC (Old Skeleton) → hub (alpha.2) → three-node hub (alpha.11) → four-type hub (alpha.15) — that survived *two* clean-slate type-collapses (alpha.11 and alpha.15).

**Prior states.** As above: the element changed name once (MOC→hub, alpha.2, ADR-50), gained an agent-handoff mechanism (alpha.5, ADR-19), and twice survived radical type-reduction (alpha.11 three-node; alpha.15 four-type) each time as an explicitly retained node — never removed across the whole alpha.1→alpha.15 arc.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 dropping the hub concept entirely is **without precedent in the alpha line** — it is the first point in the recorded history where the MOC/hub concept does not exist. Every prior collapse that shed types (alpha.11's "digest/note/hub" and alpha.15's "note/work/hub/project," which "dissolved most of the zoo" per ADR-126) *kept* hub by name and function. So this is not a return to an earlier era (no earlier era lacked it) and not a continuation of an existing trajectory (the trajectory consistently preserved it); it is a net removal. The "no hub navigation surface" half, by contrast, simply continues the invariant that held from alpha.7 onward.

**Recorded context.** The history records *why* hub was retained through alpha.15: the four types "**earn their place by creating distinct obligations the gap engine feeds on**," and hub's obligation is the controlled-vocabulary-tag ownership with "membership… mechanical, salience… curated" (ADR-126); ADR-19's rationale that hub curation "is the human's" explains why it was never automatable and thus never folded away. The history does not record a decision to *abandon* hub (that is a beta.1 change, outside this document's alpha.1→alpha.15 scope); within the recorded arc, hub is retained at every step.

### Gap: B1 mentions no Canvas / spatial projection

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side ("Canvas referenced historically, ADR-103 lineage").** The alpha.15 position — Canvas exists only as *rejected history* — is the endpoint of a full rise-and-fall arc:
- **Old Skeleton / alpha.1:** `canvas` was a **first-class note type**. The original roster was "**16 note-type names** — `fleeting-note, answer-note, paper-note, … moc, reference-note, project-note, code-note, **canvas**, draft, deliverable, candidate-note`" (§Part II.10), and the alpha.1 schema contract listed "`…moc/reference/project/code/**canvas**/draft/deliverable/candidate`" (§Part II, retrieval). So Canvas began as a durable, authored artifact type.
- **alpha.7:** Canvas re-appeared as *generated maps*, not a type — two map-lane skills shipped, "`map:graph-claims` and `map:canvas-hub`, both **propose-class JSON Canvas emitters** over the cluster engine's typed graph," modeled on an existing `map:seed-canvas`, honoring "the engine-writes/agent-judges split (ADR-33: the cluster engine deliberately does *not* emit Canvas; the skill is the propose-class half)." Simultaneously the "**Canvas/argument-graph spatial axis**" was explicitly **deferred** (ADR-81), and the "Studio" gate was dropped precisely because "'Studio' was the **spatial-synthesis/Canvas mode**, and Canvas is deferred."
- **alpha.8:** the concept was promoted to a formal proposal — "**ADR-103 Projected Canvas spatial axis** proposed — Canvas as a spatial *projection* of authored `links:`, **not an authoritative edge store**; scratch Canvas is a sense-making workspace whose edges become durable only through the ADR-83 relate control; hardest requirement is **incremental-stable layout**." Its rationale tied to ADR-79's argument-graph vocabulary, warning that "treating hand-drawn edges as truth 'would repeat the off-store failure ADR-71 rejected.'" ADR-81 was amended to point its deferred UI set at ADR-102/103.
- **alpha.15:** the proposal was **killed**. "**ADR-103 (projected Canvas spatial axis) rejected**: 'spatial-at-scale refuted; tabular/faceted is the validated default; small-graph view must beat a tabular baseline'" (ADR-130 disposition map; the consolidation sweep lists "ADRs 95, 97, **103**, 106 | Rejected… spatial-at-scale refuted"). This is why alpha.15 "references Canvas historically" only — the type is long gone, the spatial-axis proposal is now a permanent tombstoned rejection, and the validated default is the tabular/faceted view-spec (table/card).

**Prior states of the same element.** Canvas passed through three distinct forms before its alpha.15 rejection: (1) an **authored note type** (`canvas`, Old Skeleton/alpha.1); (2) **generated propose-class JSON Canvas maps** from map-lane skills over the cluster graph (alpha.7); (3) a **proposed non-authoritative spatial projection of `links:`** gated on incremental-stable layout (ADR-103, alpha.8). The through-line the history stresses is that from alpha.7 onward Canvas was never allowed to be an edge *source of truth* — "the cluster engine deliberately does *not* emit Canvas," "Canvas as editor" was a rejected alternative for the relate control (ADR-83, alpha.9: "spatial view, not authoritative"), and ADR-103 itself defined Canvas as projection-only.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** beta.1 mentioning no Canvas / spatial projection **continues the alpha.15 trajectory exactly** — it neither revives the alpha.1 `canvas` note type nor the alpha.7 map-lane Canvas emitters nor the ADR-103 proposal. It sits on the same "**tabular/faceted is the validated default**" verdict that alpha.15 recorded when it rejected ADR-103, and its view-spec vocabulary (table/card) is the concrete embodiment of that default. So this is a straight continuation of a rejection made one version earlier, not a new departure and not a return to any Canvas-bearing era.

**Recorded context.** The history pins both the *why* of the alpha.15 rejection and its evidentiary basis: ADR-130's disposition map records the rejection reason as "**spatial-at-scale refuted; tabular/faceted is the validated default; small-graph view must beat a tabular baseline**," anchored to "borrow-doc visualization defaults, Heer/Bostock." It also records the earlier *why* Canvas was ever pursued — ADR-79 "gives Project work an argument-graph vocabulary" — and the standing constraint that killed the authoritative version — "Canvas JSON is invisible to the Linter/Bases/graph ops," so hand-drawn edges as truth "would repeat the off-store failure ADR-71 rejected" (ADR-103, alpha.8). The consolidation note adds that this rejection is permanent by policy: "Rejected-alternatives memory is never pruned — 'numbers are permanent'" (§alpha.15 ADRs).

## 11. Agent / autonomy

The gap-analysis entry for this subsystem is singular: alpha.15 shipped a resident, first-party **Co-PI** agent (the surface named in ADR-130, "read-api-surfaces-and-copi") plus **operations as product code**; beta.1 declares "**Memoria is not an agent, and beta.1 adds none**," and reclassifies *any* agent — a first-party one or the researcher's own Claude Code / Cursor — as a **client** under the ADR-130 contract (read-API + request envelope, quarantined, read-scoped, never file/terminal/exec/send; chat additive, never primary). The gap the document draws: **B1 ships no resident Co-PI; the agent role is externalized to the researcher's own client.** Traced through the design history, this is the terminal move of the single longest-running arc in Memoria — the arc the synthesis section calls "(a) Seven specialist profiles → five → no agent autonomy at all."

### B1 ships no resident Co-PI agent; the agent role is externalized to the researcher's own client

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The Co-PI is one of the oldest continuously-present elements in the design, and it is a pre-alpha.1 inheritance, not an alpha-line invention. It is fixed in the two founding sources at once. In the **Origins seven-layer architecture** the Co-PI *is* a layer: the researcher's notes elaborate the Old Skeleton into "L1 the human/PI, L2 the Obsidian workspace, **L3 the co-PI (the permanent ACP-pane agent with the full Hermes learning loop)**, L4 the tasks layer … L7 the vault." That L3 role is the ancestor of everything the alpha line does with the Co-PI: a *permanent* conversational agent, pinned in a pane, carrying the Hermes self-improving loop. It sat inside the **Old Skeleton's three-layer model** ("board = control plane, workers = execution layer, vault = durable store") and under the Old Skeleton's autonomy posture — **"an L3-with-a-ceiling autonomy posture (Chen 2026's taxonomy): profiles execute multi-step unattended *within a card*, but the human sets strategy and gates promotion; L4/L5 are refused structurally because 'synthesis correctness is not scalar and synthesis changes are not reversible.'"** So both halves of the alpha.15 side — a resident Co-PI *and* a hard autonomy ceiling — are Old Skeleton (dated **2026-05-29**) properties, not late arrivals.

By the **alpha.1 baseline** (release commit `0e9ed9dd`, 2026-06-14) the Co-PI is one of the shipped five profiles (`memoria-copi`), and it is already the read-only conversational agent it stays for the rest of the arc: **"read-only over the entire vault — `policy.allow.write: []`,"** the **"sole carrier of Hermes' self-improving loop (memory · /goals · skills),"** and *"the one agent the PI converses with … Everything else in Memoria is delegated through you or run as an engine."* Its read-only wall is a hard-won, explicitly-argued property from the start: *"A conversation drifts, accumulates context, and gets persuaded; a card does not."* The alpha.1 delegation posture already ranks the Co-PI at the bottom of the delegation axis "despite delegating everything," because *"routing a write to a board lane is the system's front door, not a subtask spawn."*

**Prior states of the same element (the roster it emerged from).** The Co-PI was carved out of a larger roster in a documented sequence. The pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton named a **seven-specialist roster** (coder/writer/socratic/mapper/librarian/verifier/cmdr, formalized as ADR-02's "Librarian, Mapper, Socratic, Writer, Verifier, Coder, Linter"). By the alpha.1 cut this had consolidated to **five** (co-pi, engineer, librarian, peer-reviewer, writer) via **ADR-48**, which "supersedes: [2, 37, 42]." ADR-48 is where the Co-PI absorbs the conversational role wholesale: **"Socratic → co-PI — 'the conversational stance with the write-wall … so it folded in.'"** Two forcing pressures are recorded: a **UX failure** ("Seven specialists created a real UX failure: who do I talk to?") and a **fragmented learning loop** ("the self-improving loop … only compounds in an agent that has every conversation. Split across seven, each got a sliver of context and none grew"). ADR-48 also records the mid-build scope expansion: *"the original plan shipped only co-PI + Librarian … the PI expanded the scope mid-build — 2026-06-09."* At **alpha.7** (2026-06-18, ADR-68→ADR-81 era) the conversational surface was pinned as a **single-agent ACP pane** — the earlier "four ACP profiles behind a picker (Socratic, Mapper, Writer, Verifier)" was retired by ADR-48, because "with one agent in the pane there is no contract boundary to police mid-conversation." At **alpha.9/10** (2026-06-21/22) — the Hermes peak, with themes literally "Hermes-version decision" and "Hermes/runtime governance" — the Co-PI's singularity was mechanized: the **`memory` toolset became profile-scoped to `memoria-copi` only** (`PROFILE_SCOPED_DIRECT_TOOLS = {"memory": {"memoria-copi"}}`), and `session_search` was reserved "for Co-PI only as a deliberate privacy trade."

The autonomy-ceiling half of the element has its own prior-states chain. The Old Skeleton's "L3-with-a-ceiling" and its **"structurally blocking human review gate"** (a card cannot promote until the human sets `review_status: approved`, "positioned deliberately against the surveyed autonomous-research systems, whose LLM reviewers only advise") carried through the alpha.1 "propose-not-dispose … the PI disposes" rule. At **alpha.11** (2026-06-27) the ceiling was made explicit *in policy*: **"Autonomy ceiling made explicit in policy — no autonomous keep/discard loop for synthesis; code-lane autonomy deferred (old ADR-34 'code-artifact-autopilot' deleted)"** — with the recorded reason that an autonomous keep/discard loop *"fails for synthesis … so the knowledge lane is human-directed."*

**How the Co-PI's runtime was progressively hollowed out (alpha.11 → alpha.14 → alpha.15).** The alpha.15 side of the gap is the *end* of a demotion the history documents version by version. The Co-PI began (alpha.1–alpha.10) as an agent *on the Hermes runtime* — Hermes was "Memoria's entire execution layer" (ADR-22, "build on Hermes, don't build a runtime"). Then:

- **alpha.11 — "Profiles / runtime — operations replace profiles":** "The five-profile / seven-specialist / fleet runtime model was retired; policy now binds to **operations, not profile identity**." The Co-PI specifically is de-runtimed: "The Co-PI interview becomes engine state (`memoria ask/answer/resume`), never a pydantic-ai session." Hermes drops "from control-plane to specialized adapter"; "**Direct API is the baseline**."
- **alpha.14 (2026-06-30) — the standalone-CLI cut:** "Installed profiles and the lane/fleet runtime model were removed wholesale," "dropped, not deferred." What survived is the *posture*, not the profile: the surviving invariant is that the runner rejects "Agent session memory as authority" and "co-trained generator+reviewer … confidence-routed bypass of the human checkpoint" (the doc cites `docs/design/why-not-autonomous.md`).
- **alpha.15 (2026-07-01/02) — ADR-130 + ADR-125:** ADR-125 records "Agent posture carried without the profile system — 'Memoria never grants an agent file, terminal, code-execution, or send tools'," described as "the surviving invariant of ADR-07/32/46/48." **ADR-130 ("read-api-surfaces-and-copi")** re-founds the Co-PI as a *client of the engine*: "the engine's JSON read-API is the sole read path for every Memoria-built surface **and agent**," "Under ADR-125 the engine is the product and **every surface is a client**." The agent contract is spelled out: **per-agent read-scoping** enforced in `engine/api`, **all writes enqueue-only** ("a UI mutates only by enqueueing — it never writes concepts, verdicts, frontmatter, or commits," carried verbatim from ADR-121), the agent contract "exposes plan/sources/assumptions/unknowns/pending-writes before any enqueue," and **"chat additive not primary."** So by alpha.15 the resident Co-PI is *already* a read-scoped, enqueue-only, tool-starved client — but it is still a *first-party* surface the product ships.

The companion element, **"operations as product code,"** has the same alpha.11→15 lineage: alpha.11 made operations "Concepts with policy in frontmatter" (40 `capabilities/operations/*.md`); alpha.14 "ported all 26 legacy `.memoria/operations/` Python modules into `src/memoria_vault/runtime/subsystems/`" ("Existing operation implementations are import inputs, not disposable history"); alpha.15 pinned the stack (uv/Typer/stdlib-sqlite/pydantic-ai) with operations as product source. This is the deterministic counterpart to the agent's hollowing-out: the work the seven profiles once did as agents became engine code, leaving the Co-PI a thin conversational client over that engine.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** Beta.1 **continues the alpha.11→alpha.15 trajectory to its endpoint, and then takes one step with no precedent in the alpha line.** Everything in beta.1's contract clause is verbatim-continuous alpha.15/ADR-130 language: "every surface is a client" (ADR-130), "read-API + request envelope" (alpha.14 envelope + ADR-130 read-API), "quarantined" (ADR-127), "read-scoped" (ADR-130 per-agent read-scoping), "never file/terminal/exec/send" (ADR-125's surviving ADR-07/32/46/48 invariant), and "chat additive, never primary" (ADR-130 verbatim). The declaration "Memoria is not an agent, adds none" is the plainest statement yet of arc (a)'s destination — the same arc whose alpha.11 statement was "the PI is a director + spot-corrector, not an approval queue" and whose ADR-125 residue was "Memoria never grants an agent … tools." In autonomy terms, beta.1 is the terminal state of the Old-Skeleton L3-with-a-ceiling posture pushed all the way down: not "L3 within a card" but *no first-party agency at all*.

The **novel step with no alpha precedent** is the externalization itself. From the pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton (L3 co-PI) through alpha.15 (ADR-130's resident Co-PI client), Memoria *always shipped a conversational agent of its own* — the identity changed (seven → five → one ACP agent → posture → read-scoped client) but a first-party Co-PI was continuously present across every version in the arc. Beta.1 is the first point in the entire history at which **Memoria ships no resident Co-PI**; the agent role is moved out of the product and onto "the researcher's own Claude Code/Cursor" acting as an ADR-130 client. So the mapping is precise: beta.1 does **not** return to an earlier era (no earlier version had zero agent), and it does **not** invent a new *contract* (the client contract is alpha.15/ADR-130 verbatim) — it **continues the existing demotion trajectory** (agent: foundation → adapter → posture → client) and extends it one rung past alpha.15 by declining to ship the client, leaving only the read-API/request-envelope contract for an external client to attach to.

**Recorded context — when/why the alpha.15 form was adopted and its predecessors abandoned.** The history dates and motivates each transition. The resident single Co-PI was adopted by **ADR-48** (scope-expanded 2026-06-09; shipped in the alpha.2-stamped alpha.1 tree) to fix a named UX failure ("who do I talk to?") and a fragmented learning loop. The move off the Hermes runtime is dated and reasoned to the **alpha.9 literature correction**: the review "was run assuming `qwen2.5:7b` (local) is Memoria's production engine. It is not … the live engines call an LLM API" — "once the live engine is an API, the Hermes control-plane DB, profiles, Kanban, and MCP-as-required-API become weight the single-user local tool doesn't need." The autonomy ceiling's rationale is recorded in `why-not-autonomous.md`: Karpathy's keep/revert loop is safe only when "the metric is monotonic … changes are reversible … experiments are independent," and knowledge work "fails all three" ("synthesis quality is not scalar," "synthesis errors compound," "later sources reinterpret earlier ones"); confidence-routing (SmartPause) was refused because "confident-wrong is the failure mode." ADR-125 records the final closing argument for the standalone-engine form the alpha.15 Co-PI-as-client rides on: "The engineering ADR-22 warned against … is now owned in-house — ADR-127 is where that obligation is paid," yielding the stated goal "One install command, no external runtime, no always-on process." The synthesis section summarizes the whole arc as beta.1's immediate antecedent: agent identity moved "from agent-assisted to bounded, phase-gated knowledge production," with ADR-125 keeping "only the posture invariant with the runtime gone."

## 12. Security / OS confinement

### Gap 1 — OS-level egress enforcement is Linux-only (B1: brokered egress via Guard, engines get no sockets, `CLONE_NEWNET` netns; macOS `sandbox-exec`, seccomp fallback, Windows-Firewall/UAC cut)

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The A15 side of this gap — "network access operation-scoped + allowlisted; injection-as-untrusted-data; platform-agnostic (no OS-specific enforcement claimed)" — is the end-state of a confinement idea that begins in the pre-alpha.1 **Old Skeleton** as a *layer*, not an egress control. The researcher's notes elaborate the system into a **seven-layer architecture** in which "**L5 the MCP server(s) (the sandbox boundary)**" is the confinement locus, with "decisions flow top-down, information flows bottom-up." At that stage confinement means: the agent can only touch the world through the MCP layer.

At **alpha.1** (`0e9ed9dd`) this sandbox is realized purely as a **write gate**, not egress control: `policy_mcp.py` guards eight verbs (`read/write/append/move/delete/mkdir/auto_fix/report`) with four decisions (`allow/allow_with_log/deny/dry_run`), and enforcement is "**default-deny by toolset allowlist plus a single gated write path**" — the obsidian MCP is "the *only* write path a lane has." The seven-layer model is ratified at **alpha.2** with the explicit qualifier that fixes the era's scope: "**MCP is a policy gate, not an execution sandbox**" (ADR-46 Decision). Egress simply is not confined here — the machinery governs *what may be written to the vault*, not *what may leave the machine*.

The MCP-only sandbox was then repeatedly *reaffirmed as a write boundary* across the middle alphas: **alpha.3** rejected Shell Commands (ADR-72) and `cli-rest-mcp` because a generic `execute` "defeats the named-tool write gate"; **alpha (ADR-32 fix)** retired the Coder/Linter `terminal` exception so "no profile ships `terminal`, `file`, or `code_execution`." Egress tools (`web_*`, `browser_*`, `send_message`, `delegate_task`) were never brought under this regime.

**The decisive turn is alpha.14.** Two findings on the same page reframe the whole subsystem. First, egress-allow-by-default is filed as a **live P0**: the gate "hard-denies file/terminal/code-exec but **allow-by-defaults** `web_*`/`browser_*`/`send_message`/`delegate_task` (returns `{}`), so for egress the sandbox rests entirely on `disabled_toolsets` schema-hiding," and "on the injection path this is unmitigated" against "a **poisoned source/paper** whose text reaches the LLM on every ingest call (data > code, Greshake/Debenedetti)." Second, the founding principle is annotated: the "**MCP-only sandbox reframed 'necessary, not sufficient'**" — "least-privilege doesn't defend against poisoned *data* … reframes the whole safety cluster (ADR-27/32/46/28) as a *write* boundary that says nothing about egress under injection." This is exactly the REVIEW-REFUTATIONS "allowlists necessary-not-sufficient" line landing in the design record.

Alpha.14's *positive* egress response was built at the **application layer**, and it is the property the gap analysis attributes to A15: "**Network allowlist matching was hardened so adapter HTTP bypassing the shared allowlist fails closed**" (#1103) — "Every HTTP call passes a shared operation allowlist check before network I/O … the operation manifest is the hard ceiling for network access" (§9: "Network access is operation-scoped and allowlisted"). Note the lineage of *where* egress lives: at **alpha.13**, network was already scoped to worker operations via `providers.yaml` + `enrichment.provider_allowlist_issues()` (which "fails closed if any enabled provider `base_url` is not covered by the manifest `allowed_network`"), with the settled rule that "enrichment is a worker operation (not an agent tool) … agents never get network or code-exec (ADR-32)." **Alpha.15** carried this forward as invariant: "**No agent tool grants** … Memoria never grants an agent file/terminal/exec/send tools" and "loopback HTTP binds only loopback hosts, token-auth" (ADR-125/130), plus per-agent read-scoping "the lethal-trifecta / exfiltration mitigation" (ADR-130). Every one of these controls is enforced in Python (allowlist checks, manifest ceilings, read-scope filtering) — **there is no OS-level enforcement anywhere in the alpha line.** The gap analysis's own §14 pins this: A15 was "cross-platform by construction (**no OS-specific enforcement claimed**)."

**Prior states of the same element.** The egress boundary changed form three times inside the arc: (1) *non-existent as a confined channel* through alpha.1–alpha.10 (the sandbox was a write gate); (2) *named as a principle* at **alpha.11's clean-slate reset**, where the old policy-MCP gated-write enforcement was retired as the security boundary and replaced by "channel-separation + tool allowlists + **no-exfil** + trusted-writer-only commits" (old ADR-27 deleted) — "the security boundary is channel separation + tool allowlists + no-exfil"; and (3) *enforced at the application layer* at alpha.13–15 (operation manifest allowlist, fail-closed). The "engines/agents get no network except through the allowlisted operation path" invariant that B1's "engines get no sockets" restates is therefore a hard-won alpha.13→alpha.15 property — but always as *process-architecture discipline in Python*, never as kernel confinement.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** Beta.1's "**brokered egress via Guard** (engines get no sockets) + **Linux network-namespace (`CLONE_NEWNET`) confinement**" introduces something with **no precedent in the alpha line**: OS-kernel-level egress enforcement. Across the entire arc — Old Skeleton L5 sandbox, alpha.1 policy gate, alpha.14 fail-closed allowlist, alpha.15 no-sockets invariant — egress control lived in the Memoria process (allowlist checks before I/O). B1 relocates part of that enforcement below the application, into the OS. In doing so it also **splits the platform guarantee for the first time**: the OS-level guarantee exists *only on Linux*, and "seccomp fallback, macOS `sandbox-exec`, and Windows-Firewall/UAC flows are **cut**." On macOS/Windows, B1's own framing — egress restriction is "**process-architecture discipline, not OS enforcement**" — is a factual **return to the alpha-era posture**: for those platforms the boundary remains exactly what it was through alpha.13–15 (an in-process discipline that agents/engines reach the network only through the allowlisted path), with no OS backstop. So the same gap has two opposite historical readings depending on platform: on Linux, B1 adds a layer the alpha line never had; on macOS/Windows, B1 continues the alpha application-layer trajectory while cutting the OS-sandbox profiles that would have been net-new.

**Recorded context.** The history records *why* the alpha side stopped where it did: alpha.14's audit stated egress was "unmitigated" on the injection path and that the sandbox was "necessary, not sufficient," yet the alpha response chose the **application-layer** allowlist (`#1103`, fail-closed) and, at alpha.15, read-scoping + no-socket invariants — the arc explicitly notes the runtime target became "Python 3.12 + uv + Typer + stdlib sqlite3 … **one install command, no external runtime, no always-on process**" (ADR-125). The design history contains **no** decision adopting OS-level confinement or a per-OS sandbox profile in the alpha line; that direction (and its Linux-only scope) originates in beta.1.

### Gap 2 — Write-time exfiltration linter + canary drill + per-OS keychain added as centered machinery (B1: VaultWriter-owned default-deny link/embed/HTML neutralization at apply + export; canary drill; Keychain/DPAPI/Secret Service + mode-0600 fallback)

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The A15 side here is an *absence* the gap analysis states plainly: "**no exfiltration-linter-at-vault-write detailed**." But each of B1's three components maps onto a distinct alpha thread, so the absence is of a *centered mechanism*, not of the underlying concerns.

*The exfiltration concern itself* is not new. It is named as a boundary element at the **alpha.11 clean-slate reset**, where the security boundary became "channel-separation + tool allowlists + **no-exfil** + trusted-writer-only commits" (§12). Before that, the first concrete exfiltration *surface* recognized in the arc is the **diagnostic plane at the alpha checkpoint (ADR-105)**: it is called out as "the first place vault-derived content could land in a log," where done naively "a **forgotten debug flag plus a routine `git push` exfiltrates unpublished research**" — mitigated by out-of-Git storage, deny-by-default raw payloads, and "a **golden-corpus redaction self-test**." The same checkpoint hardened editor paste behavior "disabling VSCode paste-as-formatted-link and file/URL copy-into-workspace" (`8ab8f59d`) — a link-neutralization move at the docs-vault level. So a *redaction/neutralization* impulse and a *no-exfil* principle both existed pre-beta, but neither was a write-time link/embed/HTML linter.

*The write-time chokepoint the linter attaches to* has deep lineage. The idea of concentrating a security check at a **single write path** runs continuously: alpha.1's "single gated write path" (obsidian MCP as "the *only* write path a lane has"); alpha.11's "**trusted-writer-only commits** … only the worker commits" (old ADR-27 deleted); alpha.12's `trusted_writer.py` materialization protocol; alpha.15's fail-closed writer invariants. B1 placing the exfiltration linter in the **VaultWriter** at *apply + export* is the same architectural move — one security check at the one write chokepoint — applied to a new concern (exfil payloads). Its **default-deny** disposition also continues the fail-closed lineage that begins with alpha.1's fail-closed policy plugin ("any exception inside the gate also returns block") and is raised to an architecture invariant at **alpha.15** — "a logged denial is policy, silence is wiring" and "a gate that proceeds on its own failure is not a gate" (ADR-125, restating ADR-28's lesson).

*The exfiltration threat model* — the lethal trifecta of untrusted content + private data + egress — was addressed in the alpha line on the **input and read sides**, never at write-time. Alpha.15 sealed the input side: "**Untrusted-input sealing** … raw source text never interpolates into an instruction template … every text-reading operation carries a negative poisoned-input fixture" (ADR-129), and scoped the read side: "**Per-agent read scoping** … the lethal-trifecta / exfiltration mitigation" (ADR-130). B1's exfiltration linter closes the **payload/write channel** those left open — neutralizing links/embeds/HTML that a poisoned source could smuggle *into* a written note or export to later phone home. This continues the injection-as-untrusted lineage (Greshake/AgentDojo, confirmed in `REVIEW-REFUTATIONS.md` and cited from alpha.14 onward) but at a new point in the pipeline with no alpha precedent as a distinct mechanism.

*The canary drill* has **no direct precedent** — "canary" appears nowhere in the alpha.1→alpha.15 history. Its nearest ancestors are the arc's adversarial-verification drills: alpha.1/ADR-28's deliberately adversarial fail-closed test ("with `policy_mcp.py` moved aside, an otherwise-allowed write is **blocked**"), and alpha.15's **seeded-error verdict** harness with its "**negative poisoned-input fixture**" per text-reading operation and the seeded-error bars ("100% structural detection recall, 0 false-clean"). B1's canary continues that tradition of proving a control fires by trying to defeat it, but a *live exfiltration canary* is net-new to beta.1.

*The per-OS keychain* traces a secret-handling thread that was deliberately kept **platform-agnostic** in the alpha line. At **alpha.2**, keys were seeded per-profile into `.env` ("Hermes reads per-profile `.env` only … the installer must seed shared keys into each profile `.env`," ADR-27 Finding 6). At **alpha.13**, `providers.yaml` names `.env` variables, never values, with the rule "actual mailto/email values and API keys live in profile `.env`, **never in tracked config**." At **alpha.14**, the utilization audit explicitly *considered and declined* an OS-adjacent secret store: "Bitwarden secrets vs the 5×`.env` fan-out (fleet/sandbox win, **not a lone-laptop slam-dunk** — U1)." At **alpha.15**, the loopback HTTP surface added `MEMORIA_HTTP_TOKEN` token-auth, with the residual "any local bearer credential is readable by a trusted-local agent." So secrets always lived in untracked `.env` files (or an env-supplied token) — a platform-agnostic floor, and an OS keychain was weighed and set aside once (U1).

**Prior states of the same element.** The three components have distinct prior states: the *no-exfil* boundary went from unnamed (alpha.1–10) → principle (alpha.11) → input/read-side enforcement (alpha.15 sealing + read-scoping); the *write chokepoint* went from obsidian-MCP-only (alpha.1) → trusted-writer-only commits (alpha.11–15); *secret storage* went from per-profile `.env` (alpha.2/13) with Bitwarden explicitly declined (alpha.14) to an env token (alpha.15).

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** B1 **centers, as a single named mechanism, concerns that were previously scattered and principle-level.** The exfiltration linter *continues* the alpha.11 "no-exfil" principle and the single-write-path + fail-closed lineages, but introduces the write-time link/embed/HTML neutralizer as **new machinery with no alpha precedent**; the gap analysis's framing — B1 "adds the write-time exfiltration linter … as a centered mechanism" — is confirmed by the history showing only *precursors* (ADR-105 redaction self-test, hardened paste, alpha.15 input sealing / read-scoping), never a write-time linter. The canary is **net-new** with only the adversarial-test tradition (ADR-28, alpha.15 seeded-error/poisoned-input fixtures) behind it. The keychain **reverses a specific alpha stance**: it reaches into per-OS facilities (Keychain/DPAPI/Secret Service) where the alpha line deliberately stayed platform-agnostic (`.env`, with U1 declining an OS-adjacent store) — and its `mode-0600` fallback is precisely the alpha-era platform-agnostic floor preserved underneath the new per-OS layer. That per-OS reach is the **same platform-specialization direction** as Gap 1: beta.1 trades the alpha line's "cross-platform by construction, no OS-specific enforcement" posture for OS-native mechanisms with a portable fallback.

**Recorded context.** The history records the *why* for the alpha stopping points but not for the beta additions (which post-date this record): alpha.11 named "no-exfil" as a boundary element without a mechanism; alpha.14 declined the OS-adjacent Bitwarden store as "not a lone-laptop slam-dunk" (U1) and kept secrets in `.env`; alpha.15 sealed inputs and scoped reads as "the lethal-trifecta / exfiltration mitigation" (ADR-129/130) but recorded the residual that "any local bearer credential is readable by a trusted-local agent." The write-time exfiltration linter, the canary, and the per-OS keychain do not appear as adopted (or as abandoned earlier forms) anywhere in the alpha.1→alpha.15 record; they originate in beta.1.

## 13. Cost / snapshot / migration machinery

The three gaps in this subsystem share one historical property: each maps onto an alpha-line element whose *stated design premise was the absence of the thing beta.1 now centers*. Cost was captured but deliberately never authored; snapshot/rollback was built as SQLite-journal + output-Concept machinery with git-as-tamper-evidence, but never as a git-diff snapshot substrate; and migration was explicitly refused at every version from alpha.1 through alpha.15. Below, each gap is traced through the arc.

### B1 centers a full cost / metering / circuit-breaker machinery (BYOK price table, `max_tokens` ceilings, batch API, breaker, $20/mo soft budget, "miss row")

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** Cost discipline is one of Memoria's oldest properties, present in the alpha.1 baseline (Part II §7). It was not a metering machinery but a *ceiling*: because the system "has **no scalar quality payoff to optimize against** (L3 ceiling forecloses it)," ADR-21 ruled that "**budget discipline replaces metric discipline** … the nightly loop is bounded by a cost ceiling (~$1–3/day) rather than by a quality target." The deferred discovery/experiment loops (ADR-61) therefore carried `budget_iterations:` / `budget_cost_usd:` fields "rather than accuracy targets — a cost ceiling is the only meaningful bound when there is nothing to maximize." This is the direct ancestor of beta.1's `$20/mo default soft budget`: a budget as the governing bound, adopted at alpha.1 for a reason (no scalar payoff) that the arc never revised.

Two facts about *how cost was captured* are load-bearing for the gap:

1. **Model routing was never Memoria's concern.** At alpha.1, "Model routing is a Hermes concern, not a deployment one — *synthesis to Claude, embed/classify/quick-summary to cheaper models via OpenRouter*" (Part II §7, `deployment-options.md`). Cost per card was one of the six publication-path telemetry signals (`cost.jsonl`, ADR-20), and aggregation was deferred because it "needs real run volume before trust-score bands are meaningful."

2. **Memoria was a cost *recorder*, explicitly not a cost *authority*.** At alpha.6 the `cost`/`disposition` signals "cannot emit on the current Hermes" and were documented as an upstream known-limitation, not a gate. That framing was overturned by **ADR-106 (proposed 2026-06-19, accepted in alpha.10 on Hermes 0.17)**: verification found Hermes v0.14.0 "**does** compute and persist per-call cost … in its per-profile session store," so Memoria read it via `hermes kanban show <id> --json` joined to `~/.hermes/profiles/<lane>/state.db`. ADR-106's decision is the pointed precedent: "**Memoria does not maintain its own pricing table — it records Hermes's estimate and its provenance** (`cost_source`, `billing_provider`, `pricing_version`)." The "miss row" idea is native to ADR-106 too: missing-session cases must "**fail closed with a counted miss rather than a fabricated zero**," and "session rotation can occasionally drop an aged session (observed ~1 in 13), so a small miss rate is expected and not treated as an error." ADR-106 twice **rejected** a Memoria-owned pricing path — the "local model-gateway proxy at the profile `base_url`" was declined precisely because it "would need its own pricing table for dollars."

**Prior states of the same element.** The cost element existed continuously but shifted substrate: alpha.1 telemetry `cost.jsonl` → alpha.6 documented-empty → alpha.10 ADR-106 Hermes-session-store join (`board_export_cost.py`). Then the substrate was pulled out from under it: **alpha.14 dropped the Hermes runtime**, and **alpha.15 formally rejected ADR-106** — "Hermes session-store capture is dead with the dropped Hermes runtime, and the Obsidian/QuickAdd review-action path … is not alpha.15 implementation scope" (ADR-106 status note; consolidation sweep lists 106 among the four rejected ADRs, "Hermes cost-capture mechanism dead"). What replaced it at alpha.15 was a *build-time* cost knob, not a runtime meter: `eval select-models --operation --mode`, which "pins the cheapest candidate that passes the operation's bar — **human-triggered and inspectable, never an online optimizer**" (an explicit amendment to ADR-11). Even this was minimal: the "candidate **cost-table**/manifest-rewrite mechanics in `select-models`" were **deferred out of alpha.15** because "current data has one candidate per mode."

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** Beta.1 does not return to an earlier era; it crosses two lines the alpha arc held from alpha.1 to alpha.15:

- **A normative price table in Ledger** reverses ADR-106's explicit "Memoria does not maintain its own pricing table" and *adopts* both the alpha.15-deferred "candidate cost-table" and the twice-rejected "local model-gateway proxy … its own pricing table." Across the whole alpha line Memoria was a recorder of an upstream (Hermes) *estimate*; beta.1 makes Memoria the pricing *authority*. That is new relative to every alpha version.
- **A circuit breaker enforced at preview / pre-call / breaker** is *runtime* cost enforcement. The only cost gating the alpha line ever admitted was alpha.15's build-time `select-models`, which was defined against exactly this ("**never an online optimizer**"). Runtime cost enforcement has no precedent in the alpha line.

Two beta.1 elements *continue* alpha-line lineage rather than break it: the **`$20/mo soft budget`** is the alpha.1 ADR-21 cost-ceiling posture rescaled (~$1–3/day → $20/mo), and the **"miss row" for unattributable spend** is the direct descendant of ADR-106's "counted miss rather than a fabricated zero." **BYOK** and the **batch API (50%)** have no antecedent: model access ran through Hermes/OpenRouter (alpha.1) then `direct_api`/pydantic-ai (alpha.11–15), never a user-supplied-key posture, and batch pricing appears nowhere in the arc.

### B1 specifies a git-snapshot batch/revert substrate distinct from A15's SQLite-journal cascade-rollback

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The alpha.15 recovery substrate is a hard-won property built up over four versions, not a late arrival:

- **alpha.1 (Old Skeleton inheritance):** reversibility via SHA-256 `before_hash`/`after_hash` pairing (ADR-25/reversibility), and a deferred `golden.py restore` path. The vault was an ordinary git repo under "thin control over thick state."
- **alpha.10 (ADR-104):** the audit-integrity substrate was decided to be **Git, not in-file hash chaining** — "the vault is already a Git repo — commit history is a Merkle chain," and a linear in-file `prev_hash` chain was rejected because it "*forks* under multi-machine writes." This fixed git as the tamper-evidence root.
- **alpha.11:** **cascade rollback** became a first-class operation. "Invalidate the offending **output Concept** … and everything **transitively derived from it** … **The commit is the atomic *write* unit; the *rollback* unit is the output Concept.**" Machine-derived downstream auto-reverts; human-directed downstream "halts auto-revert but still propagates a flag." A **trace-integrity scan** quarantined untraced/foreign commits (Obsidian-Git auto-commit named as the rejected foreign committer) — the first appearance of the concern beta.1's external git-dir addresses.
- **alpha.12:** the journal became an "append-only, hash-chained SQLite table" with `RAISE(ABORT, 'journal is append-only')` triggers; a **cross-store materialization protocol** was installed on the rule "**Transactions: SQLite first, file promotion second**," with crash recovery replaying files from durable `materialization_payloads` (`worker recover`). The `derivations` edge table shipped as "the rollback-graph substrate," but the **recursive-CTE cascade-rollback itself was design-only** (rollback still ran the alpha.11 path). The **git-tracked `journal-heads.jsonl` anchor was design-specified but not wired** — only the `audit/` directory was created.
- **alpha.15 (ADR-127):** the arc's fullest recovery statement. **Cross-store recoverability (observable atomicity)**: "2PC with journal states `pending`→`materialized` (materializing commit SHA recorded after `git commit`), write-ahead fsync ordering … one workspace-scoped write lock per operation, crash-at-any-boundary recovery via `workspace recover`; connections pinned WAL + `synchronous=FULL`." **Git/journal division**: git owns file-tree history "**+ the tamper-evidence root via the tracked `.memoria/journal-head` anchor (newly *created* — at `88b7f0f5` `journal_head()` was a DB query only, no tracked file)**"; and "one commit per operation staging **only** materialized paths + control files, **never `git add -A`** (a swept foreign edit would bypass quarantine)." Rollback continued as **two-tier demotion propagation**.

**Prior states of the same element.** The rollback *unit* changed form across versions: output-Concept quarantine (alpha.11) → `derivations`-edge + design-only recursive CTE (alpha.12) → two-tier demotion with `stale` flags (alpha.15). The tamper-evidence anchor went from *design-only* (`audit/` scaffold, alpha.12) to *created* (`.memoria/journal-head`, alpha.15). SQLite backup was named a "**first-class, separate concern**" and deferred at alpha.12 (Turso adapter "spike-gated," "not part of the ownership promise"), then documented at alpha.15 as a three-store backup contract (git remote, Litestream-style SQLite replication, blob sync) that is "**`doctor`-reported, never a runtime dependency**."

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** Beta.1 splits into elements that **continue the trajectory** and elements with **no precedent**:

- The **4-step batch protocol (snapshot/apply/verify/commit)** and **pre==post manifest gate** continue the alpha.12/alpha.15 observable-atomicity line (SQLite-first-then-promote, workspace-scoped write lock, crash recovery). The "verify" step extends the alpha.1 `before_hash`/`after_hash` pairing and alpha.15's post-`git commit` SHA recording.
- **Pathspec-limited commits** are a direct continuation of alpha.15's "one commit per operation staging **only** materialized paths … **never `git add -A`**" (ADR-127, PR-E1). This is the same rule, same rationale (a swept foreign edit bypassing quarantine).
- **One-click revert with inverse-diff + DB reconciliation** continues the alpha.11→12→15 cascade-rollback arc, and "DB reconciliation" continues the alpha.12/15 cross-store recovery. The **git inverse-diff mechanism is new**: the alpha line reverted by demoting/quarantining output Concepts and re-materializing from durable payloads, never by applying a git inverse patch.
- **`git external git-dir`** has **no precedent** as a mechanism. The alpha line kept the keep-set as an ordinary in-tree git repo. It *does* continue a concern-lineage — alpha.11's trace-integrity scan and alpha.15's "never `git add -A`" both exist to keep foreign edits (Obsidian-Git) out of Memoria's commits — but beta.1's structural answer (relocating the git directory itself) is a new device.
- **`VACUUM INTO`** has **no precedent** as a mechanism; it operationalizes the SQLite-snapshot concern the alpha line repeatedly *named but externalized*: alpha.12's "SQLite backup is a first-class, separate concern … a full Memoria backup needs ordinary SQLite backup/snapshot handling" and alpha.15's "three-store backup contract … never a runtime dependency." Beta.1 brings that concern inside the runtime.

### B1 adds a migration runner as a launch blocker

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The alpha.15 "side" of this gap is the *absence* of a migration runner — and that absence was the most consistently and explicitly held position across the entire arc:

- **alpha.1 (Old Skeleton inheritance):** even the install layer refused in-place migration — "releases are **fresh-install, never in-place migration**," because "in-place migration's *half-migrated states are the failure mode D52 rejected*" (`distribution-model.md`).
- **alpha.11 §20:** "**Fresh-workspace-only initialization; no migration path** … There is no alpha.11 system installed outside the sandbox, so alpha.11 has **no migration, upgrade path, backwards-compatibility mode, or cutover** … do not map legacy records."
- **alpha.12:** "**The clean-rebuild migration target (alpha.11 §20) is retired** — alpha.12 has **no migration target**; non-sandbox use starts from a fresh workspace." The justification is the premise: "a single-machine, sandbox-only alpha with no backward-compatibility promise does not need a migration path."
- **alpha.13:** "no installed-vault migration target"; schema changes "should be additive and idempotent … **no production-vault migration target**."
- **alpha.14:** "there is deliberately **no backward-compatibility mode, no migration path, and no legacy runtime branch**," and a **schema-migration shim was actively *removed*** (#1057, `d95f7cb4`) — "with no production vault there is no migration target, so the shim is dead machinery … Existing compatibility code, tests, docs, and schema paths are removed."
- **alpha.15 (ADR-125):** the schema is versioned by "**`.sql` DDL gated by `PRAGMA user_version`**," and an "ORM + migration framework" was **rejected**: "the integrity schema is the product; single-user scale needs `.sql` + `user_version` only." So a schema-*versioning primitive* exists, but a migration *runner* was explicitly declined.

**Prior states of the same element.** There is no prior "migration runner" that changed form — the element the gap describes never existed in the alpha line. Its nearest positive precedent is the alpha.15 `PRAGMA user_version` gate, which is a version *stamp*, not a runner. The one migration shim that did briefly exist (through alpha.13's additive-schema world) was *deleted* in alpha.14. Every version restated the same rationale, always anchored to the same fact: no system is installed outside the disposable `Memoria-test` sandbox / no production vault.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** Beta.1 introduces the MigrationRunner as a launch blocker on the stated ground that "**beta is the first version whose data must survive later versions.**" That maps onto the history as a **categorical reversal with no precedent in the alpha line** — not a return to an earlier era (no earlier era had migration; alpha.1 already refused in-place migration, and alpha.14 deleted the only shim). The reversal is triggered by the disappearance of the exact premise every alpha "no-migration" statement rested on: alpha.11–15 all justified "no migration path" by "there is no … system installed outside the sandbox" / "no production vault." Beta.1 is, by its own framing, the first version to hold durable production data, so the premise that licensed the alpha stance no longer holds. The alpha.15 `PRAGMA user_version` primitive (ADR-125) is the substrate the MigrationRunner is built on — the version stamp the alpha line accepted becomes the anchor for the runner the alpha line refused.

## 14. Platform

### Gap: B1 introduces a platform split (full security only on Linux, plugin desktop-only, mobile keep-test); A15 had no such split

The gap analysis frames this as one compound gap with three legs — (a) the egress-security guarantee is Linux-only, (b) the Obsidian plugin is desktop-only, (c) mobile is a read-only-ish "keep-test mode." Each leg has a distinct lineage in the design arc, and the *reason A15 had no split at all* is itself a historical fact worth pinning first.

**Why A15 was "cross-platform by construction" — the security model never touched the OS.** Across the entire alpha line the confinement guarantee lived at the *application* layer, never at the OS/kernel layer. In the pre-alpha.1 Origins the "boundary" was the seven-layer model's **L5 = "the MCP server(s), the sandbox boundary"** — a *conceptual* sandbox implemented as MCP mediation, not OS primitives. That posture is carried, refined, all the way to the end: alpha.14's Security section hardens "network access is operation-scoped and allowlisted," with "the operation manifest is the hard ceiling for network access" and adapter HTTP that bypasses the shared allowlist made to *fail closed* (#1103, `7c843f5d`); alpha.15's §10 makes fail-closed an architecture invariant ("a logged denial is policy, silence is wiring," ADR-125) and confines the agent surface with "no agent tool grants … loopback HTTP binds only loopback hosts, token-auth (`MEMORIA_HTTP_TOKEN`)" (ADR-125/130). None of these mechanisms is an OS sandbox — they are allowlist-before-I/O, read-barrier, and request-envelope checks that run identically on any platform. That is precisely why the gap analysis can say A15 was "standalone … platform-agnostic (no OS-specific enforcement claimed)": the guarantee simply did not depend on the operating system, so there was nothing to split.

**Leg (a) — egress security Linux-only: no OS-level precedent in the alpha line.** B1's brokered egress via Guard ("engines get no sockets") plus **Linux network-namespace (`CLONE_NEWNET`) confinement** is an OS-kernel enforcement mechanism with *no antecedent* anywhere in alpha.1→alpha.15. The closest historical relatives are all application-layer: the seven-layer L5 MCP "sandbox boundary" (Origins) and the operation-scoped allowlist hardened to fail-closed in alpha.14. So beta.1 does not *return* to any earlier era here — it introduces a new enforcement tier that never existed in the alpha arc. The important historical mapping is that B1 does not remove the alpha guarantee on macOS/Windows; there, egress restriction is explicitly "process-architecture discipline, not OS enforcement" — which is *the same class of guarantee the whole alpha line relied on everywhere*. Read against the history, A15's single, uniform application-layer model becomes B1's two-tier model: a new Linux-kernel-enforced tier stacked on top of the pre-existing everywhere-else application-discipline tier. The "split" is the artifact of adding the stronger Linux-only tier, not of weakening the others.

**Leg (b) — desktop-only Obsidian plugin: a re-introduction of a surface alpha.14 had removed, carrying the alpha line's implicit desktop-only assumption.** The Obsidian lineage is long and central. Obsidian entered pre-alpha.1 as one of the three unexamined tool bets — "**Obsidian** as the vault/UI — chosen for its popularity and its markdown-plus-frontmatter substrate" — and sat as **L2, the Obsidian workspace** in the seven-layer model. It was the *required* UI through the middle of the arc: the loopback Obsidian MCP (alpha.1, hardened to HTTPS in alpha.5/6), the QuickAdd PI scripts (alpha.3/5), the persistent-gate Obsidian shell (alpha.7, "Clean-slate Obsidian UI & navigation"), and the full clean-slate rebuild of "the Obsidian surface + type schema" (alpha.10). Throughout, Obsidian was used as a *desktop workspace* — mobile was never a target (see leg (c)). Then **alpha.14 dropped Obsidian as a required surface outright**: the `.obsidian/**` tree, QuickAdd scripts/buttons, and plugin lock were retired (PR #1124, `5a93b142`), every palette action re-homed to a CLI command, and the product declared it "must remain fully operable if the editor is only `vim`, `nano`, or a file browser" (`design.md` §1). The gap-analysis appendix confirms the round-trip: "Removed in A15 … re-introduced in B1: Obsidian as required UI" and "New in B1 … Obsidian plugin + `mem serve` daemon." So beta.1's plugin is a **re-introduction of the pre-alpha.14 Obsidian-required posture**, and its *desktop-only* scope maps directly onto two historical facts: (1) Memoria used Obsidian only as a desktop workspace for the entire alpha line, so "desktop-only" is not a regression from any mobile-capable state — it re-instantiates the assumption that was always there; and (2) the plugin is bound to the `mem serve` **resident daemon**, and the alpha line had *killed* the resident daemon by alpha.14 ("no board daemon survives," §UI/Navigation) — the appendix lists "a resident daemon" as removed-in-A15/re-introduced-in-B1. A daemon-backed plugin cannot run on mobile, which is the mechanical reason the split lands where it does.

**Recorded context for leg (b): alpha.15 deliberately fenced the plugin out and de-committed future editor adapters.** The history is explicit that the Obsidian plugin was *pushed past alpha.15 by design*: the alpha.15 scope boundary states "alpha.15 cannot close with Obsidian plugin implementation counted as release scope." And at alpha.15's ADR-consolidation close, the door was left as a *future, ADR-gated* item, not an alpha commitment: "ADR-130 lost stale CLI+plugin/second-wave wording (#1208)" and "future editor adapters now **require their own ADR** rather than inheriting an alpha.15 commitment (#1203, `d5131811`)." So beta.1's desktop-only plugin is exactly the "second-wave editor adapter" that alpha.15 explicitly deferred and stripped of any inherited commitment — its arrival in beta.1 is the trajectory alpha.15 set up, not a break from it.

**Leg (c) — mobile "keep-test mode": no precedent as a target, but built on a hard-won alpha substrate.** Mobile is the one leg with *zero* lineage as a design target: the terms mobile/tablet/iOS/Android do not appear anywhere in the alpha.1→alpha.15 history. macOS itself surfaces only obliquely, as "launchd" in alpha.14's list of external schedulers ("cron, systemd timers, launchd, Task Scheduler, or a human-run script") — the named platform surface of the alpha installer was **Linux (via WSL2) and Windows**, per ADR-64's "two-OS topology." The WSL2/Windows framing is itself the ADR-29 lineage the task hint flags: alpha.4's `test-env.md` designed "a WSL2 process-test env alongside the Windows production env," keeping GUI tiers on Windows, and native-Windows install support landed in alpha.5 (#570, #538) while the full native-Windows *runtime* port (ADR-64) stayed deferred and was only reconciled-as-historical at alpha.15 (ADR-64 "lost stale `-WithHermes`/`--with-hermes` installer-flag claims," #1207). So beta.1's mobile mode is **new with no alpha precedent as a platform**. What is *not* new is the substrate that makes a daemon-less mobile mode possible: "synced markdown + Bases, no daemon" is only feasible because the keep-set was made plain, tool-independent markdown over several versions — alpha.11's "files own meaning," alpha.12's deliberate rule that "the keep-set stays plain markdown" even after SQLite became the working-state authority, and alpha.14's declaration of `knowledge/`+`capabilities/` as OKF Knowledge Bundles "consumable without Memoria's gates, MCPs, or Hermes runtime" (ADR-107). Beta.1's mobile keep-test mode is therefore a *new application* of a hard-won, incrementally-built alpha property (markdown-owns-meaning + OKF consumability), not a return to any earlier mobile design — there was none.

**Net historical shape.** A15 had no platform split because its correctness/confinement guarantees were application-layer and thus uniform across OSes, its only required *surface* had been reduced to "any text editor," and mobile was never contemplated. Beta.1 creates a split on three independent axes: it (a) adds a Linux-kernel egress tier with no alpha precedent on top of the pre-existing uniform application-layer discipline; (b) re-introduces the pre-alpha.14 Obsidian plugin — the "second-wave editor adapter" alpha.15 explicitly deferred and ADR-gated — bound to a resident daemon the alpha line had removed, inheriting the alpha era's always-desktop use of Obsidian; and (c) targets mobile for the first time, with a no-daemon read-mostly mode that stands entirely on the plain-markdown/OKF keep-set built up across alpha.11–alpha.14.

## 15. Onboarding / seeding

The gap-analysis §15 files this whole subsystem under "(new-in-B1)" and states the single gap as: **A15** has "a seeded sample corpus [that] exists as a test/onboarding fixture (alpha.10/11 sample-vault, `sample:` flag)," while **B1** centers "an onboarding wizard … an 'install → grounded answer ≤30 min' contract, and a half-built ~8-source sample corpus with a planted tension/gap." That gap bundles three distinct elements with three distinct lineages, traced separately below. The load-bearing historical fact underneath all three: the *onboarding* sample-vault the gap analysis attributes to "alpha.10/11" actually lived for exactly **one** version (alpha.10) and was **removed at the alpha.11 reset**; what survived into alpha.15 was a different artifact — the seeded-*error* gold set — which is a correctness test fixture, not an onboarding seed.

### The half-built ~8-source sample corpus with a planted tension/gap

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** A bundled onboarding corpus is a **late arrival with a single-version life**, not a hard-won property. It appears for the first time in **alpha.10** (dated 2026-06-22, "Hermes/runtime governance + onboarding"), introduced by **ADR-111** as part of the clean-slate onboarding review that produced ADR-111–119. The design history records it exactly: "A **bundled sample vault** ('mediterranean-diet', ~8 sources / ~15 claims, deliberately half-built) ships in the installer scaffold under `.memoria/samples/mediterranean-diet/` … with `Memoria: load sample vault` / `remove sample vault` QuickAdd commands (`load-sample-vault.js`, `remove-sample-vault.js`) that copy into / clean `catalog/`+`notes/` without overwriting user work (`f96b31ab`, `28540227`, ADR-111 retained by ADR-112)." The `sample: bool` frontmatter flag the gap analysis cites was introduced alongside it so "Maintenance's 'New this week' digest exclude[s] sample docs and the remove command find[s] them" (alpha.10).

**Prior states of the same element.** There was no seeded corpus before alpha.10. The onboarding *surface* did evolve earlier, but as empty-state guidance, not a seed: **alpha.8** added "New `src/spaces/{inbox,library,knowledge,project}.md` space dashboards … with first-run empty-state copy (e.g. Inbox: 'Inbox empty? That's the goal.')" (commit `9aba2c65`), and **alpha.9** added "Day-one empty-state guidance across the four spaces — `spaces/inbox.md` gains a 'First actions' `[!suggestions]` callout … the callouts turn the empty state into an onboarding surface" (#781). Those addressed the *empty* new vault; the alpha.10 sample vault was the opposite move — pre-loading state so the payoff was reachable in one sitting.

Then the element **changed by deletion one version later**. At the **alpha.11** clean-slate reset the corpus was removed outright: the history's alpha.11 "retired/superseded" list records "**The bundled `mediterranean-diet` sample skeleton** (old `folders.yaml` `.memoria/samples/…`) → removed: alpha.11 seeds 'only test projects and captured sources created through the alpha.11 pipeline' (§20)." So the onboarding sample-vault existed at alpha.10 only. What carried forward under a similar name was the **seeded-error gold set**, a separate lineage born in the same reset: alpha.11 shipped `alpha11-seeded-errors.json` (a "frozen seeded-error gold set" with planted classes including `missing-contradiction`, `crafted-injection`, `unwarranted-claim`, `stale-as-current`), re-baselined at alpha.12 (`alpha12-seeded-errors.json`), and at **alpha.15 ported to the four-type schema as `alpha15-seeded-errors.json` while `alpha12-`/`alpha11-` were deleted** (PR-M #1180, PR #1219; "a port not a build"). That alpha.15 fixture is a correctness gate keyed by `(operation, bundle, mode, provider, model, params)` licensing non-sandbox use — not an onboarding artifact. The gap analysis's "(alpha.10/11 sample-vault, `sample:` flag)" therefore conflates two things the history keeps distinct: the alpha.10 onboarding corpus (removed at alpha.11) and the alpha.11→alpha.15 seeded-error test fixture.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** B1's "half-built ~8-source sample corpus with a planted tension/gap" is a **near-exact return to the alpha.10 mediterranean-diet spec** — same size envelope (~8 sources), same defining property (the history's alpha.10 phrase "deliberately half-built" recurs verbatim as B1's "half-built"). It reverses the alpha.11 §20 removal, restoring an element that had been absent across alpha.11–alpha.15. The "planted tension/gap" fuses the two alpha lineages that the reset had split apart: (a) the alpha.10 ADR-112 pedagogy of a *visible coverage gap the learner finishes* — "Opening with the map 'sells Accumulate via the visible gap — something a from-scratch vault cannot do but the seed can'" — and (b) the surviving seeded-error fixture's planted defects (the `missing-contradiction`/tension classes). So on this axis B1 returns the design to the **alpha.10 era**, recombining the removed onboarding seed with the retained seeded-error idea.

**Recorded context.** The history pins both *why the alpha.10 form was adopted* and the constraint B1 inherits. Adoption: "Done for real the knowledge loop takes months, so a one-sitting tutorial can't reproduce the payoff (writing from a *dense* claim graph) without pre-existing state; a seeded half-built corpus the learner *finishes* is the resolution, doubling as an end-to-end test fixture and demo" (ADR-111, alpha.10). The non-negotiable constraint on any such seed, recorded as a decision: the seed is "**authored from real sources, never LLM-generated** — 'provenance *is* the product; because learners imitate the form they are shown, a faked seed teaches fabrication of the exact thing the system prevents'" (ADR-111 Decision/Consequences/Alternatives, "backed by the deep-research pass over PKM/sample-DB-pedagogy/onboarding/learning-science"). Removal at alpha.11 was recorded as a scope consequence of the reset's fresh-workspace-only stance (§20), not a rejection of the pedagogy — ADR-112 had explicitly *retained* "seeded sample vault + load/remove commands" even while superseding ADR-111's tutorial spine (alpha.10 ADR ledger).

### The onboarding wizard (guided seeding flow: import → extract+index → optional relevance prefilter → single digest confirm → first Ask)

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** There is **no onboarding wizard anywhere in the alpha line**; alpha.15's onboarding is doc-based and command-based, not a guided flow. The alpha mechanisms were: the ADR-112 **destination-first tutorial arc** (alpha.10), the `load/remove sample vault` **QuickAdd commands** (alpha.10), the empty-state **callouts** (alpha.8/9), and the alpha.10 "first-run welcome seed" `home.md` ("Welcome to Memoria… everyday navigation lives in the left-pane rail," ADR-115). The only recorded precursor to a *guided* onboarding is **ADR-113 (alpha.10), "Co-PI-guided conversational onboarding," recorded as `proposed`/deferred** — "The AI-native end state is the Co-PI walking the first loop in conversation." It was deliberately not built: "the ADR-112 doc arc is the script the agent layer would dramatize, so it must exist and stabilize first; building the agent layer against a moving doc flow would mean syncing two moving targets" (ADR-113). Its precondition was recorded as "a stable doc arc + a Co-PI onboarding skill" (ADR ledger).

**Prior states of the same element.** The "onboarding" object changed shape repeatedly but never became a wizard: from **inherited pipeline-ordered tutorials** (pre-alpha, the seven `01-set-up-from-zero`…`07-find-new-sources` files) → to the **alpha.10 destination-first arc** (`01-orient`…`07-make-it-your-own`, "orient by *reading* the Co-PI's coverage map → capture → distill&connect → draft → verify → close the loop → graduate," ADR-112, supersedes ADR-111's spine, commits `00978952`/`b361acad`). The re-derivation reasoning was recorded: the old spine "is the ontology sequenced — it starts on the wrong object (the fleeting note … not how a researcher enters) and draws a loop as a line," and setup was excluded because it "has one right way and teaches no Memoria idea by doing — it is how-to." The end-state guided walk was left as the deferred ADR-113.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** The B1 wizard **picks up the deferred ADR-113 thread** — the guided walk-the-first-loop onboarding that alpha.10 recorded as the intended AI-native end state but never shipped. On this axis B1 does not return to an earlier *shipped* era (there is none) and does not continue an existing shipped trajectory; it **realizes a design that stood proposed/deferred since alpha.10**, now as a wizard rather than a conversational Co-PI script. Its interior steps re-express prior anchors: "import → auto extract+index" maps onto the alpha.14 verb-first CLI (`work import`/`capture`, `enrich`), the "single digest confirm" onto the `digest` operation, and "prompts first Ask before digests return" onto both the alpha.14 `ask` verb and the ADR-112 destination-first instinct of *orienting by reading first* rather than pipelining from capture. The "optional relevance prefilter" step has no alpha precedent and is B1-specific sequencing. The gap analysis notes B1 itself scopes this as new **alpha.16** work (B1 §11), i.e. still forward of the alpha.15 baseline.

**Recorded context.** The reason a guided onboarding was *not* built during the alpha line is on record (ADR-113, above): it was gated behind a stabilized ADR-112 doc arc and a Co-PI onboarding skill to avoid "syncing two moving targets." B1 moving to build it corresponds to those preconditions being treated as met/superseded.

### The "install → grounded answer ≤30 min" contract (time-to-first-Ask)

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** No wall-clock time-to-first-answer contract exists anywhere in the alpha line. The alpha arc's product gates were of two other kinds, both well established: **correctness bars** (the seeded-error verdict's `recall_min: 1.0`, `false_positive_rate_max: 0.0`, `rollback_completeness_min: 1.0`, alpha.11→alpha.15) and **cost ceilings** ("budget discipline replaces metric discipline … bounded by a cost ceiling (~$1–3/day) rather than by a quality target," ADR-21). The nearest antecedents to a *time-to-value* framing are two, neither quantified: (1) the alpha.10 **"one-sitting" onboarding instinct** — the sample vault exists precisely because "done for real the knowledge loop takes months, so a one-sitting tutorial can't reproduce the payoff" (ADR-111); and (2) the alpha.14 **thin end-to-end cycle ending in `ask`** — "the alpha.14 product loop is complete only when the CLI can run a thin end-to-end cycle" (init→capture→enrich→…→ask), enforced by the `test_alpha14_runtime_gate.py` runtime replay gate (#1134). alpha.14 established that a complete cycle *terminating in a grounded `ask`* should be runnable, but bound it to nothing temporal.

**Prior states of the same element.** There is no earlier form of a time contract to name — this element has no prior state in the alpha line. The `ask` verb it terminates on is itself relatively late: it entered with the alpha.14 verb-first CLI (`memoria … ask`), and "verb-first CLI incl. `ask`" is listed as an A15-present property in the gap analysis appendix.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history.** "install → grounded answer ≤30 min" is a **quantified product SLA with no precedent in the alpha line** — it is neither a return to an earlier era nor a continuation of a shipped contract, because the alpha arc never measured time-to-first-value at all (it measured correctness and cost). It does **continue two unquantified instincts**: alpha.10's "one-sitting" onboarding goal and alpha.14's "thin cycle ending in a grounded `ask`," now fused and given a wall-clock ceiling those versions never attached. As with the wizard, the gap analysis records B1 placing this in new **alpha.16** scope (B1 §11), so it sits forward of the alpha.15 baseline rather than back-mapping onto it.

**Recorded context.** The history records *why* the alpha line gated on correctness and cost rather than time: "The system has **no scalar quality payoff to optimize against** (L3 ceiling forecloses it), so … the nightly loop is bounded by a cost ceiling … rather than by a quality target" (ADR-21), and the seeded-error harness is "the gate to real use … no structural check proves semantic truth" (alpha.14, `design.md` §9). A time-to-first-Ask ceiling is a third gate axis the alpha line's recorded rationale never introduced.

## 16. Reading / annotation

The gap analysis records exactly one gap in this subsystem, framed as **new-in-B1**: "**A15:** reading not centered on Zotero. **B1:** Zotero-first reading; one-way append-only annotation sync keyed by Zotero ID + `dateAdded`; exact-quote → `^pNNNN` anchor bridge; orphan counting; a 'processing card' batch triage. ⇒ **Gap:** B1 adds the Zotero reader + annotation-sync/anchor-bridge pipeline." The "new-in-B1" label is accurate for the *pipeline as assembled*, but the design history shows that four of its five components each have a distinct prior life in the alpha line — and that the alpha.15 "reading not centered on Zotero" state is not a stable baseline but the endpoint of a multi-version trajectory that deliberately demoted Zotero. Each component is traced below.

### Gap: B1 adds the Zotero reader + annotation-sync/anchor-bridge pipeline

#### (a) Zotero-first reading (Zotero's PDF reader as the primary read surface)

**Origin & lineage.** Zotero is one of Memoria's three founding tool bets. Part I records it under "The three tool bets — and the admission that they were unexamined": *"Zotero (with Better BibTeX) as the bibliographic backbone,"* adopted alongside Obsidian and Hermes as *"defaults adopted without evaluating fit."* Its intellectual root is even older: the whole project is *"the crossing of ... Karpathy's LLM-Wiki pattern and Elena Razlogova's *Doing History with Zotero and Obsidian* (a working research methodology)"* — so Zotero-plus-reading sits at the genesis of Memoria. But note precisely *what* Zotero was in the Origins and Old Skeleton: the **bibliographic/capture backbone**, not the reading surface. The Old Skeleton stack is named as *"Hermes/Obsidian/Zotero/ZK,"* and Zotero's role there is citation identity and PDF capture. Reading itself was a **vault-side** activity: alpha.1 shipped a `reading-pipeline` dashboard (Part II dashboard roster: *"board-state, reading-pipeline, discuss-queue, ..."*), where reading meant working `source` notes toward distillation, not reading inside Zotero.

**Prior states of the same element.** The Zotero touch-point changed form repeatedly:
- **alpha.1:** `capture-from-zotero.js` uses Better BibTeX CAYW to pull citekeys at capture.
- **alpha.3 (#525, `f615115b`):** Zotero capture switched *"from Better BibTeX CAYW to the BBT JSON-RPC endpoint"* because CAYW *"returned inconsistent citation-key fields."*
- **alpha.10 (#782):** a *"Zotero Tier-0 catalog stub"* materialized — a QuickAdd flow reading the current Zotero selection over BBT JSON-RPC, recording the load-bearing quirk that *"Zotero's local server rejects any request carrying an `Origin` header."*
- **alpha.11 (§9/§13):** Zotero was reclassified — *"Zotero / JabRef = importers + design references, not authority,"* pulled read-only via the **Zotero Local API** (`localhost:23119`). Reading in alpha.11 is explicitly the Razlogova cognitive loop — *"read → annotate → connect → synthesize → find the gap"* — but the *read surface* is Memoria-owned, not Zotero's reader.
- **alpha.14 (ADR-124, #1123):** Zotero was **de-required outright**. *"The Zotero-specific import surface (CLI `--format zotero-export`, worker operation, runtime helper, capability file `capture-zotero-source.md`) was removed."* An `rg`-based negative gate was added that *"reject[s] any reappearance of ... Zotero import/operation surfaces."*
- **alpha.15:** the standalone-catalog posture carries forward; reading is distillation of checked `digest`/`note` content over the SQLite catalog.

So "reading not centered on Zotero" (the A15 side) is the terminus of a deliberate, five-version demotion: **backbone → capture-only → read-only importer → optional → gated-out**.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1's "Zotero-first reading" **reverses the alpha.11→alpha.14 demotion and re-centers Zotero**, returning toward the Origins-era Zotero centrality that ADR-124 had explicitly ended. But it goes *further than any alpha state*: even in the Old Skeleton and alpha.1, Zotero was the capture/bibliography tool while reading happened in the vault. Making **Zotero's PDF reader the primary read surface** has no precedent anywhere in the alpha line — Origins Zotero was never the reading UI. So this component is *part return* (Zotero as a first-class, day-1 dependency — undoing ADR-124) and *part net-new* (Zotero's reader as the read surface).

**Recorded context.** The history pins *why* Zotero was de-required: ADR-124's Context states *"Alpha.14 makes Memoria a standalone CLI/engine. A required Zotero library or live Better BibTeX auto-export would violate that,"* and the ADR rejected *"keeping Zotero as required authority ('makes the standalone CLI depend on a desktop reference manager')."* The evolution arc summary (Part IV) records that *"Zotero-as-authority conflicted with the standalone direction — ADR-124 made the catalog its own citation authority so the product no longer requires an external reference manager (Zotero survives only 'as importers, not authority')."*

#### (b) One-way, append-only annotation sync (keyed by Zotero ID + `dateAdded`)

**Origin & lineage.** Annotation as an activity is genetically Razlogova (*"read → annotate → connect → synthesize"*), but the concrete *design* for importing Zotero annotations first appears at **alpha.11**. The alpha.11 design specifies: *"import Zotero PDF annotations as candidate anchored `note` Concepts (Memoria stores the canonical quote/span/hash itself; Zotero annotations are intake, not the provenance store)."* The direction and authority split are stated exactly as B1 later ships them — **one-directional (Zotero → Memoria as intake), with Memoria owning the canonical record**. alpha.11 also carried an `extract_annotations` operation in its operation-policy roster (checked by span-entailment).

**Prior states.** This element existed at alpha.11 as a **designed-but-deferred** path, not a shipped one. §13 is explicit: *"Zotero annotations are not an alpha.11 import path; Memoria-owned PDF capture stores canonical quote/span/hash selectors itself."* The deferral has a recorded cause: start-blocker verification *"reached the live Zotero Local API and item/annotation list shapes, but found no annotation sample; the first capture fixture still needs a real annotated PDF"* — and the M0 spike lists *"live Zotero annotation sample"* among items *"not completed by M0."* Then at **alpha.14** the entire Zotero import surface (which would have carried annotation import) was removed and negative-gated.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1's one-way, append-only annotation sync **operationalizes the alpha.11 design that was deferred there and dropped at alpha.14.** The core principles — *one-way*, *Zotero-as-intake-not-authority*, *Memoria stores the canonical selectors* — are the alpha.11 principles restated (a return to the pre-alpha.14 design). The specific mechanism — **keying on Zotero ID + `dateAdded`** and **append-only** application — has no alpha precedent and is new-in-B1 implementation detail. So this is best characterized as *reviving alpha.11's deferred annotation-intake design* with a concrete idempotency key that alpha never specified.

#### (c) Exact-quote → `^pNNNN` anchor bridge

**Origin & lineage.** The anchor is the most **hard-won, multi-version property** in this subsystem — the quote-anchor was built up deliberately, not a late arrival:
- **alpha.11:** the `note` type gained an *optional* **anchor** — `quote, text_sha256, source_id, annotation_ref`, where *"`annotation_ref` = a W3C Web-Annotation selector subset (`TextQuoteSelector{quote,prefix,suffix}` + `TextPositionSelector{start,end}` + optional `page`/`bbox`)"* — designed so a span *"resolve[s] regardless of parser."* The exact-quote selector is born here.
- **alpha.13 (ADR-126):** the anchor fields were **relocated off the type** — `note` *"lost ... `quote`, `text_sha256`, `source_id`, `annotation_ref`"*; the history records *"The excerpt/quote fields relocate to a W3C `TextQuoteSelector` `anchor`, not a type,"* and the note gained a first-class `anchors` field. The exact-quote anchor survives; only its home changes.
- **alpha.15 (ADR-129):** the anchor gained a durability policy — *"anchors are **re-anchored, never silently rebound or dropped** across extractor upgrades (exact-then-fuzzy; failures → orphaned-anchor attention),"* explicitly invoking *"the Hypothes.is lesson."*

So the **exact-quote** half of B1's bridge is the direct continuation of an alpha.11→alpha.13→alpha.15 lineage (W3C `TextQuoteSelector` → relocated `anchor` → re-anchoring/orphan policy).

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** The **exact-quote selector continues the alpha trajectory** unbroken. The **`^pNNNN` target is new-in-B1**: `^pNNNN` is an Obsidian block-reference anchor, and Obsidian-as-surface was dropped at alpha.14 and negative-gated. Bridging an exact-quote selector *to an Obsidian block ref* therefore has no alpha precedent — it exists only because B1 re-introduces the Obsidian plugin. So this component splits: the quote-selector side is a hard-won alpha inheritance; the Obsidian `^block-ref` bridge is a net-new consequence of B1's re-introduction of Obsidian.

#### (d) Orphan counting

**Origin & lineage.** The "orphan" signal has two distinct alpha ancestries. The one that maps directly onto B1's *annotation* orphans is **alpha.15's ADR-129**: anchor re-anchoring failures produce *"orphaned-anchor attention."* B1's orphan counting is the surfaced count of exactly that condition — an annotation whose exact-quote no longer resolves. (A separate, earlier "orphan" notion existed at **alpha.7**, where `claims.base` added an *"Open questions"* view computing orphans via *"`is_orphan: file.backlinks.isEmpty()`"* — but that counts *unlinked notes*, a different object.)

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** Orphan counting **continues alpha.15's orphaned-anchor-attention concept** (ADR-129), presenting it as a count rather than raising the lineage change. No return to an earlier era; a straight continuation of the most recent alpha state.

#### (e) "Processing card" batch triage

**Origin & lineage.** The batch-triage card is the surfaced end of a long agent→human signalling arc:
- **Old Skeleton / alpha.1:** the `inbox/` held *"agent→human message cards (candidate · gap · flag · alert · work-prompt)."*
- **alpha.2 (ADR-51/54):** these were reshaped into the **honesty card** — carrying *"argument-for/against + certainty (never a verdict on a proposal),"* with the explicit rule that *"high-cardinality screening becomes 'one aggregate work-prompt' over a Bases worklist, never N cards, which 'would flood a queue meant to converge to zero and invite select-all-accept.'"* The batch-worklist-over-N-cards principle is fixed here.
- **alpha.15 (ADR-130):** the pattern was hardened — *"high-cardinality decisions become one batch worklist; loudness graded; sessions are bounded, presentation span-first with source evidence adjacent (the Elicit table pattern), `--apply` one click,"* justified by *"review effectiveness collapses beyond ~60 minutes/bounded batches."*

**Prior states / the alpha.14–15 removal.** Crucially, the *Obsidian surface* for these cards was removed in the standalone cut. The gap analysis's §5 records that at A15 *"the review-queue approval surface and the honesty-card inbox-as-Obsidian-category mechanism were removed."* So by alpha.15 the batch-worklist *logic* survived (ADR-130, as CLI attention items) while the *Obsidian card surface* did not.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1's annotation "processing card" batch triage **continues the ADR-51/54/130 batch-worklist pattern** (one worklist over many items, not N cards) — a straight lineage continuation of the honesty-card model. But by rendering it as a **card in an Obsidian surface**, it **returns to the pre-alpha.14 inbox-card-in-Obsidian model** that alpha.14/15 had removed. So, as with (a) and (c), the *pattern* is inherited from the alpha line while the *Obsidian surfacing* is a return to the pre-standalone-cut era.

---

**Summary of the arc.** The §16 "reading / annotation" gap is labeled new-in-B1 because the *assembled pipeline* is new, but the design history shows it is largely a **recombination of alpha material re-surfaced through the re-introduced Obsidian plugin**: Zotero-first reading reverses the alpha.11→alpha.14 Zotero demotion (ADR-124) and pushes past even Origins by making Zotero's reader the read surface; one-way annotation sync revives alpha.11's deferred, Zotero-as-intake annotation design; the exact-quote anchor continues the hard-won alpha.11→alpha.13→alpha.15 selector lineage while adding an Obsidian `^pNNNN` target with no alpha precedent; orphan counting continues alpha.15's orphaned-anchor attention (ADR-129); and the processing card continues the ADR-51/54/130 batch-worklist pattern while returning it to the pre-alpha.14 Obsidian-card surface. The single element with genuinely no alpha precedent is Zotero's PDF reader *as the reading surface itself*.

## Cross-cutting historical arcs

The sixteen gaps in the alpha.15 → beta.1 analysis are not sixteen independent deltas. They cluster into a small number of large historical movements, each with a characteristic shape. Most are **reversals** — an element the alpha line built up and then removed (or removed and then re-derived), which beta.1 now swings back toward an earlier state — plus one **continuation** and one band of **net-new** machinery with no alpha-line precedent. Each arc below is stated as a factual pre-alpha.1 → alpha.N → alpha.15 → beta.1 sequence.

### Arc 1 — The re-host arc: Obsidian-hosted → standalone engine → re-Obsidian

**Participating gap subsystems:** §1 Host/runtime/distribution, §2 Vault structure, §10 Surfaces/navigation, §14 Platform (partly §16 Reading).

This is the single largest arc and the one beta.1 §11 itself flags as "new alpha.16 scope." Obsidian was one of the three unexamined pre-alpha.1 tool bets ("Obsidian as the vault/UI — chosen for its popularity and its markdown-plus-frontmatter substrate," Part I). It was foundational through alpha.1–alpha.7: the Old Skeleton's three-layer model made "the vault (Obsidian folders) = durable store," and alpha.7 was a dedicated *"Clean-slate Obsidian UI & navigation"* release (persistent-shell four-space model, ADR-81). The demotion began at alpha.11 (Obsidian kept "only as … a control panel, not a writer") and alpha.12 (*"CLI-first, Obsidian demoted to optional adapter"*). Alpha.14 *"Removes Obsidian … from the required baseline"*; alpha.15 is explicitly *"an engine and integrity checkpoint, not an Obsidian plugin release … No alpha.15 slice adds … an Obsidian plugin, plugin manifest, command-palette command, Inspector panel"* (alpha.15 Scope Boundary), shipping over "CLI, scoped MCP, and loopback HTTP." **beta.1 reverses the direction to before alpha.14**: the Obsidian plugin is "the only researcher UI," with 5 surfaces / 2 rich panes and a `ViewSpecRenderer` (§10), a resident `mem serve` daemon under LSP lifecycle (§1), Memoria nested as a subfolder in the user's existing vault (§2), and the plugin desktop-only with mobile keep-test (§14). The arc's endpoint is not a return to the alpha.7 UI but the re-establishment of Obsidian as the *required* host that alpha.14 had removed.

### Arc 2 — The catalog-authority arc: markdown → SQLite → markdown

**Participating gap subsystems:** §7 Catalog/bibliographic authority, §2 Vault structure (`record.md`), partly §4 Frontmatter.

The catalog's storage substrate traces a there-and-back. Pre-alpha.1 and alpha.1 metadata lived in Obsidian markdown backed by Zotero + Better BibTeX (ADR-05/06). **Alpha.12 flipped it to SQLite** via the keep-test (ADR-122): *"The thing you keep if Memoria dies must be markdown files in OKF, plus a portable bibliography export. Everything else is free to be SQLite,"* making SQLite SSOT for the catalog with `references.bib` a *materialized* projection. Alpha.13 (ADR-124) made the SQLite catalog its own citation authority; alpha.15/ADR-125 hardened the split-authority rule (*"SQLite is the authority for records … markdown files are the human corpus"*), with `work_id`/provider IDs the primary runtime keys and citekeys demoted to aliases (§7). **beta.1 re-materializes the record as markdown**: `record.md` holds full CSL-JSON as the fidelity/re-import authority, citekey is pinned at import as the primary key, and `bibliography.bib` is generated from the stored CSL-JSON (§7) — placing bibliographic authority back in a markdown file, the pre-alpha.12 locus, though now co-located per-work rather than in an Obsidian note.

### Arc 3 — The typed-concept arc: accretion → four-type build-up → flatten

**Participating gap subsystems:** §3 Concept/type model, §4 Frontmatter discipline, §8 Discovery/gap analysis (argument-graph project).

This maps directly onto history arc (c). The type model began as accretion: the Old Skeleton's *"~15 typed notes"* grew through ADRs 47, 50, 78, 117, 119 to a *"12+ types across three bundles"* zoo (ADR-126). Alpha.5 added the thesis/argument-graph/warrant layer (ADR-77/78/79). Alpha.11 collapsed knowledge to a three-node graph (digest/note/hub); **alpha.15 is the peak of the typed model** — exactly four Concept types (`note` with `mode: claim|question`, `work`, `hub`, `project` as argument-graph frame), each with a per-type schema (`.memoria/schemas/types/*.yaml`), ULID identity ("identity ≠ filename ≠ title"), and a relation-typed `links:` frontmatter map validated against `{supports, contradicts, extends}` (§3), landed as PR-B2 (#1164), *"the largest single change in 15."* **beta.1 flattens this to a file-class enum**: a flat frontmatter `type` with 13 class values and no per-type schema files, no `hub` type, no note claim/question mode, no ULID (identity is path/citekey-derived), and no typed relation-links map (§3); the argument-graph `project` becomes a folder of files (§8). The typed-concept structure built up over alpha.5→alpha.15 is replaced by a flat classification.

### Arc 4 — The verdict/frontmatter arc: in-file → universal → meaning-only-DB → back-in-file

**Participating gap subsystems:** §4 Frontmatter discipline, §5 Integrity/review markers, §3 (identity/links).

This maps onto history arc (d) and extends it one step. Early frontmatter was per-type, optional, and *carried verdict state* — `check_status` lived in the file. **Alpha.14 forced universal frontmatter** (PR #1157: *"universal-concept-frontmatter enforcement … `id`/`standing`/`check_status`"*). **Alpha.15 inverted it to meaning-only**: verdicts moved entirely to SQLite (*"`check_status` … is not written to frontmatter at all, and a gate asserts frontmatter carries no verdict fields"*), frontmatter appeared on every Concept, and PR-AC (#1197) *closed* the field set with a validator that *"rejects undeclared Concept frontmatter fields"* plus an `x.*` escape hatch (§4). **beta.1 swings back on two axes**: (a) frontmatter appears *only on Memoria-created files* (human/freehand files carry none, "absence of all `mem-*` markers = human"), and the reject-undeclared validator + `x.*` namespace are dropped, "flags never block" (§4); (b) in-file provenance/verdict markers return — `%%prov:h|m|mc%%` block markers plus `mem-prov`/`mem-taint`/`mem-flags` keys (§4, §5) — re-establishing an in-file verdict layer that alpha.15 had explicitly moved out of files and into the DB. beta.1 keeps the marker as a *claim* requiring DB agreement, not as authority, but the in-file marker itself is the pre-alpha.15 locus returning.

### Arc 5 — The review-gate arc: present → removed → reintroduced

**Participating gap subsystems:** §5 Integrity/review/gate, §11 Agent/autonomy, §2 (`inbox/`), §16 (processing card).

The Old Skeleton shipped *"a structurally blocking human review gate: a card cannot promote until the human sets `review_status: approved`,"* plus the Inbox and the honesty card (Part I; alpha.1 baseline §5 "The Inbox and the honesty card"). The arc then runs through the autonomy re-derivation: alpha.11 retired the Inbox, and **alpha.15 removed the approval surface outright** — quarantine-and-verify (ADR-128), the principle *"checked ≠ approved,"* with *"the review-queue approval surface and the honesty-card inbox-as-Obsidian-category mechanism … removed ('approval no longer confers correctness')"* (§5). **beta.1 reintroduces a review/disposition surface**: a dedicated Review main-pane view with a ribbon pending count, batch worklists (promote/link/drop/snooze; apply/reject/defer), one-click revert, and annotation "processing card" triage (§5), alongside the restored `inbox/` staging folder (§2). The disposition surface that was first-class in the Old Skeleton and removed at alpha.15 returns — though beta.1 frames it as disposition of proposals under the still-standing "no-write-time-oracle" rule, not as the promotion-blocking approval gate of the Old Skeleton.

### Arc 6 — The Zotero arc: required → optional → primary

**Participating gap subsystems:** §7 Catalog/bibliographic authority, §16 Reading/annotation, §9 Ingest.

This maps onto history arc (h)'s Zotero thread. Zotero (with Better BibTeX) was one of the three founding tool bets — *"the bibliographic backbone"* (Part I), required at alpha.1 via ADR-05/06. **Alpha.13 demoted it**: ADR-124 made the catalog its own citation authority and *"absorbed the old Zotero-authority ADR-06,"* so Zotero survives *"as importers, not authority."* Alpha.14 removed it from the required baseline; alpha.15 states *"Memoria does not require a live Zotero library"* (optional) (§7). **beta.1 re-centers Zotero as primary**: Zotero import is day-1, reading is *"Zotero-first"* with Zotero's PDF reader as the primary read surface, annotations sync back one-way keyed by Zotero ID + `dateAdded`, and PDF acquisition runs through a "capture in Zotero" handoff (§7, §16). The arc lands past its own origin: Zotero is not merely required again but is the primary reading/capture surface, with the annotation-sync/anchor-bridge pipeline (§16) net-new on top.

### Arc 7 — The enrichment-graph arc: expansion → contraction

**Participating gap subsystems:** §9 Ingest/enrichment graph, §6 Machine judgment (per-field authority merge), §8 Discovery (topic/concept graph).

This maps onto history arc (h)'s enrichment thread. Pre-alpha.13 metadata rode Zotero/BBT. **Alpha.13 built the multi-source enrichment subsystem** (capture→enrich split over OpenAlex, Semantic Scholar, PubMed, Unpaywall, Crossref, arXiv with per-field provenance; ADR-123/124). **Alpha.14 imported the citation graph itself** — OpenAlex authorship/primary-topic/keyword/concept/MeSH/SDG edges + Crossref relations into `work_graph_edges`. **Alpha.15 hardened the merge to its widest form**: static field-authority table, deterministic blob replay, `provider_coverage = full|partial|degraded`, and new `field_provenance`/`enrichment_runs`/`provider_payloads` tables (PR-F #1173; §6, §9). **beta.1 contracts sharply**: the graph narrows to outgoing reference edges + capped cited-by only (no OpenAlex topic/concept/keyword/mesh/SDG/authorship edges, no Crossref relation edges); enrichment becomes empty-field-fill only (the "JabRef rule," OpenAlex as a singleton), with no per-field authority merge, no `coverage=degraded` state, and no `field_provenance`/`enrichment_runs` tables (§9, §6); discovery becomes citation-graph-only (snowball ≤2 + monitor), dropping the topic/concept-graph-driven discovery (§8). The subsystem expanded across alpha.13→alpha.15 and contracts to a refs-plus-cited-by core at beta.1.

### Arc 8 — The judgment-and-autonomy contraction (continuation, not reversal)

**Participating gap subsystems:** §11 Agent/autonomy, §6 Machine judgment/LLM allocation, §8 (argument-graph gap engine).

Unlike arcs 1–7, this one **continues** the alpha-line trajectory rather than reversing it. History arc (a) traces autonomy from *"seven specialist Hermes profiles"* (ADR-02) → five / one Co-PI (ADR-48, alpha.7) → the profile system *"deleted from the product entirely"* by alpha.14/15, leaving *"a single read-only Co-PI posture"* plus ADR-130 read-API. **beta.1 carries this further in the same direction**: *"Memoria is not an agent, and beta.1 adds none"*; any agent (first-party or the researcher's Claude Code/Cursor) is a *client* under the ADR-130 contract — quarantined, read-scoped, "never file/terminal/exec/send" (§11). The machine-judgment side contracts on the same vector: alpha.15's ADR-129 layered judgment included a **Tier-2 LLM tension judge** (retrieval-grounded, abstain-first, proposer-never-authority); **beta.1 ships no Tier-2 tension judge**, reducing contradiction surfacing to NLI within the retrieved set (§6). This is the one large arc where beta.1's direction is a factual *extension* of the alpha line (moving judgment further out of the resident system) rather than a swing back toward an earlier era.

### Arc 9 — Net-new operational machinery (no alpha-line precedent)

**Participating gap subsystems:** §12 Security/OS confinement, §13 Cost/snapshot/migration, §14 Platform split, §15 Onboarding/seeding, and the write-time exfiltration/keychain layer of §12.

Several beta.1 gaps have no reversal shape because they have no alpha precedent to reverse toward — the gap analysis lists them under "New in B1, not in A15." The design history records these as absent or uncentered before beta.1: cost/measurement was *"present but not the centered machinery"* at alpha.15, whereas beta.1 centers a full BYOK cost/price-table/circuit-breaker machinery (§13); no migration runner was centered before, whereas beta.1 names a `MigrationRunner` as a launch blocker (*"beta is the first version whose data must survive later versions,"* §13); alpha.15 security was standalone and *"platform-agnostic (no OS-specific enforcement claimed),"* whereas beta.1 introduces Linux network-namespace (`CLONE_NEWNET`) confinement with a per-OS keychain and a VaultWriter exfiltration linter, plus a platform split (Linux-only security guarantee, desktop-only plugin, mobile keep-test) (§12, §14); and onboarding, a test/onboarding fixture at alpha.10/11, becomes a centered guided seeding wizard with an "install → grounded answer ≤30 min" contract (§15). These do not participate in a there-and-back movement; they are the operational-hardening band that appears for the first time in beta.1, several of which beta.1 §11 itself scopes as new alpha.16 work built on top of alpha.15.

---

**Shape summary.** Seven of the nine arcs are reversals of movements the alpha line executed: five swing beta.1 back toward a pre-alpha.11/pre-alpha.14 state (re-Obsidian host, markdown catalog authority, in-file verdict markers, the review/disposition surface, `inbox/`), and two contract subsystems the alpha line had expanded (the enrichment/citation graph, and — via the flatten — the typed-concept model). One arc (Zotero) reverses *and overshoots*, landing past its own alpha.1 origin as the primary reading surface. One arc (autonomy + machine judgment) continues the alpha-line direction. One band (cost/migration/OS-confinement/platform/onboarding) is net-new with no alpha precedent. The pivots common to the reversal arcs — Obsidian removal (alpha.14), the SQLite catalog boundary (alpha.12), meaning-only frontmatter and verdict-to-DB (alpha.15), the approval-surface removal (alpha.15), and Zotero demotion (alpha.13/14) — are the same alpha-line decisions that beta.1's host pivot (Obsidian plugin + local daemon) unwinds.
