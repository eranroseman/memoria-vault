## New bullets (parent doc did not analyze these)

### §1 bullet 5 — Python package → single-static-binary distribution
**[from-reports: adjacent]** Parent §1.4 covered the plugin distribution + "binary-acquisition" story but not the artifact-type change itself. The reports ground the B1 side in the host pivot: `design §16` acquires the engine as `uv tool install memoria` or a checksummed version-matched binary, and `req` Install ("clean-machine install reaches first value … under 30 minutes") + the keep-test are the drivers. The reports do not frame "Python package → static binary" as a *distinct* decision with its own rationale — it rides the §1 host decision (`design §11` new-alpha.16 scope), so from the reports' POV this is a consequence of the plugin/daemon pivot rather than an independently reasoned change.

### §2 bullet 5 — `references.bib` → `bibliography.bib` rename; new `local-only/` folder marker
**[from-reports: silent]** Parent §2 listed the `Memoria/` layout but analyzed neither the rename nor `local-only/`. The reports treat both as keep-test / egress mechanics, not first-class decisions: the portable bibliography is a Survival-claim artifact (`req` Survival — "plain markdown plus standard bibliography files if Memoria dies"), and `docs-bar` (line 123) records that beta.1 keeps the `.bib` generated from stored CSL-JSON rather than a BBT auto-export "second source of truth with built-in staleness." `local-only/` is the folder face of the egress model — the exfiltration linter / local-only respect that `design §10` and parent §12 already center. No report discusses the specific filename or the folder-as-marker as a delta; they are naming/packaging under mechanisms the reports do cover elsewhere.

### §3 bullet 7 — controlled-vocabulary substrate dropped (`vocabulary.md` / `vocab` / `classify` / `research_area`+`methodology` facets)
**[from-reports: silent]** Parent §3 touched only the hub's single vocab-tag. This is a genuine coverage gap the reports settle firmly, and it was the highest-confidence omission in `verify` (lines 480–482: "the controlled-vocabulary knowledge substrate is not mined"; `system/vocabulary.md` as SSOT, `vocab` command, `classify` op at floor 0.6/near-tie 0.15). From the reports' POV the drop is **deliberate and doctrine-driven, not a silent loss**: `if-design` (line 180) reframes OpenAlex topics/concepts as an explicitly-labeled **`enrichment-only (unverified)` charting tier**, never a curated controlled vocabulary, and (line 197) rules that on any revival "only human-certified gap stubs — never raw machine facets — may feed" query drafting. `docs-bar` (line 110) is the governing reject: machine-materialized frontmatter facets "violate meaning-only frontmatter … suggestions-in-YAML mix provenance into the meaning layer." So B1 replaces a curated faceting SSOT with an unverified enrichment tier plus freeform tags, on the meaning-only-frontmatter and no-machine-facets doctrines.

### §4 bullet 3 — researcher-set `superseded`/`superseded-by`/`reading` returned to frontmatter
**[from-reports: adjacent]** Parent §4.3 noted `reading`/`superseded` as producer-extension keys in passing. The reports make this a *demanded addition*, not merely a return: `paper-adv` finding 5 [HIGH] ("No supersession concept: a retracted or corrected work stays clean-provenance forever") is the origin — its recommendation is exactly "a researcher-settable … superseded/invalidated flag that down-ranks and visibly labels affected anchors in Ask," and `regression` R2 promotes the export gate to refuse on a cited superseded/retracted work (§18(2)). So from the reports' POV the frontmatter keys exist because supersession needs a **human-set, in-file, keep-test-survivable** marker with real retrieval/export consumers; the A15 "moved DB-side" framing meets the reports' rule that *machine* verdict/stage state stays DB-side while *researcher-set meaning* (`reading`, `superseded`) is legitimately in-file (`design §5` "the only pipeline-adjacent frontmatter is researcher-set, meaning-bearing decisions").

### §5 bullet 4 — derivation-graph + two-tier demotion propagation dropped
**[from-reports: adjacent]** Parent §5.2 quoted `design §11`'s "stronger than alpha.15's propagation-only demotion" but did not analyze the derivation graph as a dropped mechanism. The reports confirm B1 carries no source-changes-cascade: `design §11` (line 980) explicitly contrasts B1's per-block pre==post gate against "alpha.15's propagation-only demotion," and `design §1` (line 119) renders what was a "demotion cascade" as "one batch worklist" of per-row toggles. `history` (line 677) traces the rollback unit across versions (alpha.11 output-quarantine → alpha.12 `derivations`-edge CTE → alpha.15 two-tier demotion with `stale` flags), establishing the derivation graph as a real A15 mechanism now replaced by per-block hash divergence + footprint-scoped reconciliation. `regression` does not list this among R1–R6; its "Checked and intact" sweep treats the demotion path as an authorship-asymmetry divergence, i.e. a recorded, not silent, simplification.

### §5 bullet 5 — apply-rate rubber-stamp detector dropped
**[from-reports: adjacent]** Parent §5.1 already described the surviving mechanism without naming the A15 detector: it quoted `eval §3`'s "two-sided bands (sustained accept ≳90% = rubber-stamping, ≲20% = mistuned generator)" and the quarterly gate-efficacy sampling. So from the reports' POV the *capability* — detecting rubber-stamping from disposition telemetry — is **retained**, but as a review-time analytic band and a quarterly false-reject re-judging sample, not the A15 automatic apply-rate detector wired into the surface. `design-alternatives` graft 10 (track total attention cost incl. review) and the `req` Review bar ("reviewable without creating a catch-up queue") are the doctrines the band serves. The delta is mechanism-form, and the reports show the honesty function survives.

### §6 bullet 5 — {0,1,2}-ordinal checked-input gap scoring + saturation band dropped
**[from-reports: covered-adjacent]** Parent §8 already analyzed the argument-graph/saturation gap engine as dropped; this bullet is the ordinal-scoring facet of the same delta. The reports make the disposition explicit: saturation is **deferred to the alpha.16 writing module, not deleted** — `docs-bar` (line 19) "the alpha.16 writing module is largely pre-designed … saturation with a refutation floor and researcher stamp," and (line 52) "Saturation as an operational stop signal … all numbers placeholder" is an alpha.16 queue item. `if-research` (lines 706, 753) reframes the concept away from a fixed ordinal entirely: "Operationalize saturation as declining novelty yield, not a fixed count … cannot be pre-registered as a number." So B1's retrieval-coverage counting replaces the {0,1,2} ordinal because the reports reject fixed-ordinal saturation as a beta.1 mechanism and hold the argument-graph version for alpha.16.

### §8 bullet 3 — Librarian persona + `steering.md` priority-weighted discovery dropped
**[from-reports: silent→adjacent]** Parent §8 covered discovery narrowing and §11 covered agent externalization, but neither named the Librarian; `verify` (lines 411–416) flagged this as a high-severity omission. The reports resolve it two ways. (1) The Librarian's *external-API gap-driven discovery* is a **reject on the three-host egress allowlist**: `docs-bar` (line 99) "Librarian searched external APIs from gap questions … violates the three-host egress allowlist … alpha.16+ decision, never silent creep." (2) Priority-weighted ranking is **replaced by transparent occurrence-counting**: `docs-bar` (line 82) keeps only "the minimal hint (topic list …), membership proposals as shadow-first," and `if-design`/`if-research` ground the citation-graph occurrence-count ranking (BibliZap) as the deterministic substitute for `steering.md`-weighted surfacing. So from the reports' POV the Librarian's discovery-steering is dropped as an egress-and-determinism decision, with `steering.md`'s program-memory function unre-homed for beta.1.

### §8 bullet 4 — ADR-95 nightly/always-on discovery rejection reversed
**[from-reports: adjacent]** Parent §8 covered the bounded citation-graph discovery and the charter amendment obliquely; this bullet makes the reversal explicit. The reports ground it in the charter itself: `req` Deliberate Exclusions was **amended 2026-07-03** — "bounded citation-graph discovery (snowball ≤ depth 2 + a capped citation monitor; identifiers-only egress; output never enters Ask or gap denominators) is in scope; open-ended search and recommender feeds remain out." `if-design` (line 298) ships "snowball+monitor" as the discovery mechanism with the scheduled monitor. So the reports confirm exactly the gap doc's reading: once the no-daemon premise falls (the resident daemon, parent §1.2), the scheduled monitor becomes admissible and the ADR-95 rejection's basis dissolves — recorded as a charter amendment, not silent creep.

### §9 bullet 3 — `work interview` elicitation step dropped
**[from-reports: silent→adjacent]** Parent §16 cited `docs-bar`'s reject of the "Mandatory ingest interview" (which "duplicates annotations→notes"); this §9 bullet is the direct A15-loop version. The reports add a material nuance the one-line delta omits: a lightweight **"interview hook" survives** inside the digest pipeline — `if-design` (line 138, step 6) "**Interview hook** — 2–3 machine why/how questions + a 'connects to / conflicts with [[…]]' prompt," and (lines 219, 240, 321) the human-"digest" trigger fires "digest + interview," with connections coming "only from local linking + the interview hook, labeled suggestions." So from the reports' POV what is dropped is the **mandatory standalone `work interview` elicitation loop**; a 2–3-question hook seeding note-composition is retained, and the mandatory version is a recorded `docs-bar` reject.

### §9 bullet 4 — Semantic Scholar enrichment provider dropped
**[from-reports: adjacent]** Parent §9.1 covered the enrichment-graph narrowing to refs + capped cited-by. The reports sharpen "dropped" to **deferred with a named revive-trigger**: `if-design` (line 110) "**Semantic Scholar is deferred** — the first named post-beta expansion, revive-trigger: 'discovery recall or reading triage measurably suffers without TLDRs/recommendations,'" restated (line 266) and graded **adapt** in `if-research` (line 204, "optional, degradable tertiary source … never a hard dependency"). `paper-bar` (line 57) independently keeps it off the beta.1 allowlist because machine hypothesis-generation "needs off-allowlist egress (Semantic Scholar etc.)." So from the reports' POV S2 is not removed on the merits but scheduled as the first post-beta enrichment expansion, gated by a measured recall/triage trigger.

### §10 bullet 4 — ADR-101 `spaces/` + `_nav` Navigator + three-places model dropped
**[from-reports: silent]** Parent §10 (bullets 1–3) did not cover the spaces/navigation layer. The reports ground the drop in the interaction-model replacement rather than a spaces-specific rationale: `design §1`'s five interaction-mode surfaces (Capture/Reading/Ask/Compose/Review) are the substitute, and `docs-bar` (line 129) rejects the adjacent "deadline/task-PM project layer" and "second write-capable surfaces" ("forensics = read-only inspector inside Obsidian"), consistent with retiring the places/queues navigation. No report analyzes `spaces/`, `_nav.md`, or the three-places framing by name — from the reports' POV they are collateral of the surface-model pivot, unre-homed and unmourned.

### §10 bullet 5 — qmd → in-DB FTS5 + sqlite-vec + bge/deberta substrate swap
**[from-reports: silent]** Parent §10 had no retrieval-substrate bullet, and `verify` (line 476) flagged the swap as "mischaracterized as convergence." The reports treat the *resulting* stack as the validated default, independent of the A15 qmd baseline: `paper-adv` Survivors lists "Hybrid BM25+embedding RRF, small-k MMR, anchored paragraphs, no graph/agentic memory" as a decision "attacked from multiple angles and failed to refute" (Hu et al. 2026, Buchmann & Gurevych 2026 "both legs load-bearing"), and `regression`'s §11 convergence list names "FTS5 + sqlite-vec local retrieval" among alpha-continuous items. So from the reports' POV the endpoint (local FTS5+vec+embeddings+NLI, no external retriever) is strongly grounded; what the reports do *not* record is the A15 spike verdict that qmd had out-scored sqlite-vec — i.e. the reports support the destination but not the "concrete swap of a declined substrate" framing the gap doc adds.

### §11 bullet 2 — `.memoria/plugins` policy-gate (`pre_/post_tool_call`) hook system dropped
**[from-reports: adjacent]** Parent §11 covered the agent-as-quarantined-client contract but not the policy-gate hooks. The reports ground the B1 replacement in the single-writer spine: `design §2` routes all machine writes through one supervisor-held **VaultWriter** and agent writes through the request-envelope VaultWriter applies, and `paper-adv` Survivors ("Single-writer VaultWriter … block-level read-only") + `regression`'s intact "single-writer + staging" spine are the doctrine. The A15 mechanism (a per-tool-call fail-closed hook forcing external-adapter writes through the policy core) has no report-level analysis; from the reports' POV its function — no write reaches the vault except through the enforced writer — is preserved by construction, so the hook layer is superseded rather than lost.

### §12 bullet 3 — third-party-plugin-interference named a security corruption class
**[from-reports: adjacent]** Parent §12 centered the exfiltration linter/keychain but not this threat class. The reports show it is a **docs-history borrow re-homed into B1, not a novel invention**: `docs-bar` (line 44, "Hostile-plugin defense") records it as "a named CRITICAL corruption class (format-on-save silently rewrites machine spans, flips taint, triggers rollback loops)" with startup detection, formatter-exclusion, and `data.json` secret auditing — and `design §10` (lines 923–927) implements exactly that. From the reports' POV this class exists *because* B1 re-hosts on Obsidian (parent §1.1), and the defense is a recovered deleted-doc mechanism, grounded and re-homed.

### §13 bullet 4 — `overrides.jsonl` + tiered DB-loss replay + streaming-replication leg dropped
**[from-reports: adjacent]** Parent §13 covered the git-snapshot batch substrate and the R5 backup-status regression. The reports settle both halves. Streaming replication is an **explicit decline**: `design §18` (line 1301) "streaming replication is production hardening beyond a `restore --verify`," and `regression` R5's §18 resolution declines the streaming-replication RPO for beta.1 — grounded by `research-evidence` (lines 255, 278) on Litestream #1083 (a silent-replication failure with healthy-looking metrics), which is why B1 prefers `VACUUM INTO` snapshots + verify-restore. `overrides.jsonl` + tiered restrictions-reapply/permissions-re-earn (A15, `history` line 677: Litestream-style replication "doctor-reported, never a runtime dependency") is replaced by researcher decisions in frontmatter + a decide-events CSV. From the reports' POV the streaming leg is a recorded decline; the `overrides.jsonl` replay tiering is dropped without a dedicated report defense (nearest: the batch-protocol/one-click-revert substrate that parent §13.2 covers).

### §13 bullet 5 — row-hash-chained journal + git-anchored `journal-head` tamper-evidence dropped
**[from-reports: adjacent]** Parent §13.2 analyzed the journal replacement (git-snapshot batch vs SQLite-journal cascade) but not the tamper-evidence apparatus. The reports establish that A15 genuinely had it (ADR-127: the SQLite journal is "row-hash-chained," git owns "the tamper-evidence root … `.memoria/journal-head` anchor — 104's git-as-root, kept"), and `history` (line 677) dates `journal-head` to alpha.15. B1's substrate is the external git-dir + batch protocol (`design §6`), whose commit history is itself hash-chained. So from the reports' POV the precise thing dropped is the **DB-row-level** hash chain + the `journal-head` anchor, not tamper-evidence wholesale (git's commit chain remains) — a nuance the gap doc's "cryptographic tamper-evidence apparatus is dropped" flattens (also flagged in the review of the gap doc).

### §13 bullet 6 — derived DB + payloads relocated out of the synced vault to a per-device data dir
**[from-reports: silent]** Parent §13 mentioned the external git-dir but not the DB relocation. The reports ground this strongly and give the rationale the gap doc omits: `design §5` (lines 362–363, 437, 450) puts `memoria.db`, payloads, and the git-dir under `~/.local/share/memoria/<vault-id>/` "outside the synced tree, per-device," and `research-evidence` (line 255) supplies the why — "putting a live DB in Dropbox/Syncthing is categorically corrupting (Zotero KB, Syncthing #4242); only plain-text merges survive sync … the DB rebuildable-from-files and outside any synced tree." `verify` (line 262) adds the payload-recovery-class nuance (content-addressed replayable blob → per-device operational-ledger "backed-up not rebuilt"). So the relocation is a report-backed sync-safety decision, resting on the DB being derived/rebuildable.

## One cross-cutting pattern the reports establish

Several new "dropped in B1" bullets are, in the reports, **deferrals with named revive-triggers or alpha.16 hand-offs**, not deletions on the merits: Semantic Scholar (§9.4, "first named post-beta expansion"), saturation/ordinal gap scoring (§6.5, alpha.16 writing module), the Librarian's external-API discovery (§8.3, "alpha.16+ decision"), and streaming replication (§13.4, "production hardening beyond a restore --verify"). And several "new in B1" security/threat items are **re-homed docs-history borrows** rather than inventions (plugin-interference §12.3; the single-VaultWriter supersession of the policy-gate hooks §11.2). The gap analysis states these as flat A15→B1 deltas; the reports attach the deferral horizon or the re-home provenance to each.
