# beta.1 open questions — a rethink (requirements → research → design → trade-offs → migration)

Date: 2026-07-05. Rethink pass over the beta.1 open research questions (design §8
/ requirements §6). The 14 questions cluster into **five problem classes plus a
set of measurement/decision-only items.** Each class is treated by the rethink
method: requirements first, then how the class is solved well elsewhere (cited,
primary sources), then the clean-slate design, trade-offs, and a migration
sketch into the beta.1 design. Sources were gathered by web research against
primary literature; caveats the researchers flagged are kept inline.

**Status note (2026-07-07):** this file is a reference analysis, not the active
decision ledger. `decisions.md`, `data-structure-analysis.md`, and
`query-mechanism-analysis.md` supersede it where they disagree. In particular,
Q10's algorithm choice is now MMR baseline / facility-location fixture-gated /
DPP deferred, and Q14's licensing mechanics are settled but the actual source
list remains open.

**One result governs three of the classes:** *human + AI is frequently worse
than the better of the two alone* (Vaccaro, Almaatouq & Malone 2024, Nature
Human Behaviour, n=106 studies, Hedges g = **−0.23**;
https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-024-02024-1) — losing specifically when
the AI is better than the human. So "the human gate helps," "the researcher will
notice the anomaly," and "review improves correctness" are **hypotheses to
measure, never assumptions.** That is the through-line.

## Class A — The review gate, reviewer load, and efficacy (Q3, Q4, Q5)

**Requirements.** For a single researcher who is the sole reviewer: (a) test
falsifiably whether reviewing machine output improves correctness; (b) find the
sustainable review load before quality collapses; (c) measure whether the tool
delivers real payoff — all at n=1.

**Research (primary):**
- Human+AI < best-of-either on *decision* tasks; wins only on *content creation* — Vaccaro 2024 (above). *When the AI is more accurate than the human, the combination loses.*
- A wrong machine suggestion drags the reviewer **below their own unaided baseline** (0.299 vs 0.357, p=0.0018), and an *interpretable-but-wrong* explanation does the most damage (0.262) — Jacobs et al. 2021, Transl. Psychiatry (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7862671/).
- Explanations **raise acceptance without raising accuracy** — Bansal et al. 2021, CHI (https://arxiv.org/pdf/2006.14779).
- The load-bearing failure is **calibration**: humans can't judge their own or the model's error rate, so "discretion" doesn't selectively catch errors — Green & Chen 2019, CSCW (https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3359152). Its 3-principle test (accuracy / calibration / fairness) is the falsifiable gate spec.
- Automation bias: with a decision aid, following erroneous advice rose the wrong-decision risk **~26%**, and ~**7%** of pre-correct answers flipped to incorrect — Goddard et al. 2012, JAMIA (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3240751/). Accountability (record a reason) reduces it — Skitka.
- Accept/override rate alone is **uninterpretable** — clinicians override 46–96% of alerts and many overrides are *correct*; you must re-judge a sample against ground truth — Felisberto 2024 (https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/14604582241263242).
- **Sustainable load:** vigilance for rare targets decays within **15–30 min** (Mackworth lineage; https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8060034/); code-review defect detection is best at **200–400 units** and **falls after 60–90 min** — SmartBear/Cisco (industry, n=2,500; https://static0.smartbear.co/support/media/resources/cc/book/code-review-cisco-case-study.pdf).
- **n=1 done rigorously:** autobiographical design needs genuine need + real reliance + fast tinkering + **record-keeping** + long-term use — Neustaedter & Sengers 2012 (https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2317956.2318034); causal claims need **≥3 demonstrations at 3 time points, ABAB/multiple-baseline, ≥5 points/phase** — WWC SCD standards (https://ies.ed.gov/ncee/wwc/Docs/ReferenceResources/wwc_scd.pdf); measure **abandonment** as a first-class outcome — Epstein et al. 2020 (https://depstein.net/assets/pubs/depstein_imwut20.pdf).

**Clean-slate design.**
1. **Standing seeded-error probe.** Inject a known rate of deliberately-wrong machine proposals into the normal Review stream; measure the reviewer's catch rate and false-alarm rate (d′). This is the operational Green-&-Chen *reliability* test and the Jacobs *incorrect-recommendation* arm. If seeded errors aren't caught above chance, **the gate is theater** — surfaced, not hidden.
2. **Agreement re-judging, both directions.** Periodically re-judge a sample of accept *and* reject decisions against ground truth; track the **correct→wrong flip rate** (automation bias) vs wrong→correct. Healthy gate: wrong→correct ≫ correct→wrong. Apply-rate bands (≳90% or ≲20%) flag rubber-stamping/disengagement — but only the re-judged sample is decisive.
3. **No persuasive explanations.** Proposals show the machine's *self-rebuttal* (argument-against) + coarse certainty, never a polished rationale that inflates acceptance (Bansal, Jacobs). This is already the honesty-card rule; the research makes it load-bearing.
4. **Load caps as first-class constraints.** Review in **short bounded batches** (≤~60–90 min, ≤~a few dozen units), never an open streaming approve-queue; targeted checkpoints on high-consequence / low-confidence items, not per-item.
5. **Efficacy = an ABAB protocol, not a vibe.** Gate-on / gate-off reversed ≥3 times, ≥5 measurements/phase (WWC); incident-logged genuine dependence (Neustaedter); **abandonment tracked** (Epstein); payoff attributed against the null-workflow baseline as *events*, never a synthetic score.
6. **Remove the gate where the machine measurably beats the human** on a task class (Vaccaro) — the honest consequence of "human+AI can be worse."

**Trade-offs.** The seeded-error probe spends real attention on fake items (keep the rate low; count them as calibration, not work). ABAB gate-off phases mean windows with unreviewed machine writes — contained by no-write-time-oracle + revert, acceptable for a research tool, not for irreversible actions. Where it flips: if the researcher is measurably better than the proposer on a class, keep the gate there; if worse, drop it.

**Migration.** beta.1 §4.3 already instruments the Jacobs calibration check and §6 has disposition bands. **Add:** the standing seeded-error stream; the both-directions flip-rate re-judging; the ABAB efficacy protocol + abandonment metric (into the evaluation protocol); the "drop the gate where AI>human" rule. Q3/Q4/Q5 move from *open* to *resolved: here is the measurement design* (the answer is a protocol, because the value is genuinely unknown until measured — that is the honest resolution).

## Class B — Writing from the knowledge graph (Q6, Q7, Q8, Q9, Q13)

**Requirements.** Compose a draft from the argument graph via canvas → outline →
draft: how canvas and outline relate; how the canvas behaves at scale; how to
build the synthesis "slice" per outline node; how draft insights write back to
the graph; where deterministic extraction ends and free prose begins.

**Research (primary):**
- **Q8 slice:** "Lost in the Middle" — U-shaped positional degradation (75.8% first → 53.8% middle, *below* the 56.1% no-context baseline at 30 docs); put the strongest anchor at the slice **top or bottom**, don't pad the middle — Liu et al. 2023 (https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.03172). Whole long-context beats top-k RAG by **7.6–13%** when it fits; "Self-Route" recovers it at 39–65% lower cost — Li et al. 2024, EMNLP (https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.16833). Under ~200k tokens, **skip retrieval, pass it whole**; else prepend section context to each chunk (20 chunks best) — Anthropic Contextual Retrieval (https://www.anthropic.com/news/contextual-retrieval). Retrieve at **section/whole-note granularity** (recall 52→71%) — LongRAG (https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.15319). "Small-to-big / parent-document / auto-merging" is the direct **section-expanded-anchor** blueprint (https://docs.llamaindex.ai/en/latest/examples/retrievers/auto_merging_retriever/). Context is **non-monotonic — cap the slice** (Databricks, 2,000+ runs; https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.03538).
- **Q6 canvas↔outline:** genuine two-way exists **only when map and outline are the same node tree** (Scrivener/Gingko/Workflowy are synchronized views of one structure; even Scrivener's card is a *separate synopsis field decoupled from draft prose*). A free spatial arrangement → linear order is a **lossy, many-to-one inference** (spatial-hypertext linearization problem; Shipman & Marshall, https://people.engr.tamu.edu/shipman/spatialhypertext.html). → **map→outline one-way** is the defensible design (confirms beta.1 §1.3). JSON Canvas already encodes both cases: `file` node = pointer (two-way), `text` node = standalone.
- **Q7 canvas scale:** 2D result layouts **do not beat ranked lists** (Chen & Yu meta-analysis: not significant) — Hearst 2009, Ch.10 (https://searchuserinterfaces.com/book/sui_ch10_visualization.html); node-link readability **collapses above ~20 nodes** (matrix wins) — Ghoniem et al. 2004 (https://hal.science/hal-00340790v1); **moving nodes destroys the mental map** — Misue et al. 1995 (https://doi.org/10.1006/jvlc.1995.1010); **premature formalization makes users reject the system** — Shipman & Marshall CSCW 1999 (https://doi.org/10.1023/A:1008716330212); focus+context for scale — Furnas 1986.
- **Q9 write-back:** provenance is a **link to the source, never inlined text**, and drafting *generates* new permanent notes — Ahrens/Zettelkasten (https://zettelkasten.de/posts/concepts-sohnke-ahrens-explained/); Matuschak's "Executable strategy for writing" makes "**write new notes as needed**" a first-class step (https://notes.andymatuschak.org/Executable_strategy_for_writing); use **transclusion/block-reference, not copy** (single canonical source; https://maggieappleton.com/transcopyright-dreams); mark new notes with a **maturity tag** (seedling→evergreen).
- **Q13 extract vs compose:** constrained decoding **drops negation** ("`=` for `!=`") — Shin 2021, confirmed (https://arxiv.org/abs/2104.08768); format restriction degrades reasoning — Tam et al. 2024 (https://arxiv.org/abs/2408.02442), *contested* by dottxt (poor prompting, not structure per se; https://blog.dottxt.ai/say-what-you-mean.html); **interleave** free reasoning with constrained emission (+10 pts) — CRANE, ICML 2025 (https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.09061).

**Clean-slate design.**
- **Slice builder (Q8):** whole-note if under the per-node token budget; else the section-expanded anchor with the **section/document header prepended**; place the strongest anchor at slice **top or bottom**; **cap** total slice size well under the model window; a cheap Self-Route falls to fragments only for narrow factual nodes. Index notes at section/whole-note granularity.
- **Canvas → outline, one-way (Q6):** the outline is the canonical linear order; the canvas is the spatial thinking space; re-derivation is explicit/opt-in (already beta.1 §1.3). Two-way only ever if they become the same node tree — they are not.
- **Canvas at scale (Q7):** always ship a **list/outline as the primary or fallback** surface; node **positions are stable, persisted, user-owned** (never auto-relayout existing nodes); **infer/suggest** structure, never force tags/edges upfront; **offer the non-spatial view past ~20 nodes**; focus+context for larger graphs.
- **Draft → graph write-back (Q9):** a first-class in-draft "**extract to atomic, concept-oriented permanent note**" action that (1) creates the note in the store, (2) leaves a **transclusion/back-link in the draft (no copy)**, (3) attaches provenance as a link to the source record + a **maturity tag** (seedling), (4) is triggerable at the **squeeze point** (a draft section accumulating many loose references). Never auto-written — a PI-gated action (no-write-time-oracle).
- **Extract/compose boundary (Q13):** deterministic/structured extraction owns **only closed-vocabulary, machine-checkable slots** (IDs, links, tags, citation keys, enum status, ordering); all **semantic content — claims, negation, quantification, hedging, prose — is generated as natural language first and resolved to structure in a second pass**; alternate free reasoning with constrained emission; never constrain at the point of semantic commitment.

**Trade-offs.** Whole-note slices cost tokens (mitigated by the budget threshold + router). One-way outline means outline edits don't propagate to the canvas — the residual Q6 measurement (does the user re-derive or diverge?) stays. Write-back-as-a-gated-action adds friction, which is the price of not laundering machine prose into the human note graph.

**Migration.** beta.1 §1.4 already commits coherent-slice synthesis + evidence-sets — **add** the concrete slice-builder rule (budget threshold, top/bottom anchor placement, section-expanded-anchor, Self-Route). §1.2/§1.3 gain the ~20-node list fallback + stable-position governance. §1.4's draft→graph write-back (was open, Q9) gets the transclusion + maturity + squeeze-point design. §1.4's extract/compose boundary gets the closed-vocab-slots rule. Q6/Q7/Q8/Q9/Q13 → resolved.

## Class C — Code execution & the code lane (Q1, Q12)

**Requirements.** Run project code (as an executable warrant and as a
deliverable) without network exfiltration, secret leakage, or vault damage, on a
single user's machine, cross-platform — while the code may be influenced by
untrusted ingested content.

**Research (primary + reference implementation).**
- The **conjunction** "no network + scoped filesystem + resource/time limit + approved-commands" is the accepted minimal baseline; Anthropic states you need **both** filesystem and network isolation, or a compromised run exfiltrates keys or backdoors resources — https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/claude-code-sandboxing.
- Mechanisms: **seccomp-bpf** is a syscall-surface reducer, "not a sandbox" (kernel.org); **CLONE_NEWNET** is the cleanest no-network primitive (Linux); **Landlock** scopes filesystem unprivileged (network only on kernel ≥6.7); **bubblewrap** is the pragmatic FS+netns engine (what Anthropic uses); **gVisor** adds kernel-escape defense but **relies on host cgroups for resource limits** (https://gvisor.dev/docs/architecture_guide/security/); **containers --network=none** are soft (kernel-shared); **WASM/Pyodide** is the *only genuinely cross-platform, default-deny* option but loses the native scientific stack.
- Reference impl for exactly this threat model — Anthropic `sandbox-runtime`: **Linux = bubblewrap + netns + seccomp; macOS = sandbox-exec/Seatbelt; Windows = alpha → run under WSL2** (https://github.com/anthropic-experimental/sandbox-runtime). Stated non-guarantees: domain-fronting can bypass a domain allowlist; **secrets are readable by default** (whole-disk *read* + scoped *write* still exposes `~/.ssh`, `~/.aws` unless explicitly denied); resource limits are not auto-enforced; kernel-shared escape remains (only a VM/microVM removes it — e.g. e2b Firecracker, Modal gVisor).
- Bare Jupyter/subprocess is **not** a security boundary (Jupyter maintainers) — confinement must be an outer layer.

**Clean-slate design.**
- **Minimal sandbox = the Anthropic pattern, with a correction beta.1 was missing — a secret read-denylist.** A run executes in the credential-free worker with **no network** (CLONE_NEWNET on Linux; Seatbelt deny-outbound on macOS; WSL2 on Windows), filesystem **write-scoped to the project code dir + temp** *and* **read-denied on secrets** (`~/.ssh`, `~/.aws`, keychain, the vault DB) — the research's key finding is that write-scoping alone still leaks secrets via read; no keychain access; resource/time caps via cgroups/`setrlimit`/`timeout`; approved-commands as a permission layer, not the boundary.
- **Cross-platform = three per-OS backends** (bubblewrap / Seatbelt / WSL2), matching the alpha.16 host reality — the design earlier avoided dual-engines, but cross-platform code execution genuinely requires this; or ship **Linux+WSL2 only** for beta.1 and treat macOS/Windows-native as hardening.
- **Hardening tiers:** minimal (namespaces) → **gVisor** (kernel-escape defense) → **microVM/Kata** (full isolation) as opt-in for higher-risk runs; minimal is acceptance-eligible, tiers are the before-freeze work.
- **WASM/Pyodide fallback** for pure-Python warrants where the native stack isn't needed — simpler and default-deny.
- **Code lane (Q12):** an *operation* under the client-under-contract boundary (§13), not a resident agent: it proposes code + commands, runs them only in the sandbox, records command/env/output/exit/risk; verification = execution (re-run the warrant).

**Trade-offs.** Three per-OS backends triple the test surface (the reason to consider Linux+WSL2-only for beta.1). WASM loses numpy/scipy. The secret read-denylist must be maintained as a living list. Where it flips: if the researcher only runs pure-Python analysis, WASM/Pyodide is the safer, simpler default.

**Migration.** beta.1 §2.3 already committed a minimal sandbox — **upgrade it** with the **secret read-denylist** (a real, missing control), the per-OS backend naming, and the hardening tiers. §8 Q1 → resolved (minimal committed *with secret denylist*; per-OS backends; hardening tiers named; adversarial validation before freeze remains the open work). Q12 → the code lane is designed as an operation under §13.

## Class D — The exploration / anti-suppression channel (Q10)

**Requirements.** A reserved exploration/anomaly/diversity channel *not* subject
to the relevance ranker, so the loop doesn't suppress disconfirming or anomalous
findings; plus how to surface it and measure it.

**Research (primary; problem is real but the strong form is contested).**
- Digital access **narrows** cited literature (fewer journals/articles, more recent) — Evans 2008, Science (https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1150473). AI risks scientific **monoculture** — Messeri & Crockett 2024, Nature (https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-07146-0). AI **homogenizes ideas** (+10.7% pairwise similarity) — Doshi & Hauser 2024, Sci. Adv. (https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adn5290). Recommender retraining **compounds homogenization** — Anwar et al. 2024, WWW (https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.15013). Search **confirms beliefs** ~half the time — White 2013, SIGIR Best Paper (https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/publication/beliefs-biases-web-search/). *Caveat: the filter-bubble literature is genuinely mixed (Bakshy 2015 Science; Flaxman 2016 POQ find individual choice ≥ algorithm) — present the harm as debated, not settled.*
- Mechanisms to inject diversity/exploration: **MMR** (cheap redundancy reducer; Carbonell & Goldstein 1998, https://doi.org/10.1145/290941.291025); **DPP** (repulsion, exact algorithms; Kulesza & Taskar 2012, https://arxiv.org/abs/1207.6083); **submodular facility-location coverage** with a (1−1/e) guarantee (Lin & Bilmes 2011, https://aclanthology.org/P11-1052/); **xQuAD/PM-2** aspect coverage (TREC winners); a **reserved exploration slate** improves serendipity + long-term experience at scale (Google: Chen et al. 2019 WSDM https://arxiv.org/pdf/1812.02353; Xu et al. 2021 RecSys https://research.google/pubs/values-of-exploration-in-recommender-systems/); **literature-based discovery** (Swanson ABC, cross-silo bridges; https://doi.org/10.1353/pbm.1986.0087) — but *automated LBD evaluation is weak/retrospective*.
- **Surface it separately** — "being accurate is not enough" (McNee et al. 2006, https://grouplens.org/site-content/uploads/accurate-CHI-20061.pdf); users *prefer* diversified lists despite lower accuracy (Ziegler et al. 2005, https://files.grouplens.org/papers/ziegler-www05.pdf).
- **A separate, *salient*, labeled region — but placement is what makes it work.** A faceted breadth-navigation region beat a ranked list for open-ended exploration (90% preferred; higher recall) — Flamenco, Yee & Hearst 2003 (https://flamenco.berkeley.edu/papers/flamenco-chi03.pdf). **Placement governs whether diverse items are noticed at all:** sorting agreeable-first *lowered* satisfaction and challenge-averse readers never scrolled down to relegated challenging items — Munson & Resnick 2010 (https://www.smunson.com/portfolio/projects/aggdiversity/PresentingDiversePoliticalOpinions-chi10.pdf); prominent "obvious" recommendations *inhibit* serendipity by fixating attention — Afridi & Olsson review. A dedicated "what's-filtered-out" widget significantly raised filter-bubble awareness + control (N≈163) — Nagulendra & Vassileva 2014 (https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/2631775.2631811); separate viewpoint channels raised diverse reading vs a ranked feed — NewsCube, Park et al. 2009 (https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1518701.1518772). Serendipity needs **encounter *and* sagacity** — the channel must be salient *and* interpretable, not merely present — André et al. 2009 (http://susandumais.com/creativityandcognition09-fp392-andre.pdf). Explicitly prompting "consider the opposite" reduces confirmation bias by redirecting attention — Lord et al. 1984 / Mussweiler et al. 2000. **Evidence gap (flagged):** no controlled A/B directly tests "separate region vs blended into the list" for a *noticing* outcome — so treat it as a thing to measure, not a settled pattern.
- Surface **contradictions explicitly** — SciFact SUPPORTS/REFUTES (Wadden et al. 2020, https://aclanthology.org/2020.emnlp-main.609/).
- **Metrics:** intra-list diversity (Ziegler 2005), **α-nDCG** (TREC standard; Clarke et al. 2008, https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1390334.1390446), subtopic recall (Zhai et al. 2003), coverage + **serendipity = unexpectedness × usefulness** (Ge et al. 2010, https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/1864708.1864761). *Caveat: beyond-accuracy metrics, especially serendipity, are under-validated against real discovery — "theater vs substance" (https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10762851/).*

**Clean-slate design.**
- A **separate exploration slate**, never blended into the ranked results (Google; McNee; Ziegler) — a distinct "adjacent / anomalous / contrary" surface the researcher sees on its own so anomalies get *noticed*. It must be **salient and explicitly labeled** — not relegated below the fold, where diverse items go unseen (Munson & Resnick) — and **interpretable** (each item says *why* it's here — the "sagacity" half; André et al.), with a "consider the contrary" framing that redirects attention (Lord/Mussweiler).
- **Populated by coverage/diversity over the citation graph, not by relevance:** MMR is the cheap baseline; facility-location stays behind a fixture gate; DPP is deferred by the active decision ledger. This reuses the alpha.16 bounded citation-graph discovery (§10) rather than a new recommender.
- **A first-class contrary-evidence lane:** the local NLI already surfaces REFUTES within the retrieved set — extend it as a standing "contrary evidence" channel (SciFact framing); an empty contrary list is **never** presented as agreement (already a guardrail; the research confirms it).
- **Measured as its own objective** (already a design line): intra-list diversity + subtopic/coverage + honestly-labeled serendipity — **never a synthetic score presented as truth**. And validated by whether the researcher **acts on** the slate, not by the diversity number (theater test).

**Trade-offs.** An exploration slate costs attention and can be noise (serendipity is hard to measure). The accuracy-diversity tradeoff is real (though partly an artifact of ignoring consumption limits — Liang & Bose 2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.15142). Where it flips: if measurement shows the researcher never acts on the slate, it is theater and gets thinned — same discipline as the gate (Class A).

**Migration.** beta.1 reserves an exploration/anomaly channel and measures diversity coverage. The active decision ledger supersedes the earlier "coverage/DPP" wording: ship MMR as baseline, keep facility-location behind the fixture gate, defer DPP, and validate the channel by acted-on rate.

## Class E — Workspace / gate topology (Q11) — a design task, not a research-resolved one

**Requirements.** The gate topology and active-assistance model for the
deep-work-vs-task-work split, without settling the host.

This one has thinner external evidence and is genuinely **owner design work**,
so the honest rethink answer is *scope, don't over-specify*:
- The requirement is the **five UI-framework-independent jobs** (Read / Knowledge / Project / Review / Upkeep) already committed — *what* the workspace does, decoupled from *how* a host renders it.
- Two research touchpoints that do bear: the **deep-vs-task separation is sound** (vigilance/context-switching costs — Class A), and **spatial-vs-list** (Hearst, Class B) says the Knowledge/graph view needs a list/table companion, not a spatial-only surface.
- Everything else (gate triad, left-pane-nav/right-pane-Co-PI, where the user asks for help) stays the owner's job-to-be-done design, kept swappable by the host-neutral read-API. Q11 stays open **as a design task**, not a research question — that is the correct status.

**Honest caveats carried from the research:** human+AI is often *worse* (so the
gate is measured, never assumed); the filter-bubble harm is *debated* (present
Q10 as insurance, not settled fact); beyond-accuracy/serendipity metrics are
*under-validated* (measure acted-on, not the number); no sandbox short of a VM
removes kernel-shared escape (hence hardening tiers); OpenAI Code Interpreter
internals were **not** primary-sourced. Academic MCP connectors (Consensus,
Scholar Gateway, Scite) needed OAuth and were unavailable; findings rest on
web research against arXiv/ACL/ACM/primary docs.
