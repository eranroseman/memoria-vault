
# Tutorial 02: Your first note

**You will end with:** one permanent claim note in `30-synthesis/01-claims/`, authored in your own words, with `maturity: seedling` and `lifecycle: current`.

**Time:** 15–20 minutes.

**You will use:** Obsidian command palette (`Cmd+P` on Mac, `Ctrl+P` on Windows) and the ACP chat pane.

**Prerequisite:** [Tutorial 01](01-set-up-from-zero.md) complete.

---

## What you're about to do

The Zettelkasten method asks you to do one thing with every piece of thinking: write it as a permanent note in your own words. Not a summary, not a copy-paste — your own formulation, your own claim.

This tutorial walks the entire upstream loop in miniature:

1. **Capture** a raw thought (the Zettelkasten "fleeting note")
2. **Think** about it with Socratic (conversation that sharpens the thought without producing output)
3. **Write** the permanent claim in your own words

This loop is the core discipline. Everything else in Memoria — papers, dashboards, projects — serves it.

---

## Step 1 — Capture a fleeting note

Think of something you noticed, read, or wondered about recently. It can be small — one sentence is enough. ("I've been putting off writing because I keep waiting until I fully understand the topic." Or: "Most interventions assume users are motivated but they're actually just distracted.")

**In Obsidian:**
Press `Cmd+P` → type `capture fleeting` → select **Memoria: capture fleeting** → press Enter.

A small input box appears. Type your thought in one or two sentences. Press Enter.

**What you'll see:** A new note appears in `10-inbox/01-fleeting/` with today's date as the filename. Open it — your thought is there, timestamped.

> **Why a fleeting note first?** In Zettelkasten, the discipline is to separate the moment of capture from the moment of thinking. You don't have to get it right in the capture — you just have to get it out. Processing comes next.

---

## Step 2 — Open the Socratic conversation

The Socratic profile is a conversation partner that asks questions. It has no write access to your vault — it cannot create notes, modify files, or add anything to your vault. Your thinking stays yours.

With the fleeting note open:

Press `Cmd+P` → type `ask about` → select **Memoria: ask about this note**.

**What you'll see:** The ACP pane opens on the right side of Obsidian. The Socratic profile greets you, with your fleeting note already attached as context.

---

## Step 3 — Have the conversation

Socratic will ask you questions. Here are the kinds of questions it asks and how to respond:

**"What's the core claim of this thought in one sentence?"**
Try to state it as a claim — something that could be true or false — not as a description. Instead of "I've noticed that I delay writing," try: "Writers delay when they believe their understanding is incomplete."

**"What would make this wrong?"**
Think of a counterexample. ("People who feel confident also delay — so maybe understanding isn't the variable.") This sharpens the claim.

**"What does this connect to in your existing thinking?"**
Mention anything that comes to mind — other ideas, papers you've read, related observations.

Spend 5–10 minutes in conversation. Don't rush toward writing. The conversation is the work.

**Socratic will not produce a note for you.** When you feel you've arrived at one clear, durable claim, close the pane and move to the next step.

---

## Step 4 — Write the claim note

Press `Cmd+P` → type `write claim` → select **Memoria: write claim note**.

A new note opens in `30-synthesis/01-claims/` from the claim-note template. Fill it in:

**Title:** State your claim in one sentence. Make it falsifiable. Example: `Delayed writing correlates with belief in prerequisite completeness, not motivation deficit`.

**Frontmatter fields to set:**
- `maturity: seedling` — already set by the template; keep it
- `lifecycle: current` — already set; keep it
- `topic:` — add one or two terms that describe the area (e.g., `writing-process`)

**Body:**
Write 2–5 sentences in your own words — what the claim means, what evidence or reasoning you have for it, and what it would take to overturn it. Do not copy-paste from the Socratic conversation; write it fresh. This is the Zettelkasten discipline: the note is your formulation, not a transcript.

**sources:** frontmatter:
For now, leave this empty — you don't have a paper source yet. You have only your own thinking. That's valid; not every claim note needs a paper citation.

Save the note.

---

## Step 5 — Archive the fleeting note

Open the original fleeting note in `10-inbox/01-fleeting/`.

In the frontmatter, change `lifecycle: proposed` to `lifecycle: archived`.

This is the Zettelkasten inbox discipline: a fleeting note is a queue item. Once processed, it's archived — it doesn't stay in the inbox. The claim note is the permanent form of the thought; the fleeting note served its purpose.

---

## What you have

Open `30-synthesis/01-claims/`. Your claim note is there:

- Title: one falsifiable sentence
- `maturity: seedling`
- `lifecycle: current`
- Body: your formulation in your own words
- Fleeting note: archived

This is the Zettelkasten permanent note. You wrote it — not the agent. Socratic sharpened your thinking; you produced the artifact.

> **The rule: one note, one claim.** If your body paragraph contains "and" doing real work — two distinct ideas — split it into two notes. Atomic notes are more useful than compound ones because they can link independently.
