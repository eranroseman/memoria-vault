# Memoria literature review — refutation pass (Refute / Tension / Retire)

This is the adversarial counterpart to `_papers/REVIEW-SUMMARY.md`. The first review used a confirm-only vocabulary and reported zero disconfirmations; this pass inverts the method — attack-by-bet, one bet at a time, against the same 401-paper corpus. Of ~60 raw findings (deduped to ~30 distinct ones below), **0 of the 13 surveyed bets survived adversarial scrutiny intact** — every bet has at least one Tension; six carry a Retire or Refute that demands a wording or scope change to its ADRs. The honest summary: the corpus does not "re-derive" Memoria. It endorses the *skeleton* (durable state, nightly batch surfacing, review gate, verbatim warrants, tabular projection) while contradicting most of the *strong* claims layered on top (gate-as-quality-filter, allowlist-as-sufficient-security, atomic-claim-as-unit, frozen-matches-fine-tuned, automatic contradiction detection, self-consistency-as-confidence, spatial-graph-at-scale).

> **Correction (2026-06-21).** This pass assumed `qwen2.5:7b` (local) is Memoria's production engine. It is not — `qwen2.5:7b` is the local **test fixture**; the live engines call an **LLM API**. This *strengthens* the "frozen-matches-fine-tuned" attack into a non-issue (the live engine is an API: not frozen-small, not fine-tuneable), and on-box measurement later showed even the qwen test floor clears the extraction task — so that Retire is resolved, not open. Read every "frozen / cheap / local model" framing below as the **test split**, not the runtime engine. See the corrected alpha.9 recommendations (`docs/releasing/0.1.0-alpha.9/tmp/`) §0c.

## Retire — current bets the literature argues against; replace

- **"Least-privilege allowlists are sufficient security" → "necessary blast-radius control, insufficient against integrity/persistence."** ADR-46 already concedes "MCP is a policy gate, not an execution sandbox." Removing file/exec/send tools genuinely breaks the exfiltration step (InjecAgent: no send tool ⇒ two-step steal cannot complete; AgentDojo: read-only partition cuts write-class attacks) — keep that. But drop the word "sufficient." Reword the memory entry and **ADR-32/46/28/27** to "least-privilege + no-exfiltration-tool is necessary; content-integrity and persistence threats are handled by the gate + provenance + channel separation, none foolproof." (Greshake 2023; Zhan 2024; Debenedetti 2024)

- **"Per-note/per-item human gate is workable" → "sparse, uncertainty-routed gate is workable; item-by-item is not."** AutoResearchClaw's HITL ablation found targeted intervention at 3–6 checkpoints dominates dense per-step approval (which erases automation gains); time-constrained clinicians explicitly *refused* to re-decide trust at every point and wanted one-time validation. Scope **ADR-24**: route to the PI only on high-uncertainty/high-leverage items (lean on LTR triage to gate-by-consequence), offer one-time-validation surfaces, accept that low-stakes writes are not meaningfully human-verified. (Liu 2026 AutoResearchClaw; Jacobs 2021 CHI sociotechnical)

- **"Supersession filter excludes superseded claims by default" → "do not present a superseded claim AS current; keep it retrievable."** ADR-10 itself admits supersession is whole-claim/binary and that partial supersession is unmodeled. TEMPO shows a large share of real needs are cross-period/evolution/trend queries that require *both* the historical baseline and the current claim — hard-excluding the superseded claim removes the evidence those queries need. Narrow **ADR-09/10**: penalize stale-as-current reuse (the FAMA failure), make superseded claims first-class retrievable tagged "superseded by [[X]] on [date]," model partial/scoped supersession. (Abdallah 2026 TEMPO; Wu 2025 LongMemEval)

- **"Frozen + pinned-prompt matches fine-tuning" → "small + light fine-tuning/distillation ≈ large; frozen prompt alone does not."** The canonical "small matches GPT-3" result (Schick & Schütze) buys parity *only* with gradient PET + multi-pattern ensembling + distillation; the frozen priming analog performs far worse. This is the corpus's strongest "small can match large" paper and it is evidence *against* the frozen framing. Retire the equivalence in **ADR-22/24**; the achievable claim is narrower. (Schick & Schütze 2021)

- **"Deterministic ingest pipeline" → "deterministic *validation/parsing*, not deterministic *generation*."** LMQL states some constraints cannot be enforced eagerly and fall back to backtracking, and constraints can "force a model to generate text it would never explore… at worst wrong sentences." A forced-valid output is not a correct one. Scope **ADR-30**: "deterministic" means deterministic parse/validation; budget for retries/backtracking/forced-fill, and re-validate content even on schema-valid items. (Beurer-Kellner 2023 LMQL; Shin 2021)

- **"Cheap local LLM judges suffice for verification" → "precision-flag-then-PI; never auto-accept/reject on the local verdict."** CiteGuard measured a *frontier* judge (GPT-4o) at recall 0.16–0.38 on citation attribution (rejecting 60–84% of correct citations); reaching human parity required agentic retrieval + a 671B reasoning model. KnowledgeBerg shows self-refinement (the obvious cheap fix) *reduces* accuracy on Memoria's exact model class (Qwen/LLaMA/Mistral/Phi). Retire "cheap judges suffice" from the verification-gate bet; restrict the local judge to a high-precision *flag*, and require retrieval-grounded validation (search the source span) for any accept/reject. (Choi 2026 CiteGuard; Zhang 2026 KnowledgeBerg)

- **"Atomic claims are self-contained" → "a claim must carry explicit context fields to be interpretable in isolation."** TEMPO shows a passage's truth is determinate only relative to temporal scope + tense; a bare claim ripped from its paper loses time-window, hedging, and scope. Narrow the atomic-claims bet: require ISO temporal scope, tense, certainty/hedging, and originating section as first-class typed fields. (Abdallah 2026 TEMPO)

- **"Adequate at scale" (spatial/graph surface) → "adequate for tabular/faceted at scale; spatial degrades as density grows."** Head-to-head studies in Hearst all favor text/faceted over spatial: Chen 1998 (2/14 re-find in cluster map vs 14/16 in Yahoo directory), Granitzer 2004 (tree browser beats InfoSky starfield even after revisions favoring it), Russell 2006 (paper beats LSI scatter on 300 docs), Hornbæk 1999 (thematic-map users "got lost," read adjacency as relevance). ADR-103 already flags these exact failure modes. Narrow the **ADR-101/102/103/84** bet: tabular+faceted is the validated default; spatial/argument-graph is analyst-only, small-graph, and must pass a usability check *against a Bases/faceted baseline*, not inherit "at scale." (Hearst 2009)

## Refute — strong general disconfirmations Memoria relies on

- **The gate's load-bearing premise (human + machine > machine) is empirically false in the one corpus study that measured it.** Jacobs 2021 (N=220, within-subject): clinician+ML accuracy 0.356 ≈ clinician-alone 0.357, both far below ML-alone 0.667; an *incorrect* recommendation dragged the human *below* their own no-rec baseline (0.299 vs 0.357, p=0.0018). The reviewer did not filter the error — the error contaminated the reviewer. **ADR-03/57/24**: stop treating "PI approves" as automatically improving correctness; add a calibration check (are PI-approved writes actually more correct than raw engine writes?) or the gate is theater. (Jacobs 2021)

- **Least-privilege provides ZERO protection against the highest-probability attack on Memoria: a poisoned ingested paper.** Greshake's "Manipulated Content"/"Disinformation" attacks (wrong summaries, hidden facts, suppressed contradictions) need no write/send/exec tool — the legitimate read+reason path *is* the attack; they conclude "it is currently hard to imagine a foolproof solution." ADR-32/46 frame the sandbox as containing untrusted *code*, but Memoria's dominant threat is untrusted *data* (every ingest, every discovery fetch). **ADR-32/46/28/27**: add a threat-model section naming the gate as the (imperfect, itself-injectable) integrity control. (Greshake 2023; Debenedetti 2024)

- **On Memoria's exact frozen model + task class, frozen ≠ fine-tuned by a wide margin.** CrossTrace: untuned Qwen2.5-7B scored **0% structural compliance** and 0.221 spark cosine ("near-random") on grounded closed-schema reasoning extraction, emitting "a single undifferentiated paragraph"; QLoRA lifted compliance 0→100%, IAScore 0.828→0.968, cosine 0.221→0.620. **ADR-22/24**: a frozen 7B hits a closed schema reliably only via fine-tuning or strong few-shot; budget for a (still-local, still-governable) QLoRA adapter for novel structured outputs. (Bouras & Fellow 2026 CrossTrace)

- **Durability *without enforced supersession* actively harms continuity.** Memora benchmark: forgetting penalty for long-term-memory agents *grows* with horizon (18.2 weekly → 29.5 quarterly) while stateless LLMs *improve* (32.6 → 17.8) by truncating stale info — "retaining access to older memories without effective forgetting amplifies inconsistency." All memory mechanisms fail multi-hop selective forgetting (≤7%). This *promotes* supersession + the contradictions dashboard from feature to **precondition** for the durable-state bet (ADR-01/46/10). (Uddin 2026; Hu 2026)

- **Atomic decomposition HURTS QA accuracy via information loss — the optimal unit is the contextual round, not the fact.** LongMemEval (cited by REVIEW-SUMMARY as *validating* the atomic store): K=fact alone is the *worst* row (Recall@5 0.282 vs 0.582 for K=value at round granularity; QA 0.518 vs 0.615); facts help only as a secondary *index* key (K=V+fact), not as the stored unit. **ADR-90/56/99**: store the verbatim contextual unit as the canonical retrievable value; treat the atomic claim as an index *over* it. Memoria's "triage over claims, not PDF text" inverts the paper's actual recommendation. (Wu 2025 LongMemEval)

- **NLI inherits cosine's exact blind spot on lexical-overlap pairs — the dedup/supersession shape.** Utama 2021 (HANS): models adopt "shared words ⇒ entailment"; on the lexical-overlap non-entailment subset median accuracy drops below 10%. "Cat chased mouse" ⊨ "mouse chased cat" is precisely a near-duplicate that is actually a contradiction. **ADR-94/09**: require a HANS-style adversarial sanity set (high-overlap, opposite-direction pairs) as an acceptance gate before NLI is allowed to gate dedup/supersession; if it fails, NLI is no better than cosine on the cases that justified it. (Utama 2021)

## Tension — conditions Memoria will hit where a bet breaks; design around them

- **Explanations can *exacerbate* overreliance.** In Jacobs 2021, an incorrect rec + feature-based explanation produced the *lowest* accuracy of any condition (0.262 vs 0.357, p=0.004); confidence scores were flat across correct/incorrect ("clinicians struggled to calibrate"). Do not assume richer agent rationales/confidence make the gate safer; A/B them against actual correction rate, prefer raw source evidence over polished justifications, never display an uncalibrated confidence number. (Jacobs 2021) — **ADR-03/57**

- **Verification asymmetry: the writes worth delegating are the ones most expensive to check.** Feng & Liu §5.5: auditing an artifact "requires the same expertise as building it"; under deadline pressure the gate degrades to "a cursory nod" — the rubber-stamp failure at exactly single-PI scale. Therefore *widening* the gate floods the bottleneck; the correct move is narrow/triage + invest in per-item verification *tooling* (diff/side-by-side source, per-claim citekey+span), not throughput. Set a max-items-per-session / triage threshold as a first-class constraint. (Feng & Liu 2026) — **ADR-03/57/24**

- **AgentDojo's tool-isolation defense (the closest proxy to Memoria's design) fails in exactly Memoria's conditions.** ~7.5% residual attack success, but it breaks when (1) tools can't be planned in advance (the nightly discovery + multi-step triage loop), (2) the task's own tools suffice for the attack (the Librarian's scholarly-API/web-fetch double as SSRF/exfil, 17% of cases), (3) multi-task-without-context-reset enables "wait for the right tool" (long-lived lanes). Require: no outward-network tool reachable in a lane that just ingested untrusted text; per-task tool pinning *before* untrusted data enters context; context reset between tasks. (Debenedetti 2024; Zhan 2024) — **ADR-32**

- **The frozen cheap local model is the *most* injection-vulnerable config in the literature.** InjecAgent: prompted open models (Qwen, Llama2-70B) hit >80% ASR vs fine-tuned GPT-4 at 7.1%; fine-tuning for tool-use was the single biggest resilience factor and the frozen model has none. The allowlist is asked to carry *more* security weight precisely when the model carries less. Do not lean on the local model as any injection backstop. (Zhan 2024; Debenedetti 2024) — **ADR-32**

- **Greshake's "Persistence" attack is a near-exact structural match to the vault.** A compromised model writes an injection into a note; a later fresh session that reads it is re-poisoned — a single gate-surviving injection becomes a permanent re-infection seed, and the gate is itself an injectable LLM. Require data/instruction channel separation (the gate model must not see raw untrusted text as instructions) and provenance flags on agent-written notes pending PI promotion. (Greshake 2023; Zhan 2024) — **ADR-28/27**

- **Durable structured memory underperforms raw long-context on the integrative tasks Memoria exists for.** MemoryAgentBench: long-context (no external store) wins Test-Time Learning and Long-Range Understanding; graph systems GraphRAG/Cognee/Zep land near the *bottom* (23.4/20.6/24.0) below plain BM25 (41.5) and the un-augmented backbone (42.3); the best graph method (HippoRAG-v2, 41.6) only *ties* BM25. For synthesis, feed coherent slices/whole distilled notes, not top-k fragments; benchmark the LRU axis explicitly; demote the typed graph from "the representation" to one optional projection that must earn its place. (Hu 2026; Maharana 2024) — **ADR-01/46/08/79/52**

- **Atomic units are right *only* for single-hop pinpoint lookup and counterproductive for synthesis; the answer often lives in the edge.** MemoryAgentBench: smaller chunks help Accurate Retrieval but hurt Long-Range Understanding; RAG retrieves "only partial information." HotpotQA + GraphWalker: multi-hop reasoning load lives in the *relations between* facts (bridge entities/paths); stripping connective structure drops EM 6.8–11.1 pts. LongMemEval shows atomic wins only the narrow multi-session slice, and via format uniformity not unit-correctness. Scope the atomic win to lookup; make typed edges co-equal retrievable units; require a holistic/long-context reading path for synthesis. (Hu 2026; Yang 2018; Xu 2026; Wu 2025) — **ADR-90/56/99/08**

- **Structure enforcement guarantees FORM, not extraction CORRECTNESS — and the wrapper cannot enforce negation/omission.** Efrat & Levy separate "form consistency" from accuracy (format-correct yet content-accurate 0.2–4.2% on turking); 49% of outputs violated an explicit "don't write why/how" rule, conditional tasks dropped to 3.5%. Agrawal: GPT-3 has "a bias toward outputting a non-trivial answer even where none exists" (invented active meds when none were discontinued) — worse at 7B than 175B. A grammar can force a list of claims; it cannot force the model to *omit* an absent claim or honor polarity. Make existence-gating/chaining ("does X exist?" before "list X") and explicit abstain/N-A mandatory engine stages. State in **ADR-30** that constrained decoding buys structural validity *only*. (Efrat & Levy 2020; Agrawal 2022) — **ADR-30**

- **Constraining decoding straight to the rigid schema can corrupt negation/polarity — the worst error for the contradiction machinery.** Shin 2021 (cited by the first review as validation): GPT-3 constrained to the meaning representation scores *worse* (0.53–0.78) than to a natural-language canonical form (0.62–0.89), with concrete cases dropping a negation (`=` for `!=`) and flipping a predicate. Emit a natural-language intermediate, map to schema with a deterministic resolver (u→c→m). (Shin 2021; Beurer-Kellner 2023) — **ADR-30**

- **Adversarial source spans pass lexical/embedding validators.** Branch 2022: a single "ignore previous instructions" perturbation flips PLM classification (0.42–0.675 success) and the corrupted output passes BLEU/BERTScore/edit-distance. Gate validation must be model-free and content-anchored (verbatim substring / source-span entailment against the actual paper text). (Branch 2022) — **ADR-30**

- **A warrant is not a single span on a single edge.** FEVER: with correct verbatim evidence required, the best pipeline reaches 31.87% (vs 50.91% ignoring evidence); 16.82% of claims need multi-sentence evidence, 12.15% multi-document, and *all* required sentences must be retrieved. Buchmann & Gurevych formalize "citation failure": derived/implicit claims (e.g. computed "1960") have no single verbatim warrant, and recall drops moving up the reasoning chain. Model warrants as evidence-*sets* with single/multi-sentence/multi-hop/implicit type + an "evidence-incomplete"/abstain state; never hard-accept/reject on single-span match; route implicit/multi-hop claims to the PI. (Thorne 2018; Buchmann & Gurevych 2026) — **ADR-08/79/52** + atomic-claims bet

- **The model-free warrant checker is not a truth oracle.** FEVER: inter-annotator κ=0.68 on the 3-way label; regular annotators recovered only 72.36% of evidence super-annotators found (~28% miss), and 21.85% of "wrong" system evidence was actually valid. A substring/entity/year check proves span *existence*, not grounding — necessary, not sufficient; keep semantic warrant judgment as an agent/PI step with an explicit false-negative budget. (Thorne 2018) — atomic-claims / review-gate ADR

- **"Engines propose contradictions, gate disposes automatically" is already half-abandoned, and the corpus backs the retreat.** ADR-10 calls supersession "the least reliably automatable memory capability"; ADR-09 keeps the NLI proposer out of v1. ClawArena: belief revision "fails" on updates forcing re-interpretation of earlier claims (one contradiction round 0% exact-match across all 8 configs; hardest scenario <30% CRS for every config). LoCoMo: adversarial/unanswerable detection 83% below base, temporal reasoning 73% below human. State the bet honestly: engine proposes a *candidate*; the *human* (not the structural gate) sets the directional link; track proposer precision before promoting past candidate-suggestion. (Ji 2026 ClawArena; Maharana 2024) — **ADR-09/10**

- **A future automatic NLI/MaxSAT proposer will inject false contradictions.** ConCoRD (the deterministic mechanism ADR-09 names as future-adoptable): even with per-problem-tuned hyperparameters ~28–29% of belief flips are *wrong*; the optimal NLI model varies unpredictably across domains; β must be tuned on labeled data. Maieutic: reasoning "fundamentally bounded by correctness of explanations" (41% logical-leap, 33% negation-invariant). "Deterministic" ≠ "reliable"; any v2 proposer needs a confidence threshold + human confirmation on every candidate. (Mitchell 2022; Jung 2022; Gao 2023) — **ADR-09**

- **The human's notice-the-conflict step is itself the unreliable bottleneck the filter assumes away — a circularity.** ADR-09's own motivation: "contradictions hide in the long tail… never read side by side." LongMemEval: assistants drop 37–64% on knowledge-update; "ChatGPT tended to overwrite crucial information." The filter is sound bookkeeping *once the link exists*, but neither engine nor human reliably creates it for the long-tail case it was built for. Pair the filter with a measured candidate-proposer recall target before calling the capability "closed." (Wu 2025; Ji 2026) — **ADR-09/10**

- **Off-the-shelf NLI is high-precision, low-recall, miscalibrated, and quality-scales-with-size — wrong end of every curve for a 16GB frozen box.** ExpertQA: TRUE-NLI precision .88 but recall .38 (misses world-knowledge-mediated and multi-source contradictions); recall reached .91 only after in-domain fine-tuning Memoria cannot do. Sainz 2021: zero-shot NLI tops 57.8 F1, default T=0.5 is badly miscalibrated (30.1 vs 45.7 dev-tuned), DeBERTa-xxL beats RoBERTa-large by 7–12 F1 — "use the largest NLI model available." FEVER: even with gold evidence, entailment is the *hardest* stage and SNLI-trained NLI "performed substantially worse." Scope the §3.1 comparator to a high-precision candidate *filter*; add a threshold-fit calibration step on the PI's scarce labels; keep a lexical-overlap/cosine baseline as control and only adopt NLI where it measurably beats it (Ye & Durrett's cheap-regime finding); define contradiction operationally w.r.t. world knowledge; never present NLI silence as "no contradiction." (Malaviya 2024; Sainz 2021; Thorne 2018; Ye & Durrett 2022) — **ADR-09/59/94**

- **One time-restricted judge is not reliable ground truth on this exact task.** FEVER deliberately layered 5-way agreement + super-annotator adjudication + author validation *because* single-pass judgment on claim+evidence is untrustworthy (κ=0.68, ~25–30% evidence miss). The PI gate is structurally that single annotator with none of the redundancy. Add cheap redundancy (engine self-consistency, re-judge stability, PI-vs-engine agreement sampling) and record the expected recall gap as a known limitation. (Thorne 2018) — **ADR-24**

- **Even when mechanical checks pass, a single gate can't tell whether the item answers the question, and oversight is infeasible at machine volume.** AutoResearchClaw T10: an all-zero, uninformative run passed the numeric gate. Alkhatib & Bernstein: "technical infeasibility of oversight" at machine speed, mislabeling novel cases confidently with only after-the-fact correction. Cornelio: machine-hypothesis abundance creates a verification bottleneck manual review can't scale. Pair human approval with automated structural verification (derivability/consistency/contradiction) and a recourse/after-the-fact correction path; match nightly volume with automated pre-filtering. (Liu 2026; Alkhatib & Bernstein 2019; Cornelio 2025) — **ADR-24**

- **"PI absorbs the load" is untested — the supporting paper used scripted, not live, humans.** AutoResearchClaw's HITL result came from scripted interventions ("live researchers would require IRB"). Treat sustainable single-PI load as an *unvalidated assumption*: instrument items/night, time-to-decision, override rate; define a throughput-exceeded fallback (raise uncertainty threshold, batch low-risk auto-accepts). (Liu 2026) — **ADR-24**

- **The LTR loop is a preference-optimization loop that suppresses the disconfirmations Memoria exists to surface — an internal contradiction.** Bisht 2026 (McNamara fallacy): AI augmentation makes scientists publish 3.02× more but *shrinks* topics studied 4.63% and cuts engagement 22%; consensus-priored models converge (effective epistemic sample ≈1) and are biased against OOD anomalies (the quasicrystal class a statistical model "would have discarded"). An LTR ranker trained on what the PI up-votes down-ranks anomalous/disconfirming claims by construction. Reserve a separate exploration/anomaly/diversity channel *not* subject to the relevance ranker; make diversity coverage a separately-measured objective. (Bisht 2026) — **ADR-61/89/92**

- **"Improves knowledge work" has no efficacy evidence and the dominant failure mode is abandonment.** Epstein 2020 (523 personal-informatics papers): abandonment studied in 48; data collection is "burdensome"; the ACTION stage (≈ triage→knowledge-work) is least-studied with "few efficacy studies." Rogers 2006 argues the *proactive personalized-relevance* framing is intractable/undesirable (modeling user intent is "if not impossible"; getting it wrong is "more frustrating") — his endorsed model is non-personalized batch surfacing + lenses, supporting the nightly-batch skeleton but tensioning the LTR ambition. Narrow to "CAN surface candidates IF the PI sustains low-burden feedback"; make feedback implicit from existing gate accept/reject; instrument lapse; require a proximal-outcome check vs an unranked baseline before claiming benefit. (Epstein 2020; Rogers 2006) — **ADR-61/89/92**

- **Self-consistency is not a trustworthy uncertainty signal at 7B, cannot catch stable fabrication, and is undefined for open generation.** Validated only on PaLM-540B / GPT-3-175B / LaMDA-137B; on UL2-20B (3× larger than qwen2.5:7b) it lifts GSM8K to only 7.3%. Wang's own footnote: the LM "cannot distinguish well between correct and wrong solutions"; a fabrication reproduced stably across N samples gets *high* agreement (Liévin: 59% of wrong CoTs had a correct step). Si 2023 confirms it works as a *relative* ranker but "most calibration errors come from overconfidence." And Wang limits it to fixed-answer-set tasks — Memoria's extraction/relations/warrants are open generation, where the consistency metric is an unsolved NLI problem. Use agreement only as a one-sided relative routing signal (low agreement = uncertain; high ≠ correct), only on closed-label steps, validated on the local model first; faithfulness for stable-wrong cases must come from deterministic source-span entailment. (Wang 2023; Liévin 2023; Si 2023) — uncertainty/abstention ADR

- **"Engines reliably abstain rather than fabricate" is false even for frontier models on Memoria's home turf, and CoT *degrades* abstention.** Rao & Callison-Burch: search-enabled GPT-5/Claude-Sonnet-4.6/Gemini-3 still fabricate BibTeX fields (1.8% F) and substitute wrong records (1.0% S), only 50.9% fully correct, −27.7pp on recent papers — "a reliable mitigation must bypass LLM-based metadata construction entirely." Shaikh 2023: zero-shot CoT cut refusal/abstention 78%→25% and pushed "Unknown" items toward committed answers. Reframe: faithfulness comes from *deterministic external grounding* (citekey/DOI resolve, source-span entails), not from the engine choosing to say "I don't know"; keep an explicit instruction-level "Unknown" label; deterministic Zotero/CrossRef resolution for metadata. (Rao & Callison-Burch 2026; Shaikh 2023) — uncertainty/abstention + ADR-30

- **Version-pinning a prompt freezes unknown-quality variance and doesn't transfer.** Lu 2022: order sensitivity is fundamental across 4 orders of magnitude, *worse* for small models (SST-2 swings 51.6→88.7%), and good permutations don't transfer across sizes (Spearman ≈0.05); Zhao 2021: reversing two examples drops 88.5→51.3%. Perez 2021: true-few-shot prompt selection without a held-out labeled set barely beats random and "greatly underperforms" held-out selection — so a pinned prompt is either tuned on a hidden labeled set (a recurring per-schema-change PI annotation cost) or roughly random-quality. Require per-model prompt validation (contextual calibration / entropy-probing); make the prompt-validation labeling cost explicit. (Lu 2022; Zhao 2021; Perez 2021) — **ADR-22/24**

- **"Disposable/regenerated" is safe for data, hazardous for spatial layout.** Card & Moran 1986: a stable conceptual model drives novel-task success (67% vs 25%) and stable object position builds it; Hearst attributes the faceted-navigation win to predictability (Quintura's fluid term cloud faulted for unpredictable term position). ADR-103 already admits "regenerated views erase the PI's mental map." Treat "disposable" as a property of the *data*, not the *layout*: persisted/governed positions should be the *default* for any spatial projection; keep disposable + D3-style enter/update/exit key-join for tabular. (Card & Moran 1986; Hearst 2009) — **ADR-102/103**

- **The frozen model is empirically weakest at the very decomposition the atomic-claims bet needs — a three-way tension.** CrossTrace: untuned Qwen2.5-7B "conflates context and conclusion into a single undifferentiated paragraph"; even fine-tuned + carefully prompted, 41% partial-grounding (fuzzy/unread-section warrants). Either lean hard on grammar-constrained decoding + few-shot and track residual partial-grounding, or treat extracted atoms as candidate notes for PI review, not a faithful store. Add a partial-grounding/not-found flag to every claim. (Bouras & Fellow 2026) — atomic-claims × cheap-local × deterministic-ingest

- **Memoria's "agents judge" vocabulary cues the machine heuristic that erodes the vigilance the gate depends on.** Sundar 2020 (HAII-TIME): foregrounding the algorithmic nature of a flagging system makes users "overtrust machines, underestimate human acumen… cognitively outsource the task" — and the flip side (algorithm aversion after a visible failure) makes trust unstable either way. Present engines as bounded inspectable tools, not "judges with verdicts"; foreground the underlying evidence (PI ground-truths) over the agent's conclusion (PI co-signs). The "agents judge" label may be counterproductive to "PI approves." (Sundar 2020) — **ADR-03/57**

- **Deterministic checks are necessary but do not establish correctness.** ExpertQA: automatic attribution/factuality metrics (AutoAIS, QAFactEval) "fall short in correlating with expert judgements"; 35%/31% of medicine/law attributions incomplete, 51% from sources experts deemed unreliable. Feng & Liu: RAG "does not solve the deeper problem — agents can misinterpret correct data." Scope deterministic checks to what they decide (ID existence, span presence, numeric/contradiction consistency) as *necessary* gates; never claim they *suffice*, and keep multi-source warrants out of single-source NLI auto-rejection. (Malaviya 2024; Feng & Liu 2026) — verification-gate bet

- **The representational stance may be a category error for occasioned knowledge.** Dourish 2004: contextual relevance is relational, dynamic, occasion-particular — it "arises from the activity" and cannot be frozen in advance, as a fixed typed-edge schema does; his best exemplar (spatial hypertext) lets structure emerge through interaction. Frame the typed graph as a recomputable per-occasion projection (consistent with the disposable-projection principle), not the canonical store. *(low severity)* (Dourish 2004) — **ADR-08/79/52**

None. Every surveyed bet drew at least one Tension; the **skeleton** survived in pieces and should be stated as the validated default: durable file state for storage/provenance/audit; verbatim-warrant / store-the-raw-span (Omni-SimpleMem: +53% F1 from returning full text over summaries — the single largest gain); tabular/faceted projection (ADR-101/102/84); nightly *batch* surfacing + a *sparse* review gate with PI as final authority; deterministic external grounding (citekey/DOI resolution) for faithfulness; and supersession-as-typed-edge (now promoted to a precondition, not a feature). The strong claims layered on top did not survive.

## Highest-severity, ranked

1. **Human + machine ≯ machine; wrong engine output drags the reviewer below baseline, and explanations make it worse** — the gate's load-bearing premise is empirically false (Jacobs 2021). Add a calibration check that PI-approved writes beat raw engine writes, or the gate is theater. **ADR-03/57/24.**
2. **Least-privilege gives zero protection against poisoned ingested papers** — the dominant threat is untrusted *data*, not code (Greshake 2023; Debenedetti 2024). Drop "sufficient"; name the gate as the imperfect, itself-injectable integrity control. **ADR-32/46/28/27.**
3. **Frozen qwen2.5:7b scores 0% schema compliance on Memoria's exact task; fine-tuning is the gap** (CrossTrace 2026). Budget a local QLoRA adapter or accept degraded compliance. **ADR-22/24.**
4. **Atomic-fact-as-stored-unit hurts QA; the contextual round is the optimal unit and atomic should be an index** (LongMemEval 2025; Hu 2026). REVIEW-SUMMARY's "triage over claims not text" inverts the evidence. **ADR-90/56/99.**
5. **Automatic contradiction/supersession detection is the least-reliable memory capability; engines propose candidates, the human sets the link** (ClawArena 2026; ADR-09/10's own framing; Mitchell 2022 ~28% wrong flips). Track proposer precision before any promotion. **ADR-09/10.**
6. **NLI replacing cosine is high-precision/low-recall, uncalibrated, size-dependent, and shares cosine's lexical-overlap blind spot** (Malaviya 2024; Sainz 2021; Utama 2021). Make it a candidate filter with a HANS acceptance gate + calibration on PI labels; keep cosine as control. **ADR-09/59/94.**
7. **Durable graph memory underperforms raw long-context and even BM25 on the synthesis tasks Memoria exists for** (MemoryAgentBench: GraphRAG/Cognee/Zep at the bottom). Demote the typed graph to one optional projection that must beat a BM25+dense baseline. **ADR-08/79/52/01/46.**
8. **Self-consistency is not a confidence signal at 7B, is blind to stable fabrication, and is undefined for open generation** (Wang 2023 + footnote; Liévin 2023). Use it only as a one-sided relative router on closed-label steps; faithfulness comes from deterministic grounding, not agreement. **Uncertainty/abstention ADR.**
