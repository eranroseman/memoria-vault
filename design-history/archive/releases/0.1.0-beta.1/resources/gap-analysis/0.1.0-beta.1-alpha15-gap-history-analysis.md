# alpha.15 → beta.1 gaps — design-history analysis

For every gap in [`0.1.0-beta.1-alpha15-gap-analysis.md`](0.1.0-beta.1-alpha15-gap-analysis.md),
this document situates the gap in Memoria's evolution: where the alpha.15 side of the
gap came from, how it evolved step-by-step across the alpha line (reaching back to the
pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton" / Karpathy-Wiki-LLM × Razlogova origins), any earlier forms
the element changed from, and how beta.1's direction maps onto that history (reverts to
a named earlier era, continues an existing trajectory, or has no precedent in the alpha
line).

**Descriptive only** — no pros/cons, no "regression/improvement" verdicts, no
recommendations. Grounded in the verified design-history reconstruction
([`../../design-history/memoria-design-history-alpha.1-to-alpha.15.md`](../../design-history/memoria-design-history-alpha.1-to-alpha.15.md)),
its supporting sources, and `main`'s git history; ADRs used as reference only.

This is the version tracking the **updated** gap analysis — it covers every current gap,
including the later-added ones (the controlled-vocabulary substrate, `steering.md`/the
Librarian persona, the ADR-95 no-daemon reversal, the `work interview` step, Semantic
Scholar, the `spaces/`/Navigator model, the qmd→sqlite-vec swap, the policy-gate hooks,
the third-party-plugin threat class, `overrides.jsonl`/hash-chained journal/DB relocation,
the packaging shift, and the retired-then-re-introduced sample corpus) and the reframed
review-gate (§5), agent-boundary (§11), and onboarding (§15) entries. Sections mirror the
16 subsystems of the gap analysis, followed by the cross-cutting historical arcs.

## 1. Host / runtime / distribution

*Lineage sources: Part I Origins (the three unexamined tool bets); Part II alpha.1 (Hermes runtime + Obsidian ACP + repo-as-install-unit); Part III per-version deltas at alpha.4 (ADR-76 wheel), alpha.7/10 (Obsidian UI peaks), alpha.11 (Hermes demotion + enqueue-only plugin), alpha.12 (CLI-first / no-daemon reframe), alpha.14 (the standalone cut that dropped Obsidian/Hermes), alpha.15 (uv/Typer package, on-demand `serve --watch`, Scope-Boundary deferral of the plugin); Part IV arcs (a) profiles→no-agent, (f) Hermes rise-and-fall, (i) the alpha.7 UI clean-slate.*

### Gap: B1 re-introduces Obsidian as the *required* researcher UI; A15 had removed Obsidian as a required surface

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** Obsidian's *removal* is a late arrival won only at alpha.14, but the thing being removed reaches all the way back to Origins. Obsidian was one of the **three unexamined tool bets** adopted pre-alpha.1 — "Obsidian as the vault/UI — chosen for its popularity and its markdown-plus-frontmatter substrate" — and it entered through Razlogova's *Doing History with Zotero and Obsidian* in the genesis. The pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton" three-layer model made "the vault (Obsidian folders) = durable store," and the researcher's own notes already flagged the tension the whole alpha line then works out: "Obsidian… does not appear to be the best option for a structured knowledge graph."

Obsidian was load-bearing and *growing* through the middle of the line: alpha.1 ran the co-PI inside the `agent-client` **Obsidian ACP pane** and shipped three workspaces (Desk/Library/Studio); the installer even "installs Obsidian if absent." alpha.3 was "the first dedicated Obsidian UI research/build pass"; alpha.4 turned the three workspaces into named intent gates and bundled Commander + Modal Forms plugins; **alpha.7 was a full clean-slate Obsidian UI rebuild** (the persistent-shell four-space model, ADR-81) — the peak of Obsidian investment — and alpha.10 rebuilt the Obsidian surface again.

The demotion then runs across four versions. **alpha.11** kept Obsidian but reduced the plugin to a **control panel, not a writer** — "may mutate only by enqueueing worker jobs" (ADR-121), "because the UI never owned truth." **alpha.12** reframed the system as a "standalone CLI-first integrity engine" with "Obsidian… demoted to *optional adapter, not architectural dependency*" (design §2). **alpha.14** performed the cut: the entire `.obsidian/**` tree, QuickAdd scripts, and plugin lock were deleted (PR #1124), the product "must remain fully operable if the editor is only `vim`, `nano`, or a file browser" (§1), and an `rg` **negative gate** was added to "fail the gate if current runtime or installer paths still require… Obsidian." **alpha.15** confirmed and fenced this: it "is an engine and integrity checkpoint, not an Obsidian plugin release," the keep-test bar is that the corpus stays plain markdown a bare editor/file-browser can navigate, and "future editor adapters require their own ADR rather than inheriting an alpha.15 commitment" (#1203).

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1's "the Obsidian plugin is the only researcher UI" **reverts to the pre-alpha.14 status of Obsidian as the primary/required surface** — specifically the required-UI standing that alpha.12 began demoting and alpha.14 removed outright. It restores a status Memoria held continuously from Origins through alpha.10. Note the inheritance is selective: the B1 plugin carries forward alpha.11's **enqueue-only "control panel, not a writer"** discipline (ADR-121, itself carried verbatim into ADR-130's "a UI mutates only by enqueueing"), so what returns is Obsidian-as-required-*surface*, layered on the alpha.11–15 write-boundary rule rather than on the pre-alpha.11 gated-write model.

**Recorded context.** alpha.15's design index Scope Boundary explicitly excludes the plugin ("alpha.15 cannot close with Obsidian plugin implementation counted as release scope"), and arc (i) records that "because the UI never owned truth, dropping it cost the engine nothing" — the precise property B1 relies on when re-hosting.

### Gap: B1 makes the on-demand `serve` a plugin-supervised resident daemon; A15 ran `serve` on-demand and disclaimed daemon status

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** A resident supervising process is *not* new to Memoria — it is what the **Hermes era shipped and what the alpha.11–15 arc dismantled**. alpha.1 built the product on the external Hermes runtime, whose resident components were the Kanban board (`kanban.db`), the **dispatcher**, the MCP host, and "the API server (port 8642)" — a running control plane, with ADR-63's load-bearing invariant "exactly one Hermes dispatcher per vault." That is the "pre-A15 board daemon" the gap-analysis appendix refers to.

The no-daemon posture was then established deliberately. **alpha.11** demoted Hermes from control-plane to "a specialized library adapter" and introduced the single **worker**. **alpha.12** re-framed the whole system as a "standalone CLI-first integrity engine with three pluggable trigger types (command / file-change scan/watch / schedule)," landing `scan`, `run-scheduled`, and `recover` subcommands and `trigger_type` tagging — the on-demand model in scaffold form. The premise was made explicit as a rejection: **ADR-95's nightly always-on discovery loop was rejected because "an unattended always-on loop conflicts with the no-daemon architecture."** **alpha.15** shipped `serve` in exactly the disclaimed-daemon form: `memoria serve --watch` is a stdlib **polling** watcher over the existing `workspace scan`/`observe-pi-edits` path with `--once` for deterministic gating, and "watcher-as-required-daemon [was] rejected — the accepted form is 'real-time when the on-demand process runs, scan + consumption guard otherwise, guard never retired' (ADR-127), preserving the no-daemon architecture." Distribution was pinned to match: "no always-on process; scheduled work is plain CLI invocations under cron/systemd/Task Scheduler" (ADR-125 Consequences), the design goal arc (f) traces to its root — "One install command, no external runtime, no always-on process."

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1 **promotes** the alpha.15 on-demand `serve` to residency (`mem serve` = supervisor + per-run worker, plugin-supervised with LSP lifecycle, resident while Obsidian is open). The *concept* of a resident supervising process — and specifically the **supervisor/worker split** — echoes the Hermes-era dispatcher-plus-worker shape of alpha.1–alpha.10 that alpha.11 dismantled; in that sense B1 reaches back across the no-daemon interval to a pre-alpha.11 arrangement. The **specific `mem serve`/LSP-lifecycle form has no alpha-line precedent** (the appendix lists it under "New in B1"). The residency-by-default direction is also the point at which the ADR-95 rejection premise no longer holds — §8 records that B1's resident daemon plus scheduled citation monitor "reverses the ADR-95 rejection once the no-daemon premise is removed."

**Recorded context.** The no-daemon commitment is pinned in ADR-127 (the accepted watcher form) and ADR-125 ("no always-on process"), and traced in arc (f) as "the design goal all along." B1 amends the very architecture premise those decisions rested on.

### Gap: B1 demotes the CLI from product to survival floor and drops `ask` from the CLI; A15 shipped a verb-first CLI incl. `ask`

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The CLI began as one of several human channels — alpha.1 lists "the human channels (Obsidian, CLI, Telegram)" with Obsidian primary and the conversational co-PI living in the **Obsidian ACP pane**, not the CLI. The CLI's rise to *product* is a distinct alpha.12→alpha.15 trajectory. **alpha.12** reframed Memoria as a "CLI-first standalone integrity engine" (design intent); a caveat noted the standalone `memoria` binary and `ask/answer/resume` interview CLI were design-only there, and ADR-122 at that stage still held that "CLI is inspect/verify/administer only, **not a PI action surface**." **alpha.13** ran the "CLI-first SSOT" documentation pass. **alpha.14** elevated it decisively: the ~2274-line `cli.py` shipped the resource-oriented grammar as "the product surface for alpha.14, not operator-only" (§5), with `ask` in the roster and `--workspace` "because alpha.14 is a standalone workspace, not an Obsidian vault adapter." **alpha.15** made it verb-first and carried ADR-72's PI-direct-access principle: "every routine action reachable by the researcher directly without conversing with an agent," where "the direct-access surface is the CLI plus the read-API transports," and the roster includes `ask` alongside `new note`, `work add/enrich/interview/digest`, `link`, `check`, `export`. ADR-130 also names "the CLI [as] the no-daemon recovery surface."

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1 reverses the alpha.12→alpha.15 CLI-as-product trajectory: the CLI (`mem`) becomes a "survival + ops floor" (`serve/capture/import/export/rebuild/backup/restore/migrate/revert-run/cost/hat`) with **no `mem ask`**. This has two distinct historical echoes. First, the floor-only framing restores the pre-elevation **ADR-122 posture** ("CLI is inspect/verify/administer only, not a PI action surface") that alpha.14/15 had overturned. Second, making **Ask plugin-only reverts to the alpha.1–alpha.10 arrangement**, in which the conversational/ask surface *was* the Obsidian ACP pane and never the CLI — the co-PI "the one you talk to" lived in the pane. What B1 narrows specifically is ADR-72/ADR-130's "PI direct access… never agent-only, every routine action reachable directly," of which CLI `ask` was the alpha.15 realization.

**Recorded context.** The elevation is pinned in the alpha.14 design §5 ("the product surface for alpha.14, not operator-only") and ADR-130 (CLI as the "no-daemon recovery surface"); B1 returns the CLI toward the earlier ADR-122 administer-only role.

### Gap: B1 adds a plugin distribution + binary-acquisition story A15 had removed

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** A plugin/profile *installation* story existed for most of the alpha line. alpha.1's install unit was **the repo** (ADR-26): the bootstrap `install.sh` scaffolded the vault, wired the pre-commit gate, "installs Hermes + the profiles (pruning stale `memoria-*` profiles)… installs Obsidian if absent, and wires the crons," fronted by a thin `install.ps1` WSL2 launcher. alpha.4 extended it by bundling the **Commander** and **Modal Forms** community plugins. So through alpha.10 the distribution story *was* "install Obsidian + community/agent plugins + Hermes profiles + crons."

That story was then explicitly deleted. **alpha.14**'s installer requirements state plainly: "**Install no Obsidian plugin. Install no Hermes profiles. Require no Zotero setup**" (design §11), and the `rg` negative gate rejects any reappearance of Obsidian/QuickAdd/profile installer paths. **alpha.15** confirmed it (ADR-125): "One install command… no external runtime," installer installs no Obsidian plugin, no Hermes profiles, requires no Zotero.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1 **re-introduces a plugin-distribution story that alpha.14 removed and alpha.15 confirmed removed** — shipping an Obsidian plugin (catalog-clean, desktop-only) with a consented/package-manager binary-acquisition flow. The plugin-installation *concept* reverts to the alpha.1–alpha.10 installer, which routinely installed Obsidian plus bundled plugins. The **binary-acquisition and code-signed Windows** pieces have only a partial ancestor: the closest is alpha.1's `install.ps1` WSL2 launcher and the deferred **native-Windows ADR-64** (whose WSL2-only rationale had "since weakened"); **code-signing has no alpha-line precedent** and is new-in-B1.

**Recorded context.** The removal is pinned twice — the alpha.14 installer's "Install no Obsidian plugin. Install no Hermes profiles" and ADR-125's one-command/no-runtime install. B1 restacks a plugin-plus-binary acquisition flow on top of the alpha.15 no-runtime base.

### Gap: the distribution artifact changes from a Python package to a single static binary

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The A15 artifact — a **Python 3.12 package** installed via `uv tool install memoria` — is the endpoint of a multi-version packaging trajectory that was *always Python-package-shaped*. alpha.1 shipped **repo-as-install-unit** with `src/` + a hash-manifested golden copy and no package (ADR-26/55). **alpha.4** authored the target in deferred **ADR-76**: "Package the Python runtime as an installable wheel; vault holds data, not source" — a single-rooted `src/`-layout package `memoria`, `pyproject.toml` as single source of truth, deployed as a **SHA256-pinned wheel** into `<vault>/.memoria/.venv`; execution was deferred, and only the tooling-only `pyproject.toml` slice landed in-range. **alpha.11** rehomed the installable package to `src/memoria_vault/`, separating it from the `vault-template/` shipped vault. **alpha.14** made the install target `pip install -e .` + `memoria init`/`doctor`, with a dependency **wheelhouse** built into `dist/` for a no-index install. **alpha.15** (ADR-125) pinned it to `uv tool install memoria` with a `pip install -e .` contributor fallback — a Python package, plus a "package baseline manifest with content hashes" for restore-by-reinstall.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** The B1 artifact — a **single static binary** acquired via a package manager or a checksummed, version-matched download — has **no alpha-line precedent**; the appendix lists it under "New in B1." Every alpha-line distribution decision, from ADR-26's repo through ADR-76's wheel to alpha.14's `pip`/wheelhouse and alpha.15's `uv tool install`, was a Python-packaging shape. The one thread of continuity is **checksum-pinning**: B1's "checksummed, version-matched download" echoes ADR-76's SHA256-pinned wheel and alpha.15's content-hashed package baseline manifest — but the static-binary *form* itself continues no alpha trajectory.

**Recorded context.** The Python-package endpoint is pinned in ADR-125 (uv/pip); the wheel intermediate in the deferred ADR-76. B1 replaces the artifact class rather than continuing the ADR-76→ADR-125 line.

### Gap: the host/runtime pivot is a beta.1 addition on top of alpha.15

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** alpha.15 did not merely omit the host decision — it **explicitly reserved it for a future version**. The design index Scope Boundary states "alpha.15 cannot close with Obsidian plugin implementation counted as release scope," alpha.15 "is an engine and integrity checkpoint, not an Obsidian plugin release… No alpha.15 slice adds an Obsidian plugin, plugin manifest, command-palette command, [or] Inspector panel," and PR #1203 hard-wired the deferral forward: "future editor adapters now **require their own ADR** rather than inheriting an alpha.15 commitment." The daemon half was fenced identically — `serve` shipped on-demand only, with the resident-daemon form rejected (ADR-127). So both halves of the host decision (Obsidian plugin + local daemon) sat *outside* alpha.15 by explicit boundary, not by accident.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1 §11 records "the host decision (Obsidian plugin + local daemon)" as **new alpha.16 scope** that "alpha.15… does not yet center" — which maps exactly onto the alpha.15 Scope Boundary and PR #1203's "own ADR" requirement: the host/runtime pivot is the first concrete filling of the adapter/host slot alpha.15 deliberately left empty. As a *packaged host decision* it has **no alpha-line precedent** (it is the thing alpha.15 pushed to a later version). Its component parts, however, revert to named earlier eras: the Obsidian-required surface to pre-alpha.14 (gap 1), and the resident supervising process to the pre-alpha.11 Hermes board/dispatcher era (gap 2).

**Recorded context.** This gap is version-pinned on both sides: alpha.15's design index and PR #1203 defer the host decision; B1 §11 itself labels it alpha.16 scope. The pivot occupies the alpha.16-shaped hole alpha.15's negative gate reserved.

## 2. Vault structure / bundle layout

### Gap: B1 nests Memoria as a subfolder inside an unrelated vault (two sibling roots), and drops the top-level `catalog/`, `spaces/`, and `journal/` folder trees A15 shipped

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side — "the workspace *is* the install."** From the very beginning the vault was never a guest folder inside someone else's tree; it was the whole thing. The pre-alpha.1 **Old Skeleton** fused "store-of-record, authoring view, and safety boundary" into one Obsidian vault, and that fusion is the tension the entire history works against. **alpha.1** organized the *whole* vault root by type-first category (`catalog/ · notes/ · projects/ · inbox/ · system/`, ADR-47 superseding the numbered-lifecycle ADR-04), with `folders.yaml` declaring the complete installer `skeleton:` — i.e., Memoria owned the entire tree, top to bottom. **ADR-26** ("the repo is the install unit") hard-wired that: clone the repo, the installer *is* the vault. **alpha.11** renamed the top level a **"workspace" containing three OKF-compatible bundle roots** (`catalog/` + `knowledge/` + `capabilities/`) plus workspace-owned state (`journal/`, `.memoria/`, `steering.md`, `index.md`, `references.bib`); the design explicitly notes the parent workspace is "deliberately **not** an OKF bundle so cross-bundle state … do not have to pretend to be Concepts" (`0.1.0-alpha.11-design.md` §6.1). **alpha.15** collapsed `categories`/`bundle_roots` to `[knowledge]` (ADR-126 relocations; catalog and capabilities became SQLite records / packaged product code respectively), but the *shipped skeleton still owned the whole tree* — the verified `folders.yaml` at `5b1ea8bc` lists `catalog`, `catalog/sources`, `catalog/entities`, `journal`, `knowledge/*`, `spaces/*`, and a full `system/` subtree, and the shipped `vault-template/` root carries `home.md`, `_nav.md`, `steering.md`, `references.bib`, `index.md`. In every alpha version the vault/workspace is Memoria's in its entirety.

**Prior states of the dropped trees.**
- `catalog/` — a top-level markdown folder since **alpha.1** (six entity kinds under `catalog/`, ADR-49); reshaped in **alpha.11** to `catalog/sources/<source_id>/{source.md, content.md, raw/}` + `catalog/entities/<id>.md`. From **alpha.12** (ADR-122) → **alpha.13** (ADR-123) → **alpha.14** (ADR-124) the catalog *records* moved into SQLite, yet the **folder tree kept shipping** as a (largely projection-only) skeleton — `catalog/`, `catalog/sources`, `catalog/entities`, generated `catalog/index.md` are all present at `5b1ea8bc`, and `projections.py` still carries `BUNDLE_ROOTS = ("catalog", "knowledge")`. So by A15 `catalog/` was a vestigial tree over a DB-resident record store.
- `spaces/` — introduced in **alpha.11** as navigation projections (`spaces/{knowledge,project,inbox,library,maintenance}.md`, each embedding a `.base` view), with the naming governed by **ADR-101** ("navigation surfaces are 'spaces'; 'gate' is reserved for the approval gate," alpha.7-era vocabulary fix). All five `spaces/*.md` files ship at `5b1ea8bc`.
- `journal/` — introduced in **alpha.11** as the workspace-level append-only `journal/<machine>.jsonl` PROV-DM durable record ("partition, don't chain"). **alpha.12** moved the authoritative journal into SQLite (`journal_events`, hash-chained), so like `catalog/` the `journal/` folder persisted at A15 only as a `.gitkeep` skeleton vestige of its alpha.11 role.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 nesting `Memoria/` as a **subfolder beside unrelated existing notes** (two sibling roots) has **no alpha-line precedent**: from the Old Skeleton through alpha.15, the vault/workspace root *was* Memoria, and ADR-26's "repo is the install unit" plus alpha.15's `uv tool install` both assumed Memoria owned the tree. Dropping the `catalog/`/`spaces/`/`journal/` folder trees, by contrast, **continues the alpha.12→alpha.15 trajectory** of moving records (catalog → SQLite, ADR-122/124), navigation, and logs (journal → SQLite, alpha.12) out of the visible folder tree — at A15 those three trees were already vestigial projections/skeletons over DB-resident state, and B1 removes the now-empty shells (records/navigation/logs living in the DB and `Memoria/` projections).

**Recorded context.** The catalog-to-SQLite move is pinned to ADR-122 (alpha.13) and ADR-124 (alpha.14, `supersedes: [5]`); the journal-to-SQLite move to the alpha.12 keep-test (ADR-122); the `spaces/` naming to ADR-101; the whole-workspace-is-the-install posture to ADR-26 (alpha.1) carried to `uv tool install` at ADR-125 (alpha.15).

### Gap: B1 re-materializes the catalog record as markdown and co-locates record authority + concept + extracted-text + blob in one `works/<citekey>/` folder

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side — catalog-as-SQLite is a *late* arrival.** For most of the alpha line the catalog was **markdown**. **alpha.1** kept catalog entities as markdown files ("the records are the source of truth; nothing reads a Base as data," ADR-49) and *explicitly rejected* a SQLite/JSON store as "opaque, not git-diffable, outside the plain-text discipline." **alpha.11** restructured that into `catalog/sources/<source_id>/{source.md, content.md, raw/}` — a per-source *folder* co-locating the Concept doc, the extracted text, and raw attachments. The move to SQLite is hard-won and recent: the **alpha.12 keep-test** (ADR-122) ruled that "what must survive Memoria's death is markdown OKF + a portable bibliography; everything else is free to be SQLite," pushing catalog records into `memoria.sqlite`; **alpha.13** (ADR-123) added the capture/enrich split and provider-payload blobs; **alpha.14** (ADR-124, "Standalone catalog is the citation authority," `supersedes: [5]`) made "the SQLite catalog + source-content blobs" the citation authority, with `work_id`/provider IDs the **primary runtime keys** and **citekeys demoted to aliases** — a demotion first stated in alpha.11 ("citekeys are metadata-derived … a **generated display/export alias, never the path**"). **alpha.15** completed it: `work.yaml` holds only the subjective digest + tags 1:1 with the SQLite `work_id`; the objective record stays in the DB (ADR-126: "files own meaning, the DB owns records"), and there is no `record.md`.

**Prior states.** The per-work folder that *does* survive at A15 is `knowledge/works/<id>/` holding the `work` Concept only. Extracted text and raw payloads live under `.memoria/blobs/source-content/<work-id>/` (alpha.13/14), not beside the Concept. The last time the catalog *record* itself was markdown co-located with its content and raw blob in one folder was **alpha.11's** `catalog/sources/<source_id>/{source.md, content.md, raw/}`.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's `works/<citekey>/` co-locating `record.md` (full CSL-JSON as the re-import authority), `fulltext.md`, `digest.md`, and `source.pdf` **reverts the alpha.12→alpha.14 catalog-to-SQLite arc** (ADR-122/123/124): it puts bibliographic *authority* back into markdown, exactly the state ADR-124 superseded. Making the **citekey the primary key** reverts alpha.11's "citekey is never the path" rule and ADR-124's `work_id`-primary decision. Structurally, the per-work co-location folder (record + extracted text + blob in one directory) **reprises alpha.11's `catalog/sources/<source_id>/{source.md, content.md, raw/}` shape**, re-keyed by citekey and re-homed under `works/`.

**Recorded context.** The SQLite-authority decision is pinned to ADR-122 (alpha.13) and ADR-124 (alpha.14); the "files own meaning, DB owns records" split-authority rule to ADR-125/126 (alpha.15); the citekey-as-alias rule to alpha.11 §6.1 and ADR-124.

### Gap: B1 has no `hubs/`; B1 splits project artifacts into separate files; B1 restores a per-work `digest.md`

**`hubs/` — origin & lineage.** The hub is one of Memoria's oldest surviving types. It arrived as `moc` in the pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton, was renamed `hub` at **alpha.1** ("MOC/hub was a naming fix," ADR-50), and shipped as a **gated** `notes/hubs/` type keyed on a single `topic:` string (ADR-49/52). **alpha.11** reshaped it into a `check_status`-governed `knowledge/hubs` node ("the merged Karpathy concept-page × ZK MOC"). **alpha.15** kept it as one of the four surviving types, now owning exactly one ID-backed controlled-vocabulary `tag:` (ADR-126). A `knowledge/hubs` folder ships in every alpha version's tree, including at `5b1ea8bc`. B1 dropping the `hubs/` folder (and hub concept — see §3) has **no alpha-line precedent**: the type persisted, and was reshaped rather than removed, across the entire alpha line.

**Project-artifact split — origin & lineage.** The per-project *workspace folder with subfiles* is itself an alpha idea. **alpha.5** (ADR-77) first filled the empty `projects/` (from ADR-68) with `projects/<slug>/{code, drafts, exports}` — a multi-directory per-project scaffold. **alpha.11** collapsed that to a single `project.md` at `knowledge/projects/<id>/` (project = "a Link-defined subgraph, not a bundle"). **alpha.15** (ADR-126) redefined project as "a nested, detachable sub-bundle with one-way project→corpus references" whose deliverable targets (`outline`/`section`/`figure`/`code`) are "**artifacts, not Concepts**," carrying `outcome_frame`/`paper_plan` maps on the single `project` type. B1 splitting a project into `project.md`/`evidence.md`/`gaps.md`/`draft.md`/`export` **continues the alpha.15 "detachable sub-bundle with artifacts" framing and echoes the alpha.5 `projects/<slug>/{code,drafts,exports}` multi-file workspace**, after alpha.11's single-file interlude.

**Per-work `digest.md` — origin & lineage.** The digest began as a *separate type*. **alpha.11** introduced `digest` as a net-new machine-authored type at `knowledge/digests/<source_id>.md` — "the net-new Karpathy 'wiki' role" (1:1 with a catalog source). It stayed a distinct type through alpha.12–14. **alpha.15** **folded digest materialization onto `work`** and removed `knowledge/digests` from `folders.yaml` (ADR-126: "the old `digest` type conflated the human-authored subjective take with the machine-owned bibliographic record"). B1 restoring a per-work `digest.md` **reverts the alpha.15 fold**, returning toward the alpha.11–14 separate-digest state — though now as a file inside the `works/<citekey>/` folder rather than a top-level `digests/` tree.

**Recorded context.** hub naming: ADR-50 (alpha.1); hub reshape: alpha.11 §5, ADR-126 (alpha.15). Project scaffold: ADR-77 (alpha.5) → alpha.11 §5 → ADR-126 (alpha.15). Digest: alpha.11 §5 (introduction) → ADR-126 (alpha.15 fold onto `work`).

### Gap: `inbox/` is *repurposed* (attention-projection prefix → capture-staging), and `system/` is *repurposed* with its `templates/`/`dashboards/`/`eval/`/`patterns/`/`logs/`/`exports/` subtrees dropped (only `metrics/` survives); only `reports/` is genuinely new

**`inbox/` — a four-era lineage.** This folder name has changed meaning repeatedly. (1) The pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton / originally-built alpha.1 had a numbered **`10-inbox/`** lifecycle folder (`01-fleeting/`, `02-answers/`) — a **capture/staging** role. (2) The **alpha.1 type-first** reorg (ADR-47/51) made `inbox/` the **agent→human message category** — five durable card Concepts (`candidate`/`gap`/`flag`/`alert`/`work-prompt`) routed through one writer, with the honesty-card discipline (ADR-51). (3) **alpha.11** **retired `inbox/` entirely** — "attention is generated, not authored," replaced by generated projections over `.memoria/queue/{pending,running,done,failed}`; there was no `inbox/` folder. (4) **alpha.13** **re-introduced `inbox/` as a *transient prefix*** (`folders.yaml transient_prefixes: [inbox/]`, ADR-119) for machine-emitted attention projections/prompts — "not Concepts" — a **declared prefix, not a physically shipped folder** (verified: `5b1ea8bc` has `transient_prefixes: [inbox/]` but no `inbox/` directory in the tree; only `spaces/inbox.md` exists). This transient-prefix meaning carried through alpha.15 (AGENTS.md "`inbox/` transient attention projections").

B1's `inbox/` as an **import-staging drop folder** (minting only via the awaiting-import chip) is therefore a **repurpose**, not a re-introduction: A15's `inbox/` was an attention-projection prefix; B1's is capture-staging. Version-anchored, B1's capture-staging meaning **circles back to the era-1 `10-inbox/` capture role** and the alpha.3 "URL capture writes a candidate card to `inbox/`" flow — but it is not the same object as the A15 attention prefix it replaces.

**`system/` — origin & lineage of the dropped subtrees.** `system/` was defined at **alpha.1** (ADR-47) as "visible infrastructure — logs · templates · patterns · dashboards · board · eval · metrics." Its subtrees each have their own long histories: **`templates/`** shipped 16 starter notes from alpha.1 (their authority later reduced by schema-driven creation, ADR-119, alpha.10/13); **`dashboards/`** ran 11→12 (ADR-49, alpha.2) then consolidated to 5 (ADR-118, alpha.11) before generated `knowledge/_views/` superseded the glance role (alpha.14); **`eval/`** held gold-task fixtures from alpha.2/3 (clarified as `system/eval/` in alpha.3); **`patterns/`** hosted the `pattern` type from alpha.1 (retired as a durable Concept in alpha.11); `logs/` and `exports/` likewise date to the alpha.1 system tree. The verified A15 skeleton still ships `system/{templates, dashboards, eval, patterns, logs, logs/sessions, exports, exports/assets, metrics}` plus `system/vocabulary.md`. B1 keeps **only `metrics/`** (plus `manifest.jsonl` and `incidents/`), dropping the other five subtrees — a **repurpose** of `system/` from "rich visible-infrastructure tree" to a minimal machine-state directory.

**`reports/` — genuinely new.** A top-level `reports/` tree of machine-report *notes* has **no alpha-line precedent**. Its closest ancestors are the generated `knowledge/_views/` reports (alpha.14) and `system/dashboards/` (alpha.1–11), but a dedicated `reports/` folder of machine-authored report notes is a B1 addition.

**Recorded context.** inbox eras pinned to: ADR-47/51 (alpha.1 message category), alpha.11 §8.2/§17 (retirement, "attention is generated"), ADR-119 (alpha.13 transient-prefix re-introduction). system subtrees: ADR-47 (alpha.1 definition), ADR-118 (dashboard consolidation, alpha.11), ADR-119 (schema-driven creation reducing template authority).

### Gap: `references.bib` → `bibliography.bib` rename; `local-only/` is a new folder-level marker

**`references.bib` — origin & lineage.** A generated bibliography file predates the workspace-root name: pre-alpha.11 it lived hidden as **`.memoria/memoria.bib`** (the local identity-resolution `.bib`, referenced in the alpha.1-era 867-paper capture design). **alpha.11** moved it to the **workspace root as `references.bib`**, reclassifying it as a **generated tracked projection** ("`references.bib` / CSL-JSON are generated tracked projections … Memoria does not hand-maintain a bibliography," §13; generated by `regenerate-references-bib`). The **alpha.12 keep-test** made it load-bearing: `references.bib` is the **portable-bibliography takeout** — "enough for the user to take completed sources with the keep-set" — with a bibliography-atomicity rule (ADR-122) forcing it into the same commit as any bibliography-changing capture. **alpha.13/14** flipped its rendering to read checked SQLite catalog rows first (ADR-123/124). The **name `references.bib` was stable from alpha.11 through alpha.15**, and it ships at the workspace root at `5b1ea8bc`.

B1's rename to `Memoria/bibliography.bib` has **no alpha-line precedent** — the file's *role* (a generated portable bibliography, the keep-test takeout) continues unchanged from alpha.11–15, but every alpha version that shipped it named it `references.bib` (and `.memoria/memoria.bib` before that). The rename is purely nominal against a continuous-role history.

**`local-only/` — origin & lineage.** A folder-level local-only marker has **no alpha-line precedent**. The term **"local-only" does have alpha history — but as a *deployment-topology mode*, not a folder**: from **alpha.1**, ADR-63 named `local-only` the adopted default deployment (one workstation, Git for history, Zotero on localhost, $0 infra), carried as "the complete system on a single machine (`local-only`)" through the alpha.2 checkpoint. That is a homonym: the alpha "local-only" is a deployment mode; B1's `Memoria/local-only/` is a folder that marks contents as excluded from sync/portability. No alpha version shipped a folder-level local-only marker of any name — the closest analogs are the *gitignored* disposable trees (`.memoria/index/`, staging, blobs; alpha.11–12), which mark exclusion via `.gitignore` rather than a dedicated visible folder.

**Recorded context.** `references.bib` as a generated tracked projection: alpha.11 §13; keep-test/atomicity: ADR-122 (alpha.12); render-from-SQLite: ADR-124 (alpha.14). "local-only" deployment mode: ADR-63 (alpha.1), carried in the alpha.2 checkpoint plan.

## 3. Concept / type model

*Design-history analysis of every gap in §3 of the updated alpha.15→beta.1 gap analysis. Descriptive and version-anchored only. Primary grounding: the design-history document (Part I Origins; Part II alpha.1; Part III per-version deltas; Part IV arcs c/d/e/f). ADRs are cited as the history records them.*

### Four typed-concept schemas → flat file-class enum (per-type frontmatter+body schemas absent)

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** Per-type schema *files* are one of the oldest continuous artifacts in Memoria. The pre-alpha.1 **Old Skeleton** (2026-05-29 snapshot) already shipped "~15 typed notes with lifecycles, a controlled-vocabulary frontmatter schema." At **alpha.1** (`0e9ed9dd`) this was an **18-type roster in four categories**, each type owning an authoritative YAML contract under `src/.memoria/schemas/types/<type>.yaml`, loaded and validated by `schema.py` — declaring `required:`/`optional:` field-kind maps, an `enums:` block, and `required_any:` (Part II §3). The type roster then *accreted* rather than being designed: ADR-126 (quoted in arc (c)) records "the type roster grew by accretion (47, 50, 78, 117, 119) … 12+ types across three bundles." Two deliberate collapses followed. **alpha.2** consolidated numbered-folder types into the type-first set. **alpha.11** — "the single largest redesign in Memoria's history" — collapsed the vocabulary from **25 types to 12** and re-derived the knowledge graph as **three node-kinds by authoring role** (`digest`/`note`/`hub`). **alpha.15** (PR #1164, ADR-126) made the final cut to **four knowledge Concept types** — `note`, `work`, `hub`, `project` — deleting nine schemas outright (`source.yaml`, `person.yaml`, `organization.yaml`, `venue.yaml`, `digest.yaml`, `adapter.yaml`, `operation.yaml`, `skill.yaml`, `workflow.yaml`) and adding `work.yaml`. The four survive by a tightened discriminator: a type must be "a fundamentally different artifact"; "a flippable check regime is a *mode*," and a layer-difference (a source record) "is not a knowledge type" (ADR-126). Crucially, through all of this the *mechanism* was constant: a per-type YAML schema validated in product code (`schema.py`), which at alpha.15 was even hardened to **closed validation** (rejects undeclared fields; `x: map` escape hatch — PR-AC #1197, arc (d)).

**Prior states.** 18 typed schemas (alpha.1) → accreted to 12+ (alpha.10) → 12 (alpha.11 three-bundle) → four (alpha.15). The *unit* — one YAML schema per type, with body-section templates in the template folder — never changed from Old Skeleton to alpha.15.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1's flat frontmatter `type` **class enum** (`work|fulltext|digest|note|evidence|gaps|project|draft|ruled-out|export|report|incident|conflict`) with no per-type schema files has **no alpha-line precedent**. Every era from the Old Skeleton forward carried per-type schema+body contracts; alpha.15 tightened rather than dissolved them. The B1 enum also mixes categories the alpha line kept on separate layers — knowledge Concepts (`note`, `project`), the catalog record (`work`), machine projections (`gaps`, `report`), and file classes (`fulltext`, `export`) sit as sibling enum values, whereas alpha.11's ADR-126-era discriminator explicitly refused to make a projection or a layer-difference into a type.

### The `hub` type is dropped

**Origin & lineage.** The hub is one of Memoria's most hard-won and longest-lived concepts. It descends from the **MOC (Map-of-Content)** present in the Old Skeleton and the original alpha.1 numbered scheme (`30-synthesis/03-moc/`, type `moc`). At **alpha.1** it was renamed `hub` (ADR-50), becoming a first-class **gated** note type keyed on a required `topic:` string, carrying the ADR-19 threshold-alert mechanism "under a plainer name" (Part II §2). Its intellectual grounding is triple: Bush's Memex associative graph, Luhmann's structure note, and Karpathy's concept-page (Part II §11; arc (c)). It was reshaped, never removed: **alpha.11** turned it from a gated `topic:` node into a `check_status`-governed **machine-synthesized + human-curated** node with `members[]`, "the merged Karpathy concept-page × ZK MOC: the machine keeps the topic synthesis current, the human owns the structure." **alpha.15** (ADR-126) reshaped it once more so it "**owns exactly one ID-backed controlled-vocabulary tag**" via a required `tag:` field, with membership = "everywhere that checked tag appears" — splitting mechanical *membership* from human *salience*, directly answering ADR-19's recorded hazard "a generated list that looks curated but isn't."

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1 has **no `hub` type and no hub concept anywhere**. This is not a revert to a named earlier era — it removes a node-kind that existed *continuously from before alpha.1* (as MOC) through every alpha release. There is no point in the alpha line (or the Old Skeleton) at which the knowledge model lacked a structure/MOC/hub node; its absence in B1 has no alpha-line precedent.

### `note` claim/question mode and the claim substrate are dropped

**Origin & lineage of the claim substrate.** The claim as an object is old; the *field substrate* the gap analysis lists is a later arrival. At **alpha.1** the synthesis atom was a dedicated **`claim` type** (Luhmann's permanent note — "what the PI thinks"), gated, with a required `sources: list` ("every claim → a citekey"), a `maturity` property (`seedling→budding→evergreen`), and a prescribed **Evidence** body section where "every line traces to a `sources` citekey" (Part II §2, §3, §7). `fleeting` and `source` were separate types. The **field-level claim substrate** the gap analysis names originated at **alpha.11**, when `claim` + `fleeting` + note-`source` merged into a single `note` type carrying an *optional anchor* (`quote`, `text_sha256`, `source_id`, `annotation_ref`) and an *optional claim block* (`claim_text`, `qualifier`, `tense`, `temporal_scope`, `status`, `evidence_set`, `extraction_confidence`). The alpha.1 `maturity` and mandatory `sources` were dropped there ("trust = the checks"; CEBM `evidence_level`/entity-pointer/`claim.sources` → "`csl_json` + typed Links + `evidence_set` warrant"), and `extraction_confidence` was defined as "internal telemetry — **never surfaced**," a direct response to Jacobs 2021 (Part III alpha.11; Removed/Retired). **alpha.15** then reshaped this substrate: `note` **gained** `mode: claim | question`, `question_status: open | resolved`, and claim-context fields `temporal_scope`/`tense`/`qualifier` + `anchors`, and **lost** the flat anchor fields (`quote`, `text_sha256`, `source_id`, `annotation_ref`, `extraction_confidence`), which relocated into a W3C `TextQuoteSelector` `anchor` (ADR-126 "Relocations"). ADR-126's rationale for making claim/question a *mode* not a type: "flippable states and roles, not artifacts; separate types make the question→claim transition destructive," and "a mode is declared intent creating obligations the gap engine feeds on — a claim without evidence is an `under-warranted` gap; an open question is a compose-flow lead."

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1's `note` = "atomic + fleeting notes (human)" with **no `mode`, no claim/question distinction, no `question_status`**, and none of the claim/anchor substrate. The *fleeting-into-note* merge itself echoes alpha.11 (which folded `fleeting` into `note`; "fleeting → an unchecked note"). But the claim/question axis and its obligations have **no B1 analog at any layer** — the claim as a first-class distinction runs Old Skeleton/alpha.1 (`claim` type) → alpha.11 (optional claim block on `note`) → alpha.15 (`mode`), and B1 continues none of those lines. The dropped `extraction_confidence` is the one field the alpha line already kept invisible by design (alpha.11), so its removal ends a field that was telemetry-only from birth.

### No argument-graph `project` concept (project → folder of files)

**Origin & lineage.** The project as a typed concept was built up gate by gate. **alpha.1** had separate `project`/`thesis`/`code-note` types under a `projects/` category (which "ships empty in v0.1.0-alpha.2"); the original numbered scheme placed per-project work under `40-workbench/` with numbered subfolders (`01-map/`, `02-framing/`, `04-drafts/`, `06-code/`). **alpha.5** made the **Project gate** real ("the fourth navigation gate becomes real"). **alpha.11** collapsed `project`/`thesis`/`code-note` into a **single `project` type** with an optional `thesis` field and a `scope` map, defining "project = Link-defined subgraph, not a bundle" via `in_project` membership. **alpha.15** (ADR-126) gave `project` `outcome_frame: map` and `paper_plan: map`, kept `thesis: str`, dropped `scope: map`, and made it "the compose-flow working frame: a nested, detachable sub-bundle with one-way project→corpus references." At that point the project concept **absorbed** the argument graph outright: ADR-77 (project gate), ADR-79 (argument graph/warrant/**saturation floor** → folded into the gap engine's saturation block), and ADR-78 (thesis becomes a *role* link, "no note is born thesis-designated-and-checked; a falsified thesis is a preserved result via the refuting subgraph").

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1 makes `project` **one class value** and a `project/` **folder of separate files** (`project.md`/`evidence.md`/`gaps.md`/`draft.md`); there is **no argument-graph project concept** (cross-referenced to §8). Splitting the working frame back into per-file artifacts structurally rhymes with the **original alpha.1 numbered per-project subfolders** (`40-workbench/{01-map,02-framing,04-drafts,06-code}/`) — a folder-of-artifacts arrangement — rather than with the alpha.11→alpha.15 trajectory that had consolidated all project machinery into one typed concept plus a gap-engine saturation lens.

### ULID identity is dropped; identity is path/citekey-derived again

**Origin & lineage.** Path-derived identity is the historical baseline; ULID identity is a **single-release-old** invention. From the Old Skeleton and **alpha.1**, identity was the filename/path and the wikilink graph over it. **alpha.11** adopted OKF nouns, under which "a *Concept ID* is the file path minus `.md`" (ADR-107, arc (e)), and demoted `citekey` to "a **generated display/export alias**, never the path" (source keyed by an immutable `source_id` path; "fixture-verified across a citekey correction"). **alpha.12** enforced universal frontmatter with `id` = "its bundle-relative path without `.md`." **alpha.14** made the SQLite catalog authoritative and keyed works by `work_id`, with citekeys "aliases while stable Work IDs/provider identifiers are the primary runtime keys" (ADR-124). Only at **alpha.15** (PR #1164) was **ULID identity** introduced as a schema kind (`id: ulid`; `schema.py` added `ulid`/`is_ulid`), on the rationale "identity ≠ filename ≠ title" (ADR-126) — "decoupling identity from the path so renames/moves don't break references." So ULID is not hard-won; it is the newest layer, arriving in the final alpha release.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1 identity = **`citekey` + file path** (works keyed by citekey; no ULID). This **reverts the alpha.15-only ULID layer** and returns to the **path-derived identity regime of alpha.1→alpha.14**. It goes one step further by re-promoting `citekey` to an identity key — a role `citekey` last held at **alpha.1** (the `paper` type's required key), before alpha.11 demoted it to a display/export alias and alpha.14 made `work_id` the primary key. B1 thus reverses both the alpha.15 ULID decoupling and the alpha.11–14 "citekey is a corrected-alias, never a key" decision.

### Typed authored relation links are dropped from frontmatter

**Origin & lineage.** Relation-typed authored links in frontmatter are an alpha.1 decision with a clean lineage. **alpha.1** replaced ADR-08's single `relations:` field with **ADR-52**: notes carry authored **`links:`** — a `map` of typed edges (`supports`, `contradicts`, `extends`) whose values are wikilinks — while catalog entities carry given `relationships` (`cited_by`, `authored_by`, `published_in`). The split yielded "two typed layers — a citation/identity graph over entities and an argument graph over notes" (Part II §4); ADR-65 deferred a `similar`-first relation-vocabulary expansion. **alpha.11** standardized the predicate set to `{supports|challenges|extends|supersedes|in_project}` + Citations and retired ADR-08, keeping high-risk directional links (contradiction, supersession) human-set. **alpha.12** made `links` a required **map** (not a list). **alpha.15** kept authored `links:` as a **nested, relation-typed map** validated against `{supports, contradicts, extends}` (`schema.py` `_check_links`, `LINK_RELATIONS`; PR-AE #1199), rejecting scalar/URL/traversal targets — "ADR-52's distinction carried verbatim … authored `links:` are the human/argument layer; given `relationships` are engine-built DB records" (ADR-126). So the typed-edge frontmatter map is continuous from alpha.1 through alpha.15.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1 expresses links as **Obsidian wikilinks in the body + a DB `links` backlink table + `%%prov%%` block markers**, with **no relation-typed frontmatter links map**. This reverts *past* ADR-52 — earlier than even ADR-08's `relations:` field, which was at least frontmatter. Its direction maps instead onto the **OKF permissive-consumer shape** the alpha line deliberately confined to the export boundary: ADR-107 records that OKF "holds that 'the specific kind of relationship is conveyed by the surrounding prose, not by the link itself,'" which "Memoria deliberately rejected … in favor of typed edges (ADR-126)." B1's body-wikilink-plus-DB-backlink arrangement is closer to that untyped/prose-conveyed body-link model that alpha.11 and alpha.15 kept out of the internal representation.

### The entire controlled-vocabulary / faceting model is dropped (NEW)

**Origin & lineage.** A controlled vocabulary is one of Memoria's founding substrates and predates the first tag. The **Old Skeleton** already had "a controlled-vocabulary frontmatter schema" among its ~15 typed notes (Part I). At **alpha.1** this was fully specified (Part II §6): `src/system/vocabulary.md` (itself an `index`-typed note) was "the single source for three classification facets" — `research_area` (~27 curated health-informatics terms) and `methodology` (on `paper`/`source`), plus `topics` (on `claim`). The facets were kept separate because they "answer categorically different questions" — methodology is *how* a study is structured, research_area is *what* it is about — and `research_area` was "seeded mechanically from OpenAlex topics by `classify.py` so the vocabulary is free and consistent across sources," with vocabulary consolidation deliberately "deferred to ~50 papers" and reference taxonomies (MeSH, ACM CCS) kept out of the queryable facet (in each note's `_enrichment` namespace, "for browsing, not querying"). This facet model was the load-bearing half of the surviving part of ADR-04: "topic never lives in folders … while topics stay in frontmatter facets (`research_area`, `methodology`, `topics`) and links." The substrate then persisted and evolved: **alpha.3** routed human capture through Modal Forms with controlled vocabularies (Linter as enforcement authority); **alpha.4** added a `docs/reference/vocabulary.md` page; **alpha.8** was a "vocabulary discipline" checkpoint that renamed facet enums and swept the `engine→operation` rename through `vocabulary.md`; **alpha.9** recorded a "classify divergence" — the ADR-30 embedding+zero-shot tag-suggestion layer "is not built," and `classify` "diverged to a deterministic OpenAlex-topic mapping" (`classify.py:118-154`). The substrate survived the SQLite transition: **alpha.14** gap analysis "counts checked SQLite catalog source terms — from `csl_json.memoria.tags`, `topics`, `research_area`." At **alpha.15** the substrate is intact and multi-part: `hub` "owns exactly one **ID-backed controlled-vocabulary tag**" via a required `tag:` field (ADR-126); `analyze-gaps` runs a deterministic phrase pass over checked Work text, "suppresses existing **vocabulary**/frontmatter terms," and emits unchecked tag-candidate cards (ADR-93, PR-W #1191); and (per the alpha.15 consolidation ADRs/sources named in the gap analysis) a `vocab` command mints/manages the ID-backed tag IDs while a `classify` operation applies `research_area` from OpenAlex topic scores (floor 0.6 / near-tie 0.15) — the direct descendant of alpha.1's `classify.py` OpenAlex-topic seeding.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** B1 has **no controlled-vocabulary / faceting model at all** — no `tags` facet, no `vocabulary.md`, no `research_area`/`methodology` facets, no `vocab` command, no `classify` operation. This drops a substrate that was present **continuously from before alpha.1** (Old Skeleton controlled-vocab schema) through alpha.1's `vocabulary.md` + facets + `classify.py`, and survived every subsequent redesign including the alpha.11/alpha.12 SQLite cut (which kept `research_area` as catalog terms) up to alpha.15's `vocab`/`classify`/hub-tag machinery. There is **no alpha-line precedent** for its total absence: at no release from the Old Skeleton onward did the model lack a controlled vocabulary and a topic/facet home in frontmatter. Because the facet system was the mechanism that implemented the surviving half of ADR-04 ("topics live in frontmatter facets, never in folders"), B1's removal also leaves topical classification with no frontmatter facet home that the alpha line had maintained throughout.

## 4. Frontmatter discipline

### B1 drops universal-frontmatter-on-every-concept; human/freehand files carry none

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** Frontmatter-carrying typed files reach back to the pre-alpha.1 **Old Skeleton**, which already shipped "~15 typed notes … a controlled-vocabulary frontmatter schema" with "Diátaxis mode declared per-file in frontmatter" (Part I). The **alpha.1** baseline formalized this as the field-kind grammar: per-type contracts at `.memoria/schemas/types/<type>.yaml`, each declaring `required:`/`optional:` maps — but frontmatter was *per-type*, not universal, and it explicitly exempted `system/` infrastructure and the three vault-root nav pages (`home.md`, `research-focus.md`, `troubleshooting.md`), which shipped untyped (alpha.1 §3, line 189). *Universality* is a hard-won late arrival, not an origin property: it appeared only at **alpha.14**, where PR #1157 (`88b7f0f5`) forced "universal Concept frontmatter" across `knowledge/` and `capabilities/` — every file required `id`, an approved `standing`, and a map-shaped `links: {}` (alpha.14 Vault/Concepts). Arc (d) records the reasoning: universal enforcement "was the natural endpoint of read-barrier logic — a consumer must be able to trust every file's status, so every file must declare it." **alpha.15** kept universality but inverted its *content* to meaning-only (`id`/`type`/`title`/`tags`/`links`/`aliases`/optional `archived` + per-type), enforced in product code (alpha.15 §1).

**Prior states.** Per-type + non-universal (Old Skeleton → alpha.13) → universal-enforced with verdict fields (alpha.14, #1157) → universal-enforced meaning-only (alpha.15).

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's rule — "frontmatter only on Memoria-created files; user notes need none; absence of all `mem-*` markers = human" — has **no named earlier-era precedent** in the alpha line, which required frontmatter on every knowledge Concept from alpha.14 onward (and per-type frontmatter on all typed knowledge notes before that, exempting only infra/nav). The closest historical analog — untyped *exempt* files — existed only for `system/` infrastructure and nav pages (alpha.1 §3), never for human *knowledge* content. B1's "freehand human file carrying no frontmatter" is a posture enabled by the §2 host pivot (Memoria as a subfolder inside an unrelated Obsidian vault): the alpha line from alpha.14 on treated `knowledge/` as *the* bundle — the workspace *was* the bundle — so there were no unrelated human notes to leave unmarked.

### B1 drops the closed reject-undeclared validator and the `x.*` extension namespace

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The A15 side here is the **newest** layer of the whole subsystem. The alpha.1 baseline was the explicit *opposite* of closed: "**Unknown extra fields are allowed** — *the schema constrains, it does not enumerate*" (alpha.1 §3, line 158). That permissive stance held across the entire line. Closure arrived only at the very end: **alpha.15**'s PR-AC #1197 flipped validation "from permissive to closed" — `validate_frontmatter` now "rejects undeclared fields ('undeclared field: … put custom data under `x`')," and every type schema simultaneously gained an `x: map` extension escape hatch (alpha.15 §1). The recorded rationale ties it to the alpha.10/11 lineage: it "closes the field-closure part of ADR-119 (schema-as-single-declarative-contract), now enforced in product code rather than as a gated content edit." Both the reject-undeclared validator *and* the `x.*` namespace were therefore born together, in the final alpha.15 cut.

**Prior states.** Permissive "constrains not enumerates" (alpha.1 → alpha.14) → closed reject-undeclared + `x: map` hatch (alpha.15, PR-AC #1197).

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 ("no reject-undeclared validator; a freehand file without frontmatter surfaces a one-tap proposal; 'flags never block'") **reverts past the alpha.15 #1197 closure**, back to the permissive posture that ran from alpha.1 through alpha.14 — and extends past it, since B1 does not require frontmatter at all on human files (the earlier permissive posture still validated declared fields on typed knowledge notes). The `x.*` extension namespace, which existed only in the alpha.15 end-state, has **no B1 analog**; it disappears with the validator that introduced it.

### (NEW) B1 returns researcher-set supersession `standing`/`superseded` to frontmatter that A15 moved DB-side

**Origin & lineage — the standing/check_status in-file→DB arc.** Supersession-in-frontmatter is an **origin-era** property, not an A15 invention. **alpha.1**'s `claim.yaml` already carried optional `superseded_by: str` in frontmatter alongside a `lifecycle` enum `[current, retracted, archived]` (alpha.1 §3, line 176); ADR-10 (claim supersession) and ADR-09 (contradictions dashboard) drove it. **alpha.11**'s four-type collapse folded claim/fleeting/source into one `note` whose optional claim block carried `status{candidate|accepted|rejected|superseded|needs_review}` in frontmatter, and introduced the read-barrier field `check_status: [unchecked, checked, quarantined]` on every type (`initial_check_status: unchecked`); `lifecycle` was dropped from knowledge Concepts, surviving only on `source` (alpha.11 knowledge-model + gate sections, lines 1633/1649). **alpha.14** is the *peak* of supersession-in-frontmatter: PR #1157 made `standing` (`current|superseded|retracted|archived`) a universal, enforced frontmatter field on every file, with retrieval hiding superseded/retracted by reading it (alpha.14 §Vault/Concepts, §10). **alpha.15** then inverted the whole apparatus (arc (d)): PR-L #1179 + PR-B #1163 removed `check_status`, `standing`, and `note_status` from *all* type schemas and templates, made frontmatter meaning-only, and moved verdicts/supersession to SQLite — `work update` now writes retraction/contested `standing` as DB Concept flags, and the seeded stale-source fixture "moved from CSL `standing` into DB Concept flags" (alpha.15 §1, §9). ADR-126's recorded reason: "`check_status` lived in frontmatter, so checking a note rewrote it, hand-edits forged status, and every consumer had to distrust the file it was reading"; ADR-125's split-authority rule, "files own meaning, the DB owns verdicts and records" (arc (d)).

**Prior states.** `superseded_by` optional frontmatter (alpha.1) → note `status: …superseded…` + `check_status` frontmatter (alpha.11) → universal enforced `standing` frontmatter (alpha.14, #1157) → all removed to SQLite, meaning-only frontmatter (alpha.15, #1179/#1163).

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 re-introducing researcher-set `superseded`/`superseded-by` as frontmatter keys **reverts the alpha.15 move**, but the revert is version-specifically scoped. What alpha.15 relocated DB-side was primarily the *machine/verdict* standing (the universal, machine-enforced `standing` of alpha.14 and the `check_status` verdict). B1's return is explicitly the **researcher-set (human-authored)** supersession — which maps most directly to alpha.1's researcher-authored `superseded_by` and to the human-set `status: superseded` slice of alpha.11's note claim block, *not* to alpha.14's universal machine-enforced `standing`. In lineage terms, B1 revives the pre-alpha.15 in-file supersession specifically along the human-authored axis the line had carried in frontmatter continuously since the Origins, while leaving the machine-verdict side where alpha.15 put it. The co-introduced **`reading`** key has **no direct frontmatter precedent** in the alpha line: the nearest recorded relatives are alpha.1's `source` `lifecycle: proposed` = "awaiting reading" semantics (line 181) and the alpha.13/alpha.14 capture/ingest-stage states — but those were machine capture-stage states, not a researcher-set reading key.

### B1 re-introduces in-file provenance/verdict markers on machine files; A15 kept verdicts DB-only with no in-file marker

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** In-file verdict/provenance state is deep-rooted: the alpha.1 baseline carried "provenance at three grains" (line 476) and put soft verdicts *in the file* — `lifecycle`, `maturity{seedling|budding|evergreen}`, and `agent_recommendation{inconclusive|issues-found|clean}` all lived in frontmatter (alpha.1 §3). Through **alpha.11–alpha.14**, the read-barrier verdict field itself lived in-file: `check_status` (alpha.11) and universal `standing` (alpha.14). The A15 *end-state* is the sharp departure: **alpha.15** made verdicts **DB-only with no in-file marker** — meaning-only frontmatter, `checked_terminology_gate.py` blocking approved/verified/trusted drift, and "rebuild-from-files starts fully `unchecked` and ignores frontmatter status" (alpha.15 §1, §3; arc (d)). Critically, ADR-126 recorded and *rejected* the intermediate option of a non-authoritative in-file shadow: "keeping `check_status` as a generated-but-ignored projection — hash churn plus a forged-status foot-gun; meaning-only is stronger" (alpha.15 §1, line 2086).

**Prior states.** In-file soft verdicts (`agent_recommendation`, `maturity`, alpha.1) → in-file authoritative `check_status`/`standing` (alpha.11–14) → **no** in-file verdict marker, DB-only (alpha.15).

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's in-file `%%prov:h|m|mc%%` block markers plus `mem-prov`/`mem-taint`/`mem-flags` frontmatter keys **partially revert the alpha.15 no-in-file-marker stance**, restoring an in-file verdict/provenance layer of the kind the line carried continuously through alpha.14. But the revert is bounded and does not return to any authoritative earlier form: per §5, B1's markers are a *display claim, not authority* — green requires the marker **and** the current block hash **and** the DB `checks` row to agree — so B1 **continues** alpha.15's ADR-125 split ("the DB owns verdicts") for authority while re-adding the in-file marker alpha.15 uniquely eliminated. There is **no exact alpha-line precedent** for a non-authoritative in-file verdict shadow: the alpha.11–14 in-file fields *were* authority (until alpha.15 declared them untrusted), and the one design alpha.15 explicitly weighed for a non-authoritative in-file projection — "`check_status` as a generated-but-ignored projection" — was recorded as a **rejected alternative** in ADR-126. B1's marker layer thus instantiates the shape alpha.15 considered and declined, re-cast as a display shorthand rather than a stored projection.

## 5. Integrity / review / gate model

The whole of this subsystem descends from a single inherited mechanism — the Old Skeleton's **structurally blocking human review gate**: "a card cannot promote until the human sets `review_status: approved`," positioned deliberately against the surveyed autonomous-research systems "whose LLM reviewers only advise" (Part I, Origins). The alpha line's dominant motion was the transfer of that guarantee from an act a human performs (approve a write) to something the system structurally enforces (a trace, a DB verdict, a quarantine). Each gap below sits somewhere along that motion; where beta.1 re-hosts the surface in Obsidian it reaches back toward the alpha.1/alpha.7 era the alpha.14/15 cut had left behind, while the *approval semantics* that Origins→alpha.10 carried are nowhere restored.

### The Review surface (Obsidian main-pane view + ribbon count + one-click revert)

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The disposition surface is one of the oldest elements in Memoria. In the **Old Skeleton / alpha.1**, agent→human signalling was consolidated into the `inbox/` category with the **honesty card** (ADR-51): proposals carry "an honest argument, no verdict" (`argument_for` · `argument_against` · `what_tipped_it` · `certainty`), with graded `loudness` (`quiet`/`notice`/`alert`/`block`), and high-cardinality screening becomes "one aggregate work-prompt" over a Bases worklist rather than N cards (ADR-54, "would flood a queue meant to converge to zero and invite select-all-accept"). This inbox was rendered as an **Obsidian category** — the kanban board and queue dashboards were *views* of it (one Base grouped by type). At **alpha.11's clean-slate reset**, the per-write approval model was retired ("traceability, not approval"), but the honesty-card obligations were explicitly carried forward. At **alpha.15**, ADR-128 removed "the review queue as an **approval surface**" and "the inbox-as-Obsidian-category mechanism," but *kept* the substance: "replaced by a consequence-routed attention worklist keeping the honesty-card obligations (arguments not verdicts; batch worklists; graded loudness quiet/notice/alert/block)" — with ADR-51 and ADR-121's enqueue-only rule "carried verbatim." ADR-130 kept the presentation form: "presentation span-first with source evidence adjacent (the Elicit table pattern), `--apply` one click," bounded because "review effectiveness collapses beyond ~60 minutes/bounded batches."

**Prior states & when they changed.** The surface's *authority* changed three times while its *obligations* held constant: blocking-approval field (`review_status`, ADR-03, Old Skeleton→alpha.10) → trusted-writer + read-barrier, no approval, honesty-card obligations retained (alpha.11) → consequence-routed worklist, approval semantics deleted but worklist kept (alpha.15/ADR-128). The *rendering* moved from Obsidian-category (alpha.1–alpha.13) to CLI `attention` worklists + `--apply` and read-API view-specs when Obsidian was dropped from the required product (alpha.14/15).

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** Per the gap analysis this is a **convergence, not a re-introduction**: beta.1's Review surface (batch worklists promote/link/drop/snooze, apply/reject/defer) renders the *same* consequence-routed worklist alpha.15 kept — beta.1 §11 itself files "the Elicit-style span-first review surface" under "Converges with alpha.15." The genuine deltas are surface-form: beta.1 re-hosts the worklist as an **Obsidian main-pane view with a ribbon pending-count** — a rendering that reaches back to the alpha.1/alpha.7-era inbox-as-Obsidian-category and the persistent-shell nav that ADR-128 had declared "dead," now available again because §1's host pivot restores Obsidian as the required UI. What is *not* restored is the approval semantics of the whole Origins→alpha.10 line (`review_status: approved`, the blocking gate, the WIP-cap back-pressure); beta.1 stays on the post-alpha.11 "checks route, calibrated humans decide" model. The added **one-click revert** continues the rollback lineage (alpha.11 cascade-rollback → alpha.14 `workspace rollback` → alpha.15 ADR-127 recovery); surfacing it as a one-tap Obsidian action is the new element.

### No-write-time-oracle kept; pre==post manifest gate + block-level byte-immutability + auto-rollback added

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** "No-write-time-oracle" is an **alpha.15** coinage (ADR-128): "Correctness is not conferred at write time by any approver, human or machine … `checked` means only 'checks passed and warrants resolve — never approved,'" enforced by `checked_terminology_gate.py`. But the two mechanisms beta.1 adds on top both have deep alpha-line roots. The **never-auto-destroy-human-work** rule is hard-won: alpha.11's cascade rollback established that "machine-derived downstream auto-reverts; **human-directed downstream halts auto-revert but still propagates a flag**," and alpha.15/ADR-127 restated it as "PI-authored descendants raise an ask-routed flag and stay live (never auto-destroy human work)." The **hash-comparison** mechanism traces to alpha.1's `vault-hash-drift` detector (SHA-256 `before_hash`/`after_hash` pinned per audited write; "a legitimate human edit in Obsidian surfaces here too, by design") and to alpha.14's consumption comparison, formalized at alpha.15 into ADR-127's **consumption guard**: "at machine consumption the on-disk hash must equal the recorded `checked` hash; mismatch refuses to serve and enqueues scan — no write on the read path."

**Prior states & when they changed.** Human-content protection evolved from *detector-only* (alpha.1 `vault-hash-drift`: report a drifted file after the fact) → *quarantine-on-scan* (alpha.14: a direct edit to a checked keep-set Concept is marked `unchecked`/non-consumable, re-promoted only via `mark-checked`) → *demotion-propagation* (alpha.15 ADR-127: refuse-and-enqueue, then demote under the lock). Note the alpha.15 posture was deliberately asynchronous: "synchronous demote-on-read rejected — a write on the read path races the workspace lock; refusal alone is already fail-closed."

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** Beta.1 **continues** the no-write-time-oracle trajectory verbatim (export gate "blocks, does not edit"). On top of it, beta.1 adds a **pre==post byte gate with auto-rollback** over a human-span hash manifest and a block-level read-only rule — beta.1 §11(4) itself frames this as *stronger* than alpha.15's propagation-only demotion. Historically this converts the alpha.1→alpha.15 hash-comparison from an asynchronous refuse-and-demote signal into a **synchronous hard byte gate** — precisely the synchronous-on-write-path posture ADR-127 had declined for the *read* path, now applied at apply/export time as a pre/post envelope. The block granularity (per-human-span hashing) has no exact alpha precedent — alpha.1 hashed whole files, alpha.15 hashed whole `checked` Concepts. The auto-rollback continues the cascade-rollback/recovery lineage (alpha.11→alpha.15/ADR-127).

### In-file provenance marker (`%%prov:mc%%`) as non-authoritative display shorthand

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** "Verdicts DB-only, no in-file marker" is an **alpha.15** end-state, and it was one of the release's most deliberate reversals. Verdict-in-file was the *default* for the whole prior history: ADR-03's `review_status` lived in the card; alpha.11's `check_status` (`unchecked|checked|quarantined`) lived in frontmatter as "the authoritative field … only the trusted worker transitions it"; **alpha.14 pushed this to its endpoint** with universal-concept-frontmatter enforcement — every file under `knowledge/` had to carry `id`/`standing`/`check_status` so a consumer could trust it (PR #1157). Alpha.15 then **inverted it** (arc d): PR-L/#1179 made frontmatter meaning-only, moving `check_status` and all verdict state to SQLite, because "`check_status` lived in frontmatter, so checking a note rewrote it, hand-edits forged status, and every consumer had to distrust the file it was reading"; rebuild-from-files "starts fully `unchecked` and ignores frontmatter status" (fail-closed on forgery).

**Prior states & when they changed.** In-file status: card field (Old Skeleton→alpha.10) → frontmatter `check_status`, worker-owned but present in the file (alpha.11–alpha.14, *trusted as authority until scan*) → removed from files entirely, DB-only (alpha.15).

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** Beta.1 **partially reverts** the alpha.15 no-in-file-marker cut: it re-introduces an in-file `%%prov:mc%%` marker. But it does *not* revert to the alpha.14 model where the in-file field was trusted authority — beta.1's marker is "display shorthand … kept as a claim, not authority," and green requires a **three-way agreement** (marker + current block hash + DB `checks` row). That three-way gate is beta.1's structural answer to the exact forgery hazard alpha.15 had solved by omission (delete the in-file field so it cannot be forged); beta.1 instead keeps the field but denies it standalone authority. There is no alpha-line precedent for a *non-authoritative* in-file verdict marker — every prior in-file status (alpha.11–alpha.14) was authoritative until scanned, and alpha.15 had none at all.

### ADR-127 derivation graph + two-tier stale/invalid demotion propagation (dropped) — NEW gap

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The transitive derivation/demotion mechanism is among the most continuously-developed threads in the entire history — it is named in the founding thesis: integrity "rests on a **tamper-evident derivation graph** + continuous structural checks + **cascade rollback**" (alpha.11 §0/§2, quoted in the Part IV synthesis). Its lineage step by step:
- **alpha.1:** content provenance as "a claim-to-evidence chain by construction" (ScientistOne's 0/337 hallucinated references), plus the first *demotion-propagation* primitive — the `fama-exposure` detector (HIGH): "a downstream note wikilinking a *superseded* claim … reuse of obsolete memory," operationalizing ADR-10's FAMA metric as a deterministic graph walk over `superseded_by`.
- **alpha.5:** the argument graph + warrant (ADR-77/78/79) and structural-impact maturity logic (`>=5 relations, >=1 supports, >=1 contradicts`), confirmed unchanged through **alpha.9**'s `structural_impact.py` split.
- **alpha.11:** cascade rollback as a first-class operation — "the **rollback** unit is the output Concept … everything transitively derived from it … machine-derived downstream auto-reverts; human-directed downstream halts auto-revert but still propagates a flag." This is the direct ancestor of the two-tier scheme.
- **alpha.15 / ADR-127:** the culmination — a first-class DB **derivation graph** (consumed-at-verdict on every edge) driving **two-tier demotion propagation**: depth-1 machine consumers → `unchecked`; depth-≥2 machine descendants → DB `stale` (still served to `ask` with mandatory stale framing, excluded from correctness-bearing consumers); PI descendants → ask-routed flag, never auto-destroyed; retraction propagates one level deeper. The recorded rationale is "the build-system stale-vs-invalid lesson, so one demoted foundational node cannot cascade a whole subgraph into an attention flood"; hard exclusion was rejected because "hard exclusion breaks cross-period queries."

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** Beta.1 has **no analog** and drops the mechanism: it keeps only per-block hash divergence (edit under an `mc` marker → amber + re-check) plus footprint-scoped DB reconciliation on revert — "no source-changes-so-consumers-demote cascade, no derivation graph." This is the first version since alpha.1 to carry *no* multi-hop propagation: every alpha era from alpha.1's `fama-exposure` walk, through alpha.11's transitive cascade, to alpha.15's two-tier graph, shipped some form of derivation-following demotion. Beta.1's single-batch, footprint-scoped reconciliation is nearer in spirit to alpha.1's detector-only surfacing than to the alpha.11/15 transitive auto-cascade, but even alpha.1 walked the `superseded_by` graph; beta.1's reconciliation is scoped to a revert's own batch footprint, not derivation-transitive. The drop is the trade partner of the byte-immutability gate above: beta.1 substitutes a per-block byte-hash regime for the graph-propagation regime alpha.15 had built.

### ADR-128 apply-rate rubber-stamp / disposition-calibration detector (dropped) — NEW gap

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The *disposition telemetry* the detector sits on is alpha.1-hard-won; the *detector* itself is an alpha.15 late arrival. In **alpha.1**, `board_export.py` projected review transitions into a `disposition.jsonl` recording `accept | edit | reject`, explicitly marked **un-backfillable** because "accept/edit/reject can only be observed at the moment of the human decision" (parallel to ADR-25's un-reconstructable audit). The conceptual seed of a rubber-stamp signal is ADR-51/ADR-03: "an Inbox item the PI can clear without reading is a design smell," and "handing a human a confident verdict **reduces** scrutiny" (automation bias); WIP-cap back-pressure was the alpha.1-era counter-pressure. **Alpha.14** introduced the *falsifiability* framing: the seeded-error harness "measures the human checkpoint against a no-checkpoint baseline … a checkpoint that isn't measurably better than none is theatre and gets thinned." **Alpha.15 / ADR-128** fused these into the detector: dispositions made "first-class and non-backfillable … from first ingest; every `attention resolve` journals the routing class (act/ask/log) and outcome (apply/reject/defer) with decision time; **apply-rate bounds (>0.9 or <0.2) are the rubber-stamp detector**" — the recorded rationale being "This is what makes 'calibrated' falsifiable," grounded in the Jacobs 2021 clinician+ML degradation and Feng-Liu verification-asymmetry literature that ADR-128 used to reverse the write-time gate ("the gate degrades to a cursory nod under load").

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** Beta.1 **continues the disposition-capture lineage but drops the detector**: it "captures dispositions at the review action (accept/reject/defer; **accept-unedited vs accept-after-edit**)" — the accept-unedited/accept-after-edit split notably revives alpha.1's exact `accept | edit | reject` distinction — "but ships no apply-rate rubber-stamp detector." So the un-backfillable disposition substrate that runs alpha.1→alpha.15 is retained; what is dropped is the alpha.15-specific layer on top that made ADR-128's "calibrated-or-thinned" theatre-check *measurable*. There is no pre-alpha.15 precedent for the detector itself (it arrived only at alpha.15 as the falsifiability instrument for ADR-128's epistemic reversal), so the drop returns the disposition surface to its pre-alpha.15 state — records the decisions, does not audit the surface's calibration against a no-checkpoint baseline.

## 6. Machine judgment / LLM allocation

Historical/lineage analysis of each §6 gap, grounded in the design-history document (Part I Origins, Part II alpha.1, Part III per-version deltas, Part IV arcs). Descriptive only.

### Tier-2 LLM tension / contradiction judge

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** Contradiction detection is one of the oldest machine-judgment threads in Memoria, and the specific *layered* form the gap describes is a two-part structure whose two tiers arrived at opposite ends of the alpha line.

- *Tier-1 (NLI) origins.* In the alpha.1 ADR-30 ingest pipeline, contradiction surfacing existed only as **Tier-2 "Optional, best-effort" — an advisory NLI contradiction signal** alongside the `[!brief]` comparative-narrative LLM call (Part II §1, line 519). The two-typed-layer split of ADR-52 (an entity citation graph vs. an argument graph over notes) is what made a "contradictions dashboard (ADR-09)" and "claim supersession (ADR-10)" possible at all (line 195–196). Through the middle alpha line this signal was repeatedly pushed *down*: **alpha.8** deferred (not killed) "NLI/MaxSAT contradiction automation" (line 1599); **alpha.9** "re-tiered contradiction detection down to a cheap soft-flag" and **deferred the high-precision NLI + decomposed-variable-match gate + MaxSAT apparatus**, on the strength of on-box measurement that "NLI confidently mints false contradictions (0.94–1.00) on same-topic/different-variable pairs" plus an explicit N=1 error-cost model ("a missed/late contradiction is self-correcting and low-cost for a single PI"), and it simultaneously **retired cosine as the contradiction/supersession arbiter** (negation-blind 0/8 where NLI scored 8/8) and killed "auto-applied contradiction/supersession links" (lines 1376, 1412–1414). NLI was thus a *hard-won* re-admission: **alpha.11** shipped `surface-tensions` and `integrity-contradiction-check` as operation Concepts (lines 1657, 1685), and **alpha.15** hardened Tier-1 into the pinned local NLI comparator with tri-class SUPPORTED/REFUTED/NOTENOUGHINFO, first-class abstain, an FP budget, a HANS-style overlap-but-opposite acceptance gate, and a loud cosine/BM25→*ask* degraded mode (ADR-129; PR-I #1176, line 2168).
- *Tier-2 (LLM judge) is a late arrival.* The LLM tension judge itself is **the newest judgment layer in the entire alpha line** — it landed only in **alpha.15, PR-AF #1200 (`0a1c1b09`)**: an LLM judge "admissible only on Tier-1 abstain/HANS-degraded hard cases, retrieval-grounded, abstain-first, temperature 0, quote-grounded from both consumable checked files … a proposer, never an authority; its verdict never promotes or demotes anything by itself" (line 2170). Its recorded rationale is a rejection of LLM-judge-as-authority — "GBrain's failure; ~28% wrong belief-flips in ConCoRD-class systems" (ADR-129 Alternatives, line 2170).

**How B1 maps onto that history.** B1 keeps NLI-within-the-retrieved-set (deberta) as its contradiction mechanism and drops the Tier-2 LLM judge. Because Tier-2 had **no alpha-line precedent before alpha.15** (the alpha.9→alpha.14 posture had *no* LLM contradiction judge at all — the high-precision engine was deferred throughout), B1's removal returns machine contradiction judgment to the NLI-only shape that held from the alpha.9 re-tiering until PR-AF #1200 landed the LLM layer at the very end of alpha.15. Framed against the recorded history: B1 discards the single most-recently-added judgment layer and continues the *Tier-1 NLI* line (itself continuous alpha.1→alpha.9→alpha.11→alpha.15), while the alpha.5-onward "argument-graph"/tension framing is treated in §8.

### Field-authority enrichment merge

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** Per-field, best-source-wins merge with provenance is **original to alpha.1 and among the most empirically hard-won decisions in the project.** ADR-30's Tier-1 resolve/merge was "S2 + OpenAlex co-primary, Crossref for non-arXiv DOIs, merged per-field best-source-wins with provenance (authors/ORCID/affiliations ← OpenAlex; intents/tldr/embedding ← S2; references = union deduped by DOI)" (line 518). The rationale is a load-bearing spike, not a preference: "two adversarial red-team rounds and an empirical spike on the user's 867-entry library" found "the three metadata sources disagree and are each incomplete (reference counts of 151/129/146 for the same paper across S2/OpenAlex/Crossref; ORCID present in OpenAlex but ~0 in S2), which is why the merge is per-field-best-source rather than single-source-precedence," and the **single-source (S2-only) alternative was explicitly rejected** "because the spike showed each source is incomplete" (line 521). A `calibration.yaml` entity-resolution confidence floor of 0.85 (ADR-56) routed low-confidence merges to an Inbox flag from the start (line 527).

**Prior states and when they changed.** The pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton had catalog metadata riding **Zotero + Better BibTeX** (ADR-05/06) rather than a multi-source merge (line 2314) — that authority question is §7's territory. The merge became a *dedicated subsystem with database tables* in **alpha.13**: `enrichment._merge_doi_source` with Crossref authoritative for title/authors/issued/DOI/container/type, Unpaywall for OA/license, OpenAlex for IDs, each canonical field recording `_provenance(field_path, value, winning_provider)` via `state.replace_field_provenance()` (line 1856), backed by four new SQLite tables — `external_ids`, `enrichment_runs`, `provider_payloads`, `field_provenance` (line 1868) — with the exec-plan's top risk stated as "Provider disagreement — never silently pick a winner on a critical field." (At alpha.13 the live provider set narrowed to Crossref/OpenAlex/Unpaywall with S2/PubMed marked deferred, line 1892.) **alpha.15** then hardened it (PR-F #1173): "static field-authority table, deterministic blob replay, `provider_coverage = full|partial|degraded`, high-authority conflict marks degraded and raises attention (never silent overwrite); `update-work` cannot mark degraded coverage checked," superseding "ADR-30's tiered-ingest merge" under the principle "provider output is claims, not ground truth" (ADR-129, lines 2138, 2314). The `field_provenance`/`enrichment_runs`/`provider_payloads` tables sit in the formalized runtime schema at release end (`schema_v1.sql`, line 2098).

**How B1 maps onto that history.** B1's "OpenAlex singleton fills empty fields only (JabRef rule); conflicts render as per-field diffs through Review, never auto-applied; raw responses in a `payloads` store" **has no alpha-line precedent as the merge model** — per-field best-source-wins was continuous from alpha.1 (ADR-30) through alpha.13 (implementation + tables) to alpha.15 (hardening), and even the earliest version already rejected the single-provider path the B1 empty-field-fill rule now adopts (the 867-paper-spike conclusion "the spike showed each source is incomplete" was the explicit reason *not* to single-source). B1's `payloads` store is the one surviving element (a reduced form of alpha.13's `provider_payloads`); the `field_provenance`/`enrichment_runs` tables and the `provider_coverage=degraded` state have no B1 analog. So this is a departure from a day-one, spike-grounded decision rather than a reversion to any named earlier era.

### Shadow-first calibration

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The shadow-first / `production_enabled:false` discipline built up incrementally across the alpha line. Its seed is **ADR-66** (reserved in alpha.1, deferred — "semi-autonomous batch triage above a calibrated confidence, e.g. >0.92," line 543), with the calibration-spec work first deferred at **alpha.4** (#379/#559 "calibration spec + score-* skills," line 847) and shadow/instrument telemetry harvest appearing at **alpha.8** (ADR-38 similarity ratchet, line 1087). It became a shipped artifact at **alpha.9**: the `clustering.quality_thresholds` calibration contract in `calibration.yaml` with `production_enabled:false`, null floors, `min_reviewed_maps:20` — "null thresholds mean shadow/report-only — the same shadow-first discipline as the pre-file gate" (#379/ADR-66, line 1381). **alpha.11** shipped `calibration.yaml` proper with `production_enabled:false` and null thresholds across `clustering`, `hybrid_scores.candidate_rank`, and pinned model-version slots, under the §10 statement "shadow-first, error-budgeted calibration discipline: no score affects routing until grounded within an error budget; ungrounded ⇒ report-only; drift ⇒ revert to shadow" (lines 1658–1659).

**A15 end-state.** **alpha.15 generalized shadow-first from a per-surface contract into the rule for every score** (ADR-66 → the rule for every score): "a new score reports/journals but changes no routing/promotion/visibility until its thresholds are grounded on recorded PI dispositions with a named fixture and pinned model … every tunable number ships behind `production_enabled:false` semantics; the first grounded thresholds arrive with beta-era disposition data" (line 2172). The only calibration change in alpha.15 was removing the *stale clustering-config section* — the file itself and its `entity_resolution`/`classify`/`hybrid_scores` floors survive (PR #1204; lines 2172, 2226); "none of `calibration.yaml`'s constants were ever validated."

**How B1 maps onto that history.** Two things in the history point directly at beta. First, alpha.15 itself recorded the expectation that "the first grounded thresholds arrive with **beta-era disposition data**" — so B1's move to actually *tune* thresholds is the moment alpha.15 anticipated. Second, B1's HANS/PAWS launch-blocking acceptance test **continues the alpha.15 Tier-1 HANS acceptance-set gate** (line 2168). The *departure* from the trajectory is the mechanism: B1 tunes NLI thresholds "on a small pre-registered calibration set then **frozen**," per-engine, rather than grounding on recorded PI dispositions and retaining the `production_enabled:false`/report-only-until-grounded/drift-revert-to-shadow loop; and it carries no `calibration.yaml` floor framework. So B1 continues the "ground the thresholds at beta" arc while dropping the generalized shadow-first-until-grounded machinery that alpha.9→alpha.11→alpha.15 had built up.

### Operations runner-policy capability manifest (and `method_class`)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The runner-policy capability manifest descends from the governing principle recorded at **alpha.2**: "engines write, agents judge … any write whose correct content is derivable by rule is performed by an engine (deterministic code), never by an LLM agent" (ADR-57, line 663), itself justified by the batch-invariance literature ("one prompt 1,000 times at temperature 0 produced 80 distinct outputs"). The *manifest form* is born at **alpha.11**, when "policy binds to operations, not profiles": operations became Concepts carrying policy in frontmatter — "`operation_id`, `allowed_tools`, `allowed_paths`, `allowed_network`, `runner`, `model`, `prompt_version`, `io_schema`, `risk_class`, `required_checks`" across 40 `capabilities/operations/*.md`, with a **runner split** (`runner: direct_api | local | hermes`) and separation of duties (generators are never their own checkers) (lines 1665–1669). This is the direct ancestor of A15's `runner.test`/`runner.live` + `allowed_tools`/`allowed_paths`/`allowed_network` + `risk_class`/`required_checks`/`io_schema`/`prompt_version` manifest.

**The `method_class` step (the CHANGED framing).** `method_class` was **not** an alpha.15 field — it was an **alpha.14** addition that co-existed with the runner policy for exactly one release: alpha.14's loader required that "every operation declares `method_class` and runner policy or fails loading (§4)" (line 2024), alongside pydantic-ai becoming the sole runner ("all checked operation manifests declare `runner: pydantic-ai`," line 1940) and the loader rejecting directory-only manifests. The **alpha.15 consolidation dropped `method_class`** in favor of the runner-policy manifest: consistent with the gap analysis's finding that 0 of 47 shipped A15 manifests carry `method_class` while 47/47 carry `runner:`. At the same time, alpha.15 relocated the manifests from *gated vault data* to *PR-reviewed product code*: the `capabilities/` workspace tree + `capability-index.json` (1421 lines) were removed (PR-E2 #1172, line 2227), and the manifests became product-code capability manifests that "fail to load on a manifest/sealing violation" (ADR-129). So the A15 end-state is: manifest concept continuous from alpha.11, runner policy retained and elevated to `runner.test`/`runner.live`, `method_class` present only in the intervening alpha.14 and dropped at the consolidation.

**How B1 maps onto that history.** B1 replaces the per-operation runner-policy manifest declaration with a prose "operation test" (pinned prompt+model, deterministically checkable, arithmetic price, per-item corpus-scale) and ships no capability-manifest registry. Two mappings: (a) B1's dropping of `method_class` is **not a delta against A15** — A15 had already dropped it at the consolidation; the enum lived only in alpha.14, so its removal traces to the alpha.14→alpha.15 boundary, not to beta.1. (b) B1's prose "operation test" has **no direct alpha-line manifest precedent**, but it echoes alpha.14's *gate* apparatus around model dispatch — the live-runner proof gate (`doctor --check runner --live`, "deterministic digest fixtures do not count as runner proof," line 1956) and the seeded-error harness measuring the checkpoint against a no-checkpoint baseline (line 1960) — recast as prose ship/no-ship criteria rather than a declared registry. The nearest alpha-line analog for "which LLM calls ship" is alpha.15's build-time `eval select-models` (ADR-11 amended to permit build-time model selection, line 2198).

### {0,1,2}-ordinal checked-input gap scoring + saturation band

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** Both halves — the ordinal severity scoring and the saturation band — descend from the **alpha.5 Project gate (ADR-77/78/79).** Saturation was introduced there as *theoretical* saturation (Dey's "theoretical sufficiency," not data saturation): `structural_impact.py` computed a maturity threshold (`MATURITY_RELATION_THRESHOLD = 5`, requiring ≥1 `supports` AND ≥1 `contradicts` — "Popper: a thesis corroborated only by accumulated supports looks complete but is intellectually untested") and gated **saturation** on three conditions, including "no on-path gap with impact ≥ `HIGH_IMPACT_THRESHOLD = 2`" plus a structural-refutation floor and a PI `refutation_sufficiency` stamp (lines 969–971). The gap taxonomy shipped as `additive`/`conflict`/`fragility` confident kinds with `structural`/`refutation` advisory (line 973), and `gap.yaml` carried the derived cache (`impact`, `on_path`, `saturation_state`, `graph_maturity`; line 961). ADR-79 also reserved `warrant` optional and left the unstated-warrant detector off (line 987). These integer impact levels (`HIGH_IMPACT_THRESHOLD = 2`) are the concrete ancestors of the A15 `{0,1,2}` ordinals.

**Prior states and when they changed.** **alpha.8** renamed the enums (`graph_maturity → argument_stage`, value `immature → developing`; `saturation_state → …`, line 1272). **alpha.11** shipped `analyze-gaps` (runner:local, "reads checked Concepts only") plus `analyze-project-argument`/`surface-tensions`/`compare-and-contrast`, making "gap analysis first-class … over both graphs and their correspondence" (line 1685). **alpha.14** made `project gaps` project-scoped, feeding "checked project argument/Toulmin health into the shared `analyze-gaps` runtime path," and computed coverage from checked SQLite catalog terms/topics/`research_area` (lines 2002–2004). **alpha.15** produced the exact A15 end-state named in the gap: "native gap analysis: checked-only inputs, canonical gap kinds (`full-text-missing`, `citation-neighborhood`, mapped `argument-*`), **fixed `{0,1,2}` ordinal severity (`score 0..8`, bands ≥4/≥2/else)**, `--json` findings … project **saturation** block … saturation = a claim with ≥1 addressed support AND ≥1 counterpoint. Advisory, never gating." — explicitly "absorbs ADR-79's saturation floor" and "reads checked inputs only so unverified content can't drive the compose-flow phase signal" (ADR-129, line 2160).

**How B1 maps onto that history.** B1's "gap analysis is retrieval-coverage counting with a printed denominator (§8); no fixed-ordinal checked-input scoring, no saturation band" **drops the entire ADR-79-descended argument-topology lineage** — the `{0,1,2}` ordinals (formalization of alpha.5's `HIGH_IMPACT_THRESHOLD = 2` impact levels) and the saturation band (continuous ADR-79 alpha.5 → alpha.8 rename → alpha.11 `analyze-gaps` → alpha.14 Toulmin → alpha.15 saturation block). Retrieval-coverage counting was, in the alpha line, only *one dimension among* the argument-health dimensions (alpha.14 §10 "reads both work coverage and knowledge graph coverage … topic/facet coverage," line 2004); B1 elevates it to the *sole* gap model, which has no alpha-line precedent as the primary framing. The broader removal of argument-graph/saturation/thesis-health gap analysis is §8's subject; within §6, the specific casualty is the fixed-ordinal checked-input scoring and its saturation band — the newest formalization (alpha.15) of the oldest gap-engine machinery (alpha.5 ADR-79), retired together.

## 7. Catalog / bibliographic authority

### Bibliographic authority location and primary key: `record.md`/CSL-JSON/citekey (B1) vs. SQLite catalog/`work_id` (A15)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** A15's "SQLite catalog + blobs are the citation authority, `work_id`/provider IDs primary, citekeys are aliases, `references.bib` generated from SQLite" (ADR-124) is the *terminal* state of a five-release migration, and each of its clauses displaced an earlier markdown/citekey design:

- **Pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton" → alpha.1 (the markdown+citekey origin).** The founding catalog was markdown, and the *citekey was the identity*. The Old Skeleton shipped a `paper` type at `catalog/papers/` whose required fields were exactly `citekey`, `title`; the bibliographic backbone was **Zotero + Better BibTeX** (ADR-05), with citekey naming governed by ADR-06 — one of the three "unexamined tool bets" adopted at the outset. alpha.1 inherited this literally: ingest's canonical visible output was `catalog/papers/<citekey>.md` and the derived-extract store keyed on `.memoria/data/extracts/<citekey>.md`. At alpha.5 this citekey-backbone was completed on the capture side — a "Zotero Tier-0 catalog stub" QuickAdd flow read the BBT selection and wrote a catalog stub, explicitly "completing the ADR-05/06 citekey-backbone capture path." So in the alpha.1–alpha.10 era the answer to both of B1's clauses was already "yes": authority lived in markdown, and the citekey was the path/primary key.

- **alpha.11 (the primary-key demotion).** The `paper` + note-`source` merge into one `source` type at `catalog/sources/<source_id>/source.md` is the exact moment the citekey **stopped** being primary. `source_id` became the immutable path and `citekey` was "**demoted to optional alias**," on the recorded rationale that "citekeys are metadata-derived and get corrected, so they are a generated display/export alias, never the path (… fixture-verified across a citekey correction)" (§6.1). Note that authority was still *markdown* here (`source.md` with `csl_json` inline) — alpha.11 changed the key, not the store.

- **alpha.12 (the markdown→SQLite flip).** The keep-test (ADR-122) moved the relational catalog into `.memoria/state/memoria.sqlite` — `catalog_sources(source_id PK, doi UNIQUE, …, citekey, csl_json, …)` — judging the "relational catalog graph as working state that fails the survival test," while the source *concept file* survived as a projection carrying only a **compact citation survival copy** (`source_id`, `title`, `authors`, `issued`, one of `doi`/`url`/`citekey`) under the §3 cross-store reference rule. `references.bib` flipped to SQLite-first-with-file-fallback and became atomic with captures. This is the release where bibliographic authority left markdown.

- **alpha.14 (the completion; ADR-124).** The full standalone cut removed *all* markdown Work/Author/Source/Institution Concepts — everything became SQLite rows, and a `workspace check --assert no-legacy-work-markdown` gate plus an `rg` negative gate forbade any legacy markdown Work path from returning. ADR-124 "Standalone catalog is the citation authority" (`supersedes: [5]`, absorbing ADR-06) codified the A15 wording verbatim: catalog+blobs drive `references.bib`; "citekeys are aliases while stable Work IDs/provider identifiers are the primary runtime keys." The ADR explicitly *rejected* a "frontmatter-only catalog" ("alpha.14 needs durable worker state, provider payload provenance, full-text gates … better represented in SQLite") — i.e., it considered and declined the record.md-style design B1 now adopts.

- **alpha.15 (hardening).** The records-vs-Concepts-vs-projections model finalized this: `work` became a new type that is "a source's subjective digest + tags, 1:1 with the catalog work (`work_id`); the objective record stays in SQLite," under the ADR-125 split-authority rule ("files own meaning, the DB owns records/verdicts"). This was a hard-won terminal state, not a late arrival — the arc summary (Part IV) records it as the endpoint of a deliberate multi-release trajectory.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 puts **full CSL-JSON in `record.md` as the fidelity/re-import authority** and makes the **citekey the primary key, pinned at import (inherited from Better BibTeX)**, with `bibliography.bib` generated from the stored CSL-JSON. Both moves are **reversions to named earlier eras**, on both axes independently:

- The *store* axis reverts the alpha.12 keep-test flip: authority returns to markdown (`record.md`), and full CSL-JSON is stored in the file rather than the alpha.12 *compact survival copy* — going even further into markdown than alpha.12's projection ever did (alpha.12 deliberately kept only the compact map because the full relational catalog "fails the survival test"). It re-adopts precisely the "frontmatter-only catalog" alternative ADR-124 named and rejected.
- The *primary-key* axis reverts past alpha.11's explicit §6.1 demotion, all the way to the **Old Skeleton / ADR-05–06 citekey-backbone**: "citekey pinned at import (inherited from Better BibTeX)" restates the ADR-05 Zotero/BBT backbone and ADR-06 citekey naming as the identity source. The one wrinkle with no clean alpha precedent is *pinning at import* — alpha.11 demoted the citekey precisely *because* "citekeys are metadata-derived and get corrected," whereas B1 freezes it at import time; the alpha line never had a pinned-citekey-as-key state, so B1's key discipline is a new synthesis over an old (pre-alpha.11) key choice.

**Recorded context.** The decision that A15 is reverting is pinned to specific records: ADR-122 Context ("ADR-49's catalog-frontmatter-as-SSOT is now too strong"), the alpha.12 keep-test ("what must survive Memoria's death is markdown OKF + a portable bibliography; everything else is free to be SQLite"), and ADR-124's four rejected alternatives (Zotero-required, frontmatter-only, live-API-on-demand). The citekey demotion is pinned to alpha.11 §6.1 and fixture-verified across a citekey correction.

### Zotero as reading/capture surface: Zotero-first day-1 (B1) vs. Zotero optional (A15)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** A15's "Memoria does not require a live Zotero library" is a **late-arriving demotion** (alpha.14) of a tool that had been foundational since before alpha.1:

- **Origins.** Zotero is not a later addition — it is in Memoria's DNA. The founding synthesis explicitly crosses Karpathy's LLM-wiki with **Elena Razlogova's *Doing History with Zotero and Obsidian***, and "**Zotero (with Better BibTeX) as the bibliographic backbone**" is listed as one of the three original tool bets. The researcher's own notes flag it as a default "adopted without evaluating fit."
- **alpha.1–alpha.5 (Zotero as live capture backbone).** ADR-05/06 made Zotero the backbone; `capture-from-zotero.js` was the capture path; deployment was "Zotero on localhost." Zotero capture appears as a tracked defect (alpha.3 D-5, "Zotero capture broken") and then as an active hardening target: alpha.4/5 switched the read path from BBT CAYW to the **BBT JSON-RPC `item.citationkey` endpoint** (the stable selection API), and alpha.5 materialized the "Zotero Tier-0 catalog stub." Agents reached Zotero read-only through a `pyzotero` MCP server. Through this whole span Zotero was the primary bibliographic on-ramp.
- **alpha.11 (narrowed to read-only import; annotations excluded).** Capture adapters became "a small fixed set" including `capture-zotero-source`, with Zotero import via the **read-only Zotero Local API** — "not a translator engine." Critically for B1's reading direction, §13 recorded: "**Zotero annotations are not an alpha.11 import path; Memoria-owned PDF capture stores canonical quote/span/hash selectors itself.**" So even at its narrowest-but-present, the alpha line kept annotation/PDF capture *Memoria-owned*, not Zotero-owned.
- **alpha.14 (the demotion to optional; ADR-124).** The standalone cut *dropped Zotero as required authority*, not deferred: the Zotero-specific import surface (`--format zotero-export`, worker operation, runtime helper, `capture-zotero-source.md`) was removed; only generic BibTeX/CSL import remained; `work import --format zotero-export` was made to fail as unsupported; and an `rg` negative gate was added to reject any reappearance of the Zotero import surface. ADR-124 ("Zotero and Better BibTeX are optional user tools … Memoria does not require a live Zotero library") supersedes ADR-05. Zotero "survives only as importers, not authority" — the exact A15 stance.
- **alpha.15.** Unchanged from the alpha.14 demotion; Zotero remained optional.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 makes Zotero import **day-1**, reading **Zotero-first** (Zotero's PDF reader is the primary read; annotations sync back), and PDF acquisition a "capture in Zotero" handoff. This is a two-part mapping:

- **Re-centering Zotero as the primary bibliographic/reading surface** *reverts the alpha.14 demotion-to-optional* and reaches back past alpha.11's read-only-import stance to the **Origins/ADR-05 Razlogova "Zotero + Obsidian" backbone era** — the founding posture in which Zotero was the bibliographic backbone and localhost was the assumed deployment.
- **Annotation sync-back / Zotero's reader as the primary read has no alpha-line precedent.** Every alpha state that touched annotations kept them **Memoria-owned**: alpha.11 §13 explicitly declined Zotero annotations as an import path in favor of Memoria-owned PDF capture with canonical quote/span/hash selectors, and A15 inherited that. B1's "Zotero PDF reader is the primary read, annotations sync back" therefore does *not* revert to any earlier era — it is a new direction on the reading/annotation axis, running opposite to the standing alpha-line rule that Memoria owns its own selectors.

**Recorded context.** The demotion B1 reverses is pinned to ADR-124 (Context: "Alpha.14 makes Memoria a standalone CLI/engine. A required Zotero library or live Better BibTeX auto-export would violate that") and to the alpha.14 design/release rule that Zotero be removed from "default install, verification, package metadata, and current user docs," enforced by the `rg` negative gate; the installer requirement was stated as "Require no Zotero setup." The founding Zotero commitment is pinned to the Origins tool-bets list and the Razlogova methodology; the annotation-ownership rule that B1 crosses is pinned to alpha.11 §13.

## 8. Discovery / gap analysis

### Gap: B1 has no argument-graph / saturation / thesis-health gap analysis and no note-mode-driven gap obligations; gaps are retrieval-coverage-based only

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** Gap analysis in Memoria began as a *structural feedback loop*, not an argument audit. At **alpha.1** the only live analogue was the knowledge cycle's *map*/*verify* tasks raising Inbox `gap` cards that re-triggered *catalog* — "the output end of the cycle feeds the intake end" (`knowledge-cycle.md`); the `gap` card itself was scoped narrowly to a "missing-source need" (ADR-51). The *argument-graph* dimension the alpha.15 side carries is a **hard-won alpha.5 arrival**. It was authored in `project-starter.md` (written at alpha.4, moved to alpha.5's `tmp/`) as the Project gate's central bet: **two graphs, not one** — a bottom-up descriptive knowledge map *and* a top-down Toulmin argument graph (`supports`/`contradicts`/`warrant` rooted at a thesis), where "the outline derives from the argument graph, not the topology." alpha.5 shipped this as deterministic Operations: `structural_impact.py` (~800 lines) computing per-note impact as lost-reachability-from-thesis; a `gap_type` taxonomy on `gap.yaml` (`additive, conflict, fragility, structural, unstated-warrant, refutation`); and **saturation** as a checkable topology fact — ADR-79 fixed the floor at "at least one addressed support and one addressed contradiction so the Project gate does not report saturation over a one-sided graph." ADR-77/78/79 (accepted 2026-06-16) recorded the pieces (project gate / thesis note / argument-graph+warrant). alpha.8 renamed the enums (`graph_maturity → argument_stage`, `saturation_state → evidence_saturation`). By **alpha.14**, `memoria project gaps` became project-scoped and fed "checked project argument/Toulmin health into the shared `analyze-gaps` runtime path" alongside the workspace-wide coverage scan — the two halves (coverage + argument health) unified into one engine, "the directed/writing half of the loop, not just coverage" (`design.md` §4/§10).

**The note-mode obligation layer is an alpha.15 net-new.** alpha.15's four-type collapse (ADR-126) gave `note` a declared `mode: claim | question` precisely so gap analysis could feed on it: "a claim without evidence is an `under-warranted` gap; an open question is a compose-flow lead … field-derived typing could not tell a plain thought from a claim missing its evidence." alpha.15's native gap analysis (ADR-129, PR-G #1174) then absorbed **ADR-79's saturation floor** directly into the gap engine's `saturation` block (saturation = a claim with ≥1 addressed support AND ≥1 counterpoint, advisory/never gating), over checked-only inputs, with canonical kinds `full-text-missing`, `citation-neighborhood`, and mapped `argument-*`.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's retrieval-coverage-only gap model (unsearchable/partial/superseded + question-facet df-0 content gaps, with a printed denominator) **keeps the coverage half that alpha.1's cycle and alpha.14's `analyze-gaps` carried and drops the entire alpha.5→alpha.15 argument-graph lineage** — ADR-77/78/79's Toulmin graph, `structural_impact.py`'s impact/saturation, and alpha.15's note-mode `under-warranted`/compose-lead obligations. This is a reversion past alpha.5: the last state in which gap analysis was *coverage-only* was the pre-alpha.5 era (alpha.1's map/verify `gap` cards). There is no alpha-line precedent for the *printed-denominator, retrieval-coverage-itemized-by-cause* framing itself — that specific shape is new in B1 — but the retrieval-coverage *scope* returns gap analysis to where it stood before the Project gate introduced argument health.

### Gap: B1 discovery is citation-graph-only; no topic/concept-graph-driven discovery

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** At **alpha.1** discovery was operator-triggered `find` through the Librarian's `paper_search` MCP (web disabled, ADR-32) — keyword/candidate search, not graph traversal. The *graph* that the alpha.15 side draws discovery from is a **late arrival built in alpha.13/14**: DOI enrichment imports the first-order citation/reference/topic graph — OpenAlex `concepts`/`topics` (→ `research_area`) and `authorships`+institutions, Crossref `relation` rows, and BibTeX/CSL reference edges → `work_graph_edges` (§9). alpha.14 wired that multi-signal graph into discovery directly: gap analysis "counts checked topic/keyword graph-edge terms as source evidence" (#1138/#1143/#1149/#1155), "SQLite-detected source/topic gaps carry checked source IDs into discovery candidate generation, so candidate Works are proposed from first-order reference/related graph edges without qmd rediscovery" (#1147), and "Crossref `related`/OpenAlex graph edges feed discovery" (#1150). alpha.15 carried this forward as the A15 statement — discovery informed by the imported topic/concept/keyword/citation graph — with ADR-92 (accepted, PR-AA #1195) adding deterministic relevance scoring over those candidates.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 bounds discovery to the **citation graph only** (snowball depth ≤2 + a scheduled citation monitor, identifiers-only egress, output kept out of gap/coverage denominators). This is the discovery-side face of the same contraction §9 records on the enrichment side (B1 stores only outgoing reference edges + capped cited-by, dropping OpenAlex topic/concept/authorship and Crossref relation edges). Against the history this is not a reversion to any named earlier state: alpha.1's `find` used keyword `paper_search`, and alpha.14/15 explicitly fed *topic/concept* edges into discovery — a citation-graph-*only* discovery input existed at no point on the alpha line. B1 selects the citation subset of the alpha.13-15 imported graph and discards the topic/concept/authorship signals that alpha.14 had specifically routed into candidate generation.

### Gap: the steering-lever + Librarian-persona discovery substrate is dropped entirely (NEW)

**Origin & lineage — the Librarian.** The Librarian is one of Memoria's oldest roles. The **pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton** shipped seven Hermes profiles — "coder/writer/socratic/mapper/librarian/verifier/cmdr" — the `librarian` among them (Old Skeleton `profiles/`; Part I). The alpha.1 consolidation (ADR-48) cut seven→five (co-pi, engineer, **librarian**, peer-reviewer, writer), merging the Old Skeleton's Librarian and Mapper into one Librarian because "both were faithful — intake and corpus mapping are one research-librarian stance pointed in two directions," and folding the proposed ADR-37 retriever-scout discovery split back into the Librarian's `catalog` lane ("find included"). The alpha.1 Librarian ran four lanes (catalog·extract·link·map), "faithful" posture, propose-not-dispose.

**Origin & lineage — the steering lever.** The priorities file the Librarian weights discovery by is equally old: at **alpha.1** the homepage carried `research-focus.md` as "separate **program memory** the Librarian reads at session start to *weight discovery*" (Part II, `home.md`). ADR-61's deferred nightly loop was designed to read the same `research-focus.md` and pick top-N priorities. At **alpha.11** the workspace tree renamed it — `research-focus.md` → **`steering.md`**, "the PI's standing steering" (line 84) / "the authored **program** — the `program.md`-equivalent," making the Karpathy `autoresearch` "edit the program, never touch the code" framing literal (line 1671). By **alpha.15** the A15 side holds `steering.md` as a first-class program-memory priorities file, a named "The Librarian" persona reading it at session start to weight discovery ("the main lever you have over what gets surfaced"), and `system/eval/` rubrics scoring that behavior; ADR-92 concretely uses "deterministic `steering.md` relevance" to rank discovered candidates (line 2162).

**A complicating thread in the history.** The *live agent* Librarian did not survive intact to alpha.15: alpha.11 replaced installed profiles with operations ("operations replace profiles"), and alpha.14 (the standalone-CLI/engine cut) dropped the Hermes runtime, profiles, and board lanes from the product. `steering.md` the *file* persisted through this (feeding ADR-92's deterministic relevance scoring), while the arc synthesis notes the type-zoo cleanup mapped a distinct steering *note-type* to "the `instructions` config" (line 2286) — separate from the priorities file.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 drops `steering.md`, the Librarian persona, and PI-priority weighting entirely; discovery becomes deterministic occurrence-counting over the citation graph, and the per-batch yes/no LLM relevance prefilter is reassigned to seeding/onboarding (§15), not discovery. This severs a substrate whose "program memory the [agent] reads at session start to weight discovery" concept runs **unbroken from alpha.1 (line 237) through the alpha.11 rename to alpha.15**, and a Librarian role that traces to the pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton. §11 records the agent boundary itself as *convergence* with ADR-130 and points here for "the genuine A15→B1 removal … the shipped **Librarian** persona." Against the history there is no alpha-line precedent for discovery with *no* steering/priorities file — `research-focus.md`/`steering.md` was present from alpha.1 forward — so the removal reaches behind the entire alpha line rather than reverting to a named earlier era.

### Gap: B1 reverses the ADR-95 nightly/always-on-loop rejection once the no-daemon premise is removed (NEW)

**Origin & lineage.** The nightly/always-on discovery loop is one of Memoria's longest-deferred proposals. **ADR-61** (present in-tree at the alpha.1 cut, deferred) defined a nightly proactive discovery cron that reads `research-focus.md`, picks top-N priorities, runs `find` per priority, ingests confirmed candidates, enriches stale notes, and posts a morning summary. It never ran: alpha.1 shipped nothing of it ("Discovery in alpha.1 is operator-triggered `find`, not a cron"); alpha.2 deferred it out of the checkpoint (plan §5); and **alpha.5 explicitly refused to accept it to unblock the Project gate** — "gap-pull already works as on-demand `find` via the unified Librarian … ADR-61's own adoption conditions (always-on deploy, maintained `research-focus.md`, a written screening-plan) are unmet." Its named blockers were structural: silent-cron risk, inbox flooding, and — decisively — a dependency on **always-on infrastructure** (Syncthing + VPS, tied to ADR-63). At **alpha.8** the ADR-61 bundle was unbundled into single-decision ADRs, the nightly loop becoming **ADR-95** ("nightly proactive discovery loop," proposed; supersedes ADR-61), whose own "When this matters" required "always-on deployment is active." At **alpha.15** the consolidation sweep flipped ADR-95 to **rejected**, one of four rejections (95 loop / 97 Writer claim-notes / 103 Canvas axis / 106 Hermes cost-capture): "Rejected: unattended loop conflicts with **no-daemon arch**" (disposition map; lines 2210, 2237). The rejection rested entirely on the alpha.14/alpha.15 no-daemon premise (standalone engine cut, ADR-125; §1 A15 "there is no internal daemon").

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 removes the exact premise the alpha.15 rejection stood on. §1 promotes `serve` to a plugin-supervised **resident two-process daemon**, and §7 adds a **scheduled citation monitor** — together the two conditions (an always-on machine + an unattended scheduled loop) that ADR-95's own "When this matters" and ADR-61's adoption conditions demanded. With the no-daemon charter exclusion gone, B1 amends it and reverses the alpha.15 rejection. Version-anchored, this is not a reversion to a *shipped* alpha state — the loop ran in no alpha release — but a revival of the **ADR-61 (alpha.1) / ADR-95 (alpha.8) proposal** that was blocked at every checkpoint on infrastructure grounds and formally rejected only at alpha.15 on the specific no-daemon ground. B1 reaches back past that single rejection to the always-on-loop proposal now that its long-standing precondition (a resident daemon) is finally supplied.

## 9. Ingest / enrichment graph

### The trimmed enrichment graph (OpenAlex concepts/topics/authorship + Crossref relations → refs + capped cited-by only)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The graph the A15 enrichment subsystem imports is almost entirely an *alpha.14 build*, standing on an *alpha.13* foundation, over an *alpha.12* substrate — the whole apparatus is barely two weeks old at the alpha.15 cut.

- The pre-history is thin: early catalog metadata rode **Zotero + Better BibTeX** as the bibliographic backbone (ADR-05/06, Old Skeleton → alpha.1). There was no in-house graph.
- **alpha.12 (2026-06-28, ADR-122)** made SQLite the working-state boundary — the "source/entity/link graph" got a transactional home markdown could not give it (arc (g)).
- **alpha.13 (2026-06-30, ADR-123)** built the *dedicated multi-source enrichment subsystem* — the capture/enrich split, `enrich-source`, per-field provenance — but it was **deliberately DOI-only and narrow**: the shipped `work_graph_edges` content was minimal, and the alpha.13 `ingest.md` deferral list explicitly held back "graph enrichment" alongside Semantic Scholar/PubMed/arXiv.
- **alpha.14 (2026-06-30)** is where the graph itself lands — the design history calls Pipeline/Ingest "the change-heaviest area." The imports were built out commit-by-commit across #1135–#1155: reference edges from portable BibTeX/CSL into `work_graph_edges` (#1139 `c172a0de`); OpenAlex `topics`+`primary_topic` (#1142 `d32dca03`); `concepts` as `topic` edges (#1149 `dedccea8`); `keywords` (#1143); `mesh`+`sustainable_development_goals` as topic edges (#1155 `a618b1ed`); `authorships`+nested institutions+location Source metadata (#1154 `cf9f23ef`); and Crossref `relation` rows as `related` edges (#1150 `c3538d47`). alpha.14 fixed the depth at **first-order by design** — "Exhaustive or provider-specific graph expansion" was a stated non-goal (design §13) — and marked discovered candidate Works as *unchecked attention/worklist rows, never checked*.
- **alpha.15 (2026-07-01/02, ADR-129)** did not widen the graph; it *hardened the merge* over the same edge set (static field-authority table, `provider_coverage=full|partial|degraded`, PR-F #1173) and added the `work_aspects` DB read model (PR-AB #1196, ADR-99). The topic/concept edges were coupled outward into two other A15 systems: the OpenAlex-topic-scored `research_area` classification (part of the controlled-vocab substrate, §3) and the gap-analysis read model — A15 design §10 names "First-order citation/reference graph, OpenAlex Topics, controlled facets" as "part of the read model."

**Prior states.** alpha.13 imported a narrow reference graph; alpha.14 expanded it "far beyond alpha.13" into the topic/concept/authorship/relation edge set; alpha.15 kept that breadth and only reinforced the merge determinism on top of it.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's edge table (outgoing reference edges + *capped cited-by*) is a **partial revert to the alpha.13 narrowness** — it discards the entire alpha.14 graph-import wave. Dropping OpenAlex topic/concept/authorship edges reverses #1142/#1149/#1143/#1155/#1154; dropping Crossref `relation` edges reverses #1150. Because those edges were the alpha.15 read-model inputs for `research_area` classification and topic-facet gap analysis, their removal here is continuous with the parallel B1 removals of the controlled-vocab substrate (§3) and topic/concept-graph-driven discovery (§8). The one element with *no clean alpha-line precedent* is the **capped cited-by edge**: the alpha.14 graph was outward-facing (references/topics/authorship the Work declares) with inbound discovery handled as unchecked attention rows, not as a capped inbound-edge store — B1's capped cited-by is closer in spirit to the B1 bounded snowball monitor (§8) than to any alpha-line edge table.

### Extraction: ported multi-extractor ingest subsystems → pdfium-only, no OCR

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The extraction code is one of the oldest strata in the codebase, and A15 carries it by *import, not authorship*.

- The ingest subsystems — `classify`, `extract`, `ingest_paper`, `link`, `resolve_merge` — originate in the **Old Skeleton (pre-alpha.1)** as operation modules.
- **alpha.11 (2026-06-27)** materialized the ingest spine as operation Concepts, with a small fixed set of capture adapters (`capture-pdf/url/bibtex/zotero-source`) — "not a translator engine" (§9).
- **alpha.14** performed the decisive move for this gap: PR #1117 (`4fca09d8`) **ported all 26 legacy `.memoria/operations/` Python modules** into `src/memoria_vault/runtime/subsystems/`, including `processing/ingest/{classify,extract,ingest_paper,link,resolve_merge,resolve_merge_logic,resolve_merge_pubmed,runner}.py`, *before* deleting their old homes (#1118). The stated rule: "Existing operation implementations are import inputs, not disposable history … No old operation is deleted just because its old host was Hermes/MCP/profile-shaped" (design §5). alpha.14 also made **full-text a hard digest precondition** with an ordered OA acquisition retry loop (Unpaywall → OpenAlex → Crossref, #1140–#1151), and made an abstract-only/metadata-only digest attempt *fail* into a stable full-text attention projection (#1068/#1069/#1135).
- **alpha.15** kept `extract.py` in the live subsystem — it appears in the untrusted-input-sealing diffs (§5, ADR-129 runner policy, alongside `classify.py`/`runner.py`), each text-reading operation carrying a negative poisoned-input fixture.
- Notably, **no A15 source ever claims OCR.** The "multi-extractor" A15 state is the ported subsystem stack; OCR was never in the alpha end-state.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** Fixing a single extractor (**pdfium**) is a *narrowing* of the ported subsystem stack that rode from the Old Skeleton through the alpha.14 import. B1's deterministic distrust flags have a direct alpha-line ancestor: the `abstract_only` flag and `extract_failed:needs_ocr` failure are the descendants of alpha.14's rule that abstract-only/metadata-only input "cannot produce a checked digest" and instead mints a full-text attention projection (design §8, §14 gate). The B1 text-coherence gate is a new deterministic layer with no named alpha-line predecessor. The explicit **"no OCR"** statement is not the removal of a shipped capability — it makes explicit an absence that held across the entire alpha line (no alpha source claimed OCR); it is a codification, not a regression from a prior OCR state.

### The `work interview` elicitation step dropped (add→enrich→interview→digest)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The interview is one of Memoria's **founding through-lines** — the oldest survivor in this subsystem — and it is the "Co-PI differentiator."

- It descends from the **co-pi profile of the Old Skeleton** (the seven specialists coder/writer/socratic/mapper/librarian/verifier/cmdr), consolidated to five profiles by the **alpha.1** cut (co-pi one of the five; ADR-48).
- **alpha.11 (2026-06-27)** materialized it as the `record-copi-interview` operation ("Record a PI interview answer for later digest synthesis"), and the design named it emphatically: "**Conversation (the interview — the hinge)** … it is *not* an optional side chat," the "**core — the Co-PI differentiator, not deferrable**" step, sitting between capture and `compile-source-digest` in the ingest spine (capture → conversation → digest+hub compounding → notes).
- **alpha.14 (2026-06-30)** promoted it to a first-class CLI verb: `work interview` entered the roster (#1051 `dcf06cbc`, #1080, #1109), *records the guided conversation and returns stable Work IDs*, and `work digest` was wired to **consume interview answers**, with digest *failing* if it requires a missing/blocked interview (design §8). The full loop `init→capture→enrich→update→interview→digest` was pinned by the alpha.14 runtime replay gate (#1134).
- **alpha.15 (2026-07-01/02)** kept it through the verb-first cutover — the ADR-130 command scheme lists `work add/enrich/interview/digest` (PR-B3 #1165) — and reframed its runtime home: "The Co-PI interview becomes engine state (`memoria ask/answer/resume`), never a pydantic-ai session or `pydantic-graph`" (alpha.14 design §2, carried). The alpha.15 dogfood checkpoint shipped an **interview replay** fixture as part of proving the minimum loop end-to-end (PR-N #1181).

So the canonical A15 minimum loop `work add → work enrich → work interview → work digest` is **hard-won and continuous** — the interview survived every re-derivation (the alpha.7 UI reset, the alpha.11 architecture reset, the alpha.14 standalone cut) from pre-alpha through alpha.15.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** Dropping the interview and replacing the subjective layer with a **single-pass pinned `digest` LLM call + Zotero annotation sync** has **no alpha-line precedent** — the interactive elicitation step was present continuously from alpha.11's `record-copi-interview` to alpha.15's `work interview`, and was explicitly labeled "not deferrable." B1's substitution of Zotero annotation sync as the subjective-input channel additionally reverses a *specific* alpha.11 exclusion: alpha.11 §13 held that "Zotero annotations are not an alpha.11 import path; Memoria-owned PDF capture stores canonical quote/span/hash selectors itself." B1 routes the subjective layer through exactly the annotation channel alpha.11 declined, consistent with B1's broader re-centering of Zotero as the primary reading/capture surface (§7, §16).

### The Semantic Scholar provider dropped

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** Semantic Scholar has a distinctive lineage — **specified early, deferred through the middle, and only promoted to a live (conditional) provider at the very last alpha.**

- It was named in the **alpha.13 (2026-06-30)** enrichment design from the outset: the multi-source subsystem spec listed "OpenAlex, Semantic Scholar, PubMed, Unpaywall, Crossref, arXiv" (alpha.13 design §0). But alpha.13's *shipped* MVP was deliberately three providers — "MVP starts with DOI required providers. Implement Crossref, OpenAlex, and Unpaywall first … No provider class hierarchy until duplicated code makes it smaller" (design §8). S2/PubMed/arXiv were **explicitly deferred**: the alpha.13 `ingest.md` deferral list and the `set-up-zotero.md`/`integrations.md` updates marked "Semantic Scholar/PubMed" deferred, with enrichment shipping as "Crossref, OpenAlex, and Unpaywall" only.
- **alpha.14** did not add it — the enrichment build-out (#1135–#1155) extended OpenAlex/Crossref/Unpaywall graph and OA acquisition, but S2 stayed deferred.
- **alpha.15 (2026-07-01/02, ADR-129)** is where S2 finally became a first-class (conditional) provider: "Semantic Scholar is **conditional, default-on when keyed**" contributing to the reference union, citation intents as **free Tier-0 contradiction candidates**, TLDRs, and SPECTER embeddings — "never promotion-required," "Like every provider and judge: proposers, never authorities." (scite.ai was added as an optional opt-in in the same ADR.) This is a **late arrival** — S2 spent alpha.13 and alpha.14 on the deferral list and only entered the roster at the final consolidation.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's enrichment roster — **OpenAlex (+ Crossref for retraction/title check)** — reverts past the alpha.15 ADR-129 promotion and lands *narrower even than the alpha.13/14 shipped three-provider set* (it drops Unpaywall from the centered story too, though Unpaywall is a §9-adjacent OA-acquisition concern). Specifically, removing S2 **reverses the ADR-129 (alpha.15) decision** and returns Semantic Scholar to precisely the deferred status it held in alpha.13 and alpha.14. Because the alpha.15 S2 integration was the source of the citation-intent contradiction candidates and SPECTER embeddings, its removal is continuous with B1's parallel rework of contradiction detection (deberta NLI within the retrieved set, §6) and the retrieval substrate (§10) — the machinery S2 fed in A15 no longer exists in B1 to be fed.

## 10. Surfaces / navigation / control

### Gap: B1's Obsidian pane/surface UI + `ViewSpecRenderer`/`view-spec.v1` vs A15's CLI-first read-API with `status --json` (ADR-130's `view-spec` contract)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The surface layer A15 shipped is the terminal state of a long swing from Obsidian-hosted UI toward an engine-with-thin-clients model.

- *Pre-alpha.1 / alpha.1:* Obsidian was chosen as "the vault/UI" for its markdown-plus-frontmatter substrate. The control surface was a **homepage cockpit** (`src/home.md`, `cssclasses: [dashboard]`, ADR-13) opened on startup, carrying an above-fold **Status glance** — a `dataviewjs` block over `board-state.jsonl`/`lint-findings.jsonl` rendering "*N review(s) pending · N blocked · N HIGH/CRITICAL*" — plus an "Act" button row and a Workspaces row (Desk/Library/Studio, ADR-68). This status-glance dataviewjs line is the direct ancestor of A15's `status --json`.
- *alpha.3 (ADR-70/72):* navigation was decided as intent-named gates with JTBD dashboards and a command-surfacing layer (ADR-72).
- *alpha.7:* the whole Obsidian front end was rebuilt clean-slate (ADR-81) into a persistent gate shell over a Bases view layer; `home.md` was gutted from a ~133-line control panel to a single launch-into-Inbox link, and global actions moved to the Commander ribbon. Crucially the "**agent-rendered UI is declarative and catalog-bound … never raw HTML/JS from an agent**" rule and the "one view definition, projections disposable and never a second truth" principle (ADR-102/116) date to this Obsidian era — they are what A15's view-spec later carries forward.
- *alpha.11:* codified the agent-rendered-UI rule as a **declarative, catalog-bound component set** even though the plugin catalog was deferred ("the catalog is deferred with the plugin; the *rule* holds now," §12) — this is the conceptual seed of the fixed view-spec component vocabulary.
- *alpha.14:* the standalone-CLI-and-engine cut removed Obsidian plugins and Hermes profiles from the install unit entirely ("Install no Obsidian plugin"); the product loop became a thin end-to-end CLI cycle (init→capture→enrich→interview→digest→ask→export→journal).
- *alpha.15:* ADR-130 made the engine's **JSON read-API the sole read path** for every surface and agent, with CLI/FastMCP/loopback-HTTP as "pure marshalling skins over one `engine/api` module" ("a wrapper holding logic is a gate failure"). The command scheme cut over to **verb-first** (`new note`, `work add/enrich/interview/digest`, `new hub <tag>`, `link`, `check`, `show`, `list`, `ask`, `export`, plus `project`/`workspace`/`vocab`/`attention`/`request`/`journal` groups). The read-API may attach **declarative, verdict-carrying view-specs from a fixed component vocabulary (table/card), "render known kinds only"** (ADR-130, PR-U #1189) — carrying ADR-102/116's one-definition rule. `status --json` is the descendant of the alpha.1 dataviewjs glance. The Scope Boundary is explicit: "No alpha.15 slice adds an Obsidian plugin, plugin manifest, command-palette command, Inspector panel"; ADR-125 dropped "the Obsidian-hosted policy plugin (28, 31)."

So the view-spec **contract** is a late arrival (alpha.15, ADR-130) but a genealogically continuous one — it is the ADR-102/116 "declarative, catalog-bound, one-truth" rule (alpha.7/alpha.11) re-expressed once the surfaces became CLI/MCP/HTTP. The verb-first CLI is likewise alpha.14→alpha.15.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 splits along the contract/renderer seam the history already draws. The `view-spec.v1` contract that `ViewSpecRenderer` consumes **is the alpha.15 ADR-130 contract** — B1 realizes it, it does not invent it. But every alpha-line client of that contract was CLI/MCP/HTTP; an **Obsidian `ViewSpecRenderer` has no alpha-line precedent** (alpha.14/15 had explicitly removed the Obsidian surface). B1's reintroduction of an Obsidian pane/surface UI is a **partial revert toward the alpha.7–alpha.11 Obsidian-hosted era** that alpha.14/15 dismantled — though ADR-125's terms are that "any future editor adapter requires its own ADR instead of inheriting an alpha.15 commitment," so an Obsidian renderer is a fresh commitment, not a resumed one. The specific **five-interaction-mode framing (Capture/Reading/Ask/Compose/Review) has no alpha precedent**: the alpha line organized surfaces as gates→spaces→queue (see the spaces gap below), never as interaction modes. B1's rich Ask/Review panes and read-only inspector map onto A15's honesty-card/attention-review ergonomics (ADR-51/130) and the alpha.11 ADR-84/121 read-only-then-enqueue-only Inspector lineage, but re-hosted in Obsidian. B1's `status --json` analog and Bases usage continue A15's projection-not-truth posture.

### Gap: neither A15 nor B1 ships hub navigation; B1 additionally lacks the hub concept A15 retained

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The hub is one of Memoria's oldest surviving elements, but a *navigation surface built on hubs* is not.

- *Old Skeleton / alpha.1:* the type set included `moc` (Map-of-Content). The design's answer to topical navigation was explicitly "topical navigation is built by hubs" (`lifecycle-over-topic.md`) — folders encode what a note *is*, topic lives in frontmatter facets and in hub/MOC notes. So the *concept* of hub-as-navigation is founding, but it was a note-authoring convention, not a shipped UI surface.
- *alpha.7:* a `hubs.base` with a single "Hubs index" view shipped, and a `notes/hubs` folder portal — the closest the alpha line came to a hub navigation surface — but the design deliberately "avoid[ed] a dedicated base for a handful of files," folding hubs into one view.
- *alpha.10:* the `index` document type and `notes/indexes/` folder were removed precisely because "its register function (a list of hubs) is already the `hubs.base#Hubs index` view" (ADR-119 §6) — i.e. no separate hub-navigation register was warranted.
- *alpha.11:* the four-type collapse (source→digest→note+**hub**) made hub one of the four load-bearing types, genealogically "hub = structure note/Map-of-Content" (Luhmann; `hubs-and-navigation.md`, ADR-126). ADR-126's discriminator kept hub because it "creates distinct obligations the gap engine feeds on."
- *alpha.15:* hub is retained as a first-class type, created via `new hub <tag>` (ADR-130 CLI). But with the Obsidian surface removed, **no hub navigation surface ships** — consistent with the alpha-line pattern of hub-as-type/hub-as-authoring-convention rather than hub-as-UI.

So on the A15 side, "no hub navigation surface" is continuity, not a loss: the alpha line never shipped more than the folded `hubs.base` index view, and that lived inside the Obsidian surface that alpha.14/15 removed.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** On the navigation-surface half, B1 continues the alpha-line state — neither ships a hub navigation surface. The novel move is that **B1 drops the hub concept entirely**, which has **no alpha-line precedent whatsoever**: `moc`/hub survived every reset from alpha.1's 16-type set through alpha.10's index-type removal, the alpha.11 four-type collapse (where it was one of exactly four earned types under ADR-126), and into alpha.15's `new hub` command. Removing it departs from the Luhmann-derived structure-note role that has anchored the knowledge model since Origins.

### Gap: Canvas / JSON Canvas absent in both (continuity, not a delta)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** Canvas has a long presence as a *type/emitter* but was never shipped as an authoritative navigation axis.

- *Old Skeleton / alpha.1:* `canvas` was among the 16 note-types.
- *alpha.7:* two propose-class JSON Canvas emitter skills shipped — `map:canvas-hub` and `map:graph-claims` (closing #381), modeled on `map:seed-canvas`, writing only to `notes/fleeting/`+`inbox/` and raising a candidate card. These honor the "engine-writes/agent-judges" split (ADR-33) — Canvas as a *proposal*, never a store.
- *alpha.7→alpha.8:* the **Canvas/argument-graph spatial axis** was deferred at alpha.7 (ADR-81 deferred set) and formalized as **ADR-103 "Projected Canvas spatial axis" (proposed)** in alpha.8 — Canvas as a spatial *projection* of authored `links:`, "not an authoritative edge store," gated on an unproven incremental-stable-layout spike.
- *alpha.15:* **ADR-103 was explicitly rejected** — "spatial-at-scale refuted; tabular/faceted is the validated default; small-graph view must beat a tabular baseline" (borrow-doc visualization defaults, Heer/Bostock). Canvas thus never shipped in the alpha line and, by alpha.15, its governing ADR was declined.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's absence of Canvas/JSON Canvas **continues the alpha-line state directly** — Canvas was proposed (ADR-103, alpha.8), never shipped, and rejected (alpha.15). There is no removal event here; the element was never present as a surface to remove. This is noted only to forestall re-listing it as a B1 regression.

### Gap: the ADR-101 `spaces/` + `_nav` Navigator + three-places navigation model is dropped

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** This is the most-iterated navigation lineage in Memoria's history, built up gate-by-gate then renamed and re-homed repeatedly.

- *alpha.3 (ADR-70):* navigation decided as intent-named **gates** — three core gates (Action/Library/Knowledge), Project deferred; switching a gate changes only left-nav/main-dashboard/Co-PI focus, "never what an action does."

- *alpha.14/15:* the Obsidian surface was removed from the required product, but the model — `spaces/` tree, Navigator, three-places framing, Queue/Maintenance cadence — remained the vault-template navigation convention through the alpha line's end (governed at the naming layer by ADR-101, kept accepted).

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 **drops the entire ADR-101 spaces + `_nav` Navigator + three-places lineage** and replaces it with five interaction-mode surfaces (Capture/Reading/Ask/Compose/Review). This has **no alpha-line precedent**: from alpha.3's first gate decision through alpha.15, navigation was always organized as places/queues (gates→spaces→Queue/Maintenance), never as interaction modes. The **three-places framing** (a shipped-template convention traceable to ADR-70 and `what-memoria-is.md`) is gone; the **Friday-ritual Maintenance cadence** (the weekly-cadence Maintenance space split off in alpha.10 by ADR-116) is gone; the `_nav` Navigator rail (ADR-114) is gone. Because ADR-101 governs only the *naming* of spaces (and remained accepted), and the `_nav`/three-places pieces were template conventions rather than ADR-101 content, B1's move retires a body of shipped-template convention plus the ADR-114/116 rail-and-cadence machinery, while leaving the ADR-101 naming decision with nothing to name.

### Gap: qmd → sqlite-vec+embeddings substrate swap (A15's §10 spike kept qmd; B1 uses the declined sqlite-vec+embeddings substrate)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** qmd is a founding element; sqlite-vec appears in the record specifically as its named fallback, and the swap was a spike A15 ran and declined.

- *Old Skeleton / alpha.1:* retrieval was "deterministic search over the vault — a Search engine (**qmd** embedding stack + the obsidian MCP)," a local hybrid **BM25 + vector + cross-encoder-rerank** index, "never a learned reranker." The Librarian reached it via the `qmd` MCP; the installer substituted `{{QMD}}` at deploy.
- *alpha.8:* a **pre-file similarity shadow** ran a report-only qmd neighbour check; retrieval recommendations were "re-anchored to qmd" (#818) after a grounding error proposed building a retriever that already shipped.
- *alpha.9:* **cosine-as-arbiter was retired but kept as "qmd's retriever"**; the greenfield retriever/NLI/MaxSAT stack was explicitly deferred because qmd/cluster were already built.
- *alpha.10:* a **`qmd_filter_mcp.py` wrapper** hid superseded claims at the read boundary; the #859 baseline defined a qmd rerank-on-vs-off eval, and the decision line was "keep qmd as-is."
- *alpha.11:* the disposable index became **qmd rebuilt from checked-only Concepts** (`rebuild-checked-qmd-source`, `.memoria/index/qmd`); §6 recorded "**`qmd` is the backend** … **SQLite FTS5+sqlite-vec the zero-dependency baseline/fallback**" — this is where sqlite-vec is first named, and named as *fallback*.
- *alpha.13/14:* qmd became the **required CLI-first checked-only search backend** (`memoria ask`, `project gaps`, graph-neighborhood docs), with embedding-enabled rebuilds persisting `mode: hybrid`, `embeddings: true`; "Memoria owns the checked-only input tree … qmd owns keyword and semantic ranking"; the installer required Node ≥22 and qmd and resolved an absolute qmd path.
- *alpha.15:* the §10/§6 substrate **spike ran and kept qmd** — "the packaged product does not yet carry sqlite-vec plus a local embedding pipeline, so the semantic/hybrid replacement bar did not clear"; the verdict was committed to `tests/fixtures/retrieval-substrate-spike.json` + `runtime/retrieval_substrate.py` (ADR-125, PR-A #1162), under the ADR-129 rule "each tier must beat a declared cheap baseline or it is disabled." FTS5 cleared lexical only; **qmd remained the alpha.15 baseline** and `.memoria/index/qmd/` shipped. Separately, the NLI-for-contradiction lineage advanced here: cosine-as-arbiter had been retired at alpha.9, and alpha.15 shipped a **pinned local NLI proposer** (Tier-1, §7) — a proposer, never an authority.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 adopts **exactly the substrate A15's spike declined** — in-DB FTS5 + **sqlite-vec** + **bge-small ONNX embeddings** + **deberta NLI**, with qmd dropped and no external retriever. sqlite-vec was on record as the **zero-dependency fallback baseline since alpha.11 §6**; B1 promotes that named fallback to the primary substrate, crossing the specific **alpha.15 spike verdict** (committed fixture) that kept qmd because "sqlite-vec plus a local embedding pipeline" was not yet packaged. bge-small ONNX is precisely "the local embedding pipeline" the alpha.15 record said the product "does not yet carry." In one direction B1 **continues an established trajectory** — the standalone/no-external-runtime arc from alpha.14 (CLI-first) and alpha.15 (`uv tool install`, no-daemon, ADR-125) — since removing the external qmd/Node binary is consistent with a self-contained engine, and **deberta NLI continues the alpha.15 NLI-proposer direction** (pinned local NLI, cosine demoted from arbiter at alpha.9). In the other direction it **overturns the alpha.15 baseline-gated spike verdict**, converting a recorded "did not clear the bar" declension (qmd retained) into the shipped substrate.

## 11. Agent / autonomy

### Agent boundary as convergence — ADR-130 agent-as-quarantined-client already matches beta.1; "Co-PI" is filename-only; the genuine removal is the shipped Librarian persona

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The "agent" in Memoria was, from the very beginning, a *resident first-party* thing, and the alpha line is the story of that residency being progressively demoted until, by alpha.15, only a posture-invariant survives. The pre-alpha.1 **Old Skeleton** (2026-05-29 snapshot) put a named agent at the center of a **seven-layer architecture**: L3 was *"the co-PI (the permanent ACP-pane agent with the full Hermes learning loop)"*, sitting above L4 (ephemeral profiles + Kanban), with *"decisions flow top-down, information flows bottom-up."* The co-PI was the one agent the human converses with; everything else was delegated through it. At the **alpha.1** cut this shipped as five profiles (ADR-48, consolidating the ADR-02 seven), where `memoria-copi` was *"the one agent the PI converses with,"* structurally **read-only** (`policy.allow.write: []`), the sole carrier of the Hermes self-improving loop, and hosted alone in the ACP (Agent Client Protocol) pane. Autonomy at alpha.1 was framed as *"L3-with-a-ceiling"* (Chen 2026 taxonomy): profiles execute unattended *within a card* but the human gates promotion; *"L4/L5 are refused structurally."*

The demotion happened in named steps. **Alpha.11** retired the profile system as an *enforcement identity*: *"policy now binds to operations, not profile identity"* (§11 "A role/profile name is never the enforcement boundary"), and made the **autonomy ceiling explicit in policy** — *"no autonomous keep/discard loop for synthesis."* **Alpha.14/15** then *deleted the profile system from the product entirely*: ADR-125 supersedes ADR-26/48/120, and the design history records it as arc (a) — *"Seven specialist profiles → five → no agent autonomy at all,"* where *"the profile system was deleted from the product entirely."* What alpha.15 keeps is exactly the posture invariant with the runtime gone: ADR-125's *"Memoria never grants an agent file, terminal, code-execution, or send tools"* and *"substantive coding stays delegated to external agents"* (the alpha.16 module target). This is the **agent-as-quarantined-client contract** the gap attributes to alpha.15 / ADR-130: the agent is a read-scoped client, never a resident authority.

**Prior states of "Co-PI" and when they changed.** "Co-PI" names a real, resident, first-party agent only through alpha.1–alpha.10: the `memoria-copi` profile directory, its `SOUL.md`, its opus model routing, and its dedicated ACP pane (the pane *"hosting exactly one agent — the co-PI,"* ADR-48/68). That runtime object was dissolved at **alpha.11** (operations replace profiles) and deleted at **alpha.14/15** (ADR-125). By alpha.15 "Co-PI" therefore survives only as *naming residue* — as the gap notes, it appears in the **ADR-130 filename** and as the retired **`record-copi-interview`** operation key (§9), never as a runtime loop. There is no resident first-party agent in shipped alpha.15; ADR-130's *body* already defines the agent as a client under the read-API + request-envelope contract.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** For the agent boundary itself this is **convergence, not divergence, and continuation, not revert**: beta.1's *"Memoria is not an agent, and beta.1 adds none"* — any agent (first-party or the researcher's Claude Code/Cursor) is a **client** under one contract (read-API + request envelope, quarantined, read-scoped, never file/terminal/exec/send) — is the same boundary ADR-125 stated as the surviving invariant and ADR-130's body already encodes. Beta.1 §13 explicitly grounds this in ADR-125–130 as *continuous with* alpha.15. This is the terminal point of arc (a): the trajectory from the Old Skeleton's resident L3 co-PI, through the alpha.11 operation-binding, to alpha.15's posture-only invariant, lands unchanged in beta.1.

**The genuine A15→B1 removal — the shipped Librarian persona.** The one thing that is actually *removed* (not merely reframed) is a user-facing agent role the alpha.15 **vault-template physically shipped**. Its lineage runs the length of the series: the Old Skeleton roster named a *librarian* among coder/writer/socratic/mapper/librarian/verifier/cmdr; ADR-02's seven kept **Librarian** as a faithful specialist; ADR-48's alpha.1 consolidation folded **Librarian + Mapper → Librarian** (*"one research-librarian stance pointed in two directions"*), a faithful, haiku-routed profile running catalog·extract·link·map. Crucially, the Librarian was the persona wired to *direction*: from alpha.1 it *"reads `research-focus.md` at session start to weight discovery"* (Part II homepage note). **Alpha.11** renamed that substrate `research-focus.md → steering.md` (the Karpathy `program.md`-equivalent). Even after the profile *runtime* was deleted at alpha.14/15, the Librarian survived in the alpha.15 **vault-template as a named persona**: the shipped `vault-template/steering.md` addresses *"The Librarian reads it at the start of every session and weights discovery … the main lever you have over what gets surfaced,"* and `vault-template/system/eval/` rubrics (e.g. `find-transformer-paper.md`) score *"The Librarian"*'s behavior. **Beta.1 removes this entirely**: no `steering.md`, no Librarian persona, no PI-priority discovery weighting (discovery becomes deterministic occurrence-counting over the citation graph — §8). Historically this is the *last* user-facing remnant of the seven→five→zero profile arc being cut: the enforcement identity went at alpha.11–15, and beta.1 removes the surviving vault-template persona that outlived it.

### The `.memoria/plugins/memoria-policy-gate` pre_/post_tool_call hook system dropped

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side (hard-won, live-failure-forced).** This is one of the most hard-won mechanisms in the whole series, born at **alpha.1** from a real failure. **ADR-27** first specified fail-closed enforcement as a **`pre_tool_call` shell hook** with `matcher: "obsidian.*"`. A live re-run (Hermes v0.14.0, `hermes -z`, against `Memoria-test`) found *"the gate never fires: a write to a review-gated zone succeeded, ungated and unaudited,"* from three converging causes: (1) tool-name/matcher mismatch — Hermes registers the tool as `mcp_obsidian_obsidian_append_content`, so `re.fullmatch("obsidian.*", …)` returns `None`; (2) shell hooks are consent-gated (skipped on non-TTY cron/`-z` runs); (3) shell hooks are **fail-open** by construction. **ADR-28** replaced *only that enforcement mechanism* with the **`memoria-policy-gate` Hermes Python plugin** (`src/.memoria/plugins/memoria-policy-gate/__init__.py`), registering `pre_tool_call` (`_gate`) and `post_tool_call` (`_complete`), running in-process in every mode, matching in Python, receiving `task_id` for free, and **fail-closed** — *"any exception inside the gate also returns block."* ADR-27's config-model decisions (`mcp_servers` in `config.yaml`, the `agent.disabled_toolsets` allowlist, obsidian-as-sole-write-path) all *stood*; ADR-28 was a partial supersession of the mechanism only. This hook was the load-bearing second wall of the alpha.1 write gate — the thing that actually *stopped* a write at the Hermes tool-call boundary.

**Prior states and when they changed.** The hook peaked at **alpha.10**: `tool-registry.yaml` became the source and its header was rewritten to *"the policy-gate plugin reads this on `pre_tool_call` and blocks calls outside the profile allowlist"*; gating flipped to a **positive `platform_toolsets` allowlist** (closing new Hermes tools by default), `DENY_DIRECT_TOOLS` expanded (e.g. `session_search` after Hermes 0.17), and `memory` was profile-scoped to `memoria-copi`. This is the mechanism at its most elaborated — and it was entirely predicated on **Hermes being the runtime** and **profiles being the enforcement identity**. Both premises were then dismantled: **alpha.11** bound policy to *operations, not profiles* and demoted Hermes from control-plane to *library adapter* (ADR-22/27 retired); **alpha.14** put Hermes *"Outside the baseline"*; **alpha.15/ADR-125** *"dropped from the product"* the Hermes runtime, installed profiles, board lanes/fleet, **and the MCP policy plugin**, moving the write path to quarantine-and-verify (ADR-127: engine writes via the operation lifecycle, foreign edits via `workspace scan`).

Note the alpha.15 *residual*: despite ADR-125 declaring it dropped, the alpha.15 **vault-template still ships** `.memoria/plugins/memoria-policy-gate/{__init__.py,plugin.yaml}` (`provides_hooks: [pre_tool_call, post_tool_call]`, `version: 0.1.0-alpha.1`) — exactly the alpha.14/15 lag the design history flags (ADR-125 Context: *"the corpus still presents the Hermes-era architecture as current"*). But its *framing* had already been reframed to match the new architecture: the shipped docstring now reads *"The standalone CLI/runtime is the baseline write path. If an operator wires an external adapter, its vault writes should pass through this plugin,"* with `PROFILE`/`VAULT_PATH` substitution renamed to `ACTOR`/`WORKSPACE_PATH`. So by alpha.15 the plugin is no longer the primary gate — it is a fail-closed *bridge for an optional BYO adapter*, forcing an external agent/adapter's writes through the envelope at the tool-call boundary (as the gap describes).

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** Beta.1 **continues the alpha.11→alpha.15 trajectory** rather than reverting: it removes the `.memoria/plugins/` tree and the per-tool-call hook entirely, routing machine writes through the daemon's single **VaultWriter** and agent writes through the request envelope that VaultWriter applies. The per-tool-call hook only ever existed *because* Hermes owned the tool-call boundary (ADR-22/27/28); once ADR-125 removed Hermes as the runtime, the hook had no host loop to intercept, and alpha.15 already reduced it to a vestigial adapter bridge. Beta.1 finishes that removal. The *replacement* mechanism — a single daemon-owned VaultWriter plus request envelope — has **no direct alpha-line precedent as a shape** (the daemon itself is new, §1), but the *deletion* of the pre_/post_tool_call hook is the completion of a demolition begun at alpha.11 (operations-not-profiles) and formally decided at alpha.15 (ADR-125's "MCP policy plugin dropped"). The fail-closed intent hard-won in the ADR-27→28 arc is what carries forward — relocated from the Hermes tool-call hook to the single-writer + envelope path — while the Hermes-specific hook machinery that carried it since alpha.1 is retired.

## 12. Security / OS confinement

The gap analysis lists three deltas in this subsystem: (a) OS-level egress enforcement that in beta.1 exists **only on Linux** (brokered egress via Guard + `CLONE_NEWNET`), with seccomp/macOS-`sandbox-exec`/Windows-Firewall flows cut; (b) a beta.1 write-time **exfiltration linter + canary drill + per-OS keychain** that alpha.15 had not detailed; and (c) a **new third-party-plugin-interference corruption class** that appears in beta.1 as a consequence of re-hosting on Obsidian. Each is traced below.

### Egress confinement: brokered egress via Guard + Linux-netns-only OS enforcement

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The confinement model beta.1 builds on is the end-state of a boundary that shifted *twice* across the alpha line — first from "write gate" to "channel separation," then from "MCP-only" to "no-agent-sockets" — but that **never once reached down to the OS kernel**.

- **Pre-alpha.1 / alpha.1 "Old Skeleton."** The original security boundary was the **MCP-only sandbox** ("policy-via-MCP, MCP-only (D40/ADR-46): agents reach the vault, engines, and external APIs ONLY through MCP servers," `policy_hook.py`). Egress was governed at the application layer: `policy_hook.py` hard-denied the `file`/`terminal`/`code_execution` families for every lane, and each lane's `routing.external_api_policy` sat in a versioned `lane-overrides/*.yaml`. Memoria's stated differentiator over surveyed MCP ecosystems was "MCP as a *permission / policy boundary*, not merely interoperability." There was no OS-level enforcement; the boundary was tool-allowlist discipline inside the Hermes runtime.
- **alpha.9 — the egress reckoning.** This is the load-bearing moment for beta.1's whole containment posture. Egress allow-by-default was "recorded as a live P0" (#832 interim hard-deny, #822 structural default-deny): the gate hard-denied file/terminal/code-exec but *allow-by-defaulted* `web_*`/`browser_*`/`send_message`/`delegate_task`, so "for egress the sandbox rests entirely on `disabled_toolsets` schema-hiding." The threat model was written onto the page for the first time — "a **poisoned source/paper** whose text reaches the LLM on every ingest call (data > code, Greshake/Debenedetti) and induces a tool call" — and the **MCP-only sandbox was reframed "necessary, not sufficient"** because "least-privilege doesn't defend against poisoned *data*." This is where the posture that beta.1 states as charter ("we contain outputs, we don't detect intent") was born.
- **alpha.11 — the boundary is redrawn as channel separation.** alpha.11 §12 "**Retired the old policy-MCP gated-write enforcement as the security boundary; the boundary is now channel-separation + tool allowlists + no-exfil + trusted-writer-only commits**," expressed as `operation.yaml` `allowed_tools`/`allowed_paths`/`allowed_network`, with "agents never commit… only the worker commits, and agent code execution is read-only/mechanical." This `allowed_network` allowlist and worker-only-egress rule is the **direct ancestor of beta.1's Guard broker**; the term "no-exfil" also enters the boundary vocabulary here.
- **alpha.14.** "Network access is operation-scoped and allowlisted" and "hardened to fail-closed" (#1103); the runtime is explicitly **platform-agnostic** standalone Python 3.12 / uv / Typer; enrichment is worker-only ("agents never get network or code-execution").
- **alpha.15 (the A15 baseline).** "**No agent tool grants**: Memoria never grants an agent file/terminal/exec/send tools; untrusted work text returned as tagged data, never instructions; loopback HTTP binds only loopback hosts, token-auth (`MEMORIA_HTTP_TOKEN`), no raw SQLite/file routes" (ADR-125/130), described as **lethal-trifecta mitigation**, and fail-closed on any internal error (ADR-125, "a logged denial is policy, silence is wiring"). The recorded residual: "any local bearer credential is readable by a trusted-local agent" (cross-channel credential-reuse fixture).

**Prior states / when they changed.** The enforcement layer walked: Hermes policy-plugin write-gate (alpha.1, ADR-27→28) → egress named an unmitigated P0 (alpha.9) → channel-separation + `allowed_network` allowlist as *the* boundary (alpha.11) → operation-scoped/allowlisted/fail-closed on a platform-agnostic standalone engine (alpha.14) → no-agent-tool-grants + loopback-token surface (alpha.15). **At no point in alpha.1→alpha.15 was there any OS-kernel enforcement layer** — no network namespace, no seccomp, no `sandbox-exec`, no Windows Firewall/UAC. The alpha model was, in beta.1's own words for macOS/Windows, "process-architecture discipline, not OS enforcement," on every platform.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.**
- Beta.1's **brokered egress via Guard ("engines get no sockets at all; all egress is a typed IPC call to Guard")** is a *continuation* of the alpha.11→15 trajectory: channel separation, worker-only egress, no agent tool grants, `allowed_network`→a compiled-in requirement-driven allowlist, fail-closed when policy can't load. Guard is the alpha-line `operation.yaml allowed_network` + "agents never get network" rule reified as a resident IPC broker — the residency itself tied to the beta.1 daemon pivot (§1), since alpha.15's `serve` was explicitly on-demand and "not a daemon."
- Beta.1's **`CLONE_NEWNET` Linux network-namespace kernel enforcement has no alpha-line precedent.** The design itself says the supervisor/worker split with brokered egress "*is* the charter's 'allowlist mechanism'" — i.e. the alpha-line application-level model — and the netns is an *additional* Linux-only kernel layer on top. This is a beta.1 addition, not a revival.
- The **cut** items (seccomp fallback, macOS `sandbox-exec`, Windows Firewall/UAC) were **never in the alpha line either** — they were a beta.1-internal scope reduced back to Linux-only. Consequently, on macOS/Windows, beta.1's egress posture ("process-architecture discipline, not OS enforcement") is a **factual continuation of the alpha.15 application-level model**: only Linux departs from that model by adding a kernel layer. The platform split the gap analysis flags (§14) therefore reads, historically, as the alpha-line posture kept everywhere plus one new Linux-only kernel layer.

### Write-time exfiltration linter + canary drill + per-OS keychain

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The gap analysis correctly records that alpha.15 detailed **no exfiltration-linter-at-vault-write**. The A15 side is an absence, but the beta.1 mechanism attaches to two distinct alpha-line threads plus one genuinely new one.

1. **The "no-exfil" boundary thread (alpha.11).** "no-exfil" first appears as a *named boundary property* at alpha.11 §12 (channel separation + tool allowlists + **no-exfil** + trusted-writer-only commits). But there it was a structural property (agents can't exfiltrate because they hold no network and no write authority), **not a content-neutralizing linter run at vault-write**. Beta.1 converts the property into an active write-time transform (default-deny link/embed/HTML neutralization). No alpha version shipped that transform.
2. **The redaction-self-test thread (alpha.8).** The closest ancestor to beta.1's **canary drill** is alpha.8's diagnostic-plane **content-exfiltration contract (ADR-105): out-of-Git, deny-by-default, redaction self-test.** alpha.8 also "hardened editor paste behavior… disabling VSCode paste-as-formatted-link and file/URL copy-into-workspace" to close "silent link/file rewrites into the vault" — thematically the same "untrusted content becoming an active link in the vault" concern beta.1's linter addresses, but scoped to the *docs* vault editor config, not a product mechanism. Beta.1's canary ("weekly + post-migration, inject `MEMORIA-CANARY-<8hex>` into synthetic output to prove the redaction path fires *and* scan all artifacts/DB/logs/exports") is the alpha.8 redaction-self-test generalized from the diagnostic plane to every artifact.
3. **The structural-linter shape (alpha.1).** The *form* — a deterministic, zero-LLM, owner-bound file transform — echoes the alpha.1 **Linter (`detectors.py`, zero-LLM, report-only, "gates at commit, monitors between")**; but that engine did drift *detection* (`vault-hash-drift`, `fama-exposure`), never exfiltration *neutralization*. Beta.1 makes VaultWriter the single owner ("one owner") of a strip-never-flag transform, which is a new function on an old structural silhouette.

**The keychain has no alpha-line precedent.** Across alpha.1→alpha.15 no version stored a provider key in an OS keychain. alpha.15's only credential was the **loopback bearer token `MEMORIA_HTTP_TOKEN`**, whose documented residual was "any local bearer credential is readable by a trusted-local agent." The per-OS keychain (Keychain / DPAPI / Secret Service + mode-0600 fallback) arrives in beta.1 **because BYOK arrives in beta.1** (a new-in-B1 cost machinery, §13) — there was no long-lived provider API key to protect in the alpha line, whose live-engine-is-an-API fact was only established at alpha.9 and never came with key-at-rest handling.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.**
- The **write-time exfiltration linter** has no direct alpha analog; it *operationalizes* the alpha.9 threat model ("poisoned source/paper… markdown that fetches/resolves a URL") into containment-at-write. Beta.1's charter — "**Injection classifiers are excluded by charter — we contain outputs, we don't detect intent** (>90% adaptive bypass)" — is a straight-line continuation of the posture set at alpha.9 ("does not assume detection works"), restated at alpha.11 §12 ("the design **does not assume detection works** and **re-introduces gating where recall is low**") and at alpha.15 ("untrusted data sealing," Greshake/AgentDojo/InjecAgent). The mechanism is new; the *doctrine that produced it* is hard-won across alpha.9→alpha.15.
- The **canary drill** continues the alpha.8 ADR-105 redaction-self-test lineage, generalized.
- The **keychain** is new-in-beta.1 and supersedes the alpha.15 loopback-token-only credential model, arriving coupled to BYOK.

### Third-party-plugin-interference corruption class (new-in-B1, a consequence of re-hosting on Obsidian)

**Origin & lineage of the alpha.15 side.** The gap analysis records the A15 side as an *absence with a specific cause*: "no third-party-plugin-interference threat (**standalone engine, no Obsidian**)." That absence is not an oversight — it is the terminal state of a threat class that was **actively engaged during the Hermes/Obsidian era (alpha.1–alpha.11) and then deliberately eliminated by removing Obsidian at alpha.14/15.** The lineage:

- **alpha.1 — the class is engaged and resolved by prohibition.** ADR-12 ruled **obsidian-linter "incompatible — do not install,"** because "a deterministic tool still fails the architecture if it writes outside the Policy MCP / audit trail" — a second frontmatter authority "would collide with the agent-written `_proposed_classification`/`_enrichment` namespaces and, worse, write to audited zones ungated." This is *precisely* beta.1's third-party-plugin concern (a format-on-save plugin rewriting machine-owned spans), raised at alpha.1 and answered by outright prohibition. alpha.1 also shipped the detective backstop: `vault-hash-drift` explicitly flags files "edited outside the audited write path… A legitimate human edit in Obsidian surfaces here too, by design."
- **alpha.3 — plugin-as-attack-surface.** Shell Commands and Buttons Panel plugins were rejected for their JS-exec surface; the in-use Buttons (shabegom) plugin was flagged "our highest-risk current dependency" with a documented exit plan.
- **alpha.3 / alpha.7 / alpha.8 — bundled-plugin supply chain.** ADR-74's plugin-provenance-lock (`plugin-provenance-lock.json`, sha256 over vendored files) + CI provenance doctor governed *bundled* plugins; alpha.7 added Portals to it ("untrusted-code policy — the agent cannot self-verify this tier"); alpha.8 reaffirmed the lock. This is the supply-chain half of the plugin threat.
- **alpha.5 — the "other surface writes files directly" variant.** The human base-board drag "writes through Obsidian directly, and… the Linter monitor catches it only post-hoc" — ritual-erosion; the same root cause (Obsidian is a shared filesystem surface).
- **alpha.11.** "Agent-rendered UI is declarative and catalog-bound… **never raw HTML/JS from an agent, a code-execution/injection vector**."
- **alpha.14/15 — the class is designed out.** alpha.14 retired "Obsidian `.obsidian/**` tree, QuickAdd scripts/buttons, **plugin lock**, `system/scripts/*.js`" because "Obsidian dropped as required surface; product must run on `vim`/file browser." alpha.15 dropped "the Obsidian-hosted policy plugin (28, 31)" and, with it, the entire plugin-provenance-lock apparatus. By alpha.15 there is no plugin surface in the product, so the third-party-plugin threat class is structurally absent — the standalone-engine cut removed the surface, not merely the mitigation.

**How beta.1 maps onto the history.** This gap is a **revival of a threat class that lived in the alpha.1–alpha.11 Hermes/Obsidian era and was eliminated at alpha.14/15**, re-created as a direct consequence of the §1 host pivot (beta.1 re-introduces Obsidian as the *required* researcher UI, undoing the alpha.14 "Obsidian dropped as required surface" decision). Three notes on how the beta.1 handling relates to the earlier era:

- **The mechanism differs from the alpha-era mechanism.** Alpha-era handling was (a) prohibition of a conflicting plugin (ADR-12, obsidian-linter "do not install"), (b) provenance-lock over *bundled* plugins (ADR-74), and (c) post-hoc drift detection (`vault-hash-drift`). Beta.1's handling is **startup detection of known-hostile plugins/settings + registering Memoria-owned paths as formatter-excluded + auditing plugin `data.json` for secret-shaped fields, with the human-span manifest gate as the structural backstop** (design §10). The "formatter-exclusion" approach is new; the alpha line either forbade the plugin or caught its effect after the fact.
- **The framing shifted.** At alpha.1 the concern was a *second write authority bypassing the policy gate/audit* (the plugin ran in the same Obsidian the agents used). In beta.1, engines/agents are a **separate daemon**, not in Obsidian; the interference is a third-party plugin *corrupting the machine-owned spans the engine wrote and tripping the byte-immutability rollback* ("rewrite machine spans, flip markers, and trip rollback loops"). Both derive from the same root — Obsidian is a shared filesystem surface other apps can write — but beta.1's "human-span manifest gate remains the structural backstop" is the descendant of the alpha.1 `before_hash`/`after_hash` pairing + `vault-hash-drift` lineage (now the pre==post byte manifest, §5), not of the ADR-12 prohibition route.
- **The "secret-shaped fields in plugin `data.json`" facet has no alpha-line precedent.** It is coupled to the new keychain/BYOK key-leakage threat (T3 in beta.1's model); the alpha line held no provider key to leak, so this specific sub-concern could not have existed before beta.1's BYOK arrival.

## 13. Cost / snapshot / migration machinery

### Cost / metering / circuit-breaker machinery (BYOK, price table, `max_tokens` ceilings, batch API, $20 soft budget, miss row)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** Cost tracking in the alpha line was a *telemetry* facility, never a control surface. It arrived at **alpha.8** as one of three telemetry planes landed under ADR-104/105/106: `board_export.py` was split into `board_export_cost.py`/`_events`/`_projection`/`_common`, and "the audit confirms the cost join works end-to-end (ADR-106)." Crucially, alpha.8 already carried the direct ancestor of beta.1's "miss row": ADR-104 records a `cost-misses.jsonl` writer that captured spend that could not be joined to an attributed operation — and the design history flags its state precisely: "`cost-misses.jsonl` has a writer but no aggregator (ADR-104)." So the *concept* of a per-call cost record plus an unattributable-spend sink is hard-won and old (alpha.8); it just never grew a rollup.

Cost then moved from "export/analytics" toward "journaled per run" as the runtime became standalone. **alpha.14** (PR-H #1175) made `providers.yaml` pin `runner.test`/`runner.live` per operation and journaled "resolved model + actual token cost … per run"; **alpha.15**'s ADR-129 runner policy carried that forward (token cost journaled per `(operation, model, mode)` run). That is the whole of the A15 cost machinery: per-run token-cost journaling + a cost-join export plane + an unaggregated miss log.

Everything B1 §13 centers — **BYOK**, a **normative price table in the Ledger**, **`max_tokens` ceilings**, the **batch-API 50% path**, a **circuit breaker enforced at preview/pre-call/breaker**, and a **$20/mo default soft budget** — has *no alpha-line precedent*. The alpha corpus's own stance, quoted repeatedly (ADR-129), was "no threshold in the prior corpus was ever validated"; cost was measured, not budgeted, and never gated a call.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 continues the alpha.8→alpha.14 trajectory of "every model call carries a journaled token cost" and *revives the alpha.8 miss-row idea by name* ("miss row for unattributable spend" = the `cost-misses.jsonl` writer, now with the aggregator alpha.8 lacked). The rest — price table, ceilings, batch discount, breaker, soft budget — is net-new machinery layered on top; the gap analysis itself files it under "new-in-B1." The BYOK framing is downstream of the alpha.14/alpha.15 pivot to a standalone engine calling providers directly rather than through the Hermes runtime that formerly "owned … the write boundary" (ADR-125).

### Git-snapshot batch/revert substrate vs. SQLite-journal cascade-rollback

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** Reversibility is one of Memoria's oldest spine properties, and its substrate changed shape three times. In the **pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton** and at **alpha.1**, reversibility rode an "append-only hash-paired audit log" (ADR-25): every mutating write recorded `before_hash`/`after_hash` paired by `task_id`+`path`, explicitly "detective … rather than preventing." At **alpha.11**, the durable record became a workspace-level append-only `journal/<machine>.jsonl` of content-hashed PROV-DM events, and **cascade rollback** became a first-class operation whose "rollback *unit* is the output Concept" — invalidate an output and everything transitively derived. At **alpha.12** (ADR-122, the keep-test) the journal moved into SQLite as a hash-chained table and gained "cross-store recovery"; the alpha.12 *design* specified "recursive-CTE cascade-rollback" and a Turso backup adapter (marked design-only — shipped code landed a smaller schema + cross-store recovery). **alpha.15/ADR-127** hardened this into the A15 end-state: 2PC with journal states `pending`→`materialized` (materializing commit SHA recorded after `git commit`), write-ahead fsync ordering, one workspace-scoped write lock, crash-recovery via `workspace recover`, WAL + `synchronous=FULL`; and a **git/journal division** — "one commit per operation staging **only** materialized paths + control files, **never `git add -A`**."

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's substrate — external git-dir + `VACUUM INTO` + the 4-step **batch protocol** (snapshot/apply/verify/commit) + **pathspec-limited commits** + one-click revert via inverse-diff + DB reconciliation — is a *continuation* of the ADR-127 git-per-operation discipline in its most literal element: the pathspec-limited commit is exactly ADR-127's "stage only materialized paths, never `git add -A`" (which existed there specifically because "a swept foreign edit would bypass quarantine"). Where B1 *diverges* from the A15 lineage is the rollback engine: the alpha-line spine was a **relational cascade** over a derivation graph (alpha.11 output-Concept rollback → alpha.12 recursive-CTE cascade → ADR-127 two-tier demotion propagation), whereas B1 replaces that with **git snapshots + footprint-scoped DB reconciliation** and inverse-diffs. `VACUUM INTO` as the DB-snapshot primitive has no alpha-line precedent (alpha.12's DB-snapshot idea was the deferred Turso adapter; alpha.15's was streaming replication — see below). So B1 keeps ADR-127's commit hygiene, drops ADR-127's cascade, and introduces a snapshot primitive from outside the alpha line.

### Migration runner as a launch blocker

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The alpha line deliberately carried **no migration machinery**, and did so explicitly because no data needed to survive. **alpha.11** removed a schema-migration shim outright, recording the reason: "with no production vault there is no migration target, so the shim is dead machinery (§0 'no migration path … schema paths are removed')." **alpha.15/ADR-125** then fixed the *anti*-migration posture as a stated decision: stdlib `sqlite3`, "`.sql` DDL gated by `PRAGMA user_version`," with "ORM + migration framework rejected: the integrity schema is the product; single-user scale needs `.sql` + `user_version` only." So A15's position is not "migration not yet built" but "migration deliberately declined" — schema evolution was to be raw `.sql` under a `user_version` pragma, with no forward-data-survival contract.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's **MigrationRunner named as a beta.1 blocker** — justified by "beta is the first version whose data must survive later versions" — is a *reversal of the stated alpha-line premise*, and the reversal's trigger is exactly the condition alpha.11 said was absent ("no production vault → no migration target"). Beta.1 asserts the production-vault condition now exists. There is no alpha-line component this continues; it is new machinery answering a requirement (durable user data across versions) that every alpha release had explicitly disclaimed. It does inherit the alpha.15 substrate it must run over — the `PRAGMA user_version`-gated `.sql` schema (ADR-125) is the versioning primitive the runner would step through.

### `overrides.jsonl` + tiered DB-loss replay + streaming-replication backup leg (dropped)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** This apparatus is a **late arrival — alpha.15 only**, introduced whole by ADR-127 (PR-B #1163, PR-D2) and with no earlier form in the corpus. ADR-127 defined storage recovery *classes*: files own authored meaning (file-recoverable); provider records replay from content-addressed blobs; "verdicts/journal are DB-only accepted-loss"; and — the load-bearing exception — human `work update` dispositions live in the git-tracked append-only `.memoria/overrides.jsonl`, **"tiered on DB-loss replay — restrictions reapply, permissions re-earn."** The reasoning recorded is asymmetric-trust: "the journal witness is gone, so the log cannot be trusted to *unblock* anything," therefore restrictive human dispositions (archive, contested-block) auto-reapply while permissive ones (degraded acceptance, retraction resolution) return as attention items. `overrides.jsonl` was wired into the universal quarantine invariant — detection "covers all tracked paths including `references.bib` and `.memoria/overrides.jsonl`" (PR-C3 #1168). The **streaming-replication leg** was the second element: ADR-127 documented a "three-store backup contract (git remote, SQLite streaming replication à la Litestream, blob file-sync)," `doctor`-reported and "never a runtime dependency." Both were alpha.15-native, roughly six weeks old at the cut.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 removes the whole apparatus. Human decisions relocate to frontmatter (`superseded`/`reading`) plus an exported decide-events CSV — which structurally echoes the *pre*-alpha.11 world where researcher state lived in files rather than a dedicated replay log, though the specific keys are new (see §4). The tiered restrictions-reapply/permissions-re-earn replay semantics have **no B1 analog**. On the backup leg, beta.1 §18 explicitly *declines* the ADR-127 streaming-replication contract ("VACUUM INTO snapshots + `restore --verify` only"), i.e. it keeps one of the three A15 stores (blob/file-sync is subsumed by the vault sync; git remote persists) and swaps Litestream-class continuous replication for periodic `VACUUM INTO` snapshots — the same snapshot primitive that displaces the cascade journal in the gap above. This is a factual drop of an alpha.15-only mechanism, not a revert to a named earlier era.

### Row-hash-chained tamper-evidence journal + git-anchored `journal-head` (dropped)

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The tamper-evidence journal has a two-step lineage with an intervening rejection. At **alpha.8**, ADR-104 *rejected* in-file hash chaining on principle: "Memoria explicitly does *not* add a per-entry `prev_hash` chain … the vault is already a Git repo — commit history is a Merkle chain," and a linear in-file chain "forks under multi-machine writes (ADR-63) and is recomputable by whoever owns the file." Tamper-evidence was framed as "detective-not-preventive," continuing the alpha.1 hash-paired audit log (ADR-25). Then **alpha.12** reversed course *inside the DB*: the journal "is now an append-only, hash-chained SQLite table" — `journal_events(… prev_hash, row_hash UNIQUE)`, SHA-256 over the row, `GENESIS` seed, `BEFORE UPDATE`/`BEFORE DELETE` triggers raising "journal is append-only" — after a spike proved append-only rows "need an external git-tracked head anchor for tamper-evidence," which the alpha.12 `audit/` skeleton dir scaffolded (the `journal-heads.jsonl` write path was design-only in that commit range). **alpha.15/ADR-127** completed it: the tracked `.memoria/journal-head` anchor was "newly *created*" (at `88b7f0f5` `journal_head()` had been a DB query only), giving a "git/journal division" where git owns the tamper-evidence root and the SQLite journal is row-hash-chained. ADR-127 recorded this as *narrowing* alpha.8's ADR-104 rejection: "narrows ADR-104's rejection of hash-chaining (which targeted an in-vault log *file*) — DB rows plus a git anchor get row-level tamper evidence without that cost." So by A15 the mechanism was hard-won across three releases (alpha.8 reject-in-file → alpha.12 hash-chain-in-DB → alpha.15 add-git-anchor).

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 carries none of it: the cascade-rollback journal is replaced by the git-snapshot batch protocol, and "the hash-chain + `journal-head` tamper-evidence root is not carried." Interestingly, dropping the row-hash chain and leaning on git alone lands B1 closer to the **alpha.8/ADR-104 original position** ("git commit history *is* the Merkle chain; don't add a `prev_hash` chain") than to the alpha.12/alpha.15 end-state that ADR-127 had built on top of it — but B1 reaches that via the new git-snapshot substrate rather than by re-adopting ADR-104's reasoning, and it also drops the DB-side append-only journal that ADR-104 never objected to. Factually: an alpha.12-introduced, alpha.15-completed cryptographic apparatus is removed, and the git-only tamper posture that predates it (alpha.8) is the nearest historical rhyme.

### Derived SQLite DB (+ payloads) relocated out of the synced vault to a per-device data dir

**Origin & lineage of the A15 side.** The DB's *home* has been per-machine and gitignored since it was born, but always **inside the vault tree**. It appeared at **alpha.12** (ADR-122, the SQLite state boundary): `memoria.sqlite` lived at `.memoria/state/`, added to `folders.yaml` skeleton, with `.gitignore` "ignoring `.memoria/state/**` contents while keeping the directory," because "SQLite needs a durable, per-machine, gitignored home distinct from the disposable worker mirrors." That decision was affordable only under an explicitly relaxed constraint recorded in the alpha.12 design — "no multi-device sync (single machine)" and "ownership/portability scoped to the keep-set + bibliography export, not the whole workspace." **alpha.15/ADR-125** kept exactly this shape: SQLite as authority for records/queue/journal/verdicts, content-addressed blobs for payloads, all under `.memoria/…` within the vault. So the A15 state is: derived DB + payloads = per-machine, gitignored, but physically *inside* the synced vault folder.

A partial precedent for placing per-device state *outside* the vault existed by alpha.8: the ADR-105 diagnostics plane used an XDG directory (`memoria/runtime/diagnostics.py`, "XDG dir, content-light") — but that governed the diagnostic/telemetry plane, not the authoritative state DB.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1 moves the DB and payloads **out of the synced tree** entirely, to a per-device OS data dir `~/.local/share/memoria/<vault-id>/memoria.db`, with `<vault-id>` namespacing. This *continues* the alpha.12 trajectory in intent — the DB was already declared per-machine and gitignored precisely because it "is *authoritative* working state, not a rebuildable index" — but the physical relocation from an in-vault gitignored path to an XDG OS data dir is new-in-B1; no alpha release moved authoritative state out of the vault folder. The XDG placement rhymes with the alpha.8 diagnostics plane's XDG home, and the `<vault-id>` namespacing is downstream of the alpha.14/beta.1 host pivot to a plugin+daemon operating over a user's *existing* vault (§1–§2), which the single-machine, `.memoria`-inside-the-bundle alpha.12 model never had to address. This gap is distinct from the external git-dir noted in the batch-protocol gap above: that relocates git history, this relocates the derived store itself.

## 14. Platform

### Gap: B1's platform split (OS-enforced egress Linux-only; containment/linter/keychain/canary every-OS; plugin desktop-only; mobile keep-test) vs. A15's "cross-platform by construction, no OS-specific enforcement"

This gap collapses four distinct threads with independent histories in Memoria's evolution. Traced separately below, they show that the *axis* on which platform matters shifted between the alpha line and beta.1, rather than a single element changing state.

#### 1. The runtime-location OS axis (the only platform split the alpha line ever had) — and its erasure by alpha.14/15

The alpha line's sole notion of "platform" was **where the runtime could run**, and it was born at the alpha.1 cut, not the security layer B1 now differentiates.

- **Origins (pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton").** Obsidian was chosen as vault/UI "for its popularity and its markdown-plus-frontmatter substrate," and Hermes was adopted as the runtime — neither carried any OS-enforcement concept. The only "sandbox boundary" named in the seven-layer origin architecture was **L5, the MCP server(s)** — a process/permission boundary, never a network or OS-kernel one.
- **alpha.1 (ADR-26/55/64).** Distribution was the repo-as-unit with `scripts/install.sh` (~981 lines of bash, the single real implementation) and `scripts/install.ps1` as a **thin WSL2 launcher**. The load-bearing runtime rule was explicit: **"Hermes runs only on Linux/WSL2; Windows is the editing surface,"** with WSL2 a hard prerequisite ("No WSL2 → the installer does nothing"). This is the alpha line's one genuine OS split — a **two-OS topology** (WSL2 runtime + Windows editing surface), justified as "correctness, not just simplification: Hermes is WSL2-only on Windows … a native PowerShell `hermes profile install` could never work end-to-end."
- **Native Windows deferred, then accepted (ADR-64).** At alpha.1 native Windows was `deferred` — the port was framed as "a port that collapses the two-OS topology into one — not a runtime flag," with "a meaningful share of the work … deletion" (removing the `networkingMode=mirrored` bridge, `/mnt/c` file-lock fights, the OneDrive hazard), "revisit after alpha.3." At **alpha.4**, the 2026-06-16 revalidation against live Hermes docs changed the facts (Hermes now runs natively on Windows; analysis-stack wheels exist), so **ADR-64 was accepted** and native Windows install (#414/#296) scoped into the ExecPlan — reframed carefully as "an alpha.3 carry-over, not a v0.3 scope-up." Its implementation landed in later PRs (#570/#538), and it incidentally unblocked the HTTPS-cert Obsidian-MCP path (#1003/#1041). alpha.5 layered on the WSL2 process-test harness (`test-env.md`, ADR-80), keeping GUI tiers L3/L4/L5 on native Windows.
- **Erasure by alpha.14/15.** The standalone cut retired the two-OS topology's rationale entirely: alpha.14 made **"the Linux and Windows installers standalone by default"** (`374a702a`, `4d761efe`), removing profile/Hermes installer modes, and alpha.15 dropped Hermes from the product outright (arc f: runtime is now "Python 3.12 + uv + Typer + stdlib sqlite3 + pydantic-ai + git CLI"), yielding the design goal "One install command, no external runtime, no always-on process." ADR-64 was even stripped of its stale `-WithHermes`/`--with-hermes` flag claims at alpha.15 (#1207).

So **A15's "cross-platform by construction, no OS-specific enforcement claimed"** is the terminus of a trajectory that spent alpha.1→alpha.4 building a two-OS runtime split and then alpha.14→alpha.15 **collapsing it** — the axis of OS-differentiation (runtime location) was being *erased*, exactly as ADR-64 predicted ("collapses the two-OS topology into one").

**How B1 maps:** B1 re-introduces an OS split, but on a **different axis** with no alpha-line precedent. The alpha-line split was runtime-location (WSL2 vs native Windows); B1's split is **security-guarantee strength** (OS-enforced egress on Linux only). B1's SECURITY.md "per-OS truth" — Linux worker in a `CLONE_NEWNET` netns, macOS seccomp fallback, "on macOS/Windows, no OS egress enforcement" — has no ancestor in alpha.1–alpha.15; it is a new dimension grafted onto a codebase that had just finished flattening its *old* OS dimension.

#### 2. OS-enforced egress / `CLONE_NEWNET` — no alpha-line precedent

Network-level egress containment is **entirely new in beta.1**. Across the full alpha.1→alpha.15 history there is no `CLONE_NEWNET`, no network namespace, no egress broker, no "Guard," and no per-OS egress enforcement — grep of the design-history record returns nothing on any of these terms.

The alpha line's containment lived at two OS-agnostic layers, neither of which is a network boundary:
- **Write-layer policy gate (alpha.1).** `policy_hook.py` hard-denied the `file`/`terminal`/`code_execution` families and `command_execute`/`vault_delete`/`vault_move` for *every* lane — a permissions boundary at the write layer (ADR-22 explicitly rejected "OpenHands-style sandbox-vs-host permissions" in favor of "permissions enforced per-profile at the write layer").
- **MCP-only sandbox (L5).** The MCP server was "the sandbox boundary"; external access rode ADR-32's MCP surface.

**How B1 maps:** B1's brokered-egress + `CLONE_NEWNET` model is a **new-in-B1** kernel-enforcement layer with no named earlier era to revert to. B1's own design doc frames the supervisor/worker split with brokered egress as the "allowlist mechanism" and the Linux netns as satisfying the charter's "network namespace" wording — a charter and vocabulary that postdate alpha.15. The closest *conceptual* alpha-line ancestor is the diagnostic-plane exfiltration contract (see thread 3), but that governed logs, not sockets.

#### 3. Containment model / VaultWriter exfiltration linter / per-OS keychain / canary drill (every OS) — extends the alpha exfiltration-mitigation lineage; specific mechanisms are B1-new

The gap's every-OS layer is where B1 *continues* an alpha-line trajectory rather than inventing an axis. The lineage of "exfiltration is a first-class threat, mitigated OS-agnostically":

- **alpha.11 (ADR-105) — the first explicit "exfiltration" framing.** The content-light diagnostic plane was `accepted` with a deny-by-default content-exfiltration contract: lives outside the vault and outside Git under `$XDG_STATE_HOME`, typed error code + SHA-256 instead of payloads, golden-corpus redaction self-test, no remote telemetry. Its rationale is the direct ancestor of B1's concern: done naively, "a forgotten debug flag plus a routine `git push` exfiltrates unpublished research." Notably, this contract was **OS-agnostic** — the same everywhere — matching B1's "runs on every OS" for the linter/canary/containment layer.
- **alpha.15 (ADR-130) — read scoping as "the lethal-trifecta / exfiltration mitigation."** Per-agent `read_scope` filtering in `engine/api` before any transport sees payloads; "No agent tool grants"; loopback HTTP binds loopback only with `MEMORIA_HTTP_TOKEN`; fail-closed enforcement (ADR-125: "a logged denial is policy, silence is wiring"). This is the nearest alpha-line kin to B1's containment model — but the threat model was explicit that it is a **policy boundary for tool-limited/cooperating agents**, and "a local agent with its own file tools is in the trusted-local class regardless of transport." It is OS-agnostic and is *not* a network/OS enforcement layer.

The **specific B1 mechanisms are all new**, with zero occurrences in alpha.1→alpha.15: **VaultWriter** (the sole machine writer, owner of the exfiltration linter), the **canary** spec (`MEMORIA-CANARY-<8hex>`, `INC-canary-<date>`), and the **OS keychain** for BYOK keys (Keychain/DPAPI/Secret Service, mode-0600 Linux fallback). There is no keychain, keyring, secret-store, or BYOK-key handling anywhere in the alpha line — the alpha runtime never held a user-supplied API key as a first-class secret (model routing was a Hermes/OpenRouter deployment concern, ADR-30), so no per-OS credential-store abstraction was ever needed.

**How B1 maps:** the *stance* — exfiltration is a named threat, mitigated by an OS-agnostic layer that runs everywhere — **continues the ADR-105 → ADR-130 trajectory** (exfiltration mitigation was cross-platform in the alpha line because it lived in policy/logging code, not OS primitives). The *artifacts* (VaultWriter, linter, keychain, canary) are new-in-B1 with no named earlier era. The fact that these run on **every OS** while only `CLONE_NEWNET` is Linux-gated is itself consistent with the alpha-line pattern that all security enforcement was OS-agnostic — B1 keeps that property for everything *except* the one genuinely OS-primitive-dependent layer (kernel netns).

#### 4. Plugin desktop-only — continues alpha.12→alpha.15's Obsidian-as-optional-adapter demotion and explicit plugin-out-of-scope gate

Obsidian was a **desktop application from the Origins onward** (chosen pre-alpha.1 as vault/UI), so "desktop-only plugin" carries the desktop assumption unchanged. The relevant history is Obsidian's demotion from *the* product surface to a deferred, gated adapter:

- **alpha.1.** Obsidian is the vault/UI and the write-bridge target (the obsidian-MCP write-bridge, the MCP-only sandbox); catalog entities were Obsidian **Bases**-backed from the first cut.
- **alpha.11 → alpha.12 (arc f, ADR-122).** Hermes demoted to adapter (alpha.11); alpha.12 made the engine **standalone and CLI-first**, "Obsidian demoted to optional adapter."
- **alpha.14.** Docs mass-rewritten to the standalone-CLI baseline; installer requirement made explicit: **"Install no Obsidian plugin."**
- **alpha.15 (ADR-130, Scope Boundary).** The negative scope gate is on record: **"alpha.15 cannot close with Obsidian plugin implementation counted as release scope,"** and "adapter-specific UI needs its own ADR before it is scheduled" — ADR-130 was stripped of stale "CLI+plugin/second-wave" wording, and "future editor adapters now **require their own ADR**" (#1203, #1208, `d5131811`). Under ADR-125 "the engine is the product and every surface is a client"; the CLI "remains the no-daemon recovery surface."

**How B1 maps:** B1's "plugin is desktop-only" **continues the alpha.12→alpha.15 trajectory** — the plugin is an adapter over the engine, deliberately outside the engine's cross-platform core, exactly the posture alpha.15 froze into a scope gate. It does not revert to any earlier era where Obsidian was the required substrate (that era ended at alpha.11/12). B1 building the plugin at all is the "own ADR / next wave" work that alpha.15's Scope Boundary explicitly punted forward.

#### 5. Mobile = keep-test mode (synced markdown + Bases, no daemon) — new naming of a posture assembled entirely from alpha.1 Bases + alpha.12 keep-test + the alpha.15 no-daemon standalone stance

Mobile has **no design presence in the alpha line** — it appears zero times as a platform target across alpha.1→alpha.15 (the single "mobile"-adjacent mention is a Zotero capture quirk). B1's mobile posture is nonetheless assembled from three pre-existing alpha-line pieces:

- **Obsidian Bases (alpha.1).** The catalog shipped as Obsidian Bases from the first cut ("CATALOG: structured entity records (Obsidian Bases)"), so "Bases" as the mobile read surface reaches all the way back to alpha.1.
- **The keep-test (alpha.12, ADR-122).** The exact phrase B1 uses is the alpha.12 reframe: "what must survive Memoria's death is markdown OKF + a portable bibliography; everything else is free to be SQLite." The **keep-set** = git-tracked markdown (`knowledge/` + `capabilities/` + generated `references.bib`); working state = SQLite. B1's "mobile sees synced markdown + Bases, no daemon" is precisely a device that has *only* the keep-set — the portable markdown corpus — and none of the SQLite engine.
- **No daemon (alpha.12→alpha.15, arc f).** "CLI-first," "no always-on process," and alpha.15's CLI as "the no-daemon recovery surface" establish that a Memoria surface can exist without the running engine. Mobile "no daemon" is that property taken to its limit: a device with no engine at all, reading synced files.

**How B1 maps:** mobile keep-test mode is **new-in-B1 as a named platform posture**, but it **continues** — does not revert — the alpha.12 keep-test boundary and the alpha.12/15 no-daemon standalone stance, layered over alpha.1 Bases. There is no alpha-line "mobile era" to revert to; B1 is the first version to explicitly project the keep-set/working-state split onto a device class, using the split alpha.12 built for storage/portability reasons to define what a daemon-less mobile device can see.

**Net across the section:** A15's "no platform split" is genuinely where the alpha arc landed — the runtime-location split (WSL2 vs native Windows, ADR-64) had just been collapsed, and all security enforcement (policy gate, diagnostic-plane contract, read scoping) was OS-agnostic by construction. B1 re-introduces platform-differentiation on a **new axis** (egress-guarantee strength), gates only the kernel-primitive layer (`CLONE_NEWNET`) to Linux while keeping the alpha-line every-OS pattern for the containment/linter/keychain/canary layer, and formalizes the plugin (continuing alpha.15's out-of-scope gate) and mobile (new naming over the alpha.12 keep-test) as adapter tiers over the cross-platform engine.

## 15. Onboarding / seeding

The gap analysis frames §15 as a single new-in-B1 subsystem bundling three distinguishable elements against the alpha.15 end-state: (a) the seeded sample corpus, (b) a guided onboarding/seeding wizard, and (c) an "install → grounded answer ≤ 30 min" contract. Each is traced separately below. The through-line is that onboarding in the alpha line peaked at **alpha.10**, was **dismantled at alpha.11**, and stayed dormant through the alpha.14–15 standalone-engine cut — so at alpha.15 there is no onboarding subsystem at all, only a vestigial `sample`-flag clause.

### Sample corpus (retired alpha.10 mediterranean-diet fixture; re-introduced in B1)

**Origin & lineage.** There is no sample-vault ancestor before alpha.10. The pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton" carried only a `tutorials` root folder (`OLD-SKELETON-pre-alpha.1.md`), not a seeded corpus, and the alpha.1 baseline (Part II) shipped none. The bundled sample vault is a **late arrival, born whole at alpha.10** under ADR-111: a `mediterranean-diet` corpus of "~8 sources / ~15 claims, deliberately half-built," shipped in the installer scaffold at `.memoria/samples/mediterranean-diet/` (added to the `folders.yaml` skeleton) with `Memoria: load sample vault` / `remove sample vault` QuickAdd commands (`load-sample-vault.js`, `remove-sample-vault.js`; commits `f96b31ab`, `28540227`). Its recorded rationale: "done for real the knowledge loop takes months, so a one-sitting tutorial can't reproduce the payoff … a seeded half-built corpus the learner *finishes* is the resolution, doubling as an end-to-end test fixture and demo." A hard constraint was attached at birth: the seed is "authored from real sources, never LLM-generated — 'provenance *is* the product; because learners imitate the form they are shown, a faked seed teaches fabrication of the exact thing the system prevents'" (ADR-111). The `sample: bool` frontmatter flag was added across content/space schemas in the same release (`5a7209ed`/`cb81a255`) specifically so the Maintenance "New this week" digest could exclude sample docs and the remove command could find them.

**Prior states & when they changed.** The fixture had a single-release life. ADR-111 was **superseded by ADR-112** within alpha.10, but the sample vault and its load/remove commands were *explicitly retained* — only ADR-111's two-mode tutorial spine was dropped (history ADR table: "seeded sample vault + load/remove commands retained by ADR-112"). It was then **retired at alpha.11**: the clean-slate reset removed "the bundled `mediterranean-diet` sample skeleton (old `folders.yaml` `.memoria/samples/…`)," recording that alpha.11 "seeds *only test projects and captured sources created through the alpha.11 pipeline*" (§20). From alpha.12 through alpha.15 nothing re-seeded it. What survives at alpha.15 is exactly the vestige the gap analysis names: the `sample` flag's consumer clause `WHERE sample != true` still stands in `vault-template/spaces/maintenance.md` (lines 28, 37) — the digest-exclusion mechanism outliving the corpus it was built to exclude.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's ~8-source sample corpus with a "planted tension/gap" is, as the gap analysis states, a **re-introduction of the retired alpha.10 fixture**, not a wholly new idea — the source count (~8) and the "deliberately half-built" shape both match the ADR-111 corpus. It reverts to the **alpha.10 onboarding era** on the corpus question after a four-release absence (alpha.11–15). The "planted tension/gap" framing continues the alpha.10 pedagogical logic (a half-built corpus the learner finishes) and echoes the alpha.11-era ingest reasoning where "thin/thin → a *new topic* … propose a seed" and "cold start (no seed) is legal" (alpha.11 design §"gap analysis").

### Onboarding / seeding wizard

**Origin & lineage.** The wizard has two distinct ancestral threads. The **guided-flow-as-conversation** thread begins at alpha.10 as **ADR-113 (Co-PI-guided conversational onboarding)**, recorded `proposed`/deferred: "the AI-native end state is the Co-PI walking the first loop in conversation," held back because "the ADR-112 doc arc is the script the agent layer would dramatize, so it must exist and stabilize first." That was the nearest thing to a wizard the alpha line ever specified, and it was never built. The **mechanical-pipeline** thread is the alpha.11 ingest spine itself — "open a project → seed → gap analysis → source → capture → conversation → digest + hub compounding → notes → re-run gap analysis" (alpha.11 design §"the loop", M-milestone). B1's wizard (import → auto extract+index → optional relevance prefilter → single digest confirm → prompts first Ask) is that same pipeline recast as a linear first-run sequence.

**Prior states.** Before any guided flow, onboarding surfaced only as **empty-state copy**: alpha.8 introduced first-run empty-state text on the new spaces dashboards ("Inbox empty? That's the goal.", commit `9aba2c65`), and alpha.9 added "Day-one empty-state guidance" — a "First actions" `[!suggestions]` callout (capture source / capture fleeting / ask the Co-PI) that "turn[ed] the empty state into an onboarding surface" (#781). alpha.10 then rebuilt the **tutorial arc** into "one destination-first project arc" (ADR-112: `01-orient … 07-make-it-your-own`, setup delegated to the Quickstart) plus a "Your first month" practice guide (`cd544392`). The alpha.11 reset dissolved this surface: the empty-state dashboards' underlying model changed (attention became "a generated projection," the Inbox space became the queue, the homepage front door was retired for a "first-run welcome seed" under ADR-115), and alpha.14–15 removed the Obsidian surface entirely for the standalone CLI+engine cut — leaving no launch surface for onboarding copy or a wizard to attach to at alpha.15.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** B1's onboarding wizard **continues the ADR-113 trajectory** (guided first-loop walkthrough) while grounding it in the **alpha.11 ingest-pipeline** shape rather than pure conversation — the "single digest confirm" and "optional relevance prefilter" are pipeline stages, not the Co-PI "walking the loop in conversation" that ADR-113 envisioned. It is version-anchored as new alpha.16 scope (gap analysis notes B1 §11 lists it as such), consistent with ADR-113 having been kept "on record with preconditions rather than built." No wizard was ever shipped in the alpha line, so B1 builds the deferred idea rather than reverting to a working prior state.

### "Install → grounded answer ≤ 30 min" time-to-first-Ask contract

**Origin & lineage.** This contract has **no alpha-line precedent** as a stated, measurable target. The closest recorded ambition is alpha.10's "one-sitting tutorial" language, but ADR-111 reasoned in the *opposite* direction from a time contract: it took as its premise that "done for real the knowledge loop takes months, so a one-sitting tutorial can't reproduce the payoff," and resolved that not with a fast-path but with a *pre-seeded* corpus so the payoff could be shown without the wait. The alpha line otherwise measured onboarding only qualitatively (empty states that "read as intentional," a tutorial arc that "sells Accumulate via the visible gap") and never bound install-to-first-answer time.

**How B1 maps onto the history.** The ≤ 30-min "install → grounded answer" contract is a **new B1 framing with no earlier era to revert to**. It inverts the alpha.10 stance: where ADR-111 conceded the real loop takes months and substituted a seed for speed, B1 makes time-to-first-Ask an explicit contract. It is anchored as forthcoming alpha.16 scope (gap analysis / B1 §11) and leans on the two other B1 elements above — the re-introduced sample corpus and the seeding wizard — as the mechanism that makes a bounded time-to-first-Ask feasible, mirroring the alpha.10 logic that a pre-seeded corpus is what lets a learner reach the payoff inside a single sitting rather than over months.

## 16. Reading / annotation

*Section 16 of the updated gap analysis carries a single, bundled gap, flagged **new-in-B1**: alpha.15 reading is "not centered on Zotero," while beta.1 introduces Zotero-first reading plus a one-way append-only annotation-sync/anchor-bridge pipeline (Zotero-ID + `dateAdded` keying, exact-quote → `^pNNNN` anchor, orphan counting, and a "processing card" batch triage). The lineage below traces each strand — the reading substrate, the annotation import path, the anchor mechanism, and the triage surface — separately, because they have different histories: one is a **revert**, one is **new with no alpha-line precedent**, and one is a **continuation of an alpha.11 idea re-scoped**.*

### Zotero-first reading + annotation bridge (Zotero reader, one-way annotation sync keyed by Zotero-ID + `dateAdded`, exact-quote → `^pNNNN` anchor bridge, orphan counting, processing-card triage)

**Origin & lineage of the reading/annotation aim (pre-alpha.1 Origins).** Reading-and-annotation is not a late addition to Memoria's intellectual stack — it sits at the *founding* crossing. The Origins section names two explicit progenitors: Karpathy's LLM-Wiki pattern and **Elena Razlogova's *Doing History with Zotero and Obsidian*** — "a working research methodology" (history §"The goal it started from"). Zotero-plus-Obsidian reading was therefore baked into the name of the seed methodology. The pre-alpha.1 "Old Skeleton" adopted the two tools that make that methodology run: **Obsidian** as vault/UI (markdown + frontmatter) and **Zotero (with Better BibTeX) as the bibliographic backbone** (history §"The three tool bets"; ADR-05/06). The founding thesis — "maintaining a knowledge base is a bookkeeping problem, not an intelligence problem … let the human do the irreducibly judgment-laden work" — maps directly onto reading: the researcher reads and annotates in their own words; the agent does not. The researcher's own notes pin annotation as *the* entry gate to the knowledge cycle: "**Annotate**: first gate to new ZK notes = work annotation; guided reading/annotation process" and "Notes 'in your own words' distinguish source vs permanent/annotation … LLM writing a source note = delegation" (`researcher-notes.md`). The open question "**Is Razlogova's annotation note a type of permanent note?**" (researcher-notes, Round 2) shows the annotation-as-first-class-note concept was live from the earliest design rounds — it is the direct ancestor of beta.1's "processing session promotes survivors to attributed stubs."

**Prior states of the Zotero reading path (alpha.1 → alpha.13).** Through the alpha.1 line the Zotero relationship was *capture*, not *reading of annotations*. The path was hardened repeatedly: alpha.4 switched Zotero capture from the Better BibTeX CAYW URL to the **BBT JSON-RPC endpoint** (`item.citationkey`, #525) because CAYW returned inconsistent citation-key/title fields, and retired the CAYW read path as a drift source (history alpha.4, #525/#941); alpha.9 materialized the **Zotero Tier-0 catalog stub** — a QuickAdd flow reading the current Zotero selection over BBT JSON-RPC to write a catalog stub + an `intake:source` Librarian card (#782), completing the ADR-05/06 citekey-backbone capture path, with the load-bearing quirk recorded inline (Zotero's local server rejects any request carrying an `Origin` header). Throughout, Zotero was read-only and item-level.

**The annotation-import path was explicitly refused in alpha.11 — this is the load-bearing precedent.** Alpha.11 is where the annotation question came to a head, and the answer was *no import*. Capture adapters were fixed to a small set (`capture-pdf/url/bibtex/zotero-source`), "not a translator engine," leaning on the **read-only Zotero Local API** for item/source import only. The design is unambiguous (§13): "**Zotero annotations are not an alpha.11 import path; Memoria-owned PDF capture stores canonical quote/span/hash selectors itself.**" The alpha.11 verification record repeats it four ways — "annotation_import_in_scope=False"; "Zotero annotation import is not an alpha.11 capability or release gate"; "Zotero annotation import was removed from alpha.11 scope after the scope correction that Zotero is used only for item/source imports with no annotations" (`tmp__start-blocker-verification-results.md`, `validation-log.md`, `tmp__wp4-capture-results.md`). What alpha.11 *did* build was the **Memoria-owned annotation selector**: `capture_pdf_source()` used the PyMuPDF parser to derive page/text/bbox `annotation_ref` selectors for requested quotes, fail-closed (a missing or ambiguous quote moves the job to `failed` without writing), and note candidates preserved caller-supplied `annotation_ref` maps (`tmp__wp4-capture-results.md`, `tmp__wp6-note-gap-results.md`). So the alpha line's annotation provenance was *self-generated from captured PDFs*, deliberately not *imported from Zotero's annotation store*.

**The alpha.14 cut removed the Zotero/Obsidian reading substrate wholesale — this is the state alpha.15 inherits.** Alpha.14 reframed Memoria from "Obsidian app + worker" to a **standalone CLI-first engine**, dropping — not deferring — Obsidian, Zotero, Hermes, MCP, and profiles from the required baseline (history alpha.14 §Theme; `design.md` §0/§7). The Zotero-specific import surface (CLI `--format zotero-export`, worker operation, runtime helper, and the capability file **`capture-zotero-source.md`**) was removed (#1123); **ADR-124 "Standalone catalog is the citation authority"** (`supersedes: [5]`) made the Memoria SQLite catalog its own citation authority and demoted Zotero/Better BibTeX to "optional user tools … Memoria does not require a live Zotero library." An `rg`-based **negative gate** was added specifically to reject any reappearance of Zotero/Obsidian import surfaces (#1118–#1130). Alpha.15 continued on that standalone engine unchanged in this respect. This is exactly what the gap analysis means by "**A15: reading not centered on Zotero**" — by alpha.15 there is no Zotero reader, no Obsidian reader, and (a fortiori) no Zotero annotation import; reading is captured-source-content + digest inside the CLI engine.

**How beta.1's direction maps onto the history — three distinct mappings.**

- *Zotero-first reading (the substrate): a **revert to the pre-alpha.14 era.*** Restoring "Zotero as primary reading/capture" and "Obsidian as required UI" undoes the alpha.14 cut and returns to the founding Razlogova "Zotero + Obsidian" configuration named in Origins. The gap analysis appendix files both under "**Removed in A15, re-introduced in B1**" (Obsidian required UI; Zotero as primary reading/capture). Beta.1's own information-flow design re-anchors on it: "the reading-and-notes loop is the spine," works read in "**Zotero's reader**" (Pass 2 is "highlight-only reading in Zotero's reader"), PDFs "ingested via local API attachment access," and "**No `zotero.sqlite` reads; no Zotero Web API**" (info-flow-design §Sync/§Reading). The read-only-Local-API constraint is itself an inheritance from alpha.11's `capture-zotero-source` (Local API, read-only) — so beta.1 reverts the *substrate* (Zotero+Obsidian) while re-adopting alpha.11's *access discipline*.

- *One-way append-only annotation sync (the bridge): **new-in-B1, and specifically the inversion of a recorded alpha.11 refusal.*** There is **no alpha-line precedent for importing Zotero annotations** — alpha.11 named annotation import out of scope four times over (§13; verification logs above), and no later alpha built it (alpha.14 removed the Zotero surface entirely). Beta.1 introduces it for the first time: "**Annotations flow Zotero→vault append-only (§6) … Nothing flows back**," "**Annotations sync append-only, keyed by Zotero annotation ID with a `dateAdded` watermark**," each carrying verbatim quote, color, and a `zotero://open-pdf` deep link (info-flow-design §Sync/§Annotate). Where alpha.11 said "Memoria-owned PDF capture stores canonical selectors *itself*," beta.1 instead *ingests* Zotero's annotation objects — a direct reversal of the alpha.11 sourcing decision. The one-way / append-only / never-flows-back discipline is grounded in beta.1's own source review ("Notero/Better Notes/mdnotes → one-way only"; info-flow-design §Sync sources).

- *Exact-quote → `^pNNNN` anchor + orphan counting: a **continuation of alpha.11's selector idea, deliberately re-scoped down.*** Alpha.11's `annotation_ref` stored page/**text**/**bbox** selectors and preflighted quotes fail-closed (`tmp__wp4-capture-results.md`). Beta.1 keeps the quote-anchoring principle but reduces it to "an **exact-quote anchor only**: orphan = 'exact quote not found verbatim,' with a per-work orphan count, never silent drops," explicitly sending "**TextPosition/fuzzy re-anchoring**" — the richer selector machinery closest to alpha.11's text/bbox approach — to the **deferral ledger** (trigger: measured orphan rate exceeds X%), citing Hypothesis's anchoring-stack cost (info-flow-design §Annotate, Deferral ledger, §Sources "Hypothesis → explicit orphans; its anchoring stack's cost → exact-quote-only in beta.1"). The `^pNNNN` block-anchor form is Obsidian-native and therefore rides the re-introduced Obsidian UI; it has no alpha-line analogue because alpha.11's selectors were engine-internal `annotation_ref` maps, not Obsidian block references. The **orphan-count-never-delete** rule continues alpha.11's fail-closed posture (a bad selector never writes partial state) but expresses it as a visible per-work counter rather than a job `failed` transition, and draws on a beta.1 source lineage ("citar-denote-nobib → orphan-flag, never delete").

- *The "processing card" batch triage: **new-in-B1 as a surface**, realizing the Origins-era annotation-as-first-note aim.* No alpha version shipped a "processing card." Its conceptual ancestor is the Origins/`researcher-notes` "guided reading/annotation process … first gate to new ZK notes" and the Razlogova "annotation note as permanent note" question; its alpha.11 mechanical ancestors are the `propose-note-candidates` / `curate-note-candidate` operations. Beta.1 crystallizes these into an explicit initiation surface — "the sync-triggered processing card is the loop's starter motor," raised on annotation-sync when ≥N highlights arrive, opening a Pass-3 "processing session" that promotes survivors to **attributed** idea-note stubs (`suggested-*` keys, `unauthored` lint) — grounded in "Doto + Ahrens → collapse the pipeline; **initiation, not execution, is the collapse point → the processing card**" (info-flow-design §Annotate/§Loop/§Sources). The machine-attributed-until-human-edits rule enforces the same Origins boundary that made annotation the human's gate: "LLM writing a source note = delegation" (researcher-notes) becomes beta.1's "one-tap promotion … would launder injectable prose into the human-owned region."

**Recorded context that pins the versions.** The refusal that beta.1 reverses is version-pinned to **alpha.11 §13** and its start-blocker verification (2026-06-29 live Zotero Local API smoke, "annotation import … removed from alpha.11 scope"). The removal of the whole Zotero reading substrate is pinned to **alpha.14** (ADR-124 `supersedes: [5]`; `capture-zotero-source.md` deletion #1123; negative gate #1118–#1130). Beta.1's re-introduction is recorded in the gap-analysis appendix under both "Removed in A15, re-introduced in B1" (Zotero primary reading/capture; Obsidian required UI) and, for the annotation bridge specifically, "New in B1, not in A15" ("Zotero annotation bridge"). The fuzzy-re-anchoring deferral is pinned to beta.1's deferral ledger with an explicit orphan-rate trigger.

## Cross-cutting historical arcs

The fourteen arcs below cluster the alpha.15→beta.1 gaps. Each names its participating subsystems (by gap-analysis section) and traces the version-anchored **pre-alpha.1 → alpha.N → alpha.15 → beta.1** sequence. Purely descriptive; no evaluation. Nine are the established arcs (refreshed); five are newly surfaced by the fuller gap set (arcs 10–14).

### 1. Re-host: Obsidian/runtime required → stripped → required again
**Subsystems:** §1 host/runtime/distribution, §2 vault structure, §10 surfaces/navigation, §12 security, §14 platform.
- **Pre-alpha.1 / alpha.1:** Obsidian is L2 of the seven-layer architecture — the required workspace, hosted on the Hermes runtime; the write gate is an Obsidian-hosted policy plugin (ADR-28/31).
- **alpha.7:** the Obsidian UI is rebuilt clean-slate (persistent-shell four-space model, Bases, capture forms; ADR-81), still the product surface.
- **alpha.11:** Obsidian demoted to "a control panel, not a writer"; Hermes kept only as a specialized adapter.
- **alpha.12:** CLI-first; Obsidian demoted to an optional adapter (ADR-122).
- **alpha.14:** Obsidian, Zotero, Hermes, MCP, and installed profiles removed from the required baseline.
- **alpha.15:** standalone Python-3.12/uv/Typer engine ("not an Obsidian plugin release"); surfaces are CLI + scoped MCP + loopback HTTP; `serve` exists on-demand and explicitly "is not a daemon" (ADR-125).
- **beta.1:** the Obsidian plugin is re-instated as the *only* researcher UI; `mem serve` becomes a plugin-supervised resident daemon; the `mem` CLI is demoted to a "survival + ops" floor with `ask` dropped; the artifact changes from a Python package to a single static binary. (§1 records the host pivot as net-new alpha.16 scope.)

### 2. Catalog authority: markdown → SQLite → markdown
**Subsystems:** §2 bundle layout, §7 catalog/bibliographic authority, §3 identity.
- **Pre-alpha.1 / alpha.1:** catalog metadata rides Zotero + Better BibTeX; source records are markdown notes (ADR-05/06).
- **alpha.12:** the keep-test makes SQLite the SSOT for the catalog; markdown reserved for the keep-set (ADR-122).
- **alpha.13:** multi-source enrichment builds on the SQLite catalog; ADR-124 makes the catalog its own citation authority, absorbing the Zotero-authority ADR-06.
- **alpha.14/15:** SQLite is the catalog authority, `work_id`/provider IDs primary and citekeys aliases; `references.bib` generated from SQLite; the `work` Concept holds only the subjective digest (no `record.md`); Concept identity is ULID.
- **beta.1:** the record is re-materialized as markdown — `works/<citekey>/record.md` holds full CSL-JSON as the re-import authority, `bibliography.bib` is generated from it, the citekey is the primary key (pinned at import from BBT), ULID identity is dropped for citekey+path. Record, concept, extracted text, and blob co-locate in one folder. (§2, §7)

### 3. Typed-concept model: accretion → four types → flat enum
**Subsystems:** §3 concept/type model, §2 layout.
- **Pre-alpha.1 / alpha.1:** ~15 typed notes with per-type lifecycles and a controlled-vocab schema (ADR-02 era).
- **alpha.2–10:** the roster grows by accretion (ADR-47/50/78/117/119) — claim, question, excerpt, synthesis, thesis, steering, vocabulary, asset, digest, fleeting: 12+ types across three bundles.
- **alpha.11:** collapsed toward a three-node graph — digest (machine) / note (human) / hub (both).
- **alpha.15:** four typed concepts — `note` (with `mode: claim|question`), `work`, `hub`, `project` — each with a per-type schema, ULID identity, typed relation-links frontmatter map (ADR-126, PR-B2 #1164).
- **beta.1:** replaced by a flat frontmatter `type` class enum (12 file-class values), no per-type schemas; the `hub` type is dropped, the note claim/question mode and claim substrate are dropped, the argument-graph `project` concept becomes a folder of files, typed relation-links leave frontmatter for wikilinks + a DB backlink table. (§3)

### 4. Verdict / frontmatter: in-file → DB → back-in-file
**Subsystems:** §4 frontmatter discipline, §5 integrity/verdict markers.
- **alpha.1:** frontmatter is per-type and optional and carries verdict state (`check_status` lives in the file).
- **alpha.14:** universal-concept-frontmatter enforcement (#1157) — `id`/`standing`/`check_status` enforced across every concept.
- **alpha.15:** inverted to *meaning-only* — verdicts move to SQLite, a gate asserts frontmatter carries no verdict fields, and a closed validator rejects undeclared fields with an `x.*` escape (PR-L #1179, PR-AC #1197).

(§4, §5)

### 5. The review gate: continuous, re-surfaced (not reversed)
**Subsystems:** §5 integrity/review/gate, §10 surfaces, §11 agent boundary.
- **Pre-alpha.1 / alpha.1:** the PI-disposes review gate + promotion model + honesty-card inbox-as-category (ADR-03/50/56).

**Subsystems:** §7 catalog, §9 ingest, §16 reading/annotation.

### 7. Enrichment / graph: expand → contract
**Subsystems:** §9 ingest/enrichment graph, §6 machine judgment.

### 10. Controlled-vocabulary / faceting: build-up → drop

- **Alpha.1 (Part II §6 "Controlled vocabulary"):** faceted vocabularies `research_area` / `methodology` / note `topics`; `system/vocabulary.md` the PI-editable controlled-value home; `classify.py` seeds `research_area` from OpenAlex topic scores ("free and consistent across sources"; vocabulary-discipline.md).
- **alpha.3:** the vocabulary is surfaced at capture through schema-generated Modal Forms so values stay in lockstep with the list (ADR-71).
- **alpha.8:** "vocabulary discipline" is a headline checkpoint theme.
- **alpha.14:** `vocabulary` and `classify` are first-class CLI verbs; `init` seeds the vocabulary.
- **alpha.15:** the vault-template ships `vocabulary.md` as "the single source of truth for the controlled values," a `vocab` command mints/manages ID-backed tag IDs, `classify` applies a `research_area` (floor 0.6 / near-tie 0.15), and the `hub` type owns exactly one ID-backed controlled-vocab tag.
- **beta.1:** the entire controlled-vocabulary / faceting model is dropped — no `tags` facet, no `vocabulary.md`, no `research_area`/`methodology`, no `vocab` command, no `classify` operation. (§3)

### 11. Steering-lever + Librarian: discovery steering removed
**Subsystems:** §8 discovery, §11 agent boundary.
- **Pre-alpha.1 Old Skeleton:** the Librarian is one of the seven ADR-02 specialists.
- **alpha.1:** `research-focus.md` is "program memory" the Librarian reads at session start to *weight discovery*; the Librarian is one of the five profiles (ADR-48).
- **alpha.11:** `research-focus.md` is renamed `steering.md` — the authored `program.md`-equivalent program/steering substrate (Karpathy framing).
- **alpha.14/15:** the profile *system* is deleted (ADR-125), but the vault-template still ships `steering.md` as first-class program memory and the named "Librarian" persona that reads it to weight discovery; ADR-92 discovery relevance scoring sorts candidates by deterministic `steering.md` relevance (PR-AA #1195), and `system/eval/` rubrics score the Librarian's behavior.
- **beta.1:** no `steering.md`, no Librarian, no PI-priority weighting; discovery is deterministic occurrence-counting over the citation graph, and the per-batch relevance prefilter is a seeding/onboarding step, not discovery. (§8, §11)

### 12. Integrity: derivation-graph + tamper-evidence apparatus dropped
**Subsystems:** §5 integrity/gate, §13 snapshot/recovery.
- **Pre-alpha.1 / alpha.1:** the gate is best-effort (ADR-25) with per-write SHA-256 audit pairing; the vault is a git repo (a Merkle chain), backstopped by `vault-hash-drift`/`audit-unpaired-writes` detectors and a golden copy.
- **alpha.8:** ADR-104 re-derives telemetry into three planes and explicitly *rejects* an in-file `prev_hash` chain in favor of Git, scaffolding a git-tracked journal-head anchor.
- **alpha.11:** a workspace-level append-only `journal/<machine>.jsonl` with PROV-DM events becomes "the tamper-evident derivation graph that substitutes for authorship" (§4.1).
- **alpha.12:** the journal becomes a hash-chained SQLite table (`prev_hash`/`row_hash`, append-only triggers) anchored by a git-tracked journal-head; design specifies recursive-CTE cascade-rollback over an entities graph.
- **alpha.15:** ADR-127 lands a row-hash-chained append-only journal + git-anchored `journal-head` root (ADR-104-contributed), a first-class DB derivation graph (consumed-at-verdict edges) driving two-tier stale/invalid demotion propagation, an `overrides.jsonl` tiered DB-loss replay, and a Litestream-class streaming-replication backup leg; ADR-128 adds an apply-rate rubber-stamp detector.
- **beta.1:** the cascade-rollback journal is replaced by the git-snapshot batch protocol; the hash-chain + `journal-head`, the derivation graph + two-tier demotion (→ per-block hash divergence + footprint-scoped DB reconciliation on revert), the `overrides.jsonl` tiered replay, the streaming-replication leg (§18 declines it), and the rubber-stamp detector are all dropped. (§5, §13)

### 13. The no-daemon premise and the ADR-95 reversal
**Subsystems:** §8 discovery, §1 runtime, §7 citation monitor.
- **Alpha.1:** ADR-61 defines a nightly proactive discovery loop but *defers* it — it is gated on always-on infrastructure (Syncthing + VPS, ADR-63) and the invariant "exactly one Hermes dispatcher per vault."
- **alpha.2:** the nightly loop is deferred out of the checkpoint.
- **alpha.14/15:** the standalone architecture is explicitly no-daemon (`serve` is on-demand, "not a daemon"); the ADR-95 disposition *rejects* the always-on discovery loop — "an unattended always-on loop conflicts with the no-daemon architecture."
- **beta.1:** the resident `mem serve` daemon (§1) plus a scheduled citation monitor (§7) restore both halves the ADR-95 rejection rested on; the charter exclusion is amended once the no-daemon premise is removed. (This arc shares the residency dimension with arc 1's daemon re-instatement.) (§8, §1, §7)
