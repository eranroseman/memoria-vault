### R-2 — Hub is required (wiki<->ZK bridge)

**Verdict: PARTIAL** · survived opposite-side skeptic

**For the position:** Hub is the only knowledge concept that survived every alpha reset with a fresh re-derivation each time: Old Skeleton MOC -> ADR-50 rename keeping the ADR-19 threshold-alert mechanism (design-history L148-149), alpha.11's 25->12 collapse kept and reshaped it as "the merged Karpathy concept-page x ZK MOC: the machine keeps the topic synthesis current, the human owns the structure + argument" (L1637), and alpha.15's ADR-126 kept it among four types while deleting nine schemas (L2078). Alpha.11's three-node graph — digest (machine) / note (human) / hub (both) (L2284) — makes the owner's bridge framing literal architecture: hub was the one node both authors write, and digests were defined as "what hubs draw from" (L1635), so the digest->hub link was real, not retrofitted. Beta.1's own commissioned research reached verdict adopt on editorial hubs as the organizing layer (info-flow-research L612 "editorial at the hub layer"; L614, L625, L689 all adopt), yet design §17's seven resolutions never dispose of these verdicts, breaking §17's own "resolved... folded into the body — recorded so they are not re-litigated" contract; the design's only hub text is a rationale-free parenthetical (§7 L623). Docs-history Borrow #42 (line 42, target beta1) requires "a worked-example starter hub set marked example-not-default" in the sample corpus — an authoritative recorded borrow that cannot land in a no-hub design. **For beta.1:** No charter row requires hubs and no Deliberate Exclusion covers them: beta.1 is "a retention experiment... not a feature milestone" (requirements L9-11), every Required Product Scope bar is met without a hub type, and the knowledge-format row demands OKF conformance parity, not type-roster parity (requirements L36). The owner's direction "every digested work connects to a hub" is exactly the import-time taxonomy those adopted verdicts reject, at a corpus scale (~10 imports/week, design §7 L626) that will not reach the squeeze point inside the beta window. Every documented alpha failure around hubs is a failure of hub maintenance — membership rot ("every relevant new note requires a hub edit") and curated-looking generated lists (design-history L2078) — never of hub absence, and researcher-authored MOC-style notes remain writable as freehand files today. Finally, the alpha.15 hub form depends on the owned ID-backed controlled-vocabulary tag, a substrate beta.1 also dropped (S3.7); restoring that form drags in a dependency chain the charter never asks for.

**Decision:** The citation audit already established the drop is silent; the live question is RECORD vs CHANGE. Three findings push past RECORD. (1) Process debt inside the design itself: §17 claims all info-flow divergences "were resolved... and folded into the body," but three hub-layer adopt verdicts (L614/L625/L689) are unresolved — a §11 divergence line would leave §17's completeness claim false; only a resolution (adopt-in-some-form or reasoned reject) repairs it. (2) Corpus self-contradiction: docs-history Borrow #42 targets beta1 with a starter hub set for the sample corpus (itself justified by the cold-start abandonment window the retention experiment measures), which cannot ship in a no-hub design; either hub exists or that recorded borrow must be explicitly overturned. (3) The concept is separable from the machinery: the research's size-gating verdicts gate hub tooling (suggestion, staleness detectors, per-hub diagnostics), not the concept's existence, and the research-adopted candidates-section mechanism (L689: machine suggestions appended in a clearly-marked section the human promotes from; L627) answers the documented membership-rot failure without the controlled-vocab substrate — so a minimal hub (one enum value, one folder, one flow affordance) restores coherence at near-zero machinery cost. But the owner's full direction does not survive the same corpus test: mandating every digest connect to a hub contradicts the adopted bottom-up-emergence and no-import-time-taxonomy verdicts, and no charter bar or documented failure supports it. Verdict: partial — hub returns as a first-class, researcher-created editorial concept (upheld; entries flip to CHANGE), the digest->hub link returns as a candidates affordance rather than a hard flow terminus (R-5's mandate not upheld as stated), and hub tooling stays post-beta.1, size-gated, per the research beta.1 itself adopted.

### R-3 — Authorship-blind change reaction: quarantine then verify; graph-integrity verification

**Verdict: PARTIAL** · survived opposite-side skeptic

**For the position:** The alpha line ran an unbroken, authorship-blind change-detection lineage from its first release: alpha.1's vault-hash-drift walked the audit log and flagged any file whose on-disk SHA-256 diverged — "a legitimate human edit in Obsidian surfaces here too, by design" (design-history L487); alpha.14's workspace scan recorded observed_external_edit events, demoted foreign-edited keep-set Concepts to unchecked (re-promoted only via mark-checked, a human act), and quarantined worker-owned paths (L1954); ADR-127 universalized it — every mutation, regardless of origin, non-consumable until a verification pass dispositions it, covering all tracked paths (L2122). The verification was indeed structural/graph integrity, never semantic: the shared suite was integrity-link-target-check, integrity-quote-anchor-check, evidence-resolves and citekey-resolution checks (L1657, L196), the history explicitly records structural checks as blind to crafted injection and unwarranted claims (L1725, L1735), and the borrow doc concedes "the mechanical slice (anchors resolve, links well-formed) is already quarantine-and-verify's job" (borrow/adapt/reject L118). Beta.1 is the first version with no out-of-band change witness at all: an agent or sync process editing human-class text is silently trusted as human, deletion of a researcher-set `superseded: true` silently re-admits a retracted work into Ask and past the export refusal (the fail-open direction, un-witnessed — design §4 L339-353), and the charter's hardest bar ("machines never overwrite or delete human-authored text; a violation stops the beta immediately," requirements L72) has neither enforcement nor detection for non-VaultWriter writers such as the researcher's own file-tooled agent. The detection infrastructure is already paid for: the manifest human_hash is "recomputable from the file alone" (design §4 L354-357) and every run's pre-tag commit-all materializes the out-of-band diff in plain git (§6 L460-462, L477-479).

**For beta.1:** Beta.1 did not drop quarantine-then-verify; it kept it where machines write and where machine verdicts are consumed: engine writes are staged then verified by the pre==post human-span manifest gate with auto-rollback (§6 steps 2-3), any edit under an `mc` marker fail-closes to amber and re-checks (§4 L325-338), rebuild demotes every verdict and re-earns it via the $0 local Checker, and export refuses on unresolved/superseded/unwarranted citations (§8 L828-836) — the regression sweep verified "quarantine-and-verify realized as staging + the human-span manifest gate" among 151 held invariants (regression review L62-68). The maximal reading — quarantine human-authored changes until verified — contradicts the alpha record itself: ADR-127's propagation kept PI-authored descendants live ("never auto-destroy human work," history L2126), the docs-history adapt row mandates "automated propagation may never demote/quarantine human-authored derived content — flag-and-route only" (borrow doc L85), and refuse-to-serve only ever guarded machine consumption under a stale checked hash (L2124), which beta.1's amber demotion reproduces fail-closed. The alpha scan's guarded objects largely do not exist in beta.1's substrate (no consumed references.bib, no overrides.jsonl, human notes not embedded — S05.md L36), prompts carry quotes/citekeys/sections only so foreign-edited notes structurally never reach a model (§8 L806-807), and the borrow doc records an explicit reject of foreign-commit policing as "detect-and-apologize" superseded by "VaultWriter+ledger+manifest (enforce, don't detect-and-apologize)" (L125). The charter requires none of this beyond what beta.1 has, and sync-conflict handling is a Deliberate Exclusion (requirements L87).

**Decision:** The decision splits on detection versus quarantine. (1) Detection is upheld: beta.1 has named residual holes with fail-open direction — no witness for out-of-band edits to human text, so the stop-the-beta human-text bar is undetectable for non-VaultWriter writers (exactly the "agent edits a human block = silently trusted as human" case), and no witness for removal of researcher-set restriction frontmatter (`superseded`), whose forging direction was adjudicated fail-safe (S04.md S4.4) but whose removal direction silently relaxes Ask exclusion and export refusal. Both were covered continuously from alpha.1 (vault-hash-drift) through alpha.15 (workspace scan / ADR-127) by authorship-blind hash detection, and beta.1's drop of the scan half is what S5.4's RECORD verdict papered over as merely unrecorded. The cost of restoration is near zero: the manifest hash is file-recomputable by design and the git pre-tag sweep already captures the out-of-band diff. (2) Verification-as-graph-integrity is upheld: the owner's characterization matches the alpha structural suite exactly, and beta.1 currently runs those checks only at export and Ask, so link/anchor/citekey rot between consumption points has no home; folding it into §5's periodic reconciliation pass is a small doctrine-consistent extension (flags, never blocks). (3) Quarantine/refuse-to-serve for human or foreign edits is rejected: the alpha line was authorship-blind in detection but deliberately authorship-asymmetric in reaction — human-authored content was flag-and-route only, never withheld (history L2126; borrow doc L85) — and refuse-to-serve guarded only stale machine verdicts at machine consumption, which beta.1's marker+hash+checks fail-closed amber plus the export gate reproduce. Restoring universal withhold-until-verified would overrule a documented alpha lesson with no documented failure justifying it. Also decisive: ADR-127 was a designed belt for ADR-128's no-write-gate epistemics ("errors become detectable, attributable, and reversible instead of gated at creation," L2122) — so what beta.1 must preserve is that property; without the detection sweep, foreign changes to non-machine-block content are not detectable at all, breaking the property the alpha mechanism existed to deliver. Hence partial: S5.4 becomes CHANGE (detection sweep + graph-integrity verify, flag-and-route; non-restored halves recorded), S4.1's absence-of-markers rule stands as a detection/classification rule — the owner's own scope note concedes it "may stand" as detection — with its trust consequence mitigated by the new witness rather than by changing the rule.

**History:** All in scratch/design-history/memoria-design-history-alpha.1-to-alpha.15.md unless noted. (1) L487, L495: alpha.1 `vault-hash-drift` (CRITICAL detector) compared last recorded after_hash to on-disk SHA-256 — authorship-blind by design ("a legitimate human edit in Obsidian surfaces here too"); the detective backstop to imperfect prevention. (2) L1655/L1657: alpha.11 trace-integrity scan quarantined untraced/foreign commits; the structural detection suite was graph-integrity checks (integrity-link-target-check, integrity-quote-anchor-check, evidence/claim-quote, citekey sweeps; L196 frontmatter-link detector "checks every links: wikilink resolves"). (3) L1725/L1735: structural checks were explicitly blind to crafted injection and well-formed-but-unwarranted claims — alpha verification was never semantic; the semantic residual got a human checkpoint, not the suite. This confirms the owner's graph-integrity characterization. (4) L1954: alpha.14 workspace scan — observed_external_edit journal events; keep-set foreign edit imported as PI edit and demoted to unchecked (human re-promotion via mark-checked); worker-owned paths/references.bib quarantined, never re-consumed. (6) Borrow/adapt/reject doc L85: adapt row "automated propagation may never demote/quarantine human-authored derived content — flag-and-route only." (8) Same doc L125: recorded REJECT of git-as-enforcement foreign-commit quarantine ("enforce, don't detect-and-apologize") — the philosophy under which beta.1 dropped scan-based detection, but enforcement cannot cover writers outside VaultWriter, where detection is the only option. ### R-4+R-9 — Catalog records/entities are SQLite; bibliography.bib is the survival artifact, never local-only

**Verdict: PARTIAL** · survived opposite-side skeptic

**For the position:** The alpha line's mature doctrine was "files own meaning, the DB owns records" (ADR-124/126, gap report S2 row, L31), and record.md is a machine record dressed as a markdown file — kin to the machine-materialized-state pattern beta.1 itself calls "a standing reject with three documented generations of decay" (design §5 L418-421). Everything genuinely relational in the catalog — entities, mentions, citation edges, annotations — already lives DB-side in beta.1's own schema (§5 L397-404; §7 L615-631; §17.5), which validates the owner's "relational by nature" intuition. The charter's Survival claim names the .bib's form as the survival floor ("plain markdown plus standard bibliography files", charter L22-23), so a reading where the catalog's survivor IS the bibliography file is charter-compatible; and for Zotero-sourced works the researcher's Zotero library independently preserves full fidelity, making per-work CSL-JSON copies look redundant. R-9's operational demand is genuinely unmet in the text: no design sentence exempts bibliography.bib from local-only, while beta.1 does make the *other*, same-named artifact — the per-export references.bib — local-only by inheritance from draft.md (§8 L840-841), so an unrecorded guarantee invites exactly the conflation the owner fears. One recorded sentence closes it.

**For beta.1:** The .bib alone cannot carry re-import fidelity, on alpha's own record: "references.bib is portable but lossy relative to the catalog DB; this is accepted for alpha.12 simplicity" (history L1802), and the Zotero re-import key rides csl_json.custom.zotero_key (design §7 L557-558), which has no standard BibTeX home — a nonstandard field is dropped by precisely the standard tools Success Claim 4 is about. DB-authoritative catalog + exported survivor is the shape whose failure alpha documented: the catalog-recovery spike FAILED ("Periodic export loses Memoria-owned catalog changes made after the last export", L1802), forcing same-commit bibliography atomicity as a patch; beta.1 removes that failure structurally because each record is itself a keep-test file, so catalog survival no longer depends on export freshness or backup horizon. Every reason ADR-122/124 gave for SQLite — worker state, provider payload provenance, full-text gates, projections (L1896, L2042) — stays DB-side in beta.1 (§5), so the delta re-opens no earned position; and non-Zotero works (DOI/arXiv, BibTeX-file, repo — required Capture inputs, charter L29) have no Zotero fallback, so their fidelity and the researcher's metadata corrections need a file home or die with the DB, violating §5's own rule that no researcher judgment lives only in the DB (L441-443). R-1 strengthens, not weakens, this: a stable work_id decoupled from citekey must survive DB loss to keep identity stable, and record.md is the only file-side carrier available. Alpha itself already denormalized a citation survival copy into keep-set files after the cross-store spike FAIL ("the embedded Citation is the survival copy", L1776); full CSL-JSON expands that earned position rather than reversing it.

**Decision:** Both briefing tests resolve against the position's core. (1) Can the .bib alone carry re-import fidelity? No — the lossiness finding is the alpha line's own accepted record (history L1802), CSL-JSON is the native ≥95%-fidelity format from the Zotero API (design §7 L554-556), and the idempotent-upsert key (custom.zotero_key) has no durable BibTeX representation; making the .bib the catalog's source also reintroduces the rejected BBT-.bib-as-interface shape ("a second source of truth with built-in staleness", docs-history Reject #123). (2) Does R-1 change the equation? Yes, in beta.1's favor: a stable work_id must survive total DB loss or rebuild severs identity, links, and anchors — the file-side record is the natural carrier, so R-1 makes record.md more load-bearing, not less. ### R-19 — Did beta.1 lose the 2PL/ACID design?

**Verdict: PARTIAL** · survived opposite-side skeptic

**For the position:** The alpha line built a hard-won transactional discipline over two releases and beta.1's s6 does not visibly restate all of it.
