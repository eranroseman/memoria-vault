## 5. Integrity / review / gate model

7 gaps — leave 5 · record 2 · change 0

### S5.1 — First-class Review surface (Obsidian main-pane view + ribbon badge, one-click revert, cognition-bound throttle) vs alpha.15's consequence-routed attention worklist

**LEAVE — deliberate and recorded** · verification: confirmed (citation audited) · confidence: high

The substance A15 kept when it removed the approval surface — honesty cards (arguments not verdicts), batch worklists, graded loudness, dispositions at the review action — is carried verbatim in design §1 'Review discipline' (L112-134), and §11 files the Elicit-style span-first review surface plus 'no approval dialogs' under Converges (L970-972). The surface-form delta (main-pane view + ribbon count) follows from §11 item (10), the recorded host decision that beta.1 is an Obsidian plugin; one-click revert and the no-catch-up-queue property are charter Review-row requirements ('reversible by run, reviewable without creating a catch-up queue'). The cognition-bound throttle is a recorded docs-history borrow ('Attention back-pressure', target beta1). Nothing restores approval-confers-correctness: proposals carry no verdict line, no 'approved' machine state exists, and the graduated pre-write approval gate is a docs-history keep-dead reject.

- **Recorded:** design.md §11 L970-972 (converges: Elicit-style span-first review surface; no approval dialogs) and L987-989 (item 10, host decision); design.md §1 L112-134 (Review discipline); docs-history borrow rows 'Attention back-pressure' (L30) and 'Batch worklists' (L28); charter Required Product Scope, Review row
- **History:** ADR-128 removed the review queue's approval semantics because per-write approval measurably bought no correctness — Jacobs 2021 (N=220): clinician+ML 0.356 ≈ clinician-alone 0.357, and a wrong recommendation dragged the human below baseline; Feng & Liu verification asymmetry degrades the gate to a cursory nod under load (history L2110, L2116). ADR-51/ADR-121 honesty-card and enqueue-only obligations were carried, and the inbox-as-category died as collateral of the inbox retirement, not on merits (docs-history 'Patterns' L134). Beta.1 keeps the failure fixed: the Review surface dispositions and reverts already-applied runs, it never gates writes, so the rubber-stamp failure mode ADR-128 documented is not re-opened.

### S5.3 — Synchronous pre==post human-span manifest gate + block-level byte-immutability + auto-rollback (stronger than alpha.15's asynchronous read-path guard)

**LEAVE — deliberate and recorded** · verification: confirmed (citation audited) · confidence: high

This is a beta.1 strengthening explicitly adjudicated in §11 back-pressure item (4): 'the block-level human-content read-only rule + span-hash manifest with pre==post gate and auto-rollback (stronger than alpha.15's propagation-only demotion)' (L978-981), with the mechanism specified in §6 steps 2-3 (per-file recompute under the write lock, mismatch ⇒ git reset --hard + incident + run failed). It directly serves the charter's hardest trust bar — 'Machines never overwrite or delete human-authored text; a violation stops the beta immediately' — and the Overreliance row's demand for vigilance-independent mechanisms. The per-human-span granularity having no exact alpha precedent is fine: the gap report itself grades it survivor-grade, not a regression.

- **Recorded:** design.md §11 item (4) L978-981; design.md §6 L463-476 (apply/verify steps); charter Required Trust Scope, 'Human text' and 'Overreliance' rows
- **History:** Alpha's lineage ran vault-hash-drift (alpha.1, whole-file) → quarantine-on-scan (alpha.14) → ADR-127's deliberately asynchronous read-path consumption guard; the synchronous form was rejected there only for the READ path — 'a write on the read path races the workspace lock; refusal alone is already fail-closed' (history L2124). Beta.1 applies the synchronous gate on its own WRITE path, per file, under the per-file lock immediately before/after its own write (§6 step 3), so the race that motivated alpha's rejection structurally cannot occur where beta.1 put the gate. The alpha reason is respected, not re-opened.

### S5.4 — Universal quarantine-until-verified narrowed: human content trusted-by-default, no workspace foreign-edit scan, no read-path refuse-to-serve

**RECORD — sound but unrecorded** · verification: confirmed (skeptic found no prior record) · confidence: medium

The narrowing is nowhere adjudicated: §11 claims convergence on 'the consumption-guard / hash-manifest family' (L967-969) without naming the dropped scan or refuse-to-serve, the §18 resolutions cover only R1-R6, and no docs-history entry or charter exclusion touches it. On the merits it is sound: the charter attributes trust defenses only to vigilance-independent mechanisms (taint, manifest gate, revert, export gate) and never requires universal quarantine, and beta.1 enforces consumption integrity at point of use instead — green requires marker + current block hash + checks-row agreement, an edited block demotes to amber and re-checks, export refuses on unwarranted or superseded citations (§8 L828-836), and the periodic reconciliation pass flags artifact divergence into Review (§5 L421-427). The alpha scan's objects largely do not exist in beta.1's substrate: there is no overrides.jsonl, bibliography.bib is generated from stored CSL-JSON rather than consumed, and human notes are not embedded in retrieval (§5 L414-416). Serving foreign-edited content amber-with-framing rather than withholding it mirrors alpha.15's own stale-served-with-framing posture for non-correctness-bearing consumers, while the refuse behavior survives where it is load-bearing (the export gate).

- **Recorded:** nowhere - silent (§11 L967-969 claims family-level convergence; regression-review sweep L66-68 verified 'quarantine-and-verify realized as staging + the human-span manifest gate' but the Resolution adjudicates only R1-R6)
- **History:** ADR-127's universal invariant — every mutation non-consumable until verified, one shared check suite with a workspace-scan ingress covering all tracked paths incl. references.bib/overrides.jsonl, hash-mismatch refuses to serve and enqueues a scan — existed to make ADR-128's no-write-gate epistemics safe: 'errors become detectable, attributable, and reversible instead of gated at creation' (history L2122, L2124). It was a designed belt for the epistemic reversal, not the residue of a specific foreign-edit incident. Beta.1 addresses that reason by moving detection to point-of-use hash agreement plus export-time refusal and run-scoped revert, and by removing the consumption paths the scan guarded — but never says so.
- **Action:** Add a recorded divergence to design §11 (or an §18-style recorded decision): "Beta.1 narrows alpha.15's universal quarantine-until-verified: there is no workspace foreign-edit scan and no read-path refuse-to-serve. Consumption integrity is enforced at point of use — green requires marker + current block hash + `checks` row agreement, an edited block serves amber and re-checks, and export refuses on unwarranted or superseded citations — with the periodic reconciliation pass flagging artifact divergence into Review. The alpha scan's objects (overrides.jsonl, a hand-consumed references.bib, human notes in the retrieval substrate) do not exist in beta.1."

### S5.5 — Non-authoritative in-file %%prov:mc%% marker (three-way agreement, 'citation-correct' semantics) vs alpha.15's DB-only no-marker end-state

**LEAVE — deliberate and recorded** · verification: confirmed (citation audited) · confidence: high

The marker-as-claim contract is the recorded §18 fix of regression R1 (design §18 item 1, L1282-1286: verdict of record is the DB checks row bound to block_sha256; green requires marker + current hash + row agreement; rebuild/restore fails closed to amber), and design §4 L325-338 states the same decision with rationale in the body. The 'citation-correct' relabel and the UI-copy rule ('verified against anchors,' never 'safe/approved') are an explicit decision-with-rationale at §4 L311-320, grounded in the Wallat post-rationalization evidence with the faithfulness gap documented rather than papered over, and the charter's Overreliance row independently forbids crediting the marker as a defense. Beta.1's terminology gate being narrower than alpha's (which also blocked 'verified') is inside that recorded decision, and the load-bearing safety is carried by the byte-substring quote guarantee and export gate, not the label.

- **Recorded:** design.md §18 item 1 (L1282-1286); design.md §4 L311-338 (marker-is-claim + citation-correct naming with rationale); charter Required Trust Scope, 'Overreliance' row
- **History:** Alpha.15 removed in-file verdict state (ADR-126, PR-L #1179) because frontmatter check_status meant 'checking a note rewrote it, hand-edits forged status, and every consumer had to distrust the file it was reading,' with DB-loss failing closed to unchecked and rebuild ignoring forged frontmatter (history L2086, L2110, L2132, L2290). Beta.1 addresses each documented failure: a forged or restored mc marker cannot render green (no matching checks row / hash), rebuild demotes every mc to amber and re-earns via the $0 local Checker, and markers are trailing comments on machine blocks only, so checking never rewrites human content. The alpha failure mode (authoritative-until-scanned in-file status) is structurally closed, not re-opened.

### S5.6 — Derivation graph + transitive two-tier stale/invalid demotion cascade dropped (first version since alpha.1 with no multi-hop propagation)

**RECORD — sound but unrecorded** · verification: confirmed (skeptic found no prior record) · confidence: medium

No record adjudicates the drop: §11 item (4) compares the manifest gate to 'propagation-only demotion' only for the human-content-protection function, §1 L118-119 mentions 'a demotion cascade' merely as a batch-worklist example, and the docs-history 'Authorship asymmetry in propagation' adapt row targets alpha15, not beta1. On the merits the drop is sound because beta.1's substrate structurally removes what the graph walked: only fulltext plus opt-in digest chunks (keyed by citekey) are retrievable, so supersession's citekey-scoped exclusion covers derived chunks of the same work; export re-verifies every mapped claim at the gate; and verdicts are lazily re-derived at consumption via marker + block-hash + checks agreement instead of eagerly propagated — no machine artifact feeds another at depth ≥ 2 in a correctness-bearing way. Retraction/supersession fallout keeps its recorded consumers (§4 superseded machinery + weekly Crossref sweep) and renders as one batch worklist, honoring both halves of the alpha lesson (never serve stale as current; never flood attention). The silent part is the trade itself, which deserves a named home so the cascade is not re-proposed or its absence mistaken for an oversight.

- **Recorded:** nowhere - silent (§11 item (4) L978-981 covers only the human-protection comparison; §1 L118-119 presupposes a demotion-fallout worklist without a mechanism record)
- **History:** ADR-127/PR-C2 #1167: two-tier demotion (depth-1 demote, depth-≥2 stale-with-framing, retraction one level deeper) was motivated by the build-system stale-vs-invalid lesson — 'so one demoted foundational node cannot cascade a whole subgraph into an attention flood,' with hard exclusion rejected because it 'breaks cross-period queries' (history L2126); lineage runs to alpha.1's fama-exposure superseded_by walk. The mechanism presupposed alpha's multi-hop machine-consumes-machine artifact chains. Beta.1 moots that precondition rather than re-opening the failure: the retrieval substrate has no depth-≥2 machine consumption, and point-of-use verdict re-derivation replaces eager propagation.
- **Action:** Add an §18-style recorded decision (or §11 divergence entry): "Beta.1 carries no derivation graph and no transitive demotion cascade: verdicts are re-derived at consumption (marker + block-hash + `checks` agreement), supersession/retraction fallout routes through the `superseded` consumers (§4) and one batch worklist (§1), and no machine artifact consumes another's output at depth ≥ 2 in the retrieval substrate. Multi-hop propagation is re-earned the day machine outputs begin consuming each other (e.g. digests over digests)."

### S5.7 — Surface-wired apply-rate rubber-stamp detector dropped; capability moved to evaluation-protocol analytics

**LEAVE — deliberate and recorded** · verification: confirmed (citation audited) · confidence: medium

The honesty function survives intact in the charter-designated home: evaluation-protocol §3 reads the disposition stream through the same two-sided bands (sustained accept ≳90% = rubber-stamping, ≲20% = mistuned generator, n-stated with a sample floor) and §5 adds quarterly gate-efficacy sampling ('is review improving correctness or is it theater') that alpha.15 never had. This bands-plus-blind-re-review shape is a recorded decision — the docs-history borrow entry 'Disposition interpretation layer' adopts exactly it — and the charter's scope map assigns all evaluation-scope rows to the protocol, whose pre-registered cadence guarantees the stream is actually read. The docs-history failure pattern this area is prone to (enforcement half shipped, consumption half lost) is satisfied because the consumer is named in the governing documents; the non-backfillable inputs (accept-unedited vs accept-after-edit, decision time) are instrumented from day 1.

- **Recorded:** evaluation-protocol §3 L57-60 (two-sided bands) and §5 L82-85 (gate-efficacy sampling); docs-history borrow row 'Disposition interpretation layer' (L29); charter Scope Map (evaluation scope → evaluation-protocol) and Verdict-basis/Calibration rows
- **History:** ADR-128/PR-B3 #1165 made dispositions first-class non-backfillable telemetry with apply-rate bounds (>0.9 / <0.2) as the rubber-stamp detector because 'this is what makes calibrated falsifiable' — without recorded dispositions the theatre-check cannot be evaluated (history L2114). The motivating function was falsifiability of the review mechanism, an evaluation-time need, not a live-UI need; beta.1 preserves the recorded stream and its two-sided interpretation on the protocol's pre-registered cadence and strengthens it with gate-efficacy re-judging, so the reason alpha held the position is fully addressed in a different mechanism-form.
