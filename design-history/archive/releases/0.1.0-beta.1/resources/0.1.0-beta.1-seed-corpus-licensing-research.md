## Findings

- **PMC's Open Access Subset splits into three official, machine-readable
  license tiers, and licensing is not uniform corpus-wide.** Commercial Use
  Allowed (CC0, CC BY, CC BY-SA, CC BY-ND — `oa_comm` directory);
  Non-Commercial Use Only (CC BY-NC variants — `oa_noncomm`); Other (no
  machine-readable license, no license, or custom). A seed-corpus builder
  must filter to the Commercial-Use-Allowed tier and verify per article, never
  assume a blanket license for the subset. (confidence: high, vote: unanimous
  3-0/2-0 across multiple verifier passes)
  - Evidence: confirmed verbatim on three separate official NCBI/NLM pages;
    also confirms PMC prohibits systematic/bulk scraping of the main site —
    bulk assembly must go through the dedicated FTP/OAI-PMH/Cloud channels.
  - Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/tools/openftlist/ , https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/about/copyright/ , https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/tools/ftp/
  - Beta.1 implication: design §7's "~8 real, citable sources" corpus, if
    drawn from PMC, must be filtered to the `oa_comm` (Commercial Use Allowed)
    directory specifically, with each article's individual license verified —
    not just "PMC Open Access" as a blanket label.

- **PMC's bulk-redistribution mechanism is officially supported but is
  mid-transition right now.** FTP baseline + daily-incremental packages
  (XML/plain text) and per-article download packages are the sanctioned bulk
  route — but classic FTP bulk bundles were marked deprecated 2026-04-13, with
  full removal slated for **August 2026** (i.e., imminent relative to today's
  date), replaced by a new PMC Cloud Service on AWS. (confidence: high, vote:
  3-0, 2-0, 2-1)
  - Evidence: confirmed on NCBI's own FTP and OA-subset pages; a verifier
    flagged the live transition as directly relevant given the current date.
  - Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/tools/ftp/ , https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/tools/openftlist/
  - Beta.1 implication: any implementation work should check the PMC Cloud
    Service's *current* documentation, not the classic FTP workflow described
    in older secondary sources (including this project's own superseded
    planning docs) — the mechanism this research describes is itself
    time-sensitive.

- **PMC's dedicated "cc0 license[filter]" search does not isolate pure CC0
  content — it's a convenience grouping that also includes unrelated
  public-domain works (e.g. US-government-authored articles).** Treat it as a
  starting filter requiring per-article follow-up, not a precise CC0
  identifier. (confidence: high, vote: 3-0, 2-0)
  - Evidence: NLM's own technical bulletin states the filter covers
    "unrestricted Creative Commons or public domain (CC0) licenses" and
    explicitly clarifies the public-domain component includes
    US-government-agency-authored content; the current live PMC User Guide
    still labels this filter "CC0 (Public domain)," confirming the conflation
    persists today.
  - Source: https://www.nlm.nih.gov/pubs/techbull/nd15/nd15_pmc_filters_commons_articles.html
  - Beta.1 implication: a corpus-selection script cannot trust this filter
    alone as a redistribution gate — it must additionally check each
    candidate article's actual license statement.

- **arXiv is never the copyright holder of any e-print and, for the majority
  of papers, cannot itself grant redistribution rights to third parties.**
  Authors retain copyright and grant arXiv only a non-exclusive distribution
  license by default (not a copyright transfer, no sublicensing right); arXiv
  states verbatim it is "unable to grant others the right to distribute arXiv
  articles" for default-licensed papers — tools must link back to arXiv for
  those, not embed full text. (confidence: high, vote: 3-0/2-0 across
  sub-claims)
  - Evidence: confirmed verbatim across three official arXiv pages, including
    the direct quote above.
  - Source: https://info.arxiv.org/help/license/reuse.html , https://info.arxiv.org/help/bulk_data.html , https://info.arxiv.org/help/policies/submission_agreement.html
  - Beta.1 implication: an arXiv-sourced seed corpus can only bundle full text
    for papers where the *author* separately selected a permissive license
    (next finding) — arXiv hosting the paper is not itself sufficient
    permission.

- **Only a minority of arXiv papers carry a redistribution-permissive
  license, and the specific ~15-18% figure is a secondary-source estimate,
  not primary-confirmed.** Authors choose at submission from: arXiv's own
  non-exclusive license (majority default, not redistribution-permissive),
  CC BY 4.0, CC BY-SA 4.0, CC BY-NC-SA 4.0, CC BY-NC-ND 4.0, or CC0 1.0. Only
  CC BY / CC0 (and arguably CC BY-SA, with share-alike obligations) support
  unrestricted redistribution. (confidence: **medium** — downgraded because
  the 15-18% adoption figure comes from a Creative Commons "Open Preprints"
  project page and one arXiv paper on LLM training corpora, not arXiv's own
  primary documentation, and was not independently 3-vote verified as a
  standalone claim; one related sub-claim on the license menu split 1-1)
  - Evidence: arXiv's Terms of Use states verbatim "a very small subset of
    arXiv e-prints are submitted with licenses that permit redistribution."
  - Source: https://info.arxiv.org/help/policies/submission_agreement.html , https://info.arxiv.org/help/api/tou.html
  - Beta.1 implication: an arXiv-sourced bundle must be built by filtering
    specifically for the CC BY/CC0 minority — a random or citation-count-based
    selection of "famous" arXiv papers (e.g. Transformer, BERT-style seminal
    papers) will very likely include papers under arXiv's own
    non-redistributable default license, and must be checked per-paper before
    assuming eligibility.

- **arXiv's bulk full-text dumps (Kaggle dataset, AWS S3 buckets) exist but do
  not themselves confer a redistribution license — the per-paper license
  still governs.** (confidence: high, vote: 2-0)
  - Evidence: arXiv's bulk-data help page confirms both mechanisms exist,
    immediately followed by "we are thus unable to grant others the right to
    distribute arXiv articles."
  - Source: https://info.arxiv.org/help/bulk_data.html
  - Beta.1 implication: don't treat "it's in the bulk dump" as a license
    signal — the dump is a convenience access mechanism, not a rights grant.

**Explicitly refuted — do not restate:** "For the PMC cc0-filtered subset, NLM
states users may use and reproduce the content without needing to seek
special permission" (vote 1-2) — this specific framing did not hold up; per-article
verification remains necessary even within the cc0 filter (consistent with the
conflation finding above, not contradicting it).

**Unverified — re-check before relying on it:** "arXiv descriptive metadata
(title, abstract, authors, identifiers, classification) is released under
CC0 1.0" (2 of 3 verifier votes errored on infrastructure failures, not
content disagreement; very likely true given arXiv's public API ToU language
quoted in other verified claims, but not independently confirmed here).

## Explicitly incomplete — needs a follow-up pass, not a decision yet

The original research question asked for **specific, concrete candidate
papers/datasets** (an actual ~8-item bundle with confirmed per-item licenses)
and coverage of **DOAJ and Semantic Scholar/S2ORC**. Neither was delivered by
this pass:

- No specific candidate paper, DOI, or dataset was named or verified anywhere
  in the 23 confirmed claims — every confirmed finding is about licensing
  *mechanics* (how PMC/arXiv license and distribute content), not concrete
  bundle-ready items.
- DOAJ and Semantic Scholar/S2ORC were never examined — their search angle
  failed outright on rate limits before any source was fetched.

**This means design §7's "~8 real, citable sources" corpus pick remains
genuinely unresolved** — this research narrows *how* to evaluate a candidate
(PMC `oa_comm` tier + per-article check; arXiv CC BY/CC0 papers only,
verified per-paper) but does not itself supply the 8 items. A follow-up
research pass — or a manual selection-and-verification pass using the
mechanics established here — is required before this can support an actual
seed-corpus build.
