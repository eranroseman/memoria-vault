### S16.1 — Zotero-first reading substrate reverts the alpha.14 Zotero cut

**LEAVE — deliberate and recorded** · verification: confirmed (citation audited) · confidence: high

Design §17 item 1 explicitly records "Reading is Zotero-first" — primary read in Zotero's PDF reader, fulltext.md demoted to evidence substrate and kept as the full path for non-Zotero works — as a resolution "recorded so they are not re-litigated," and §16 records Zotero as an app reached over its read-only local API with no Obsidian-side Zotero plugin. The premise that motivated the alpha.14 cut (standalone CLI must not depend on a desktop reference manager) is itself deliberately reversed on the record: §11 item 10 names the host decision (Obsidian plugin + local daemon) as new beta.1 scope amending ADR-125, and §16 names the required ADR. The charter's null workflow is Zotero+Obsidian and the capture bar requires Zotero import, so Zotero-first reading serves the retention experiment directly. The two documented Zotero failure modes are avoided: the .bib auto-export second-source-of-truth is replaced by the read-only local API, and PDFs are copied into works/<citekey>/ so the corpus survives Zotero's death (design §7 L554–557; docs-history Reject "Zotero anti-patterns").

- **Recorded:** design §17 item 1 (L1241–1244); §11 item 10 (L987–989); §16 plugin-coexistence bullet (L1206–1211); docs-history Reject "Zotero anti-patterns" (L123)
- **History:** ADR-124 (history L2042, L2316) cut Zotero because "Alpha.14 makes Memoria a standalone CLI/engine. A required Zotero library or live Better BibTeX auto-export would violate that" — a premise, not a Zotero failure. Beta.1 reverses that premise on the record (§11 item 10, §16) and re-adopts alpha.11's read-only Local API discipline (history L1681); the genuinely earned Zotero lessons (stale .bib exports, deep-link PDF ownership dying with Zotero) are addressed by the local API and vault-owned PDF copies.

### S16.3 — One-way append-only Zotero annotation sync inverts the alpha.11 sourcing refusal

**LEAVE — deliberate and recorded** · verification: confirmed (citation audited) · confidence: high

Design §17 item 1 records the append-only annotation sync as part of the Zotero-first resolution folded into the body, and the mechanism is fully specified with its discipline in §7 L697–705 (one-way, keyed by Zotero annotation ID, dateAdded watermark, nothing flows back) and §5's append-only annotations table (L401–402), routed through the single-writer machine-write module. The alpha.11 refusal was premised on Memoria-owned PDF capture storing its own selectors; beta.1 removes that premise by making Zotero the primary reader, so annotation sync becomes the necessary conduit rather than a competing import path. The one-way pattern matches the settled community precedent (Notero/Better Notes/mdnotes) the gap report cites, and no write-back path exists to create the sync races the alpha line feared.

- **Recorded:** design §17 item 1 (L1241–1244); §7 (L697–705); §5 annotations table (L401–402)
- **History:** Alpha.11 §13 refused Zotero annotation import because "Memoria-owned PDF capture stores canonical quote/span/hash selectors itself" (history L1681) — a scoping decision under a Memoria-owned reading architecture, not a documented failure of annotation import. Alpha.14 then removed the whole Zotero surface on the standalone-CLI premise (ADR-124, history L1982). Beta.1 reverses both premises on the record (§17.1, §11 item 10), so the refusal's reason no longer applies.

### S16.4 — Exact-quote-only anchoring; W3C fuzzy re-anchoring deferred with a revive trigger

**LEAVE — deliberate and recorded** · verification: confirmed (citation audited) · confidence: high

The narrowing is an explicit decision-with-rationale in the design body: §7 L701–705 states the exact-quote bridge and that "fuzzy re-anchoring is Hypothesis-grade engineering, deferred until a measured orphan rate demands it," with the pre-registered revive trigger; §17 item 1 records the exact-quote anchor bridge as a resolution. The load-bearing half of the alpha position — never silently rebind or drop, failures surfaced as orphaned-anchor attention — is preserved intact via per-work orphan counting and annotations.status. Extractor-drift exposure is separately mitigated: ^pNNNN block IDs are assigned once at deterministic extraction with geometry in the anchors table keyed by text_sha256, rebuildable by re-running the same extraction.

- **Recorded:** design §7 (L701–705, explicit deferral with rationale and revive trigger); §17 item 1 (L1241–1244)
- **History:** ADR-129 bound the exact-then-fuzzy policy as "the Hypothes.is lesson": anchors are "re-anchored, never silently rebound or dropped" across extractor upgrades, with failures routed to orphaned-anchor attention (history L2140); ADR-126 relocated the excerpt to a W3C TextQuoteSelector (history L2076). The earned invariant is the never-silent posture, not the fuzzy algorithm; beta.1 keeps that invariant (visible orphans, pre-registered revive trigger) while deferring the fuzzy half until measured data justifies it.

### S16.7 — Mandatory standalone `work interview` elicitation dropped; 2–3-question hook retained

**LEAVE — deliberate and recorded** · verification: confirmed (citation audited) · confidence: high

The drop is adjudicated in an authoritative record: the docs-history Reject entry "Mandatory ingest interview" keeps it dead with a full rationale — a required LLM conversation per source violates no-mandatory-agent/PI-direct access, inflates exactly the per-source burden the retention experiment measures, and duplicates annotations→notes — and the Adapt entry "Researcher takeaways as digest inputs" records the surviving replacement shape (direct affordance, no mandatory agent). Beta.1 honors the elicitation intent in substance through the annotation sync plus processing-card promotion path (§7) and the retained 2–3-question hook in the digest pipeline, so the subjective layer the interview served still has a channel. The charter's Capture bar ("no mandatory per-item decisions") independently forbids a mandatory per-source interview, making the drop charter-required, not merely defensible.

- **Recorded:** docs-history Reject "Mandatory ingest interview" (L124) and Adapt "Researcher takeaways as digest inputs" (L92); charter Capture bar (requirements L29); design §7 processing-card path (L708–720)
- **History:** Alpha.11 made the interview "the hinge … not an optional side chat" on the Karpathy-lineage belief that conversation turns capture into knowledge (history L1679), and alpha.14/15 carried `work interview` in the loop with digest failing on a required-but-missing interview (history L1984, L2188) — an aspiration, never a position earned by a documented failure. Beta.1 addresses the same aim at lower per-source cost via annotations→notes plus the in-pipeline hook, exactly the substitution the Reject entry names.
