# Beta.1 — alpha-regression review

Date: 2026-07-04. Method: the full alpha.14 and alpha.15 design docs (recovered
from git, the alpha.15 omnibus at blob `09bf018f`) were mined for hard-won
invariants — 255 candidates — and each was adversarially checked against the
governing beta.1 documents ([0.1.0-beta.1-design.md](0.1.0-beta.1-design.md),
[0.1.0-beta.1-requirements.md](0.1.0-beta.1-requirements.md),
[0.1.0-beta.1-evaluation-protocol.md](0.1.0-beta.1-evaluation-protocol.md)),
defaulting to *not-a-regression* and treating any delta recorded in §11–§17 or
the charter's exclusions as deliberate. 158 candidates reached a verdict before
the tail hit session limits; the seven `regression-*` verdicts below were all
among the verified set (the workflow ran on both Fable 5 and Opus).

## Headline

- **151 of 158 verified invariants held** — 70 present, 67 **strengthened**, 14
  recorded-divergence. The clean-slate beta.1 preserved or sharpened the great
  majority of alpha's safety, honesty, integrity, cost, and survival
  invariants; the aphorism-worthy result is how *little* slipped.
- **Six distinct regressions**, none catastrophic, every one fixable with a
  small edit. Five are *weakened* (the mechanism exists but lost a protective
  property); one is *absent* (found by two readers independently).
- **The one that also breaks an internal contract:** in-file `mc` (green
  "citation-correct") is rendered straight from the file marker, so a
  file-only restore or a human edit under the marker keeps the verdict — yet
  §11 claims convergence with alpha on "provenance verdicts DB-side." That is
  a regression *and* a zero-tolerated-contradiction defect.
- **Two are honesty-surface regressions** (export gate too narrow; provider
  evidence not auditable); two are ops/robustness (backup-status doctor;
  rejection→fixture loop); one is schema-strictness traded for OKF permissive
  toleration (a defensible judgment call, flagged as borderline).

## Borderline / judgment calls

- **R6 (frontmatter strictness).** *Regression:* beta.1 lost alpha's
  reject-undeclared-fields validator and `x`-namespace confinement. *Not a
  regression:* beta.1 deliberately adopted OKF v0.1's permissive consumption
  model (§9 "consumers MUST NOT reject… unknown additional frontmatter keys"),
  and OKF §4.1 explicitly permits bare additional keys — so beta.1's looser
  stance is *more* OKF-idiomatic, not less. The recoverable middle: confine
  Memoria's **own** extensions to `x.*` (compatible with OKF, and it keeps the
  meaning layer clean) while still tolerating others' unknown keys on read.
  Worth doing only if the meaning-layer cleanliness is judged to outweigh the
  churn across every frontmatter example.
- **R5 (backup doctor).** Arguably out of scope — the charter's Backup-and-
  restore bar is met, and a proactive status report is polish. Include it only
  if "warn before disk-death" is felt to belong in the first beta.

## Checked and intact (swept, holding)

The invariant families verified present or **strengthened** span every
subsystem: the single-writer + staging + block-level human-content protection
spine; monotone never-green taint; quarantine-and-verify realized as
staging + the human-span manifest gate with pre==post byte-check and
auto-rollback; no-write-time-oracle and checks-only promotion; the
proposer-never-authority rule and act/ask/log routing (not an approval queue);
NLI tri-class verdicts with a first-class abstain (never cosine-as-truth) and
the adversarial calibration/HANS gate; coverage denominators, absence-as-
absence, and shadow-first scores; the keep-test (markdown + BibTeX survive the
tool); brokered fail-closed egress with the compiled allowlist and the
exfiltration linter; cross-store recoverability / observable atomicity with
paired rollback; discovery-proposes-never-imports and human-budgeted rounds;
per-operation model pins with test/live modes; the read-API as the sole read
path; the request-envelope integrity fields; and — the finding that started
this pass — OKF bundle conformance. Fourteen further deltas (agent/co-PI
machinery, kanban/spaces, hub/tag graph, claim-context fields, qmd, provider
attention routing) verified as **recorded** divergences in §11–§17 or the
charter's exclusions, not silent drops.

## Recommended disposition

R1 is the one I'd fix first and unconditionally — it is both a safety
regression and an internal contradiction with §11. R2 and R3 are real honesty/
auditability gaps with cheap, doctrine-fitting fixes. R4 is a one-sentence
addition. R5 and R6 are judgment calls to accept or decline explicitly (so the
drop is *recorded*, satisfying the same "name every divergence" discipline the
rest of the design follows).

## Resolution (2026-07-04)

Applied — see design **§18** for the recorded resolutions. R1–R4 fixed in the
body (R1: `checks` table + block-hash + rebuild-to-amber, §2/§4/§5; R2: export
refuses on superseded/retracted and unwarranted mapped claims, §8; R3:
content-addressed `payloads` store, §5/§7; R4: rejection→fixture ratchet,
§1/§10). R5 accepted in light form (per-store backup status on the inspector
pane; streaming-replication RPO declined for beta.1). R6 declined
(OKF-permissive toleration is deliberate; the `x.*` extension-namespace
confinement is deferred).
