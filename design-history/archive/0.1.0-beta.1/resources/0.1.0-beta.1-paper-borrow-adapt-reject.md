Scope: paper-centered findings folded into

## Borrow (take nearly directly)

| Idea | Source paper(s) | Beta.1 move |
|---|---|---|
| Confidence-thresholded off-the-shelf NLI with statement conversion | Mitchell et al. 2022 | Before Checker/stance NLI, convert each (question, quoted-anchor) pair into a declarative statement matching the NLI training distribution; keep a λ-style cutoff; treat equivalence as entailment in both orderings. |
| Tiny-dev-set threshold calibration | Sainz et al. 2021; Zhong et al. 2021 | Don't ship .8 as an assumed constant: a one-hour pass on a few dozen hand-labeled claim/anchor pairs from the real corpus is worth ~10 F1; weekly audit oversamples near-threshold items where miscalibration concentrates. |
| Five-aspect digest schema | Zhang et al. 2024 (MASSW) | Adopt the human-validated context / key idea / method / outcome / projected-impact schema for per-work digests and compose evidence/gap fields instead of inventing a template. |
| Recursive batch-and-condense digests | White et al. 2023 (CliniDigest) | Digest engine batches ~15 works and condenses recursively inside the ≤$0.10 ceiling; print the sources-used denominator (their 54%-of-sources result backs the printed denominator over any aggregate score). |
| vote-k seed selection | Su et al. 2022 | Guided corpus seeding picks the 20–40 works via the deterministic embedding → k-NN graph → discounted-degree algorithm; reuses the same embedding machinery as Ask's MMR. |
| TELeR prompt-level declaration | Santu & Feng 2023 | Document Ask/digest/synthesis prompts at a fixed TELeR level; the level feeds the deterministic token estimate and the normative price table. |
| Model id + prompt version in the run ledger | Feng & Liu 2026 | Each ledger entry records exact model identifier and prompt-template version, not just cost and pathspec — a mid-flight pin bump would otherwise silently confound the ChatGPT counterfactual, audits, and revert forensics. |
| Deterministic-first cascade with tier logging | Li et al. 2026 (CiteTracer) | Keep byte-substring/anchor-resolution deciding before NLI (the empirically winning order); log which tier decided each mc promotion so audit effort targets the model-judged residue. |
| Two-stage reconcile against authoritative records | Rao & Callison-Burch 2026 | Metadata correction runs as a distinct revise-against-Zotero/Crossref pass (78.3% fully correct, 0.8% regression) rather than an interleaved tool loop; both sources already sit on the egress allowlist. |
| Existence-gated prompts + structural abstain | Agrawal et al. 2022 | Digest/synthesis prompts ask "does X exist?" before "list X" and include an explicit empty path — byte-substring assertion protects quotes, not fabricated list items. |
| Instructions-after-data + stop sequences | Perez & Ribeiro 2022 | Place fixed instructions and output constraints after every nonce-delimited untrusted block and use stop sequences (63→52% and 60→48% hijack reductions); nonces alone are insufficient since delimiters are also an attack tool. |
| Structured-record, extract-then-answer synthesis prompt | Wu et al. 2025 (LongMemEval) | Present retrieved anchors as structured records and instruct extract-then-answer (~10 points even under oracle retrieval); fact-augmented key expansion is the priced fallback lever if Ask recall disappoints. |
| ALCE citation-audit rubric | Gao et al. 2023 (ALCE) | Weekly audit scores citation recall and precision with shortcut-robustness; the ~50%-unsupported rate for frontier models makes the entailment gate mandatory, not advisory. |
| Anytime-valid verdict statistics | Huang et al. 2025 (POPPER) | The 10-week experiment peeks weekly then decides — optional stopping; use e-values / e-process aggregation for proceed/redesign/kill so repeated looks stay statistically honest. |
| SRQR-structured reporting | O'Brien et al. 2014 | Structure the final verdict report against SRQR items; the researcher/developer hat-tag is the S6 reflexivity instrument that makes an n=1 verdict defensible. |
| One preattentive signal per job | Fekete et al. 2008; Healey & Enns 2012 | Review queue encodes risk rank as a single preattentive channel; taint gets its own dedicated hue as feature *presence* — never a conjunction of markers or an absence, which do not pop out. |
| Schema-constrained inter-step artifacts | Hong et al. 2024 (MetaGPT) | Where an engine chains >1 LLM call, pass schema-validated structured artifacts and verify each step mechanically; borrow the practice, not the multi-agent role-play. |
| Source-changed-since-capture check | Brandt et al. 2010 (Blueprint) | Lightweight "source changed since capture" signal on captured items, distinct from the taint/manifest integrity path. |
| Coverage-triggered re-query | Wu et al. 2023 (AutoGen) | When the coverage denominator is low, Ask may re-run retrieval with expanded context ("UPDATE CONTEXT") — the one transferable piece of an otherwise-rejected architecture. |

## Adapt (reshape to beta.1's local/checked/PI-directed model)

| Idea | Source paper(s) | Beta.1 move |
|---|---|---|
| Privacy as measured floor, not retention lever | Coopamootoo & Groß 2017; Acquisti et al. 2015 | Privacy is forgotten in appetitive compose/export moments; score trust incidents against objective events (egress-log audits, canary drills), render the no-OS-enforcement statement so it can't read as a reassurance badge, and surface "this text leaves your machine" at the priced button where salience measurably works. |
| Ask-before-act only where reversal is costly | Horvitz 1999 | Byte-identical revert makes pre-action approval unnecessary — cheap post-action reversal dominates. Keep Horvitz's threshold pattern exactly at the irreversible points: >$1 confirms, monthly cap, circuit breaker. |
| Capability-hierarchy ramp | Engelbart 1962 | Pre-register the expectation that verb mix shifts capture-heavy → ask/compose-heavy across 10 weeks, so a slow compose ramp reads as methodology reorganization, not failure; abandonment gate unchanged. |
| Design for instrumentation decay | Grudin 1988 | The researcher-hat does the tagging work; the developer-hat benefits. Keep everything possible passive, bind counterfactual capture to the compose-payoff moment, and pre-decide how to score weeks where the instrumentation (not the tool) was abandoned. |
| Log provenance friction as data | Ackerman 2000 | Rigid h/m/mc + permanent taint will chafe against fluid co-authorship; keep block granularity (the right coarseness) and log marker-fighting / retype-to-launder incidents as first-class evaluation data. |
| Scoped Ask + query-type segmentation | Wei et al. 2026 (RIR survey) | No local/free fix exists for reasoning-intensive retrieval; accept the lexical+semantic scope and segment Ask reliance by query type so reasoning-heavy failures diagnose as scoping (route to priced synthesis), not flow-kill. |
| Prove the embedding leg earns its place | Hu et al. 2026 (MemoryAgentBench); Ajith et al. 2024 (LitSearch); Tam et al. 2022; Buchmann & Gurevych 2026 | Evidence splits: BM25 dominates some memory benchmarks while dense wins paraphrase/implicit relations. Add a BM25-only vs RRF ablation to instrumentation before attributing Ask quality to fusion; keep both legs meanwhile; reranking only if LLM-based and priced. |
| Expect mc scarcity; never loosen | Malaviya et al. 2024 (ExpertQA); Gao et al. 2021 | Small-NLI under-promotion is systematic and fail-closed: treat scarce mc as normal, render supports/contrasts as revisable estimates, keep human review as the trust path. Contingency: 16–32 audit-derived labels suffice for a $0 local prompt-based fine-tune as a pre-registered redesign lever. |
| Multi-anchor entailment | Thorne et al. 2018 (FEVER) | ~1 in 6 claims needs composed evidence: Checker entails against the concatenation of all anchors a sentence cites, not per-anchor; κ=0.68 trained-human agreement bounds how authoritatively stance labels are ever presented. |
| Audit for completeness and near-misses | Buchmann & Gurevych 2026; Press et al. 2024 (CiteME) | Resolution+entailment miss incomplete citation sets and plausible-but-wrong papers that resolve fine; the audit protocol samples for evidence sufficiency and names "near-miss paper" and "citing-paper-instead-of-source" as expected failure classes. |
| Contrastive risk explanations in Review | Jacobs et al. 2021 | Experts abandon surprising unexplained output; the queue surfaces *why* an item is risk-ranked (distrust flag, taint, entailment failure) on demand at the diff — never as forced per-item explanation. |
| Notation pre-flight + verifiable markers | Blackwell et al. 2001 (CDs); Gilbert et al. 2025 | One-day cognitive-dimensions pass on the provenance notation before freezing syntax — the sha256 manifest is a textbook hidden dependency and %% markers score high on error-proneness under human editing; every rendered marker must be one click from its substrate (ledger entry, manifest, anchor). |
| Calibrate failure thresholds to normal-use baselines | Zheng et al. 2026 (LvL UP); Beneteau et al. 2020; Epstein et al. 2020 | Engagement is the criterion most likely to miss even in paid trials, and narrowing to few verbs is the normal trajectory; an engagement miss alone maps to targeted redesign, reserving kill for pre-declared compound conditions; abandon/resume gating on a resumption attempt is validated. |
| Parallel redesign candidates | Dow et al. 2010 | On a targeted-redesign verdict, sketch 2–3 divergent candidates for the failing flow before committing — serial iteration under critique breeds fixation, and each redesign is existential under the two-redesigns=kill rule. |
| Staged extraction inside the digest budget | Jacobson et al. 2026 (Elhuyar) | k=10 consensus re-extraction is flatly unaffordable; adapt the cheap yes/no-relevance-before-expensive-extraction stage and route middling-confidence items to the risk-ranked queue rather than blocking writes. |

## Reject (tempting but conflicts with beta.1 integrity or scope)

| Idea | Source paper(s) | Beta.1 move |
|---|---|---|
| Machine hypothesis/idea generation | Gottweis et al. 2025; Baek et al. 2025; Li et al. 2024 (CoI); Wang et al. 2024 (SciMON); Yang et al. 2024 (MOOSE); Xu et al. 2025 (DeepReport) | The tempting adjacent product. Measured quality is weak (novelty 3.2/5; "low technical depth"), it needs off-allowlist egress (Semantic Scholar etc.), and buys novelty by sacrificing validity. Compose stays human; gaps stay deterministic. Keep only the structural echo: Gottweis's supervisor + credential-free workers already parallels memoriad. |
| Deep research / external literature discovery | Xu & Peng 2025; Huang et al. 2025 (DR survey); Xiong et al. 2026 (AutoResearchBench, <10% accuracy); Kang & Xiong 2024 (agents lose to BM25); Gridach et al. 2025 | Unsolved even for frontier agents and requires browser automation + open egress that dissolves the containment story. Architecture-protecting exclusion; revisit only when benchmarks move an order of magnitude. |
| Credentialed, socketed LLM orchestrator | Abbasian et al. 2024 (openCHA) | The reasoning engine holding credentials and network access is the exact inversion of beta.1's broker; engines stay credential-free and socketless. |
| Agent runtimes / multi-agent frameworks | Wang et al. 2025 (OpenHands); Wu et al. 2023 (AutoGen); Yue et al. 2026 | Shell+browser+code-exec makes injection-to-exfiltration a single hop for adversarial PDFs; multi-agent coordination pathologies are structural. Defend as a category difference, not a capability gap to close later. |
| Machine-owned knowledge store / world model / KG substrate | Long 2026 (AI-Supervisor); Qiao et al. 2026 (SciAtlas); Ghafarollahi & Buehler 2024 (SciAgents) | Duplicates span-level provenance at graph level, breaks the keep-test and single-writer invariant; KG payoffs materialize only at web scale, and graph-traversal + LLM elaboration fabricates precise-sounding numbers. The vault + in-file provenance IS the world model. |
| Self-evolving personalization / agent-curated memory | Xu et al. 2026 (NanoResearch); Qu et al. 2026 (CORAL) | Policy/memory layers that silently reshape behavior across sessions are unauditable and break run-scoped provenance and reversibility; bet retention on trustworthy determinism. |
| Machine-drafted compose / auto-research writing | Lu et al. 2024 (AI Scientist); Yamada et al. 2025 (v2); Tang et al. 2025; Funkquist et al. 2023 (CiteBench) | Reward-hacking, ~57% dataset overlap missed by three expert reviewers, best consistency 2.36/5, LLM-judged quality claims only. "The researcher will notice" is not a safety mechanism; machine effort stays on checkable artifacts. |
| Parametric fill-in (recitation, generated knowledge, weight-memory KB) | Sun et al. 2023 (RECITE); Liu et al. 2022 (GKP); Taylor et al. 2022 (Galactica) | Proven accuracy wins that manufacture unfalsifiable provenance and hollow out the coverage denominator; the synthesis prompt must never instruct the model to fill gaps from its own knowledge — prefer an honest "corpus has nothing". |
| Local cross-encoder reranker | Su et al. 2025 (BRIGHT) | Looks like a free quality win; actively degrades reasoning-intensive retrieval (14.3 → 8.3 at k=100). Rerank only LLM-based and priced. |
| Sample-consensus as verification or quality upgrade | Wang et al. 2023 (self-consistency); Imani et al. 2023 (MathPrompter) | Multiplies per-call cost ~40x against fixed ceilings and validates against the model's own distribution; even independent derivations converge on shared errors. Agreement-among-samples is never a Checker signal. |
| Watermarking machine text | Kirchenbauer et al. 2024 | No logit access to the pinned API tier; vault machine spans are short and low-entropy; a probabilistic signal is strictly weaker than the byte-exact span manifest already committed to. |
| Researcher-facing prompt configuration | Strobelt et al. 2022 (PromptIDE) | Imports a documented prompt-curation burden the null workflow doesn't impose, attacking attention-minutes parity; prompts stay pinned, versioned engine internals shipped via releases. |
| Proactive goal inference from the event log | Horvitz et al. 1998 (Lumière) | Beta.1 has exactly the event stream Lumière needed, so the temptation will recur; record the refusal explicitly — the event log exists for evaluation only, never for intervention timing. |
| LLM capture enrichment (auto-tagging, claim sidecars, ingest prompt chains) | Shahi & Hummel 2026 (~50% topic-label error); Yu & Wang 2026 (Knows); Wu et al. 2022 (PromptChainer); Li et al. 2025 (SciLitLLM) | Would write high-error m-class metadata into the vault, inflate the review/taint surface, and import cascading-error debugging; capture stays deterministic — flag, don't LLM-repair. |
| Machine-first vault restructuring | Liu et al. 2026 (ARA) | Take the measured benefit (bindings, anchors) as a layer on human markdown; refuse demoting the narrative to a "compiled view" — it breaks the keep-test, and ARA's own extension results show machine-first structure costs even machines. |
| Synthetic users / simulated self-report | Hämäläinen et al. 2023 | LLM self-report is human-indistinguishable; never substitute simulated responses for the real single-user event log in the retention experiment. |

## Immediate design feed

1. **Checker spec:** add statement-conversion preprocessing; entail against the concatenation of all anchors a sentence cites; treat equivalence as bidirectional entailment. (Mitchell 2022; Thorne 2018)
2. **Pre-launch calibration task:** hand-label a few dozen corpus claim/anchor pairs to tune stance/Checker cutoffs and validate zero-shot transfer, then freeze — any change is a new pre-registered arm; audit oversamples near-threshold items and NLI-tier promotions. (Sainz 2021; Zhong 2021; Li 2023 DIVERSE; Perez 2021; CiteTracer 2026; Karpas 2022)
3. **Ledger schema:** record exact model id and prompt-template version per run; re-validate every prompt-based engine after any pin change. (Feng & Liu 2026; Lu 2022; Zhao 2021)
4. **Supply chain:** pin and hash deberta-mnli and bge-small weights from a trusted source — a poisoned checker silently defeats never-green taint. (Xu 2022)
5. **Prompt-template standard:** instructions and output constraints after every nonce-delimited untrusted block; stop sequences; no step-by-step scaffolds over untrusted text; existence-gating plus explicit abstain for list outputs. (Perez & Ribeiro 2022; Shaikh 2023; Agrawal 2022)
6. **Digest engine spec:** MASSW 5-aspect schema; recursive batch-condense within the $0.10 ceiling; staged yes/no relevance filter before extraction. (Zhang 2024; White 2023; Jacobson 2026)
7. **Audit protocol rewrite:** ALCE recall/precision rubric; sample for evidence completeness; name near-miss and citing-paper failure classes; size the sample assuming a double-digit fabrication base rate; weight toward marginal/unusual items. (ALCE 2023; Buchmann 2026; CiteME 2024; Ouyang 2022; Alkhatib & Bernstein 2019)
8. **Evaluation plan:** e-value/anytime-valid verdict arithmetic; pre-register the verb-mix-shift expectation and engagement-shortfall calibration — an engagement miss alone maps to targeted redesign. (POPPER 2025; Engelbart 1962; Zheng 2026; Beneteau 2020)
9. **Event-log additions:** BM25-only vs RRF ablation flag; Ask reliance segmented by query type; relegation leading indicator (empty-Ask cluster → capture-only narrowing); export-refusal counts (read refusals as the gate working); confirm-latency as fatigue signal; provenance-friction/laundering incidents; "corpus has nothing" scored as correct behavior, tracked separately. (Hu 2026; Wei 2026; Luger & Sellen 2016; AutoResearchClaw 2026; Waldman 2020; Ackerman 2000; PaperQA 2023)
10. **Provenance rendering spec:** dedicated preattentive taint channel (feature presence, never a marker conjunction); mc styling reads "verified against anchors", never safe/approved; one-click marker→substrate traversal; paid synthesis carries the same per-quote stance labels and coverage denominator as raw Ask — no neutral narrative overview; one-day CDs pass before freezing syntax. (Healey & Enns 2012; Sundar 2020; Gilbert 2025; Govers 2026; Blackwell 2001)
