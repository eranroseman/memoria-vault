# Claim

Across prospective cohorts, people who adhere more closely to a Mediterranean diet have lower total and cardiovascular mortality.

# Evidence

- A Greek cohort of about 22,000 adults found that a higher 0–9 adherence score tracked lower total and cardiovascular mortality, with the association strengthening as the score rose [trichopoulou2003].
- A meta-analysis of prospective cohorts found that a 2-point higher adherence score predicted lower mortality and lower cardiovascular incidence and mortality [sofi2010].
