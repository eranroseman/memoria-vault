# Claim

The Lyon benefit appeared without a large cholesterol change, implying the Mediterranean-style diet acts through non-lipid pathways.

# Evidence

- In the Lyon Diet Heart Study the protective effect was seen even though traditional risk factors, including serum cholesterol, did not differ much between the diet and control groups, pointing to mechanisms other than lipid lowering [delorgeril1999].

# Connections

This sharpens *why* the diet works in secondary prevention, so it `supports` [[secondary-prevention-benefit-post-mi|A Mediterranean diet lowers recurrent events after a heart attack]]. If the benefit were purely about cholesterol, the diet would be redundant with statins; that it is not is part of what makes the dietary pattern interesting.
