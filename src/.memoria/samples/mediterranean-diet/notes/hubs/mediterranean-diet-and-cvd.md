# Shape of the topic

This hub organizes what the sample vault holds about whether a Mediterranean diet protects the heart, and how confident we can be.

The cluster is **not all agreement**. The trial evidence ([[meddiet-reduces-major-cv-events|A Mediterranean diet reduces major cardiovascular events in high-risk adults]], with its likely mechanism [[evoo-and-nuts-active-components|Extra-virgin olive oil and nuts are the components most linked to the benefit]]) supports a causal reading. Against it sits [[observational-associations-confounded|Observational Mediterranean-diet benefits may be confounded by healthy lifestyle]] — the caution that cohort signals may be confounded by healthy lifestyle — sharpened by [[predimed-randomization-caveat|PREDIMED's randomization deviation qualifies but did not overturn its result]], a reminder that even the trials have seams. The shape to notice is that the trials are what let the causal claim survive the caution.

Underneath that headline tension sits a fuller evidence base. The hypothesis itself traces back to [[seven-countries-origin-of-hypothesis|The Seven Countries Study originated the diet–heart hypothesis]], and the observational pillar that grew from it — [[higher-adherence-lower-mortality|Higher Mediterranean-diet adherence is associated with lower mortality]], with adherence pinned down by [[adherence-measured-by-score|Mediterranean-diet adherence is operationalized as a 0–9 dietary score]] — points the same direction as the trials, which is the convergence captured in [[observational-and-trial-evidence-converge|Observational and trial evidence converge on the Mediterranean-diet benefit]]. On the trial side, [[secondary-prevention-benefit-post-mi|A Mediterranean diet lowers recurrent events after a heart attack]] adds the already-sick population, and [[lyon-benefit-beyond-lipids|The Lyon benefit appeared without a large change in cholesterol]] hints the diet works through non-lipid pathways. The randomization seam is documented concretely in [[predimed-original-retracted|PREDIMED's original 2013 report was retracted over randomization deviations]].

# The gap

Every claim here is about **high-risk or already-sick adults in Mediterranean and European populations** (Spanish and French trials, a Greek cohort). The corpus says nothing about **primary prevention in low-risk people**, or whether the benefit **transports to other populations and diets**. That hole is intentional: the coverage map surfaces it when you first map the corpus, and at the end it becomes the gap that sends you back out to find new sources — your first turn of the compounding loop.
