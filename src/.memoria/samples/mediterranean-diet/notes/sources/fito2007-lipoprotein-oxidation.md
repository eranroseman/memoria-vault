# In my words

This PREDIMED substudy looked below clinical events at a mechanism: whether Mediterranean-diet interventions changed lipoprotein oxidation. Participants assigned to Mediterranean diets had more favorable oxidation markers than the control group, especially in the intervention centered on virgin olive oil. The source does not prove by itself that oxidation changes caused fewer cardiovascular events, but it makes the trial result more biologically plausible.

# Worth distilling

- Mediterranean-diet interventions in PREDIMED improved lipoprotein oxidation markers compared with control advice.
- Virgin olive oil gives the corpus a plausible oxidative-stress pathway for why the dietary pattern might protect the vasculature.

# Tensions

This strengthens [[evoo-and-nuts-active-components|Extra-virgin olive oil and nuts are the components most linked to the benefit]] by pointing to a mechanism, but it remains a biomarker outcome. It should not be read as an independent cardiovascular endpoint like [[estruch2018-predimed|Estruch et al. 2018 — PREDIMED primary-prevention trial]].
