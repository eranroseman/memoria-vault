# Claim

Observational cohort evidence and randomized trial evidence point the same way on the cardiovascular benefit of a Mediterranean diet, which strengthens the overall case.

# Evidence

- A large Greek cohort found higher adherence associated with lower total and cardiovascular mortality [trichopoulou2003].
- The PREDIMED randomized trial found roughly 30% fewer major cardiovascular events on a Mediterranean diet supplemented with extra-virgin olive oil or nuts than on a control diet [estruch2018].

# Connections

The two evidence types have complementary weaknesses — cohorts can be confounded, single trials can fail to replicate — so their agreement is more persuasive than either alone. This `supports` [[meddiet-reduces-major-cv-events]] and is the direct answer to [[observational-associations-confounded]]: the confounding worry bites the cohorts, but the trial is not vulnerable to it in the same way.
