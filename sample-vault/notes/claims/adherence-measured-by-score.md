# Claim

Adherence is operationalized as a 0–9 dietary score that cohorts relate to outcomes.

# Evidence

- The Greek cohort assigned each participant a 0–9 score — points for higher intake of vegetables, fruit, legumes, fish, and a favorable unsaturated-to-saturated fat ratio, fewer points for meat and dairy — and related that score to mortality [trichopoulou2003].

# Connections

This is a definitional claim: it makes explicit what "adherence" *means* in the cohort literature, which is what lets results like [[higher-adherence-lower-mortality]] be pooled and compared across studies. Different teams use slightly different scores, so the scale is a modeling choice, not a fact of nature.
