# Claim

The hypothesis that a Mediterranean dietary pattern is associated with lower coronary heart disease death rates originated in the Seven Countries Study.

# Evidence

- Following groups of middle-aged men across seven countries for fifteen years, the study related habitual diet — especially the share of calories from saturated fat — to coronary death rates, with lower rates where the diet was closer to the Mediterranean pattern [keys1986].

# Connections

This is the origin point of the whole cluster, but it is observational and ecological: differences between whole populations cannot rule out other explanations, so it sits under [[observational-associations-confounded]]. It generated the idea that the later trials were built to test.
