# Claim

A Mediterranean diet lowers the rate of major cardiovascular events — heart attack, stroke, and cardiovascular death — in adults at high cardiovascular risk.

# Evidence

- PREDIMED, a randomized trial in about 7,400 high-risk adults, found roughly 30% fewer major cardiovascular events on a Mediterranean diet supplemented with extra-virgin olive oil or nuts than on a control diet [estruch2018].
- The Lyon Diet Heart Study found large reductions in recurrent cardiac events on a Mediterranean-style diet after a first heart attack [delorgeril1999].

# Connections

Two randomized trials — one primary-prevention-like, one secondary prevention — point the same way. The standing counter-claim is [[observational-associations-confounded]], but that caution targets cohort data, not these trials.
