# In my words

The Lyon Diet Heart Study randomized 605 patients who had survived a first heart attack to either a Mediterranean-style diet (rich in alpha-linolenic acid, from more bread, vegetables, fruit, fish, and a canola-oil margarine) or the usual post-infarction advice. After a median of about four years the Mediterranean-diet group had a large reduction in cardiac death and non-fatal heart attack. Because everyone had already had an MI, this is *secondary* prevention — the benefit was measured in an already-sick population.

# Worth distilling

- A Mediterranean-style diet lowered recurrent cardiac events in patients who had already had a heart attack.
- The protective effect appeared without large changes in traditional risk factors like cholesterol, suggesting the diet acts through other pathways.

# Tensions

This is secondary prevention in post-MI patients; PREDIMED (estruch2018) is closer to primary prevention in high-risk-but-event-free adults. Whether the same benefit holds for low-risk primary prevention is not something either trial answers.
